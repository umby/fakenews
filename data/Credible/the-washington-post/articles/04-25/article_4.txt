for conservative news publications, a <a designed to help farmers and ranchers, an assurance that construction will begin soon on a wall along the nation\u2019s southern border \u2014 \u201c<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/video/politics/trump-the-wall-is-going-to-get-built/2017/04/25/1b4a63ac-29f0-11e7-9081-f5405f56d3e4_video.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">The wall\u2019s going to get built</a>,\u201d the president said \u2014\u00a0and media briefings by senior administration officials who gave their takes on Trump\u2019s successes.\u00a0</p> <p>And by Wednesday, the White House plans to unveil its tax plan, which would\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/economy/trump-seeks-15-percent-corporate-tax-rate-even-if-it-swells-the-national-debt/2017/04/24/0c78a35c-2923-11e7-be51-b3fc6ff7faee_story.html?utm_term=.5a0d1062154d\u00a0\" target=\"_self\">cut the corporate tax rate drastically</a>, to 15 percent.\u00a0</p> <p>Trump has called Saturday\u2019s 100-day marker \u2014 a remnant of President Franklin D. Roosevelt\u2019s initial flurry of legislative action \u2014 an \u201cartificial\u201d construct, and he is not incorrect. Yet the kinetic energy emanating from the West Wing, which at times borders on frenetic, reveals a White House eager to cross the threshold with some tangible wins.\u00a0</p> <p>The whirlwind of activity this week seems aimed at demonstrating forward momentum from a young administration criticized for a lack of signature legislative achievements \u2014 a sense that doing something, anything, is better than the perception of stagnation.\u00a0</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-photo-left\" style=\"width:300px;\"> <a name=\"fa6189e79f60b2e597640076059a9da561324cde\"></a> <img alt=\"\" class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1024w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/25/National-Politics/Images/Botsford170410Trump13831.JPG?uuid=qmQmTh4bEee7WadMyvHQLw\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/25/National-Politics/Images/Botsford170410Trump13831.JPG?uuid=qmQmTh4bEee7WadMyvHQLw\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/25/National-Politics/Images/Botsford170410Trump13831.JPG?uuid=qmQmTh4bEee7WadMyvHQLw\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/25/National-Politics/Images/Botsford170410Trump13831.JPG?uuid=qmQmTh4bEee7WadMyvHQLw\"/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">The confirmation of Supreme Court Justice Neil M. I won\u2019t bother reading everything.\u201d</p> <p>Trump is under considerable pressure, some of it self-imposed, to deliver. From funding construction of the border wall to spurring $1 trillion in infrastructure investments over the next decade, the president\u00a0has implemented zero of the 10 major pieces of legislation he promised as a presidential candidate for his first 100 days in\u00a0<a href=\"https://assets.donaldjtrump.com/_landings/contract/O-TRU-102316-Contractv02.pdf\" target=\"_self\">a contract with American voters.</a> </p> <p>Only one bill has been introduced in Congress \u2014 an ill-fated measure to scale back President Barack Obama\u2019s health-care law that culminated in\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/the-closer-the-inside-story-of-how-trump-tried--and-failed--to-make-a-deal-on-health-care/2017/03/24/3e6353d6-0fdc-11e7-9d5a-a83e627dc120_story.html?utm_term=.05dabbb9222d\u00a0\" target=\"_self\">an embarrassing defeat</a> at the hands of Trump\u2019s own party.\u00a0White House officials and several Republican lawmakers said Tuesday that they were nearing a deal to try again, though details were sparse.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/everyone-tunes-in-inside-trumps-obsession-with-cable-tv/2017/04/23/3c52bd6c-25e3-11e7-a1b3-faff0034e2de_story.html?utm_term=.533c78535b98\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">\u2018Everyone tunes in\u2019: Inside Trump\u2019s obsession with cable TV</a>]</i> </p> <p>In many ways, Trump, more than any modern president before him, runs his White House like a television drama, believing that sometimes projecting an image of energy and progress is as important, if not more so, than the reality.\u00a0</p> <p>But Peter Wehner, a former official in the George W. Bush White House who is now a senior fellow at the Ethics and Public Policy Center, said no\u00a0administration can bluff its way through four years.\u00a0</p> <p>\u201cYou have to produce,\u201d Wehner said.\u00a0 \u201cUltimately the achievements will matter. Gorsuch on the Supreme Court.</p> <p>\u201cWe see a president that\u2019s working at breakneck speed and somebody who\u2019s going as fast as he can in the confines of the law, running through that punch list of promises he made during the campaign,\u201d Reince Priebus, Trump\u2019s chief of staff, said Tuesday.</p> <p>But on his largest promises \u2014 those boomed from campaign trail stages and enshrined in his voter contract \u2014 Trump has fallen short, a gap in signature legislative triumphs that has sent the president and his aides scrambling to notch victories, some of them more tenuous and less substantial than others. \u00a0</p> <p>Out of 60 promises from Trump\u2019s voter contract, <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/politics/trump-promise-tracker/\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">The Washington Post\u2019s Fact Checker finds</a> that five have been kept, five have been broken and 36 others have had no action at all. The remainder have either been launched or are in limbo. </p> <p> </p> <p>Asked about Trump\u2019s failure to implement many of the key items in the contract, which he unveiled in October, Marc Short, the White House director for legislative affairs, said the administration had, in some ways, run up against the inherently slow-moving federal bureaucracy.</p> <p>\u201cThere are certain promises that you need to work with House and Senate leadership on, and it\u2019s a process,\u201d Short said in a morning meeting with reporters.\u00a0\u201cI think on the House side, obviously, the health-care legislation took longer than we would have wanted, but we\u2019re excited as to where that stands today, and we think we\u2019ll get that completed.\u201d</p> <p>Short said the administration was not backing down on its commitment to its early promises but added,\u00a0\u201cPerhaps the timetable was ambitious.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/trump-says-his-first-100-days-have-been-a-historic-success-history-disagrees/2017/04/25/3ef61374-2616-11e7-a1b3-faff0034e2de_story.html?hpid=hp_no-name_opinion-card-d%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.b50cc67eaf7f\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Opinion: Trump says his first 100 days have been a historic success. History disagrees.</a>]</i> </p> <p>Still, in the run-up to the 100-day mark, the administration has become a whirling dervish of activity.\u00a0</p> <p>In a memo Tuesday that contained a number of factual inaccuracies \u2014 including <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/fact-checker/wp/2017/04/25/the-white-house-boo-boo-in-counting-roosevelts-executive-orders/?utm_term=.4fac56c16a5f\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">a claim</a> that Roosevelt signed only nine executive orders, rather than the actual 99, in his first 100 days \u2014 the White House boasted of the president\u2019s\u00a0\u201chistoric accomplishment,\u201d citing the 13\u00a0Congressional Review Act resolutions, the 28 laws, and the 30 executive orders that Trump has implemented or passed.\u00a0</p> <p>The president\u2019s team\u00a0has also deployed Cabinet secretaries throughout the country\u00a0to tout what they say are Trump\u2019s robust successes. And Saturday, the president will headline a 100-day political rally in Harrisburg, Pa.\u00a0</p> <p>But even as Trump sought to project strength, a federal judge in San Francisco delivered <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/social-issues/2017/04/25/c9e212c8-29f7-11e7-b605-33413c691853_story.html?hpid=hp_rhp-top-table-main_sanctuary-cities-550pm%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.f6d77e19c572\" target=\"_self\">a new setback\u00a0Tuesday</a>, blocking\u00a0the administration\u2019s plans to withhold federal funding from \u201csanctuary cities,\u201d those that refuse to detain undocumented\u00a0immigrants for deportation.</p> <p>On Capitol Hill, Republicans largely defended the president, with some seeking to separate his domestic achievements from his foreign policy moves as they reflected on what Trump has and has not achieved so far.\u00a0</p> <p>Sen. John McCain (R-Ariz.), who had dinner with Trump on Monday night, said he was pleased with Trump\u2019s national security team and praised the cruise missile strikes the president launched in Syria. But he sized up Trump\u2019s domestic agenda differently.\u00a0\u201cObviously, it\u2019s been stalled,\u201d McCain said.</p> <p>However, McCain partially blamed members of the hard-line conservative House Freedom Caucus for thwarting the effort to overhaul Obama\u2019s Affordable Care Act last month, echoing a criticism Short had offered more gently earlier in the day.\u00a0</p> <p>\u201cWe\u2019ve learned that the House Republican Party, to its credit, is enormously diverse in its opinions, but that also sometimes creates larger challenges in bringing them together on a big legislative issue,\u201d Short said, reflecting on lessons his legislative-affairs team had learned in the failed health-care effort.\u00a0</p> <p>Rep. Chris Collins (R-N.Y.), a Trump ally, faulted Congress for the president\u2019s lack of domestic accomplishments.\u00a0</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/powerpost/paloma/daily-202/2017/04/25/daily-202-trump-is-caving-on-border-wall-funding-after-showing-his-base-that-he-tried/58fea413e9b69b3a72331ec4/\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">The Daily 202: Trump is caving on border wall funding after showing his base that he tried</a>]</i> </p> <p>\u201cCongress has to pass bills for him to sign them into law,\u201d Collins said. \u201cIf there is a frustration, it\u2019s really aimed at Congress, not the president.\u201d</p> <p>For congressional Democrats, who have waged a full-fledged war against much of the president\u2019s agenda, there is some relief that he has not delivered on many of his promises, most notably his vow on the ACA, also known as Obamacare.\u00a0\u00a0</p> <p>But Democrats are also trying to blame Trump for what he hasn\u2019t accomplished. They argue that while he campaigned on a populist platform, vowing to help average Americans, he has instead spearheaded efforts that benefit the wealthy at the expense of the working class.\u00a0\u00a0</p> <p>In some ways, Trump\u2019s blustery two-step leading up to Saturday is simply the repackaging of a strategy he learned as a real estate developer \u2014\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/book-party/wp/2015/07/30/i-just-binge-read-eight-books-by-donald-trump-heres-what-i-learned/?utm_term=.487fce18cdc3\" target=\"_self\">a technique he described in\u00a0\u201cThe Art of the Deal\u201d as\u00a0\u201ctruthful hyperbole.\u201d</a> In the 1987 book, he chronicled creating an aura of success before he\u2019d actually achieved it \u2014 such as when he ordered\u00a0his Atlantic City construction crews to dig up dirt on one side of a site to simply deposit it back on the other, in order to present a sense of progress.\u00a0</p> <p>But\u00a0Warren Tompkins, a longtime Republican strategist based in South Carolina, said that at some point, voters will demand evidence of signature legislation.\u00a0</p> <p>\u00a0\u201cOur problem is people voted to give us the keys to the bus, and we\u2019ve forgotten how to drive,\u201d Tompkins said.\u00a0</p> <p>Amid Trump\u2019s struggles, even the Jimmy Carter Presidential Library felt emboldened this week. On Monday, the library posted\u00a0<a href=\"https://twitter.com/CarterLibrary/status/856474432164044800\" target=\"_self\">a tweet</a> noting the laws and executive orders President Carter had signed in his first 100 days, before ending with the most devastating statistic of all\u00a0\u2014 Carter\u2019s approval rating of 63 percent.\u00a0</p> <p>Trump, the least-popular new president in modern
