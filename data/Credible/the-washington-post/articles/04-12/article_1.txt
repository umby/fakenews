<p>When Stephen K. Bannon reported for work Wednesday, he did not act like a man who had just been publicly humiliated by his boss. </p> <p>The White House chief strategist cycled in and out of the Oval Office for meetings with President Trump and took a seat in the front row of the East Room for the afternoon visit of NATO\u2019s secretary general, flanked by some of the very advisers with whom he has been feuding.</p> <p>But for Bannon, the day\u2019s routine obscured the reality that he is a marked man \u2014 diminished by weeks of battles with the bloc of centrists led by Trump\u2019s daughter and son-in-law and cut down by the president himself, who belittled Bannon in an interview with the New York Post.</p> <p>The president\u2019s comments were described by White House officials as a dressing-down and warning shot, though one Bannon friend, reflecting on them Wednesday, likened Bannon to a terminally ill family member who had been moved into hospice care.</p> <p id=\"U12001661561977N7H\">The man not long ago dubbed the \u201cshadow president\u201d \u2014 with singular influence over Trump\u2019s agenda and the workings of the federal government \u2014 is struggling to keep his job with his portfolio reduced and his profile damaged, according to interviews Wednesday with 21 of Trump\u2019s aides, confidants and allies. Some colleagues described Bannon as a stubborn recluse who had failed to build a reservoir of goodwill within the West Wing.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"analysis\" data-duration=\"136640\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"How the Bannon-Kushner feud sums up the Trump administration's inner turmoil\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58ed1fcbe4b07c67ff039225\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/04-11-2017/t_1491935165195_name_Botsford161201TrumpIN9146.JPG\" data-uuid=\"5dd1f35a-1ee4-11e7-bb59-a74ccaf1d02f\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"7b5i2s_hpS0\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\"> (Peter Stevenson/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>\u201cBannon is a brilliant pirate who has had a huge impact,\u201d said former House speaker Newt Gingrich, a Trump supporter. \u201cBut White Houses, in the end, are like the U.S. Navy \u2014 corporate structures and very hard on pirates.\u201d </p> <p>For now, at least, Bannon may survive the turmoil, and he and other White House staffers are striving to be on their best behavior after their infighting earned them a scolding by the president over the weekend, according to the aides and allies, many of whom requested anonymity because they were not authorized to talk about internal dynamics. Bannon declined to be interviewed.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/bannon-wants-a-war-on-washington-now-hes-part-of-one-inside-the-white-house/2017/04/06/ec4a135a-1ada-11e7-9887-1a5314b56a08_story.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Bannon wants a war on Washington. Now he\u2019s part of one inside the White House.</a>]</i> </p> <p>But the mercurial president has a long history of turning quickly on subordinates, and the <a href=\"http://nypost.com/2017/04/11/trump-wont-definitively-say-he-still-backs-bannon/\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"nypost.com\">political hit late Tuesday in the New York Post </a>was trademark Trump, using the friendly Manhattan tabloid to publicly debase his chief strategist. The president said Bannon was hardly the Svengali of his caricature, but rather \u201ca good guy\u201d who \u201cwas not involved in my campaign until very late.\u201d</p> <p>Bannon\u2019s associates were caught off guard by Trump\u2019s comments. Some interpreted them as a paternal \u201clove tap\u201d by Trump to assert his own dominance, while others worried they amounted to an indirect firing. Bannon himself was humbled, people close to him said, and his allies scrambled to defend him, including Attorney General Jeff Sessions, who <a href=\"http://www.cnn.com/2017/04/12/politics/kfile-jeff-sessions-on-bannon/index.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.cnn.com\">praised him </a>in an appearance on Laura Ingraham\u2019s radio program.</p> <p>In a second interview, <a href=\"https://blogs.wsj.com/washwire/2017/04/12/wsj-trump-interview-excerpts-china-north-korea-ex-im-bank-obamacare-bannon/\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"blogs.wsj.com\">with the Wall Street Journal</a> on Wednesday, Trump referred to Bannon as \u201ca guy who works for me\u201d \u2014 and pointedly noted, as he did with the New York Post, that he was his own \u201cstrategist,\u201d even though chief strategist is Bannon\u2019s job title.</p> <p>Trump also is increasingly embracing more mainstream policy positions championed by daughter Ivanka Trump, son-in-law Jared Kushner and their allies, including ascendant National Economic Council Director Gary Cohn, instead of Bannon\u2019s brand of combative nationalism.</p><div Barrack Jr., a close Trump friend who chaired his inaugural committee, spent Tuesday and Wednesday in Washington meeting with the president and his senior team. He characterized the ideological disagreement between Bannon and others as natural and even healthy.</p> <p>\u201cThe way this president makes decisions is he encourages different points of view from different people, and he curates those and comes up with his own positions,\u201d Barrack said. \u201cThe lack of unanimity is just the way this president manages. He is in command and control.\u201d</p> <p>Trump and his team are scrambling to notch accomplishments that they can hail at the 100-day mark later this month and to impose new discipline on a White House that has been riven by disorder and suspicion since Trump took office.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/inside-trumps-fury-the-president-rages-at-leaks-setbacks-and-accusations/2017/03/05/40713af4-01df-11e7-ad5b-d22680e18d10_story.html?utm_term=.cdea2ebeb435\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Inside Trump\u2019s fury: The president rages at leaks, setbacks and accusations</a>]</i> </p> <p>Justice Neil Gorsuch was confirmed to the Supreme Court and sworn in this week, upending Senate procedure and marking a significant victory for Trump's conservative base. But the absence of any other major legislative achievement and the public failure of a health-care overhaul has gnawed at the president, and other White House advisers have been quick to assign partial blame to Bannon, according to Trump staffers and outside allies.</p> <p>Bannon\u2019s effective demotion began last week when he was removed from the National Security Council\u2019s principals committee. But his real problems began much earlier. Trump bristled at the media depiction of Bannon as a puppeteer, punctuated by the<a href=\"https://backissues.time.com/storefront/2017/the-great-manipulator-steve-bannon/prodTD20170213.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"backissues.time.com\"> Feb. 13 Time magazine cover </a>labeling Bannon \u201cThe Great Manipulator.\u201d</p> <p>Trump fashions himself as the leading man \u2014 the protagonist of every story in which he stars \u2014 and was content to have Bannon as his sidekick, but he did not welcome the competition for top billing.</p> <p>Bannon further imperiled his standing with the president by getting crosswise with Kushner, officials said. The two men were close during last year\u2019s campaign; Kushner came to see Bannon as a wartime consigliere. But in the White House, Bannon went to war with the business leaders Kushner helped recruit to the administration \u2014 Cohn and others, including Dina Powell, the senior economic counselor and deputy national security adviser. </p> <p>Bannon privately derided them as \u201cglobalists\u201d and \u201cDemocrats,\u201d officials said, even though Powell worked in George W. Bush\u2019s administration and has been called a principled conservative by leading Republican senators.</p> <p>Bannon\u2019s supporters believe he is an essential conduit between Trump and his nationalist, populist base. The wealthy Mercer family, which has nurtured Bannon\u2019s political rise and infused Trump\u2019s campaign and allied groups with millions of dollars, is closely monitoring Bannon\u2019s falling fortunes. Rebekah Mercer, who directs the family\u2019s political activities, is unnerved and worried about losing her best link to a president her family takes credit for helping get elected but believes Bannon will be able to maintain his influence, people close to the family said.</p> <p>Ingraham wrote in an email that Bannon is \u201ca reminder in the West Wing of what the president\u2019s core supporters expect of the administration and the promises that must be fulfilled. I think the president has really keen political instincts \u2014 and I have to believe he knows his chances of a successful first term are better with Steve on the inside than on the outside.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/horrible-pictures-of-suffering-moved-trump-to-action-on-syria/2017/04/07/9aa9fcc8-1bce-11e7-8003-f55b4c1cfae2_story.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Horrible pictures of suffering moved Trump to action on Syria</a>]</i> </p> <p>But other Trump loyalists dispute the idea that Bannon is the id of the Trump movement, pointing out that Trump has been advocating some of the same populist positions \u2014 especially on immigration and trade \u2014 for decades and for more than a year on the campaign trail before Bannon\u2019s hiring last summer.</p> <p>These people argue that a better representative of Trump\u2019s voters inside the White House is Stephen Miller, the senior policy adviser and former Sessions aide who joined the campaign early and helped Trump hone and communicate his ideas. They said Miller has worked closely with Bannon but also has strategically aligned himself with Kushner, who came to see him last year as indispensable at Trump\u2019s side.</p> <p>As tensions have heightened in recent weeks, the Bannon and Kushner camps have devolved into opposing firing squads. Team Bannon believes the hosts of MSNBC\u2019s \u201cMorning Joe,\u201d a show the president watches regularly, are speaking regularly with Kushner and projecting his anti-Bannon sentiments. Kushner allies, meanwhile, finger Bannon as responsible for unflattering stories involving the president\u2019s son-in-law, including those focusing on Kushner\u2019s talks with Russians.</p> <p>Inside a White House led by a president increasingly hungry to make deals, even with Democrats, Bannon\u2019s dogmatism appears to have weakened him.</p> <p>\u201cThe West Wing is finally appreciating it\u2019s a democracy, not a dictatorship, but the rules are hard to navigate when there is such a high degree of polarization,\u201d said Richard Hohlt, a longtime Republican consultant who has observed seven different presidents since his arrival in Washington. \u201cIt becomes difficult in a democracy if you\u2019re going to be all ideological purity, all the time.\u201d</p> <p>Trump\u2019s three oldest children \u2014 Donald Jr., Ivanka and Eric \u2014 and Kushner have been frustrated by the impression of chaos inside the White House and feel that their father has not always been served well by his senior staff, according to people with knowledge of their sentiments. The Trump heirs are interested in any changes that might help resuscitate the presidency and preserve the family\u2019s name at a time when they are trying to expand the Trump Organization\u2019s portfolio of hotels.</p> <p>\u201cThe fundamental assessment is that if they want to win the White House in 2020, they\u2019re not going to do it the way they did in 2016, because the family brand would not sustain the collateral damage,\u201d said one well-connected Republican operative, speaking on the condition of anonymity to discuss the president\u2019s family. \u201cIt would be so protectionist, nationalist and backward-looking that they\u2019d only be able to build in Oklahoma City or the Ozarks.\u201d</p> <p>Bannon has borne at least part of the blame for the administration\u2019s problems governing. He was intimately involved in the entry ban, which was twice blocked in federal court, and the failed health-care push especially hurt him. Trump thought Bannon would be able to bring along the House Freedom Caucus \u2014 a group of hard-line anti-establishment conservatives \u2014 but they helped tank the bill to scale back the Affordable Care Act.</p> <p>Reince Priebus, the often-embattled chief of staff, is among the aides who feel growing pressure from the president to show that the administration can govern. Priebus has been telling confidants, \u201cI\u2019m not going to have a Memorial Day where the number one headline is \u2018Republicans can\u2019t produce a budget\u2019 when everyone else in America can,\u201d according to multiple people with knowledge of his plea.</p> <p>For Trump, one bright spot was the decision to launch 59 missiles in Syria last week. The president was pleased with the process, overseen by national security adviser H.R. McMaster, that brought together his war cabinet and corralled its expertise in a way that resembled a more traditional White House.</p> <p>\u201cHe\u2019s in the best place that I\u2019ve seen him since the inauguration,\u201d Barrack said. \u201cHe\u2019s confident. He thinks he\u2019s found the groove, and with his team too. .\u2009.\u2009. He looks great. His energy level is off the map. And I think he now feels the commander in chief role.\u201d</p> <p class=\"trailer\"> <p>Damian Paletta contributed to this report.</p> </p> <p channel=\"wp.com\"></p> </article> <div
