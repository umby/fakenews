data-show-count=\"false\" data-lang=\"en\">Follow @karoun</a></span> </span> </div> <article itemprop=\"articleBody\"> <p>Former Obama national security adviser Susan E. Rice said Tuesday that she \u201cabsolutely\u201d never sought to uncover \u201cfor political purposes\u201d the names of Trump campaign or transition officials concealed in intelligence intercepts, and she called suggestions that she leaked those identities \u201ccompletely false.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cI leaked nothing, to nobody, and never have and never would,\u201d Rice said in response to the latest charges and countercharges flowing from politically charged investigations into Russian interference in the presidential election.</p> <p>Since they first surfaced over the weekend, the Rice reports have quickly overtaken the steady drumbeat of revelations about connections to Moscow that have dogged President Trump for months. On Tuesday, the subject dominated cable news and flooded Twitter.</p> <p>\u201cRICE ORDERED SPY DOCS ON TRUMP?\u201d the president <a href=\"https://twitter.com/DRUDGE_REPORT/status/849238202992836608\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"twitter.com\">retweeted</a>, with a link to the Daily Caller and a Drudge Report headlined \u201cBoiled Rice.\u201d</p> <p>A number of Republican lawmakers said that Rice should be called to testify before congressional inquiries into what U.S. intelligence has said were Russian efforts not only to roil the presidential race, but also to tip the scales in Trump\u2019s favor.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5621\" data-category-id=\"segments\" data-duration=\"196650\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"What to know about the back-and-forth over 'unmasking'\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58e3f14de4b0e929b8938bc0\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/04-04-2017/t_1491333769825_name_susanricethumb.jpg\" data-uuid=\"5bc9df60-196b-11e7-8598-9a99da559f9e\" data-variants=\"1\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"rNiT7ei_n0w\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\"> (Jenny Starrs/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>\u201cIf the reports are right, then she will be of interest to us,\u201d said Sen. Richard Burr (R-N.C.), chairman of the Senate Intelligence Committee, which, along with its House counterpart and the FBI, is investigating the matter.</p> <p>\u201cWhen it comes to Susan E. Rice, you need to verify, not trust,\u201d Sen. Lindsey O. Graham (R-S.C.) said in an interview with Fox News. \u201cI think every American should know whether or not the national security adviser to President Obama was involved in unmasking Trump transition figures for political purposes.\u201d</p> <p>Sen. Tom Cotton (R-Ark.), called Rice the \u201cTyphoid Mary of the Obama administration.\u201d</p> <p>Beyond Trump\u2019s tweets, however, the White House was uncharacteristically restrained on the subject, as its media and Capitol Hill allies expressed outrage on its behalf. \u201cIt\u2019s not for me to decide who should testify or how they should do it,\u201d press secretary Sean Spicer said.</p> <p>Rep. Adam B. Schiff (Calif.), the ranking Democrat on the House Intelligence Committee, called the attention focused on Rice a diversionary tactic. He has also called on the Intelligence Committee\u2019s chairman, Rep. Devin Nunes (R), a Trump transition official and fellow Californian, to recuse himself from the investigation. The White House, Schiff said, has a \u201cstrong desire .\u2009.\u2009. that we lose our focus, that we not pursue the investigation of Russia, particularly as it might impact the Trump campaign.\u201d</p> <p>At the same time, Schiff told CNN, Rice has long been a target of what he called the \u201cBreitbart crowd .\u2009.\u2009. the hard right\u201d since the September 2012 attacks on a U.S. diplomatic facility in Benghazi, Libya. Four U.S. officials were killed in an extremist attack that Rice initially described as an anti-American protest that evolved into violence.</p> <p>White House chief strategist Stephen K. Bannon is the former chairman of Breitbart News, which he has described as \u201cthe platform for the alt-right,\u201d an antiglobalist movement that seeks a whites-only state.</p><div></div> <p>Asked Tuesday whether she was willing to testify, Rice told MSNBC, \u201cLet\u2019s see what comes.\u201d Investigations on \u201cRussian involvement in our electoral process are very important, they\u2019re very serious, and every American ought to have an interest in those investigations going wherever the evidence indicates they should,\u201d she said.</p> <p>\u201cI have an interest as an American citizen, as a former official,\u201d Rice said. \u201cI would want to be helpful in that process if I could.\u201d</p> <p>The focus on Rice comes as lawmakers are trying to iron out why Nunes went to the White House two weeks ago to view documents that he later said suggested that the names of Trump transition team members had been improperly \u201cunmasked.\u201d Top officials can ask intelligence agencies to reveal the names to them for national security or other reasons. </p> <p>The term refers to revealing a name that has been blacked out in an intelligence surveillance report. The law does not permit surveillance of U.S. people without a warrant; if one shows up in authorized surveillance of a foreign person, it is \u201cmasked.\u201d</p> <p>News media reports on contacts between Russia and Trump associates, including in The Washington Post, have included names said to have appeared in intelligence reports \u2014 either persons named in conversations between foreigners, or conversations directly between foreigners and U.S. persons.</p> <p>Most prominent among them is former Trump national security adviser Michael Flynn, whose \u00adDecember phone conversations with Russia\u2019s ambassador in Washington included references to U.S. sanctions imposed under President Barack Obama. After <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/national-security-adviser-flynn-discussed-sanctions-with-russian-ambassador-despite-denials-officials-say/2017/02/09/f85b29d6-ee11-11e6-b4ff-ac2cf509efe5_story.html?utm_term=.26c0e3171582\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">a Post report</a> on the conversations, Trump ousted Flynn for mischaracterizing them to Vice President Pence. </p> <p>Trump later charged that he, himself, had been surveilled under orders from Obama, who allegedly was seeking to undermine him. The administration has said repeatedly that attention should be focused on who unmasked and leaked, rather than on the information revealed.</p> <p>After Nunes\u2019s statement about the unmasking, The Post reported last week that at least three White House officials were involved in handling the files shown to him. On Monday, <a href=\"https://www.bloomberg.com/view/articles/2017-04-03/top-obama-adviser-sought-names-of-trump-associates-in-intel\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\" title=\"www.bloomberg.com\">a report</a> by Bloomberg View suggested that those officials began examining the files after discovering that Rice, while working as Obama\u2019s national security adviser, had requested the names be unmasked.</p> <p>The Daily Caller followed with <a href=\"http://dailycaller.com/2017/04/03/susan-rice-ordered-spy-agencies-to-produce-detailed-spreadsheets-involving-trump/\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\" title=\"dailycaller.com\">a report</a>, sourced to former U.S. attorney Joe DiGenova and \u201cother official sources,\u201d that said that Rice ordered U.S. spy agencies to produce \u201cdetailed spreadsheets\u201d of phone calls involving Trump and his aides.</p> <p>The White House immediately drew attention to a PBS interview in which Rice, on the same day as Nunes\u2019s statement about the documents, said, \u201cI know nothing about this.\u201d</p> <p>Speaking Tuesday to MSNBC, Rice said that she, like other top officials in all administrations, sometimes asked for names of U.S. persons that had been blacked out in intelligence reports \u2014 \u201con every topic under the sun when it seemed relevant\u201d \u2014 when necessary to \u201cprotect the American people and do our jobs.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cLet me give you an example \u2014 completely made up,\u201d she said. \u201cLet\u2019s say there was a conversation between two foreigners about a conversation they were having about an American who was proposing to sell to them high-tech bomb-making equipment.</p> <p>\u201cNow, if that came to me as the national security adviser, it would matter enormously \u2014 is that some kook sitting in his living room communicating via the Internet to sell something he doesn\u2019t have, or is this a serious person or company or entity with the ability to provide this technology perhaps to an adversary? That would be an example of how it is necessary knowing who that person was to assess that information.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cI can\u2019t say that the pace of unmasking requests\u201d rose during the Trump transition, Rice said. But, she said, \u201cthe pace of intelligence reports on Russian interference efforts increased\u201d beginning in late summer. Intelligence officials went back to rescrub the reports after Obama requested a compilation of them, which was delivered in January, she said.</p> <p>\u201cThere was no spreadsheet, nothing of the sort,\u201d Rice said.</p> <p>\u201cThe allegation is that somehow Obama administration officials utilized intelligence for political purposes,\u201d she said. \u201cAbsolutely false.\u201d</p>
