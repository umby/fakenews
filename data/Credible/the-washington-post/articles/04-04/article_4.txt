class=\"twitter-follow-button\" data-show-count=\"false\" data-lang=\"en\">Follow @farhip</a></span> </span> </div> <article itemprop=\"articleBody\"> <p>An advertiser boycott against Fox News\u2019s Bill O\u2019Reilly picked up dramatic speed Tuesday in a \u00adrapid-fire reaction to the news that the outspoken host has been the subject of a succession of sexual-harassment allegations over the past 15 years.</p> <p>At least 11 companies, including leading car, financial and pharmaceutical advertisers, confirmed that they had pulled ads or planned to shift upcoming ads away from <a href=\"http://www.foxnews.com/shows/the-oreilly-factor.html\" shape=\"rect\">\u201cThe O\u2019Reilly Factor\u201d</a> in the wake of reports that O\u2019Reilly and Fox had settled five complaints made by women who have worked with him at the news network since 2002.</p> <p>Collectively the advertiser retreat, which seemed to grow in a chain reaction throughout the day, represented the most significant threat to O\u2019Reilly seen during his long, successful and occasionally controversy-riven career at Fox. A similar but slower retreat by advertisers led to <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/glenn-beck-to-end-daily-program-on-fox-news/2011/04/06/AFFU8wpC_story.html?utm_term=.366564aa7d30\" shape=\"rect\">the demise of Glenn Beck\u2019s </a>highly rated Fox program amid boycott calls by African Americans and Jewish groups in 2011.</p> <p>Among the companies that confirmed they were suspending or removing ads from his program were the automakers Hyundai, BMW, Mercedes-Benz and Mitsubishi Motors; financial firms T. Rowe Price and Allstate Insurance; drugmakers <a href=\"http://en.sanofi.com/index.aspx\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"en.sanofi.com\">Sanofi </a>and GlaxoSmithKline; plus <a href=\"http://www.ainsworthpets.com/\" shape=\"rect\">Ainsworth Pet Nutrition, </a>the online marketing company <a href=\"https://www.constantcontact.com/index.jsp?utm_id=MSN-115517&amp;cc=MSN-115517&amp;utm_source=Bing&amp;utm_medium=cpc&amp;utm_term=constant+contact+Exact&amp;utm_campaign=Brand&amp;utm_content=Constant+Contact&amp;pn=search&amp;ef_id=Ul7k9wAABX6FqjML:20170404211838:s\" shape=\"rect\">Constant Contact</a> and men\u2019s apparel seller <a href=\"https://www.untuckit.com/\" shape=\"rect\">Untuckit</a>. The list continued to grow late in the day; by early evening, CNN had pegged the number of companies pulling their ads at 18.</p> <p>A prolonged advertiser boycott of O\u2019Reilly could prove financially painful to Fox. O\u2019Reilly\u2019s 8 p.m. news-discussion program is the highest-rated on cable, with an average 4\u00a0million viewers. It is also a tent-pole show upon which the rest of the conservative-<br align=\"block\"/>leaning network depends. Fox counts on O\u2019Reilly to generate an outsize share of its revenue and profit, which reached an <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/one-billion-dollars-profit-yes-the-campaign-has-been-a-gusher-for-cnn/2016/10/27/1fc879e6-9c6f-11e6-9980-50913d68eacb_story.html?utm_term=.4f3806cca50c\" shape=\"rect\">estimated $1.7\u00a0billion last year</a>, a record since the network\u2019s founding in 1996.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"wirereuters\" data-duration=\"349417\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Wendy Walsh details sexual harassment allegation against Bill O'Reilly\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58e28646e4b06dfdada52f80\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/04-03-2017/t_1491240508578_name_WendyWalsh2.jpg\" data-uuid=\"fcb02d3c-1892-11e7-8598-9a99da559f9e\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"RPX1LiCY35g\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\"> (Reuters)</span> </div> </div> <p>On the other hand, it is not clear how long advertisers intend to stay away from O\u2019Reilly, or what the short-term financial effect, if any, will be. Sponsors tend to buy cable ads in blocks, so removing an ad from one program usually means it is simply moved to another time slot, making little financial difference. What is more, cable-news channels derive the bulk of their revenue from licensing fees paid by cable and satellite operators, not advertising.</p> <p>Despite knowledge of his multiple settlements, Fox and its parent company, 21st Century Fox, have stood by O\u2019Reilly. The parent company last week <a href=\"http://variety.com/2017/tv/news/bill-oreilly-fox-news-contract-harassment-allegations-1202020875/\" shape=\"rect\">re-signed the combative host </a>to a new long-term contract that will pay him a reported $18\u00a0million per year and keep him on the air until at least 2020.</p> <p>The advertisers who pulled out of O\u2019Reilly\u2019s program said they were responding to a <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/04/01/business/media/bill-oreilly-sexual-harassment-fox-news.html\" shape=\"rect\">New York Times story </a>published Saturday that found the host and the network have separately or jointly paid at least $13\u00a0million to five female employees in exchange for their promise not to sue or publicly discuss their cases against O\u2019Reilly. A sixth woman, <a href=\"http://deadline.com/2017/04/lisa-bloom-bill-oreilly-wendy-walsh-fox-news-sexual-harassment-1202058615/\" shape=\"rect\">former \u201cO\u2019Reilly Factor\u201d occasional guest Wendy Walsh</a>, said that O\u2019Reilly had sexually harassed her, too, but that she did not intend to sue or seek compensation.</p> <p>\u201cWhile it\u2019s hard to tell what the facts are, the allegations are disturbing,\u201d said Donna Boland, a spokeswoman for Mercedes-Benz. \u201cGiven the importance of women in every aspect of our business, we don\u2019t feel this is a good environment in which to advertise our products right now.\u201d</p> <p>Untuckit\u2019s co-founder and chief executive, Aaron Sanandres, had a similar comment: \u201cAs a company in which more than two-thirds of our employees are women, we take sexual harassment claims very seriously. .\u2009.\u2009. In light of the disturbing allegations, we instructed our media buyer this morning to reallocate our ad dollars to other shows effective immediately.\u201d</p> <p>The harassment complaints against O\u2019Reilly are on top of a separate wave of allegations against Roger Ailes, Fox News\u2019s co-founder. The allegations surrounding Ailes came to light after Gretchen Carlson, a former Fox host, filed a lawsuit in July alleging that Ailes had pressured her for sex and demoted her when she refused.</p> <p>The suit touched off an internal investigation that brought forward other complaints and led to <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/roger-ailes-resigns-as-ceo-of-fox-news-rupert-murdoch-will-be-acting-ceo/2016/07/21/816c1dc4-4f80-11e6-a422-83ab49ed5e6a_story.html?utm_term=.1d1c05927d9b\" shape=\"rect\">Ailes\u2019s ouster as chairman of the network</a>. Carlson received $20\u00a0million from Fox to settle her suit. Ailes, who has denied the allegations, received $40\u00a0million in severance.</p><div></div> <p>At the time, Fox and 21st Century asserted that it had \u201czero tolerance\u201d for sexual harassment.</p> <p>But Fox has struggled to put the issue behind it. On Monday, a Fox News contributor named <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/fox-news-faces-yet-another-sexual-harassment-claim-against-network-leadership/2017/04/03/0c992796-1882-11e7-855e-4824bbb5d748_story.html?utm_term=.3475fedb8691\" shape=\"rect\">Julie Roginsky filed another sexual-harassment suit </a>against Ailes and his former top lieutenant, Bill Shine. In response, the network\u2019s top human-resources executive, Kevin Lord, issued a companywide memo that read, in part, \u201cIf any employee has any concerns about behavior in our workplace, I urge you to raise those concerns with me\u201d and other company executives.</p> <p>The mounting harassment allegations paint Fox as \u201cthe next Bill Cosby,\u201d said Eden Gillott Bowe, who heads <a href=\"http://www.gillottcommunications.com/\" shape=\"rect\">a communications firm </a>that represents celebrities, athletes and nonprofit organizations facing adverse publicity.</p> <p>\u201cIt\u2019s death by a thousand cuts,\u201d she said. \u201cUntil Fox can clean house, until they can make changes in good faith, I think they\u2019ll keep hemorrhaging advertisers. They need to make a clean break,\u201d even by taking the painful step of suspending or firing O\u2019Reilly.</p> <p>As the advertiser backlash grew Tuesday, both Fox and 21st Century Fox were muted in their defense of O\u2019Reilly throughout most of the day. In the past, both have been quick to defend him.</p> <p>Late in the afternoon, Fox issued a statement from its executive vice president of advertising sales, Paul Rittenberg, reading: \u201cWe value our partners and are working with them to address their current concerns about the O\u2019Reilly Factor. At this time, the ad buys of those clients have been re-expressed into other FNC programs.\u201d</p> <p>21st Century referred to a statement it issued after the Times article in which it said it \u201ctakes matters of workplace behavior very seriously.\u201d It said \u201cno current or former Fox News employee ever took advantage\u201d of a company hotline to raise concerns about O\u2019Reilly, \u201ceven anonymously.\u201d</p> <p>O\u2019Reilly did not address the unfolding controversy around him on his program Tuesday.</p> <p>In a statement over the weekend, he defended himself, saying, \u201cI\u2019m vulnerable to lawsuits from individuals who want me to pay them to avoid negative publicity.\u201d He said he settled the cases because \u201cI\u2019m a father who cares deeply for my children and who would do anything to avoid hurting them in any way.
