of them women and young children, were killed Tuesday in one of the deadliest chemical attacks of the country\u2019s six-year war, according to doctors, rescue workers and witnesses.</p> <p>Airstrikes on the northwestern town of Khan Sheikhoun began just after daybreak, delivering an unidentified chemical agent that killed at least 58 people and filled clinics across the area with patients foaming at the mouth or struggling to breathe.</p> <p> President Trump blamed the attack, which he said was carried out by the Syrian government, on former president Barack Obama, calling it a \u201cconsequence\u201d of Obama\u2019s \u201cweakness and irresolution.\u201d The reference was to Obama\u2019s decision not to follow through with <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/obama-issues-syria-red-line-warning-on-chemical-weapons/2012/08/20/ba5d26ec-eaf7-11e1-b811-09036bcb182b_story.html?utm_term=.7f04d683a105\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">a threat to use military force</a> against Syrian President Bashar al-Assad after a 2013 chemical attack.</p> <p>In a statement released by the White House \u2014 just days after the administration said action against Assad was <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/white-house-says-reality-changing-with-regard-to-syria/2017/03/31/218ea968-1644-11e7-bb16-269934184168_story.html?utm_term=.9b22dcc39842\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">not a U.S. priority</a> \u2014 Trump called the Tuesday attack \u201creprehensible\u201d and said that it \u201ccannot be ignored by the civilized world.\u201d At the United Nations, U.S. Ambassador Nikki Haley called for an emergency meeting of the Security Council.</p> <p>The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, a Britain-based monitoring network, put the death toll at 58, including at least 11 children. Doctors at the scene cited higher figures, saying entire families were killed in their sleep.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"segments\" data-duration=\"79509\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Suspected chemical attack kills scores of men, women and children in Syria\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58e3c95be4b0e929b8937a1b\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/04-04-2017/t_1491323063207_name_AFP_N899A_4124.jpg\" data-uuid=\"901c323a-1953-11e7-8598-9a99da559f9e\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"EjjM4HrrKm4\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\"> (The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>Three doctors said in interviews that the symptoms they saw were far more serious than they would expect from chlorine, which Syrian government forces <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/europe/timeline-of-chemical-weapons-use-in-syria/2017/04/04/0faf1b76-195d-11e7-8598-9a99da559f9e_story.html?utm_term=.8518e582114b\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">have used as a chemical weapon</a> in the past. The Hague-based Organization for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons expressed \u201cserious concern\u201d and said it was investigating.</p> <p>Images from the area showed the bodies of at least a dozen men, women and children splayed across the ground between two houses. It was not possible to independently verify the reports, but video footage showed lifeless bodies wrapped in blankets and packed on the back of a truck. The youngest were wearing diapers.</p> <p>In another video, several children were seen slumped on hospital\u00a0beds, apparently unresponsive to the medics and chaos around them.</p> <p>Syrian government warplanes in recent months have launched heavy attacks across northern Idlib province, where hundreds of thousands of civilians \u2014 many having fled other battle zones \u2014 are squeezed together among much of what remains of the armed opposition to Assad.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/middle_east/the-hospitals-were-slaughterhouses-a-journey-intosyrias-secret-torture-wards/2017/04/02/90ccaa6e-0d61-11e7-b2bb-417e331877d9_story.html?utm_term=.84d75e6b7b39\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">\u2018The hospitals were slaughterhouses\u2019: A journey into Syria\u2019s secret torture wards</a>]</i> </p> <p>Syria\u2019s Foreign Ministry denied involvement in Tuesday\u2019s attack, saying the government was committed to its obligations under the international Chemical Weapons Convention. Syria joined the convention in 2013 after launching sarin attacks on\u00a0several Damascus neighborhoods \u2014 strikes that killed hundreds of civilians and pushed the United States to the brink of military intervention.</p> <p>\u201cAssad calculates, reasonably, that military dynamics play in his favor. By using chemical weapons and other weapons, he is demonstrating the powerlessness of international actors,\u201d said Emile Hokayem, a Middle East analyst at the International Institute for Strategic Studies.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-graphic-embedded\"> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1484w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/04/Foreign/Graphics/2300-SYRIA-idlib-0404.jpg?uuid=7_imgBk0EeeFmJqZ2lWfng\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/04/Foreign/Graphics/2300-SYRIA-idlib-0404.jpg?uuid=7_imgBk0EeeFmJqZ2lWfng\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/04/Foreign/Graphics/2300-SYRIA-idlib-0404.jpg?uuid=7_imgBk0EeeFmJqZ2lWfng\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/04/Foreign/Graphics/2300-SYRIA-idlib-0404.jpg?uuid=7_imgBk0EeeFmJqZ2lWfng\"/><br/> </div> <p>Congressional refusal to vote in favor of U.S. military action after Assad\u2019s use of sarin, a nerve agent, in 2013 provided Obama with cover to withdraw his ultimatum, although he continued to call for Assad to leave power. The United States and Russia, Assad\u2019s main backer, then negotiated a plan for the internationally supervised removal of Syria\u2019s chemical weapons stocks.</p> <p>Throughout his presidential campaign, Trump criticized Obama for what he said was a waste of attention and resources on Syria\u2019s internal struggle at the expense of the fight against the Islamic State. Late last week, \u00adHaley said the U.S. priority was no longer to \u201cfocus on getting Assad out,\u201d while Secretary of State Rex Tillerson said the Syrian people should decide Assad\u2019s future.</p> <p>A senior State Department official told reporters Tuesday that Assad has committed \u201cwar crimes\u201d and blamed his Russian and Iranian backers for failing to control him. Those two countries are \u201cguarantors\u201d of a cease-fire they negotiated, the official said, but \u201cclearly, they are not able to deliver.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cThat\u2019s a very significant problem. Obviously that\u2019ll be something that we\u2019ll be looking at and discussing and reviewing,\u201d said the official, who noted that Tillerson will visit Moscow this month. The official spoke on the condition of anonymity under rules set by the State Department.</p> <p>The attack came as European diplomats gathered in Brussels for a flagship conference aimed at pledging billions of dollars for Syria\u2019s reconstruction, six years into a civil conflict that has shattered much of the country and prompted refugees to pour out across the Middle East and Europe.</p> <p>Photographs of lifeless bodies in Khan Sheikhoun were passed from phone to phone in the Brussels conference hall, attendees said, a stark reminder of the limitations of European power in a war now heavily driven by Iranian and Russian influence.\u00a0</p> <p>In Washington, Senate Foreign Relations Committee Chairman Bob Corker (R-Tenn.) echoed Trump\u2019s criticism of Obama, saying that the former president had \u201cfiguratively jumped in [Russian President Vladimir] Putin\u2019s lap\u201d to agree on the weapons withdrawal.</p> <p>Sen. John McCain (R-Ariz.) said that \u201cAssad believes he can commit war crimes with impunity,\u201d and he challenged Trump to take action. The question now confronting Washington, he said, \u201cis whether we will take any action to disabuse him of this murderous notion.\u201d\u00a0</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/josh-rogin/wp/2017/03/17/syrian-defector-who-documented-assads-atrocities-returning-to-washington/?utm_term=.0c1a7b4e20d6\" shape=\"rect\">Syrian defector comes to Washington after documenting atrocities</a>]</i> </p> <p>Doctors and activists in rebel-held areas have accused the government of sharply increasing chemical\u00a0attacks across Idlib, Aleppo and Hama\u00a0provinces since the end of last year. </p> <p>In the Khan Sheikhoun attack,\u00a0Samer al-Youssef, a resident, described watching people running toward the homes of relatives, then wrenching open the doors to find them dead inside.</p> <p>\u201cWe did our best, but we couldn\u2019t save people. Around 30\u00a0percent of those who were brought to us were dead on arrival,\u201d said Usama Darwish, a doctor.</p> <p>Although a nationwide cease-fire has technically been in place across Syria since late December, civilians and rebel groups\u00a0now say it exists in name only.\u00a0</p> <p>\u201cPeople are terrified. They don\u2019t know where to go,\u201d said\u00a0Ahmad Rahhal, a 22-year-old activist.\u00a0\u201cThey can\u2019t cross into Turkey because the borders are closed, but if they stay in their houses, they will be attacked by bombs. What can they do?\u201d</p> <p>As a displacement crisis burgeons on its southern border, Turkey has limited new Syrian arrivals to those\u00a0seeking medical treatment, often in the wake of attacks. Reports Tuesday suggested that ambulances were lined up at the border crossing, ready to bring the next wave of casualties to\u00a0Turkish hospitals.</p> <p class=\"trailer\"> <p>Zakaria Zakaria in (Ammar Abdullah/Reuters)\n\nScores of Syrians, many of them women and young children, were killed Tuesday in one of the deadliest chemical attacks of the country\u2019s six-year war, according to doctors, rescue workers and witnesses.\n\nAirstrikes on the northwestern town of Khan Sheikhoun began just after daybreak, delivering an unidentified chemical agent that killed at least 58 people and filled clinics across the area with patients foaming at the mouth or struggling to breathe.\n\nPresident Trump blamed the attack, which he said was carried out by the Syrian government, on former president Barack Obama, calling it a \u201cconsequence\u201d of Obama\u2019s \u201cweakness and irresolution.\u201d The reference was to Obama\u2019s decision not to follow through with a threat to use military force against Syrian President Bashar al-Assad after a 2013 chemical attack.\n\nIn a statement released by the White House \u2014 just days after the administration said action against Assad was not a U.S. priority \u2014 Trump called the Tuesday attack \u201creprehensible\u201d and said that it \u201ccannot be ignored by the civilized world.\u201d At the United Nations, U.S. Ambassador Nikki Haley called for an emergency meeting of the Security Council.\n\nThe Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, a Britain-based monitoring network, put the death toll at 58, including at least 11 children. Doctors at the scene cited higher figures, saying entire families were killed in their sleep.\n\n(The Washington Post)\n\nThree doctors said in interviews that the symptoms they saw were far more serious than they would expect from chlorine, which Syrian government forces have used as a chemical weapon in the past. The Hague-based Organization for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons expressed \u201cserious concern\u201d and said it was investigating.\n\nImages from the area showed the bodies of at least a dozen men, women and children splayed across the ground between two houses. It was not possible to independently verify the reports, but video footage showed lifeless bodies wrapped in blankets and packed on the back of a truck. The youngest were wearing diapers.\n\nIn another video, several children were seen slumped on hospital beds, apparently unresponsive to the medics and chaos around them.\n\nSyrian government warplanes in recent months have launched heavy attacks across northern Idlib province, where hundreds of thousands of civilians \u2014 many having fled other battle zones \u2014 are squeezed together among much of what remains of the armed opposition to Assad.\n\n[\u2018The hospitals were slaughterhouses\u2019: A journey into Syria\u2019s secret torture wards]\n\nSyria\u2019s Foreign Ministry denied involvement in Tuesday\u2019s attack, saying the government was committed to its obligations under the international Chemical Weapons Convention. Syria joined the convention in 2013 after launching sarin attacks on several Damascus neighborhoods \u2014 strikes that killed hundreds of civilians and pushed the United States to the brink of military intervention.\n\n\u201cAssad calculates, reasonably, that military dynamics play in his favor. By using chemical weapons and other weapons, he is demonstrating the powerlessness of international actors,\u201d said Emile Hokayem, a Middle East analyst at the International Institute for Strategic Studies.\n\n\n\n\n\nCongressional refusal to vote in favor of U.S. military action after Assad\u2019s use of sarin, a nerve agent, in 2013 provided Obama with cover to withdraw his ultimatum, although he continued to call for Assad to leave power. The United States and Russia, Assad\u2019s main backer, then negotiated a plan for the internationally supervised removal of Syria\u2019s chemical weapons stocks.\n\nThroughout his presidential campaign, Trump criticized Obama for what he said was a waste of attention and resources on Syria\u2019s internal struggle at the expense of the fight against the Islamic State. Those two countries are \u201cguarantors\u201d of a cease-fire they negotiated, the official said, but \u201cclearly, they are not able to deliver.\u201d\n\n\u201cThat\u2019s a very significant problem. Obviously that\u2019ll be something that we\u2019ll be looking at and discussing and reviewing,\u201d said the official, who noted that Tillerson will visit Moscow this month. The official spoke on the condition of anonymity under rules set by the State Department.\n\nThe attack came as European diplomats gathered in Brussels for a flagship conference aimed at pledging billions of dollars for Syria\u2019s reconstruction, six years into a civil conflict that has shattered much of the country and prompted refugees to pour out across the Middle East and Europe.\n\nPhotographs of lifeless bodies in Khan Sheikhoun were passed from phone to phone in the Brussels conference hall, attendees said, a stark reminder of the limitations of European power in a war now heavily driven by Iranian and Russian influence.\n\nIn Washington, Senate Foreign Relations Committee Chairman Bob Corker (R-Tenn.) echoed Trump\u2019s criticism of Obama, saying that the former president had \u201cfiguratively jumped in [Russian President Vladimir] Putin\u2019s lap\u201d to agree on the weapons withdrawal.\n\nSen. John McCain (R-Ariz.) said that \u201cAssad believes he can commit war crimes with impunity,\u201d and he challenged Trump to take action. The question now confronting Washington, he said, \u201cis whether we will take any action to disabuse him of this murderous notion.\u201d\n\n[Syrian defector comes to Washington after documenting atrocities]\n\nDoctors and activists in rebel-held areas have accused the government of sharply increasing chemical attacks across Idlib, Aleppo and Hama provinces since the end of last year.\n\nIn the Khan Sheikhoun attack, Samer al-Youssef, a resident, described watching people running toward the homes of relatives, then wrenching open the doors to find them dead inside.\n\n\u201cWe did our best, but we couldn\u2019t save people. Around 30 percent of those who were brought to us were dead on arrival,\u201d said Usama Darwish, a doctor.\n\nAlthough a nationwide cease-fire has technically been in place across Syria since late December, civilians and rebel groups now say it exists in name only.\n\n\u201cPeople are terrified.
