high-level session of the U.N. Security Council called to review what the Trump administration calls its most dire national security concern. \u201cThe more we bide our time, the sooner we will run out of it.\u201d</p> <p>Tillerson\u2019s push at a special session of the Security Council came as the Trump administration said it is <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/in-an-apparent-shift-tillerson-says-us-willing-to-hold-direct-talks-with-n-korea/2017/04/27/a2093a0e-2bac-11e7-b605-33413c691853_story.html?hpid=hp_hp-top-table-main_usnkorea-955pm%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.a4b8cb233305\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\">willing to bargain</a> directly with North Korea over ending its nuclear weapons program, but under strict conditions that make talks unlikely anytime soon.</p> <p>Ahead of the diplomatic effort at the United Nations, President Trump said direct conflict is possible. \u201cThere is a chance that we could end up having a major, major conflict with North Korea. Absolutely,\u201d Trump told Reuters in an interview this week. \u201cWe\u2019d love to solve things diplomatically, but it\u2019s very difficult.\u201d</p> <p>Hours after the U.N. meeting, North Korea fired another missile early Saturday local time, but it exploded within seconds of being launched, American and South Korean defense officials said. \u201cThe missile did not leave North Korean territory,\u201d U.S. Pacific Command spokesman Cmdr. Bad!\u201d Trump tweeted.</p> <p>In the most-detailed explanation to date of the Trump administration\u2019s emerging policy for dealing with North Korea, Tillerson told the Security Council on Friday that U.S. urgency is driven by the current nuclear threat to allies South Korea and Japan as well as the likelihood that North Korea will soon be able to strike the United States.</p> <p>\u201cAll options for responding to future provocation must remain on the table. At the same time, the administration reiterated that it would act alone if necessary.</p> <p>At issue is North Korea\u2019s simultaneous effort to perfect a nuclear warhead that could be delivered far from its shores and to develop missiles with a range long enough to be a threat to the United States. Analysts think North Korea, if undeterred, could have that capability within a few years \u2014 likely during Trump\u2019s first term in office. Tillerson also asked other countries to suspend or downgrade diplomatic relations with the communist state, alleging that the regime of Kim Jong Un abuses diplomatic privileges to support illicit missile and nuclear programs.</p> <p>In blunt terms, Tillerson said North Korea is unlikely to give up its weapons or change its bellicose behavior under current sanctions and diplomatic condemnations. He said new economic penalties are necessary, as well as more vigorous enforcement of existing sanctions that he said North Korea has found ways to evade.</p> <p>\u201cI urge this council to act before North Korea does,\u201d Tillerson said. \u201cWe must work together to adopt a new approach and impose increased diplomatic and economic pressure on the North Korean regime.\u201d</p> <p>In a clear warning to North Korean ally China, Tillerson said nations that help North Korea evade sanctions \u201cdiscredit this body.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/in-south-korea-mystification-over-trumps-defense-and-tradecomments/2017/04/28/e5fa3e8c-2c12-11e7-9081-f5405f56d3e4_story.html?hpid=hp_hp-top-table-main_trumpkorea-1036am%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.a755e48f2896\" with North Korea is partly in deference to China\u2019s long insistence that the only way to lessen tension is through direct talks.</p> <p>\u201cThe United States also would much prefer countries and people in question own up to their lapses and correct their behavior themselves, but we will not hesitate to sanction third-country entities and individuals\u201d that help North Korea go around sanctions, Tillerson said.</p> <p>Although the council did not vote on new sanctions or other measures Friday, the Trump administration hoped for a show of force, with the full council, including China, Russia and the United States, uniting to air concerns about North Korea\u2019s behavior.</p> <p>Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi told the council that diplomacy is not hopeless, and he said China cannot be expected to rein in North Korea on its own.</p> <p>\u201cThe state of affairs on the Korean Peninsula is not caused by any single party, nor is it reasonable to ask any party to take sole responsibility,\u201d Wang said via an interpreter.</p> <p>\u201cWe call upon all parties, especially those directly concerned \u2014 DPRK and the U.S. \u2014 to demonstrate sincerity for dialogue and restart dialogue,\u201d Wang said, using the abbreviation for the North\u2019s formal name, the Democratic People\u2019s Republic of Korea. \u201cChina will be open to all useful proposals from the parties.\u201d</p> <p>U.N. Secretary General Ant\u00f3nio Guterres, who joined Tillerson and foreign ministers from countries that sit on the decision-making council, condemned what he called North Korea\u2019s repeated violations of the body\u2019s resolutions over nuclear and missile testing and development.</p> <p>\u201cI am alarmed by the risk of a military escalation in the region, including by miscalculation or misunderstanding,\u201d Guterres said.</p> <p>The U.N. Security Council session Friday comes at a particularly tense time in relations between North Korea and the United States, with the Trump administration sending warships to the region in a show of force against Kim\u2019s regime.</p> <p>This week, North Korea conducted large-scale artillery drills, showing off conventional weaponry that can easily reach South Korea\u2019s capital, Seoul, the center of a metropolitan region of about 25\u00a0million people.</p> <p>A North Korean propaganda outlet released a video clip Thursday showing a simulated attack on the White House and declaring that the ability to destroy the United States \u201cis in our sights.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i> <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/powerpost/wp/2017/04/16/trumps-first-100-days-what-comes-next-on-north-korea/?utm_term=.7ac204a096d8\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\">[What comes next on North Korea?</a>]</i> </p> <p>\u201cNorth Korea must understand that respect will never follow recklessness,\u201d Tillerson said Friday. \u201cNorth Korea must take concrete steps to reduce the threat that its illegal weapons programs pose to the United States and our allies before we can consider talks.\u201d</p> <p>The last <span>round of </span>direct talks, initiated in 2003 and involving the United States, China and other nations, produced no rollback of the North Korean program. Last month, during his first trip to South Korea, Japan and China as secretary, Tillerson declared that the \u201cera of strategic patience\u201d that included those talks was over and that \u201call options\u201d were on the table.</p> <p>Trump has been urging China to pressure North Korea and has warned that his administration will act if Beijing does not.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/worldviews/wp/2017/04/13/trump-thought-china-could-get-n-korea-to-comply-its-not-that-easy/?utm_term=.37265d22ee32\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\">Trump assumed China could rein in North Korea. It\u2019s not that easy.]</a> </i> </p> <p channel=\"!Daily\">China supports talks and has long argued that although it also wants to rid North Korea of nuclear weapons, it cannot persuade the country to give them up without direct assurances from the United States.</p> <p channel=\"!Daily\">Tillerson offered some Friday, telling the council that the United States is not seeking \u201cregime
