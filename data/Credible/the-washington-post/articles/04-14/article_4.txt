But it actually helps pay her bills.'\"><span class=\"fa fa-envelope\"></span></a></span> <span class=\"tweet-authors\"><span class=\"pb-twitter-follow unprocessed\"><a href=\"https://twitter.com/@michaelbirnbaum\" class=\"twitter-follow-button\" data-show-count=\"false\" data-lang=\"en\">Follow @michaelbirnbaum</a></span> </span> </div> <article itemprop=\"articleBody\"> <p> <span class=\"dateline\">BRUSSELS \u2014</span> Those who fear for the future of the European Union are confronting a painful paradox: Many of the strongest bids to tear apart the E.U. are being underwritten by E.U. cash.</p> <p>France is careening toward a nail-biter presidential election this month that pits a crowded field against anti-E.U. titan Marine Le Pen. But E.U. funds pay her salary, support her assistants, and underwrite the conferences and books she churns out to attack the 28-nation bloc. Key British leaders of the successful Brexit campaign got their financial lifeline from Brussels euros. Elsewhere in Europe, self-identified fascists are paying for rallies to further the future of the \u201cwhite race\u201d by breaking up the E.U. \u2014 all thanks to E.U. money.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/europe/one-of-europes-most-powerful-jobs-is-up-for-grabs-itsa-bad-sign-for-the-eu-that-no-ones-paying-attention/2017/01/15/b6aabfb2-d6c2-11e6-a0e6-d502d6751bc8_story.html?utm_term=.2998f71be348\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">One of Europe\u2019s most powerful jobs is up for grabs. It\u2019s a bad sign for the E.U. that no one\u2019s paying attention.</a>]</i> </p> <p>With the European Union under threat as never before, lawmakers have been pushing to tighten generous rules that make it easy for fringe political parties to qualify for hundreds of thousands of euros every year. Bigger forces \u2014 such as the European affiliate of Le Pen\u2019s National Front party \u2014 get millions because of their heft in elections for the European Parliament, an institution that is short on power but flush with cash.</p> <p>Some groups, including Le Pen\u2019s, are backed by a wide range of voters. But others have done little to qualify for money other than show a scattering of support across several European countries. Some lawmakers want to end funding for those organizations.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"segments\" data-duration=\"155946\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Who is Marine Le Pen?\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58c82a57e4b01a7d50423715\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-14-2017/t_1489513018562_name_20170311_lepen_thumbnail.jpg\" data-uuid=\"e728c28c-08dc-11e7-bd19-fd3afa0f7e2a\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"0g45dI7wnmk\"> <script src=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-stat/posttv/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\"> (Bastien Inzaurralde/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>\u201cWe have free speech. They can do lots of things, but they should not be financed by a union that is bound to totally different values,\u201d said Marita Ulvskog, a Swedish center-left member of the European Parliament who is fighting to stop funding for extreme-right parties.</p> <p>The group she has targeted, the Alliance for Peace and Freedom, is slated to receive $723,000 this year. Last year, some of that money went toward a conference where a British National Party leader, Nick Griffin, said that white people \u201chave a catastrophically low birthrate.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cWe already in most of our countries have so few young girls of childbearing age or younger that even if they each had 20 children, it would take us 80 years to restore our numbers,\u201d said Griffin, a former member of the European Parliament.</p> <p>Another member of the Alliance for Peace and Freedom, European Parliament member Udo Voigt, was convicted in Germany of inciting racial hatred by advocating that the national soccer team include only white players.</p> <p>While the Alliance for Peace and Freedom takes just a tiny share of E.U. funding, bigger far-right beneficiaries of E.U. money also pose a far more potent threat.</p> <p>As an elected member of European Parliament, Le Pen can work full-time to undermine the E.U. because of <a href=\"http://www.europarl.europa.eu/meps/en/about-meps.html\" shape=\"rect\">her generous E.U. salary</a>. Her E.U.-paid Euroskeptic assistants bolster her labor. Her pan-European party \u2014 the Movement for a Europe of Nations and Freedom, an alliance of anti-E.U. lawmakers from across Europe \u2014 receives yet more E.U. money. And still more official funding funnels to an affiliated think tank that sponsors anti-E.U. conferences and policy papers.</p> <p>As many as a third of the European Parliament\u2019s 751 members are Euroskeptic, including 23 members of Le Pen\u2019s National Front party.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-photo-left\" style=\"width:300px;\"> <a name=\"a8e0205de3fb0d87e944b731eb5b867e2159800e\"></a> <img alt=\"\" class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1024w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/13/Foreign/Images/2017-04-13T092700Z_1814526500_RC145B2F2A00_RTRMADP_3_GLOBAL-ECONOMY-WEEKAHEAD.jpg?uuid=9CiuPCBJEeetdDp0Km6Tpw\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/13/Foreign/Images/2017-04-13T092700Z_1814526500_RC145B2F2A00_RTRMADP_3_GLOBAL-ECONOMY-WEEKAHEAD.jpg?uuid=9CiuPCBJEeetdDp0Km6Tpw\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/13/Foreign/Images/2017-04-13T092700Z_1814526500_RC145B2F2A00_RTRMADP_3_GLOBAL-ECONOMY-WEEKAHEAD.jpg?uuid=9CiuPCBJEeetdDp0Km6Tpw\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/13/Foreign/Images/2017-04-13T092700Z_1814526500_RC145B2F2A00_RTRMADP_3_GLOBAL-ECONOMY-WEEKAHEAD.jpg?uuid=9CiuPCBJEeetdDp0Km6Tpw\"/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">From left to right: Campaign posters for candidates Marine Le Pen of the National Front, Jean-Luc Melenchon of the Parti de Gauche and Benoit Hamon of the Socialist Party. (Charles Platiau/Reuters)</span> </div> <p>\u201cWhen like Marine you come in with 23 seats, you get these mandates and assistance,\u201d said Gerolf Annemans, who is a leader of Le Pen\u2019s pan-European parliamentary group and a member of Belgium\u2019s Euroskeptic Flemish Interest party. \u201cThe various possibilities and means that you get are splendid to develop your political message.\u201d</p> <p>After decades at Europe\u2019s fringe, anti-E.U. parties blossomed <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/vote-denotes-the-growing-disunity-of-the-european-union/2014/05/26/0815378b-b0e3-4064-9b77-fb03a83100f1_story.html?utm_term=.265633d81127\" shape=\"rect\">during 2014 elections</a> that delivered a powerful if fractious contingent to the European Parliament. Politicians benefited from a backlash to years of E.U.-driven austerity policies and a growing fear among voters that Europe\u2019s open borders were hurting their job prospects.\u00a0</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/eu-leaders-converge-on-brussels-for-brexit-crisis-talks/2016/06/28/ca75f2e6-3c9e-11e6-9e16-4cf01a41decb_story.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">E.U. leaders toughen line over British \u2018divorce\u2019</a>]</i> </p> <p>British members of the European Parliament as part of the U.K. Independence Party, or UKIP, led the successful charge to pull their country out of the E.U. Now Le Pen is leading French members of the European Parliament to <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/frances-presidential-election-may-determine-the-future-of-the-european-union/2017/03/30/df1d4c7e-13c8-11e7-bb16-269934184168_story.html?utm_term=.86f3cde0b614\" shape=\"rect\">deliver the death </a>blow if she wins France\u2019s presidency. European funding has been so critical to both groups that many politicians say the insurgents never would have threatened Europe\u2019s entrenched establishment without it.</p> <p>\u201cWithout the European Parliament, you wouldn\u2019t have a UKIP or a [National Front] as powerful and cohesive as they are now,\u201d said Giles Merritt, a longtime observer of the E.U. who leads the pro-European Friends of Europe think tank.</p> <p>With only national elections to run in, \u201cthey probably would have died on the vine,\u201d Merritt said. \u201cBut the fact they were able to get substantial numbers of people in Parliament, and through that, funding \u2014 that has been significant in the whole populist tide.\u201d</p> <p>In both France and Britain, a quirk of election law meant that small parties largely shut out of national politics could win big in the European Parliament. Le Pen\u2019s National Front is the largest party in the French delegation to the European Parliament, but it holds only two of 577 seats in France\u2019s National Assembly. Similarly, UKIP is the largest British party in Brussels but has no seats in the House of Commons.</p> <p>Euroskeptic leaders have long turned to the European Parliament as a ready source of funds and legitimacy. Le Pen\u2019s father, the founder of the National Front, has been elected to the body since 1984. UKIP\u2019s Nigel Farage has held office since 1999. But only after the 2014 elections did the money truly start to expand, since it is tied to the number of lawmakers holding office.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/frances-presidential-election-may-determine-the-future-of-the-european-union/2017/03/30/df1d4c7e-13c8-11e7-bb16-269934184168_story.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">France\u2019s presidential election may determine the future of the European Union</a>]</i> </p> <p>The resources available to E.U. lawmakers are wide-ranging, starting with their $108,000 annual salaries plus daily stipends when the European Parliament is in session. Each can hire up to three Brussels-based assistants and more in their home nation. E.U. funds also pay for offices back home.</p> <p>Additional resources go toward pan-European political parties and affiliated policy think tanks. Le Pen\u2019s Movement for a Europe of Nations and Freedom is slated to receive $1.8\u00a0million this year. Its think tank, which funds publications such as \u201cEnough With the Euro!\u201d and sponsors anti-E.U. conferences, will receive another $1.1\u00a0million.</p> <p>Although the European Parliament\u2019s assistants and foundations technically are forbidden from political campaigning, the lines are blurry. Both mainstream and anti-E.U. leaders have been cited for mixing national politics with their legislative work.</p> <p>\u201cThe European Parliament is really important, because most of them at a national level, they don\u2019t get much. Her pan-European party \u2014 the Movement for a Europe of Nations and Freedom, an alliance of anti-E.U. lawmakers from across Europe \u2014 receives yet more E.U. money. Similarly, UKIP is the largest British party in Brussels but has no seats in the House of Commons.\n\nEuroskeptic leaders have long turned to the European Parliament as a ready source of funds and legitimacy. Le Pen\u2019s father, the founder of the National Front, has been elected to the body since 1984. UKIP\u2019s Nigel Farage has held office since 1999.
