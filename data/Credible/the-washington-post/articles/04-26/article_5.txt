conservative radio show were not happy with reports this week that President Trump was, as Limbaugh put it, \u201ccaving on his demand for a measly $1\u00a0billion in the budget for his wall on the border with Mexico.\u201d</p> <p> <a href=\"https://www.rushlimbaugh.com/daily/2017/04/25/looks-like-trump-is-caving-on-1b-for-the-wall-in-the-face-of-democrat-threats-to-shut-down-the-government/\" shape=\"rect\">Tim from Detroit</a> called in to say he\u2019s worried Trump will \u201ckick this can down the road\u201d like any other politician. Ray from Chattanooga, Tenn., wished he could tell Trump: \u201cBe the man we elected you to be.\u201d </p> <p>And John from Polk City, Fla., added: \u201cNumber one, build the wall. Every time he spoke: Build a wall. I\u2019m afraid he\u2019s starting to dip his foot into the swamp. And, boy, I just don\u2019t want to see that happen.\u201d</p> <p>In his bumpy first three months in office, Trump has reversed himself on campaign promises now seen as impractical or unnecessary, from repealing and replacing Obamacare in a single day to labeling China as a currency manipulator. </p> <p>But Trump\u2019s promise to build an \u201c<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/video/politics/trump-says-he-will-build-impenetrable-physical-tall-powerful-beautiful-border/2016/08/31/34eceacc-6fb6-11e6-993f-73c693a89820_video.html\" shape=\"rect\">impenetrable, physical, tall, powerful, beautiful southern border wall</a>\u201d paid for by Mexico was special. It was his most vivid campaign promise, and its <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2016/02/26/so-how-high-will-donald-trumps-wall-be-an-investigation/?utm_term=.506de7a2cb07\" shape=\"rect\">proposed height grew</a> with every obstacle thrown in Trump\u2019s way, from naysayers saying it wasn\u2019t possible to the Mexican government saying it wouldn\u2019t foot the bill. There is also peril for fellow Republicans \u2014 who have been split for years on how the United States should reform its immigration system \u2014 in not taking the idea of the wall seriously enough.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2016/02/12/build-that-wall-has-taken-on-a-life-of-its-own-at-donald-trumps-rallies-but-hes-still-serious/?utm_term=.1751fa44718c\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Flashback: \u2018Build that wall\u2019 takes on a life of its own at Trump rallies</a>]</i> </p> <p>Mark Krikorian, the longtime executive director of the Center for Immigration Studies, a think tank that advocates reduced immigration, said that he has never considered a border wall a top priority.</p> <p>But now that the <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2016/02/12/build-that-wall-has-taken-on-a-life-of-its-own-at-donald-trumps-rallies-but-hes-still-serious/\" shape=\"rect\">wall has taken on a life of its own</a>, Krikorian said that Trump and Republicans have to make some sort of progress in securing a chunk of the border with a wall or heavy-duty fence \u2014 or else blow the opportunity to show Americans the party is ready to take action to crack down on illegal immigration. </p> <p>\u201cFollowing through on wall construction is one of the ways that the political class can win back trust on this topic,\u201d Krikorian said. \u201cIt\u2019s a tangible thing. You can take pictures of it and imagine it in your head.\u201d</p> <p>He added: \u201cEven if the border wall did no good at all to control immigration, it would be important to build .\u2009.\u2009. Even if it did nothing, even if it was completely ineffective, it\u2019s important politically.\u201d</p> <p>To Trump, the wall is a concrete symbol of his commitment to cracking down on illegal immigration. To many lawmakers, it\u2019s a fantastical joke that they have to carefully navigate.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-photo-left\" style=\"width:300px;\"> <a name=\"21a5b1c804a2d44cd44b67cb82c6b859fcaf1bc5\"></a> <img alt=\"\" class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1024w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/27/National-Politics/Images/2A8A6294.JPG?uuid=XrsXLirdEee-UbP8b_f67g\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/27/National-Politics/Images/2A8A6294.JPG?uuid=XrsXLirdEee-UbP8b_f67g\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/27/National-Politics/Images/2A8A6294.JPG?uuid=XrsXLirdEee-UbP8b_f67g\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/27/National-Politics/Images/2A8A6294.JPG?uuid=XrsXLirdEee-UbP8b_f67g\"/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">President Trump\u2019s hard-line positions on a border wall and other immigration issues has prompted protests such as this one in Washington in January. (Oliver Contreras/For The Washington Post)</span> </div> <p>Since Trump took office, many lawmakers have been trying to steer him toward more practical solutions for the border involving fencing, increased border patrols and technology. They argue that constructing a wall would cost tens of billions, still wouldn\u2019t solve many of the country\u2019s border problems and could create whole new challenges as well. </p> <p>\u201cI think we\u2019re all recognizing that his message is one that he wants a secure border. I think at one point that he may have very well thought that that was the best way to do it,\u201d said Sen. Mike Rounds (R-S.D.). </p> <p>Even at the White House, aides have been shifting their messaging to the broad topic of \u201cborder security,\u201d with White House Chief of Staff Reince Priebus using the phrase six times on NBC\u2019s \u201cMeet the Press\u201d on Sunday while only saying the word \u201cwall\u201d twice. The next day, Trump <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/white-house-confident-of-averting-shutdown-as-trump-shows-flexibility-on-wall/2017/04/24/72aa945a-2923-11e7-be51-b3fc6ff7faee_story.html\" shape=\"rect\">softened</a> his demand to include wall funding in a must-pass spending bill \u2014 a comment widely viewed as caving to pressure from the same lawmakers that Trump once bragged he could outsmart. </p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/trump-scrambles-to-show-progress-as-the-100-day-mark-approaches/2017/04/25/9fc6803c-29d4-11e7-b605-33413c691853_story.html?utm_term=.5082deed5a58\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Trump scrambles to show progress as the 100-day mark approaches</a>]</i> </p> <p>Trump began talking about a southern border structure even before he launched his campaign, bragging that he was \u201cthe king of building walls.\u201d In his announcement speech in June 2015, Trump promised: \u201cI will build a great wall on our southern border, and I will have Mexico pay for that wall. Mark my words.\u201d </p> <p>He continued that messaging this week, telling the Associated Press that \u201cthe wall\u2019s getting built, okay?\u201d White House press secretary Sean Spicer reiterated the point during a Tuesday briefing \u2014 \u201cThere will be a wall built,\u201d he said \u2014 but dodged questions about when and how. </p> <p>But with an estimated <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/powerpost/wp/2017/02/11/trump-insists-he-can-bring-the-cost-of-21-6-billion-border-wall-way-down/?utm_term=.2fecec853bbd\" shape=\"rect\">price tag as high as $21.6 billion</a>, lawmakers have been slow to lend their support.
