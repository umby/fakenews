is further endangering the GOP health-care push, with a chorus of influential voices suspicious of the proposal warning the president to abandon it. </p> <p>From headlines at Breitbart to chatter on Fox News Channel and right-wing talk radio, as well as among friends who have Trump\u2019s ear, the message has been blunt: The plan being advanced by congressional Republican leaders is deeply flawed \u2014 and, at worst, a political trap.</p> <p>Trump\u2019s allies worry that he is jeopardizing his presidency by promoting the bill spearheaded by House Speaker Paul D. Ryan (Wis.), arguing that it would fracture Trump\u2019s coalition of working- and middle-class voters, many of them older and subsisting on federal aid.</p> <p>Vice President Pence and administration officials scrambled Tuesday to salvage the plan amid widespread dissatisfaction in both the Senate and House over the Congressional Budget Office\u2019s <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/obamacare-revision-would-reduce-insured-numbers-by-24-million/2017/03/13/ea4c860a-0829-11e7-93dc-00f9bdd74ed1_story.html?utm_term=.03c1c4ea7438\" shape=\"rect\">estimate</a> that 24 million fewer people would be insured in a decade under the Ryan proposal, titled the American Health Care Act. </p> <p> Trump \u2014 who has not yet fully used the bully pulpit of the presidency to rally support for the plan \u2014 spoke privately with Ryan on Tuesday afternoon. They discussed the various factions, the opinions of several key lawmakers and developing a closing strategy, according to two people with knowledge of the call.</p><div class=\"inline-content and preserving Medicaid and other entitlement programs \u2014 in service to an ideological project championed for years by Ryan and other establishment Republicans.</p> <p>\u201cTrump figures things out pretty quickly, and I think he\u2019s figuring out this situation, how the House Republicans did him a disservice,\u201d said Christopher Ruddy, a longtime Trump friend. \u201cPresident Trump is a big-picture, pragmatic Republican, and unfortunately the Ryan Republican plan doesn\u2019t capture his worldview.\u201d</p> <p>Ruddy, the chief executive of Newsmax Media, published <a href=\"http://www.newsmax.com/ChristopherRuddy/trump-healthcare-health-paul-ryan/2017/03/14/id/778568/\" shape=\"rect\">a column</a> Tuesday urging Trump to \u201cditch\u201d the current bill.</p> <p>Inside the White House, senior officials said they are taking note of the mounting opposition. \u201cYou can\u2019t be so blind that you\u2019re not seeing the outside noise,\u201d said one adviser, who spoke on the condition of anonymity because the adviser was not authorized to speak publicly.</p> <p>A second adviser, who also requested anonymity to speak candidly, said, \u201cWe take their views seriously and we\u2019re listening, but we do appreciate when those concerns are shared privately and with a smaller megaphone.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/obamacare-revision-would-reduce-insured-numbers-by-24-million/2017/03/13/ea4c860a-0829-11e7-93dc-00f9bdd74ed1_story.html?utm_term=.365aeb14e7bf\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Obamacare revision would reduce insured numbers by 24 million</a>]</i> </p> <p>Keeping Republicans on edge are several Trump advisers with tenuous ties to Ryan and the party establishment who might be more responsive than others to outside pressure, including chief strategist Stephen K. Bannon and senior policy adviser Stephen Miller.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-photo-left\" style=\"width:300px;\"> <a name=\"68f7a74da79ed25fb0c68a15462baa81e7c0265e\"></a> <img alt=\"\" class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1024w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/03/14/National-Politics/Images/Botsford170314Trump12745.JPG?uuid=ZEorxAj3Eee9Gf06-g9-Kg\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/03/14/National-Politics/Images/Botsford170314Trump12745.JPG?uuid=ZEorxAj3Eee9Gf06-g9-Kg\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/03/14/National-Politics/Images/Botsford170314Trump12745.JPG?uuid=ZEorxAj3Eee9Gf06-g9-Kg\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/03/14/National-Politics/Images/Botsford170314Trump12745.JPG?uuid=ZEorxAj3Eee9Gf06-g9-Kg\"/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Vice President Pence swears in Seema Verma, center, as administrator of the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services at the White House complex on Tuesday. (Jabin Botsford/The Washington Post)</span> </div> <p>The White House strategy has been to spotlight what it sees as the urgent need to overhaul the health-care system rather than the legislative fix itself. At his Tuesday briefing, press secretary Sean Spicer highlighted \u201creal-life examples\u201d of \u201cactual Americans\u201d who shared with Trump stories of suffering in a listening session Monday.</p> <p>\u201cThese are the stories that are at the top of the president\u2019s mind as he works towards reforming our health-care system,\u201d Spicer said.</p> <p>This fresh public agitation from Trump loyalists comes as hard-line Republicans in Congress already stand opposed to the health-care bill. These lawmakers, many of whom are members of the House Freedom Caucus, see the proposal as insufficiently conservative because it is too reliant on tax credits and does not fully repeal the Affordable Care Act, the law often known as Obamacare that was signed by President Barack Obama.<b></b> </p> <p>Former House speaker Newt Gingrich, a Trump ally, said the president should not be tempted by outside calls to abandon the Ryan bill and should instead \u201clet the legislative process work.\u201d</p> <p>\u201cWhat\u2019s happening now is a gut check,\u201d he said. \u201cIt\u2019s not unusual. People are figuring out what they\u2019re going to stand tight on.\u201d</p> <p>The cascade of opposition within Trump\u2019s movement started flowing soon after the bill was unveiled last week and picked up speed this week. On Breitbart \u2014 the anti-establishment, conservative news site that has been a platform for Trumpism and was once run by Bannon \u2014 article after article has railed against a bill its headline writers excoriate as \u201cRyanCare.\u201d</p> <p>One heavily promoted story said that RyanCare was \u201ca perverse economic system\u201d and featured an interview with Rep. David Brat (R-Va.), a tea party hero who unseated then-Majority Leader Eric Cantor in 2014.</p> <p>Breitbart <a href=\"http://www.breitbart.com/big-government/2017/03/13/exclusive-audio-emerges-of-when-paul-ryan-abandoned-donald-trump-i-am-not-going-to-defend-donald-trump-not-now-not-in-the-future/\" shape=\"rect\">published leaked audio</a> of Ryan on Monday that could undermine his relationship with Trump. The recording is of Ryan on a conference call with House Republicans last year, immediately following the release of the \u201cAccess Hollywood\u201d video in which <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/trump-recorded-having-extremely-lewd-conversation-about-women-in-2005/2016/10/07/3b9ce776-8cb4-11e6-bf8a-3d26847eeed4_story.html?utm_term=.27d4deb20635\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Trump bragged</a> about sexually assaulting women. Ryan said on the call that he was \u201cnot going to defend Donald Trump \u2014 not now, not in the future.\u201d</p> <p>Breitbart also has been <a href=\"http://www.breitbart.com/big-government/2017/03/14/after-years-gop-repeal-bills-obama-now-back-down/\" shape=\"rect\">promoting a rally </a>Wednesday on Capitol Hill organized by FreedomWorks, a right-wing advocacy group that stoked the tea party movement. The event will feature Sens. Ted Cruz (Tex.), Mike Lee (Utah) and Rand Paul (Ky.), who oppose Ryan\u2019s health-care bill.</p> <p>Paul told reporters Tuesday that the plan was losing momentum: \u201cThere\u2019s not really any kind of coalition that\u2019s going to get the Ryan bill to pass. I think the CBO score makes it harder for him to pass his bill, and he\u2019s losing the argument.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/trump-goes-into-dealmaking-mode-works-behind-the-scenes-on-health-bill/2017/03/08/1dc87bb2-0422-11e7-b9fa-ed727b644a0b_story.html?utm_term=.1ba57716ca1c\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Trump goes into dealmaking mode, works behind the scenes on health bill</a>]</i> </p> <p>The rush by Ryan to quickly pass the health-care bill concerns some of Trump\u2019s top supporters, including Sen. Tom Cotton (R-Ark.). \u201cI don\u2019t think Americans care whether this bill passes by Easter or Memorial Day,\u201d Cotton said Tuesday on Hugh Hewitt\u2019s radio show. </p> <p>Republican strategist John Brabender said he observed a number of focus groups of Trump voters in Rust Belt states and found them willing to be patient about the timetable for overhauling health care.</p> <p>Brabender said the White House \u201cshould take a deep breath and not feel like anybody has to rush out and sign something they don\u2019t feel good about. .\u2009.\u2009. They have to be careful not to just quickly check a box and then own something around their neck for years.\u201d</p> <p>Cotton dismissed promises from Trump and Republican congressional leaders of an evolving, multiphase process that would continue throughout the year.</p> <p>\u201cThere is no three-phase process,\u201d Cotton said on Hewitt\u2019s show. \u201cThere is no three-step plan. That is just political talk. It\u2019s just politicians engaging in spin.\u201d</p> <p>At the White House, Spicer was confronted at his briefing about Cotton\u2019s criticism as an example of the administration lacking control over what its own supporters are saying. \u201cIt\u2019s a free country,\u201d Spicer said. \u201cHe can say what he wants.\u201d</p> <p>Rush Limbaugh echoed Cotton\u2019s concerns <a href=\"https://www.rushlimbaugh.com/daily/2017/03/14/do-they-really-want-to-repeal-obamacare/\" shape=\"rect\">on his airwaves Tuesday</a>. The conservative talk-radio commentator said the message from congressional leaders is: \u201cWe gotta pass the bill. We gotta send it up to president. Ryan (Wis.), arguing that it would fracture Trump\u2019s coalition of working- and middle-class voters, many of them older and subsisting on federal aid.\n\nVice President Pence and administration officials scrambled Tuesday to salvage the plan amid widespread dissatisfaction in both the Senate and House over the Congressional Budget Office\u2019s estimate that 24 million fewer people would be insured in a decade under the Ryan proposal, titled the American Health Care Act.\n\nTrump \u2014 who has not yet fully used the bully pulpit of the presidency to rally support for the plan \u2014 spoke privately with Ryan on Tuesday afternoon. These lawmakers, many of whom are members of the House Freedom Caucus, see the proposal as insufficiently conservative because it is too reliant on tax credits and does not fully repeal the Affordable Care Act, the law often known as Obamacare that was signed by President Barack Obama.\n\nFormer House speaker Newt Gingrich, a Trump ally, said the president should not be tempted by outside calls to abandon the Ryan bill and should instead \u201clet the legislative process work.\u201d\n\n\u201cWhat\u2019s happening now is a gut check,\u201d he said. \u201cIt\u2019s not unusual.
