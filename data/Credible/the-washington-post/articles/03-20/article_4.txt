As with any counterintelligence investigation, this will also include an assessment of whether any crimes were committed.\u201d</em></p></blockquote> <p>In an <a href=\"https://www.intelligence.senate.gov/sites/default/files/documents/ICA_2017_01.pdf\" target=\"_blank\">unusual declassified report</a> released in January, the CIA, the FBI and the National Security Agency had announced that they had \u201chigh confidence\u201d that \u201cRussian President Vladimir Putin ordered an influence campaign in 2016 aimed at the US presidential election\u201d and that \u201cPutin and the Russian Government developed a clear preference for President-elect Trump.\u201d Comey\u2019s statement was the first official confirmation that activities of people associated with the Trump campaign also were being investigated.</p> <p>Moreover, Comey firmly rejected <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/fact-checker/wp/2017/03/05/trumps-evidence-for-obama-wiretap-claims-relies-on-sketchy-anonymously-sourced-reports/?utm_term=.df5dc04b8863\" target=\"_blank\">Trump\u2019s tweeted claim</a> on March 4 that former president Barack Obama had ordered wiretaps of him in the Trump Tower. \u201cI have no information that supports those tweets, and we have looked carefully inside the FBI,\u201d Comey said. \u201cThe Department of Justice has asked me to share with you that the answer is the same for the Department of Justice and all its components. The department has no information that supports those tweets.\u201d</p> <p>Comey made it clear that no president on his own could order a wiretap; such an action must be approved by a judge.</p> <p>Meanwhile, NSA Director Michael S. Rogers dismissed the White House suggestion that Obama asked British intelligence to spy on Trump \u2014 a claim already angrily rejected by Britain. Rogers confirmed that such a request would be a violation of U.S. law and that such a claim by Trump\u00a0\u201cclearly frustrates a key ally of ours.\u201d</p> <p>The testimony overall was clearly damaging to the White House. But then the president\u2019s other Twitter account tried to make lemonade out of lemons.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">The NSA and FBI tell Congress that Russia did not influence electoral process. <a href=\"https://t.co/d9HqkxYBt5\">pic.twitter.com/d9HqkxYBt5</a></p> <p>\u2014 President Trump (@POTUS) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/POTUS/status/843865267008655360\">March 20, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>This was a strange claim, especially because if you watch the video, Rogers makes clear that, when asked about whether Russian cyber-actors changed vote tallies in Michigan and other states, he replied: \u201cI would highlight we are a foreign intelligence organization, not a domestic intelligence organization. So it would be fair to say, we are probably not the best organization to provide a more complete answer.\u201d</p> <p>Indeed, when later asked about the presidential tweet, Comey said it did not reflect what he and Rogers had said: \u201cIt certainly wasn\u2019t our intention to say that today.\u201d</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">Comey on Trump tweet that he testified Russia \"did not influence electoral process\": \"It certainly wasn't our intention to say that today.\" <a href=\"https://t.co/21ASPvFzh1\">pic.twitter.com/21ASPvFzh1</a></p> <p>\u2014 ABC News Politics (@ABCPolitics) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/ABCPolitics/status/843893560415936512\">March 20, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>Undeterred, the president\u2019s Twitter account sent up another flare.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">FBI Director Comey: fmr. DNI Clapper \"right\" to say no evidence of collusion between Russia and Trump Campaign. <a href=\"https://twitter.com/hashtag/ComeyHearing?src=hash\">#ComeyHearing</a> <a href=\"https://t.co/MqBG2xd4s7\">pic.twitter.com/MqBG2xd4s7</a></p> <p>\u2014 President Trump (@POTUS) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/POTUS/status/843902944579936256\">March 20, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>But this was also false. Comey was specifically talking about the Jan. 6 document issued by the intelligence agencies \u2014 a document that the Trump White House has largely ignored, except to suggest it was fake. Former director of national intelligence James Clapper later issued a statement saying it was \u201cin the best interest of all Americans\u201d to investigate possible Trump-Russia ties.</p> <p>Another misleading Trump tweet was this one:</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">FBI Director Comey refuses to deny he briefed President Obama on calls made by Michael Flynn to Russia. <a href=\"https://t.co/cUZ5KgBSYP\">pic.twitter.com/cUZ5KgBSYP</a></p> <p>\u2014 President Trump (@POTUS) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/POTUS/status/843863057516380161\">March 20, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>Comey was very careful not to discuss individuals, such as fired national security adviser Michael Flynn. He noted that the listeners were not to draw any unwarranted conclusions from his refusal to comment. But apparently his warning fell on deaf ears at the White House.</p> <h3>The Pinocchio Test</h3> <p>The president\u2019s tweets throughout the day were misleading, inaccurate or simply false. The gravity of the disclosures might have called for a more restrained response, as the White House\u2019s well of credibility is only so deep.
