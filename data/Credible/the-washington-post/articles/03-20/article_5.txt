Comey and National Security Agency Director Mike Rogers testified before the House Intelligence Committee in a rare public hearing about\u00a0what they know and what they want to know.</p> <p>There was no smoking gun from either side's perspective, but we did learn more about what the FBI is investigating and what Republicans and Democrats in Congress want to investigate.</p> <p>Below\u00a0are six takeaways from the hearing. (For the full rundown of what happened, <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/fbi-director-to-testify-on-russian-interference-in-the-presidential-election/2017/03/20/cdea86ca-0ce2-11e7-9d5a-a83e627dc120_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">read The Washington Post's national security team's report</a>. You can also <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2017/03/20/full-transcript-fbi-director-james-comey-testifies-on-russian-interference-in-2016-election/\" target=\"_blank\">read the entire hearing's transcript</a>.)</p> <h3>1. There's no evidence of Trump's accusation that Obama tapped his phones</h3> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0\" data-category-id=\"live\" data-duration=\"0\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"House committee hearing on Russian involvement in 2016 election\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58cfccb1e4b01a7d50446621\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-20-2017/t_1490019734068_name_Comey_getty_images.jpg\" data-uuid=\"92d280a2-7d90-11e0-b6cc-e4e4a8a38cf0\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"_BWqSqgp7qY\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">The House Intelligence Committee holds its first hearing on Russia's involvement in the 2016 presidential election. (Reuters)</span> </div> </div> <p>Comey quickly confirmed where the last few weeks seemed to have been leading: There is no evidence to back up President Trump's claim that Obama ordered wiretapping of his Trump Tower phones.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal\"> <a name=\"f06aa6a60c\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/files/2017/03/wiretappingtweets1.png&amp;w=1484\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/files/2017/03/wiretappingtweets1.png&amp;w=480\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/files/2017/03/wiretappingtweets1.png\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/wp-apps/imrs.php?src=https://img.washingtonpost.com/blogs/the-fix/files/2017/03/wiretappingtweets1.png&amp;w=480\"/><br/> </div><p></p> <p>\u201cI have no information that supports those tweets,\u201d Comey said.</p> <p>Comey's clear-as-day comments\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/03/20/james-comey-just-made-it-impossible-for-donald-trump-to-keep-saying-he-was-wire-tapped/\" target=\"_blank\">make it impossible for Trump to keep saying he was \u201cwiretapped.</a>\u201d</p> <p>But really, the whole wiretapping thing felt like a sidebar in this hearing to the FBI's broader investigation into Russia and any Trump associates' ties. That's in part because most lawmakers feel it IS a sidebar to the real issue: A foreign country interfered in a U.S. election with the intent of undermining the United States' political process.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">FBI Director stated there\u2019s no evidence of baseless wiretapping claim. Only remaining question: Does <a href=\"https://twitter.com/POTUS\">@POTUS</a> have the decency to apologize? <a href=\"https://t.co/4zy9jp9KEg\">pic.twitter.com/4zy9jp9KEg</a></p> <p>\u2014 Adam Schiff (@RepAdamSchiff) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/RepAdamSchiff/status/843847576222941188\">March 20, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>\u201cThe stakes are nothing less than the future of our democracy and liberal democracy,\u201d said Rep. Adam B. Schiff (Calif.), the top Democrat on the committee.</p> <p>So let's get into that.</p> <h3>2. The FBI is investigating connections between President Trump's campaign associates and the Russian government</h3> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"wirereuters\" data-duration=\"271846\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Comey confirms FBI is investigating Russian interference in 2016 election\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58cfe4ede4b01a7d50446c59\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-20-2017/t_1490028250555_name_20170320_comey_rogers_testimony_mcclain_twp.jpg\" data-uuid=\"3aac1b94-0d78-11e7-aa57-2ca1b05c41b8\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"IDd0ClhOveg\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">FBI Director James B. Comey confirms the Bureau is investigating Russian interference in the 2016 presidential election and possible collusion between Kremlin and Trump associates during a House Intelligence Committee hearing March 20. (Reuters)</span> </div> </div> <p>This might seem\u00a0to be an obvious takeaway. The Washington Post (and other news organizations) <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/us-counterintelligence-officials-are-examining-possible-ties-between-russia-and-trump-associates/2017/01/19/7e10f31c-debd-11e6-918c-99ede3c8cafa_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">reported this</a> two months ago.</p> <p>But members of Congress <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/is-there-an-investigationgraham-demands-answers-from-fbi-on-russia/2017/03/15/9d98c330-097a-11e7-93dc-00f9bdd74ed1_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">have been extremely frustrated</a> that, until Monday, the FBI has refused to privately acknowledge the existence of an investigation, let alone what it is looking into. (The FBI rarely acknowledges publicly the existence of an ongoing investigation except, in Comey's words, in \u201cunusual circumstances.\u201d)</p> <p>This, apparently, is one of those circumstances. On Monday, Comey told Congress and the world that, yes, the FBI is investigating Russia's meddling in the U.S. election. In addition, it is investigating whether there was any coordination between the Trump campaign and Russia and \u201cwhether any crimes were committed.\u201d</p> <h3>3. But the FBI is going to be VERY tight-lipped about the investigation</h3> <p>In the next breath, Comey said he is not going to share much else about the investigation other than it exists: \u201cWe just cannot do our work well or fairly if we start talking about it while we're doing it.\u201d</p> <p>And he cautioned\u00a0Congress and the public not too read too much into the FBI's silence about it: \u201cI know speculating is part of human nature, but it really isn't fair to draw conclusions simply because I say that I can't comment.\u201d</p> <p>(A caution Trump himself <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/03/20/comey-please-dont-draw-conclusions-from-my-no-comments-trump-nah-im-good/\" target=\"_blank\">did not seem to heed</a>:)</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">FBI Director Comey refuses to deny he briefed President Obama on calls made by Michael Flynn to Russia. <a href=\"https://t.co/cUZ5KgBSYP\">pic.twitter.com/cUZ5KgBSYP</a></p> <p>\u2014 President Trump (@POTUS) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/POTUS/status/843863057516380161\">March 20, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>Throughout the hearing, Comey would stay true to his word, especially when\u00a0lawmakers asked about what investigators knew with regard to Trump officials: \u201cI really don't want to get into answering any questions about any U.S. person,\u201d he said at one point.</p> <h3>4. Democrats seem pretty sure associates with Trump's campaign colluded with Russia</h3> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"wirereuters\" data-duration=\"1070636\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Schiff: We'll never know if 'Russian intervention' determined 2016 election\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58cfe79be4b01a7d5044701e\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-20-2017/t_1490020101597_name_Screen_Shot_2017_03_20_at_10_28_11_AM.png\" data-uuid=\"d5380758-0d79-11e7-aa57-2ca1b05c41b8\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"FsHj2bFXCAU\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">At the House Intelligence Committee's first hearing on Russia's involvement in the 2016 election, ranking Democrat Adam Schiff (Calif.) said the U.S. is engaged in \"a new war of ideas.\" (Reuters)</span> </div> </div> <p>Here's what we know about Trump's campaign and Russia:</p> <ol> <li>The intelligence community has concluded that Russia meddled in the U.S. election to undermine faith in the democratic process and harm Hillary Clinton's candidacy, in part because Russian President Vladimir Putin blamed then-Secretary of State Clinton for domestic protests against his authority\u00a0in 2011-2012.</li> <li>Two members of Trump's inner circle \u2014\u00a0former national security adviser Michael Flynn and Attorney General Jeff Sessions \u2014\u00a0have publicly acknowledged that they failed to disclose private conversations with the Russian ambassador to the United States. Flynn lost his job; Sessions agreed to recuse himself from the FBI's investigation into Russian meddling in the U.S. election as a result.</li> </ol> <p>But meeting with the Russian ambassador to Washington isn't illegal \u2014\u00a0in fact, you could argue\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/03/02/so-is-it-ever-appropriate-for-a-senator-to-meet-with-the-russian-ambassador/\" target=\"_blank\">it's part of diplomacy</a>. It's the lack of disclosure that got both men in trouble.</p> <p>Democrats are suggesting that Russia's involvement in the election and Trump officials' lack of disclosure about their ties to Russia point to something more. In a 15-minute opening statement, Schiff laid out a series of ties between Trump's campaign and Russia, citing former British spy\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/fbi-once-planned-to-pay-former-british-spy-who-authored-controversial-trump-dossier/2017/02/28/896ab470-facc-11e6-9845-576c69081518_story.html?utm_term=.17e01478bef8\" target=\"_blank\">Christopher Steele</a>, who compiled an unverified dossier on Trump and Russia. They included accusations that:</p> <ul> <li>One of Trump's national security advisers during the campaign, Carter Page,\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/economy/trump-advisers-public-comments-ties-to-moscow-stir-unease-in-both-parties/2016/08/05/2e8722fa-5815-11e6-9aee-8075993d73a2_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">has ties to Russia</a> and has praised its president, Vladimir Putin.</li> <li>Former Trump campaign manager Paul Manafort <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2016/08/19/paul-manaforts-complicated-ties-to-ukraine-explained/\" target=\"_blank\">had been on the payroll for pro-Russian interests</a> in Ukraine.</li> <li>Trump officials met with the Russian ambassador to Washington during the Republican National Convention. At that convention, Republicans changed their platform to remove a section that supported giving weapons to Ukraine as it battles Russia for territory.</li> <li>Former Trump adviser Roger Stone boasted in a speech that he knew of impending WikiLeaks documents related to Hillary Clinton's campaign before they were published.</li> <li>And Flynn and Sessions would go on to avoid disclosing their conversations with the Russian ambassador during or shortly after the campaign.</li> </ul> <p>After spelling all that out,\u00a0Schiff (rhetorically) asked this:</p> <blockquote class=\"citation\"><p>\u201cIs it possible that all of these events and reports are completely unrelated and nothing more than an entirely unhappy coincidence? Yes, it is possible. But it is also possible, maybe more than possible, that they are not coincidental, not disconnected and not unrelated, and that the Russians use the same techniques to corrupt U.S. persons that they employed in Europe and elsewhere. We simply don't know. Not yet. And we owe it to the country to find out.\u201d</p></blockquote> <p>Comey and Rogers would not comment. But we know what Democrats\u00a0have concluded.</p> <h3>5. Republicans, meanwhile, want to focus on\u00a0intelligence leaks to the press</h3> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"wirereuters\" data-duration=\"202468\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Nunes: Russian hack is 'no surprise' to House Intelligence Committee\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58cfe7f1e4b059a9e42771b2\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-20-2017/t_1490019150634_name_20170320_nunes_open.jpg\" data-uuid=\"075609ce-0d7a-11e7-aa57-2ca1b05c41b8\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"6it3GBhjhfQ\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">In his opening statement at the House Intelligence Committee hearing on alleged Russian interference in the 2016 election, Chair Devin Nunes (R-Calif.) spoke about what questions they hope to investigate. (Reuters)</span> </div> </div> <p>If Democrats' line of questioning focused on alleged Trump ties to Russia, Republicans zeroed in on charges that someone in the intelligence community <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/national-security-adviser-flynn-discussed-sanctions-with-russian-ambassador-despite-denials-officials-say/2017/02/09/f85b29d6-ee11-11e6-b4ff-ac2cf509efe5_story.html\" target=\"_blank\">was leaking these intelligence reports to the media</a>.</p> <p>Rep. Trey Gowdy (R-S.C.) stressed that the leaks\u00a0may have been politically motivated to weaken the Trump administration.</p> <p>This has the potential to be a major point of friction between Democrats and Republicans on the House Intelligence Committee as it pursues its own investigation into Russian meddling:\u00a0Should the committee put more effort into finding out who leaked the information than whether there is any evidence of collusion between the Trump campaign and Russia?</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i> [<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/03/01/your-cheat-sheet-to-the-four-potential-investigations-of-russia-and-president-trump/\">Your cheat sheet to the four potential investigations of Russia and President Trump</a>] </i> </p> <h3>6. Intelligence officials still don't think there's any evidence Russia's meddling directly influenced\u00a0votes</h3> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"wirereuters\" data-duration=\"71838\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Rogers, Comey say no evidence vote tallies were changed in 2016 election\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58d00c27e4b059a9e42786d5\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-20-2017/t_1490029983322_name_20170320_comey_rogers_together_mcclain_twp.jpg\" data-uuid=\"9a764e16-0d8f-11e7-aa57-2ca1b05c41b8\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"yZGGzqOOVts\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">House Intelligence Committee Chair Devin Nunes (R-Calif.) asked FBI Director James Comey and National Security Agency head Michael S. Rogers about alleged Russian meddling in the 2016 presidential election at a hearing, March 20. (Reuters)</span> </div> </div> <p>Early on, Rep. Devin Nunes (R-Calif.), the committee chairman, asked Rogers and Comey whether they had any evidence that Russia actually changed the vote tallies in key states.</p> <p>Both men said there is no evidence that Russian meddling actually changed votes, something <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/01/05/six-big-takeaways-from-the-extraordinary-congressional-hearing-on-russian-hacking/\" target=\"_blank\">they also said in a January Senate hearing</a>.</p> <p>To some degree, the laser focus on vote tallies is a straw man argument, <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/03/20/the-gop-is-sticking-to-trumps-talking-points-on-russia/\" target=\"_blank\">points out The Fix's Aaron Blake</a>: It's <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2016/08/31/theres-almost-no-chance-our-elections-can-get-hacked-by-the-russians-heres-why/\" target=\"_blank\">nearly impossible to hack voting machines</a>, and intelligence reports never implied that happened.</p> <p>But Republicans like Nunes also can't emphasize enough\u00a0that Russia's meddling to help Trump win probably did not\u00a0<em>actually</em> help Trump win. They do this to underscore the legitimacy of his presidency, yes, but also to get Trump to\u00a0recognize his own legitimacy.</p> <p>Trump was originally hesitant to even acknowledge Russian meddling. He since has acknowledged it,\u00a0but Republicans (and the intelligence community) need his full support to continue to investigate this, wherever it leads.</p></article> <div class=\"clear\"></div> At that convention, Republicans changed their platform to remove a section that supported giving weapons to Ukraine as it battles Russia for territory.\n\nFormer Trump adviser Roger Stone boasted in a speech that he knew of impending WikiLeaks documents related to Hillary Clinton's campaign before they were published.\n\nAnd Flynn and Sessions would go on to avoid disclosing their conversations with the Russian ambassador during or shortly after the campaign.\n\nAfter spelling all that out, Schiff (rhetorically) asked this:\n\n\u201cIs it possible that all of these events and reports are completely unrelated and nothing more than an entirely unhappy coincidence?
