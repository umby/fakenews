Rodriguez/Getty Images)</span> </div> <p>After riots and violent\u00a0protests in and around their campus during the past three months,\u00a0officials at the University of California at Berkeley\u00a0said Wednesday that the school is canceling\u00a0a planned speech by conservative firebrand Ann Coulter because of\u00a0safety concerns.</p> <p>In a letter to a campus Republican group that invited Coulter to speak, university officials said that they made the decision to cancel Coulter\u2019s appearance after assessing the violence that flared on campus in February, when the same college Republican group invited\u00a0right-wing\u00a0provocateur and now-former Breitbart News senior editor Milo Yiannopoulos to speak.</p> <p>The violence and protests caused by Yiannopoulos\u2019s invitation garnered <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/grade-point/wp/2017/02/01/berkeley-cancels-speech-by-breitbrart-writer-milo-amid-intense-protests/?utm_term=.98435e5a9101\">national attention </a>and forced officials to put\u00a0the campus on lockdown. And after the university canceled Yiannopoulos\u2019s talk, President Trump criticized the school and threatened in a tweet to pull\u00a0federal funds from\u00a0UC-Berkeley.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">If U.C. Berkeley does not allow free speech and practices violence on innocent people with a different point of view \u2013 NO FEDERAL FUNDS?</p> <p>\u2014 Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/827112633224544256\">February 2, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>The decisions by UC-Berkeley to cancel both events involving high-profile conservatives are\u00a0especially notable given\u00a0the campus\u2019s role\u00a0during the 1960s and 1970s as the birthplace of the Free Speech Movement and its\u00a0long tradition of social protest.</p> <p>Coulter said in an email to The Washington Post on Wednesday that the university had been trying to force her to cancel her speech by \u201cimposing ridiculous demands\u201d on her but that she still agreed \u201cto all of their silly requirements.\u201d She said she believes that her speech \u201chas been unconstitutionally banned\u201d by the \u201cpublic, taxpayer-supported UC-Berkeley.\u201d</p> <p>Coulter said the university insisted that her speech take place in the middle of the day, that only students could attend\u00a0and that the exact venue wouldn\u2019t be announced until the last minute. She said that she agreed with the conditions but that that apparently\u00a0wasn\u2019t good enough.</p> <p>\u201cThey just up and announced that I was prohibited from speaking anyway,\u201d Coulter said, noting that her speech topic was to be immigration, the subject of one of her books. \u201cI feel like the Constitution is important and that taxpayer-supported universities should not be using public funds to violate American citizens\u2019 constitutional rights.\u201d</p> <p>A conservative national group that was helping to organize the event, Young America\u2019s Foundation, said Coulter also made demands of her own, including that any students engaging in violence be expelled. In her email, Coulter said she is still planning to give her speech, and YAF spokesman Spencer Brown said she has told them that she plans to appear at Berkeley on April 27.</p> <p>\u201cIf Berkeley wants to have free speech, they are going to get it,\u201d Brown said.</p> <p>A university spokesman\u00a0said the school\u00a0has not been in direct contact with Coulter but conveyed its concerns with the student group that invited her. He said the university was especially concerned that holding the event in the late afternoon would risk protests and potential violence stretching into the evening when the area would get crowded with commuters and students.</p> <p>\u201cEverything we\u2019re doing is so the speaker and students can actually exercise their rights without disruption,\u201d Berkeley spokesman Dan Mogulof said. \u201cIt\u2019s unfortunate that there are people who think the university\u2019s efforts to keep students and the speaker herself safe are \u2018silly.\u2019\u00a0\u201d</p> <p>On Wednesday, university officials said they hope to\u00a0reschedule Coulter\u2019s\u00a0event for sometime in September, and they emphasized that they are not canceling her event\u00a0because of her controversial nature\u00a0or sharply conservative views.</p> <p class=\"p1\">\u201cIt has nothing to do with anyone\u2019s political views. We believe in unqualified support to the First Amendment. But we also have an unqualified focus on safety of our students,\u201d <span class=\"s1\">Mogulof said. \u201cWe are going to be making a concerted effort to explain the reasons behind this.\u201d</span></p> <p class=\"p1\">On Saturday, <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/grade-point/wp/2017/04/16/the-berkeley-rally-aftermath-mass-arrests-a-stabbing-and-weaponized-pepsi/?utm_term=.962d99d4e519\">protests again turned violent </a>\u2014 though in the city of Berkeley, not the university campus \u2014 as pro-Trump and anti-Trump protesters\u00a0clashed in the streets. The violence on Saturday was further heightened later in the day as far-left activists and far-right activists joined the fray.</p> <p class=\"p1\">And on Tuesday at Auburn\u00a0University in Alabama, <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/morning-mix/wp/2017/04/19/federal-judge-stops-auburn-from-canceling-white-nationalists-speech-violence-erupts/?utm_term=.53342d30217f\">three people were arrested</a> amid\u00a0protests and a fistfight that occurred over a speech by self-proclaimed white nationalist leader Richard Spencer.</p> <p class=\"p1\">At Berkeley, university officials said the recent violence has caused them to rethink\u00a0where and when to hold such events. In their letter, university officials also partly blamed the college Republican group for inviting Coulter and setting a date for the event \u2014 April 27 \u2014 without consulting the university.</p> <p class=\"p1\">Officials learned of\u00a0Coulter\u2019s event, the letter said, from reading about it in newspapers. And after consulting with university police,\u00a0officials said, they could not find a venue available on that date\u00a0that would allow them to protect Coulter, the audience and Rodriguez/Getty Images)\n\nAfter riots and violent protests in and around their campus during the past three months, officials at the University of California at Berkeley said Wednesday that the school is canceling a planned speech by conservative firebrand Ann Coulter because of safety concerns.\n\nIn a letter to a campus Republican group that invited Coulter to speak, university officials said that they made the decision to cancel Coulter\u2019s appearance after assessing the violence that flared on campus in February, when the same college Republican group invited right-wing provocateur and now-former Breitbart News senior editor Milo Yiannopoulos to speak.\n\nThe violence and protests caused by Yiannopoulos\u2019s invitation garnered national attention and forced officials to put the campus on lockdown. And after the university canceled Yiannopoulos\u2019s talk, President Trump criticized the school and threatened in a tweet to pull federal funds from UC-Berkeley.\n\nIf U.C. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) February 2, 2017\n\nThe decisions by UC-Berkeley to cancel both events involving high-profile conservatives are especially notable given the campus\u2019s role during the 1960s and 1970s as the birthplace of the Free Speech Movement and its long tradition of social protest.\n\nCoulter said in an email to The Washington Post on Wednesday that the university had been trying to force her to cancel her speech by \u201cimposing ridiculous demands\u201d on her but that she still agreed \u201cto all of their silly requirements.\u201d She said she believes that her speech \u201chas been unconstitutionally banned\u201d by the \u201cpublic, taxpayer-supported UC-Berkeley.\u201d\n\nCoulter said the university insisted that her speech take place in the middle of the day, that only students could attend and that the exact venue wouldn\u2019t be announced until the last minute. She said that she agreed with the conditions but that that apparently wasn\u2019t good enough.\n\n\u201cThey just up and announced that I was prohibited from speaking anyway,\u201d Coulter said, noting that her speech topic was to be immigration, the subject of one of her books. \u201cI feel like the Constitution is important and that taxpayer-supported universities should not be using public funds to violate American citizens\u2019 constitutional rights.\u201d\n\nA conservative national group that was helping to organize the event, Young America\u2019s Foundation, said Coulter also made demands of her own, including that any students engaging in violence be expelled. In her email, Coulter said she is still planning to give her speech, and YAF spokesman Spencer Brown said she has told them that she plans to appear at Berkeley on April 27.\n\n\u201cIf Berkeley wants to have free speech, they are going to get it,\u201d Brown said.\n\nA university spokesman said the school has not been in direct contact with Coulter but conveyed its concerns with the student group that invited her. He said the university was especially concerned that holding the event in the late afternoon would risk protests and potential violence stretching into the evening when the area would get crowded with commuters and students.\n\n\u201cEverything we\u2019re doing is so the speaker and students can actually exercise their rights without disruption,\u201d Berkeley spokesman Dan Mogulof said. \u201cIt\u2019s unfortunate that there are people who think the university\u2019s efforts to keep students and the speaker herself safe are \u2018silly.\u2019 \u201d\n\nOn Wednesday, university officials said they hope to reschedule Coulter\u2019s event for sometime in September, and they emphasized that they are not canceling her event because of her controversial nature or sharply conservative views.\n\n\u201cIt has nothing to do with anyone\u2019s political views. We believe in unqualified support to the First Amendment. But we also have an unqualified focus on safety of our students,\u201d Mogulof said. \u201cWe are going to be making a concerted effort to explain the reasons behind this.\u201d\n\nOn Saturday, protests again turned violent \u2014 though in the city of Berkeley, not the university campus \u2014 as pro-Trump and anti-Trump protesters clashed in the streets. The violence on Saturday was further heightened later in the day as far-left activists and far-right activists joined the fray.\n\nAnd on Tuesday at Auburn University in Alabama, three people were arrested amid protests and a fistfight that occurred over a speech by self-proclaimed white nationalist leader Richard Spencer.\n\nAt Berkeley, university officials said the recent violence has caused them to rethink where and when to hold such events.
