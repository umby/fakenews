A place called Scandals Saloon sits across the street, but it turns out Stone has sent the wrong address.</p> <p>\u201cWell, you will have to kill that lede,\u201d says the man who knows enough about this dance with the journalistic profession to recognize what might have been a resonant detail in a first paragraph. Stone, after all, has seldom met a scandal he couldn\u2019t curl up with and adore.</p> <p>The next part of the directions to his personal office and broadcast studio calls for locating a \u201cdoor discreetly marked \u2018A,\u2019\u2009\u201d which might be helpful, except for the crucial fact that there is no door discreetly marked \u201cA\u201d at the new address next to a bathroom supplies depot. There is, however, a blacked-out door with no markings, and the man who emerges from behind it, wearing shorts, loafers with no socks, a rakishly loosened tie and an untucked white dress shirt, is unmistakably Stone.</p> <p>\u201cWhat happened to our \u2018A\u2019?\u201d he calls out to his two young assistants.</p> <p>Inside, Stone keeps a bong in the shape of Richard Nixon\u2019s head and a framed drawing<b> </b>of a roll of toilet paper with Nixon\u2019s face on each sheet. In the early 1970s, Stone became a master of the darker political arts, meddling surreptitiously with the Democrats, as part of Nixon\u2019s Committee to Re-Elect the President, nicknamed CREEP.</p> <p>More than 4\u00bd decades later, Stone still likes to think of himself as a man who operates in the shadows. \u201cP.S.: I have no boring clients,\u201d he tantalizing says one evening, without naming names or explaining exactly what he does. But the darkened office of this buddy of President Donald Trump is also buzzy because the old trickster is having a very public moment.</p> <p>A regular human might find it unsettling that Congress is threatening to haul him before a committee investigating possible Russian meddling in the 2016 presidential election. Stone is reveling in it.</p> <p>\u201cI want to get called,\u201d Stone, 64, says over dinner one night. \u201cI just want a fair chance to tell my side of it.\u201d </p> <p>Stone dismisses one of his main pursuers, the former federal prosecutor and California <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/03/22/meet-rep-adam-schiff-the-quiet-lawmaker-fast-becoming-democrats-face-of-opposition-to-president-trump/?utm_term=.80d7ff9e5656\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Democratic congressman Adam B. Schiff</a>, as being \u201cfull of Schiff.\u201d Stone is thinking about suing Schiff for defamation, even though congressional immunity makes such legal action nearly impossible.</p> <p>Schiff, in a sense, is a fantastic, accidental PR man for Stone. The congressional attention couldn\u2019t arrive at a better time for Stone, who has a book, <a href=\"https://read.amazon.com/kp/embed?asin=B01MTV4G5K&amp;preview=newtab&amp;linkCode=kpe&amp;ref_=cm_sw_r_kb_dp_7ZP9ybABQN70D&amp;tag=thewaspos09-20\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.amazon.com\">\u201cThe Making of the President 2016,\u201d</a> to hawk and who will be featured next month in a much hyped <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/reliable-source/wp/2017/03/29/in-netflix-documentary-roger-stone-compares-donald-trump-to-a-racehorse/?utm_term=.310ee9602dc3\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Netflix documentary</a>, \u201cGet Me Roger Stone.\u201d</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal\"> <a name=\"\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1484w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/12/Style/Images/STONE_01.JPG?uuid=KQm8nh_JEee7WadMyvHQLw\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/12/Style/Images/STONE_01.JPG?uuid=KQm8nh_JEee7WadMyvHQLw\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/12/Style/Images/STONE_01.JPG?uuid=KQm8nh_JEee7WadMyvHQLw\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/12/Style/Images/STONE_01.JPG?uuid=KQm8nh_JEee7WadMyvHQLw\"/><br/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Proud to be a Nixon man: Stone in his memorabilia-overrun office. (Andrew Innerarity/For The Washington Post)</span> </div> <p>Stone, a prolific author who hosts a radio program and runs a website, <a href=\"http://stonecoldtruth.com/\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"stonecoldtruth.com\">StoneColdTruth.com</a>, never really went away. He\u2019s been talking nonstop for decades, pointing an accusing finger at Lyndon Johnson for alleged complicity in the Kennedy assassination, rooting around in Bill Clinton\u2019s extramarital misdeeds, depicting the Bushes as a \u201ccrime family.\u201d It\u2019s just that now there are more people listening. </p> <p> His studio \u2014 a man-cave-style haunt slathered floor to ceiling with Nixon memorabilia and conspiracy books \u2014 has been stocked with professional lighting and a broadcast-quality audio line by Stone\u2019s 19-year-old grandson, Nick.</p> <p>The door opens, and a woman in workout clothes strolls in with a 9-month-old Yorkshire terrier named Mimi. \u201cAh, Mrs. Stone!\u201d Roger Stone says.</p> <p>Mimi pulls at her leash and lets out a torrent of high-pitched yips.</p> <p>\u201cShe usually only chases Russians,\u201d Stone deadpans.</p> <p>Stone met his Cuban American wife, Nydia, during one of Ronald Reagan\u2019s presidential campaigns, where she was working as a photographer. In a Polaroid picture taped to her computer monitor, the future married couple are slender and stylish with deep 1980s tans. </p> <p>Stone can\u2019t be bothered to reminisce. He\u2019s got an interview coming up, but he\u2019s not sure about the host.</p> <p>\u201cWhere\u2019s he from?\u201d Stone calls out.</p> <p>\u201cMinnesota, I think,\u201d John, his driver and studio assistant, says.</p> <p>\u201cI think it\u2019s Sweden,\u201d Stone says.</p> <p>Turns out it\u2019s neither. The program is hosted by Stefan Molyneux, an Irish-born Canadian.</p> <p>\u201cAll day it\u2019s like this. Every day,\u201d Stone\u2019s wife says, with a sigh and little smile. \u201cI\u2019ve gone deaf listening to him!\u201d</p> <p>Stone thinks Google and other big Internet firms are trying to crush his business by blocking traffic to his website, which has dropped precipitously. Still, he says, \u201cI don\u2019t make a million dollars. But I do okay.\u201d He also believes he\u2019s under surveillance by federal investigators. His evidence is a <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/19/us/politics/trump-russia-associates-investigation.html\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.nytimes.com\">New York Times report</a> that said he was being scrutinized for possible Russia ties and news reports about his private communications that he believes were leaked by government snoops.</p> <p>On top of that, he thinks someone is trying to kill him \u2014 either with poison or via weaponized automobile after the car he was riding in was <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/morning-mix/wp/2017/03/16/trump-adviser-roger-stone-said-he-was-a-victim-in-florida-hit-and-run-possibly-deliberate/?utm_term=.d34a03b449a2\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">T-boned recently</a> by a vehicle with tinted windows that sped off after the collision.</p> <p>\u201cThe suspect list,\u201d he says, \u201cwould be infinite.\u201d</p> <div class=\"subhead\">No mincing of words</div> <p>A large Buddhist shrine, known as a butsudan, is stationed in the living room at Stone\u2019s spacious bungalow along a canal in Fort Lauderdale. Nydia \u2014 whose father was a military attache in the United States for Cuba\u2019s Batista regime before it was toppled by Fidel Castro \u2014 hosts Buddhist gatherings there.</p> <p>\u201cI chant for Roger\u2019s Buddha nature to emerge,\u201d she says.</p> <p></p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo-left\" style=\"width:300px;\"> <a name=\"\"></a> <img alt=\"\" class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1024w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/18/Style/Images/Merlin_44868.jpg?uuid=CJYdjCSHEee7nYzWEY4UCQ\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/18/Style/Images/Merlin_44868.jpg?uuid=CJYdjCSHEee7nYzWEY4UCQ\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/18/Style/Images/Merlin_44868.jpg?uuid=CJYdjCSHEee7nYzWEY4UCQ\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/18/Style/Images/Merlin_44868.jpg?uuid=CJYdjCSHEee7nYzWEY4UCQ\"/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Roger Stone with his wife Nydia in their Washington home in 1996. (Ron Edmonds/AP)</span> </div> <p>The effectiveness of those appeals is certainly open to interpretation. In his book, Stone calls Sen. Ted Cruz (R-Tex.), \u201ca scoundrel\u201d and \u201cthe most prolific liar ever to run for president.\u201d He regularly engages in vituperative Twitter tirades. Stone allows that he\u2019s been prone over the years to tweet while drinking cocktails. \u201cTwo martinis,\u201d he says. \u201cI\u2019m a lightweight.\u201d </p> <p>Last year, he was banned from CNN after calling commentator Ana Navarro <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/erik-wemple/wp/2016/02/23/cnn-ditches-trump-supporter-roger-stone-after-nasty-bigoted-tweets/?utm_term=.dd1a16aa6126\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">\u201can entitled diva bitch,\u201d</a> on Twitter. He\u2019s also called her \u201cfat and stupid.\u201d In 2012, he tweeted that the liberal broadcaster Roland Martin is \u201ca stupid negro.\u201d</p> <p>Stone says he regrets the Martin remarks and points to statements he\u2019s made urging Republicans to do a better job of outreach to African Americans. But he doesn\u2019t plan to let up on Navarro, a co-chair of then-presidential candidate John McCain\u2019s Hispanic advisory council, because he thinks she is overstating her credentials as a \u201cRepublican strategist.\u201d</p> <p>Navarro did not respond to an interview request. Martin called Stone a \u201csuperficial, petulant, racist child.\u201d The media, Martin said, tends to \u201csay, \u2018Oh, he\u2019s a colorful character.\u2019 No, he\u2019s a racist, sexist individual.\u201d</p> <p>For years, Stone has maintained residences in Florida and in New York, where he rents a small place in Harlem. In Florida, the Stones used to live on South Beach, but he tired of the noise and moved north to Fort Lauderdale, where his stepson is a Broward County sheriff\u2019s deputy. The couple share their house with two Yorkies \u2014 Mimi and Max, a 16-year-old who is blind, incontinent and nearly deaf \u2014 and an 80-year-old former pilot with cancer who is crashing in a back bedroom. Boxes fill the living room because the Stones have had some tensions with their landlord here, he says, and are in the process of moving to another rental nearby.</p> <p></p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-normal\"> <a name=\"\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_1484w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/04/06/Style/Images/Merlin_272326.jpg?uuid=KbDmLvwPEeWIb6A326ODAQ\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/04/06/Style/Images/Merlin_272326.jpg?uuid=KbDmLvwPEeWIb6A326ODAQ\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/04/06/Style/Images/Merlin_272326.jpg?uuid=KbDmLvwPEeWIb6A326ODAQ\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_480w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2016/04/06/Style/Images/Merlin_272326.jpg?uuid=KbDmLvwPEeWIb6A326ODAQ\"/><br/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Republican operatives Paul Manafort, Roger Stone and Lee Atwater in 1985. (Harry Naltchayan/The Washington Post)</span> </div> <p>The house is thick with heavy art deco furniture that presents a considerable challenge for his grandson, who is loading pieces precariously into a pickup truck parked out front next to Stone\u2019s sleek Jaguar XKR. Stone, a prodigious accumulator of things, says he has 50 winter suits and 20 summer suits, all custom made. In his home office, a dozen or so hats rest on head-shaped forms like those commonly used for wigs. He places a stiff-brimmed hat with a center crease on his head, and says, \u201cThis is an open roader.\u201d</p> <p>A similar hat was worn by the man who was escorting Lee Harvey Oswald when he was fatally shot, Stone says.</p> <p>Stone worked briefly for the Trump campaign in its early days and was either fired (Trump\u2019s version) or quit (Stone\u2019s version), but stayed in contact as an informal adviser and energetic promoter, <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/how-alex-jones-conspiracy-theorist-extraordinaire-got-donald-trumps-ear/2016/11/17/583dc190-ab3e-11e6-8b45-f8e493f06fcd_story.html?utm_term=.536684af5d9a\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">including connecting Trump with the conspiracy theorist Alex Jones of Infowars.com</a>. When he left, Stone says, the campaign tried unsuccessfully to get him to sign a confidentiality agreement that would have prevented him from talking to reporters.</p> <p>\u201cI would have had to agree to not breathe any air,\u201d he says. </p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/politics/wp/2017/03/03/the-web-of-relationships-between-team-trump-and-russia/?utm_term=.4f69c2ec3838\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">The alleged connections between Trump\u2019s campaign and Russia</a>]</i> </p> <p>The truth is reporters flock to Stone, in part, because he talks their language and because he\u2019s dishy and quotable. He\u2019s forever referencing his \u201csources,\u201d lately focused on tantalizing inside dope about the White House.</p> <p>The chaos of packing up an overstuffed household does little to distract Stone. After all, the Hollywood Reporter is on the line, asking about the documentary premiere.</p> <p>\u201cI have two tickets,\u201d Stone says. One is for himself, he quips, and the other for \u201ca libel lawyer.\u201d</p> <p>In between calls, Stone annotates a transcript of Schiff, the California congressman, talking at a hearing last month about hacked emails released by WikiLeaks. Stone has aroused Schiff\u2019s attention by boasting about communicating with Julian Assange, the WikiLeaks founder (Stone now says it was \u201cback-channel communication\u201d through a journalist source). Schiff also questioned Stone\u2019s direct-messaging with \u201cGuccifer 2.0,\u201d a Twitter user who a U.S. intelligence assessment concluded with \u201chigh confidence\u201d was in league with Russian military intelligence, known as the GRU, to tamper with the U.S. presidential election.</p> <p>At his desk, Stone reads aloud from the transcript.</p> <p>\u201cSchiff: Mr. Stone was in direct communication with a creature of Russian GRU, Guccifer 2.0,\u201d Stone recites in a loud, mocking tone.</p> <p>\u201cNo, I don\u2019t concede that! Wrong!\u201d Stone says in a booming voice as if Schiff were in the room with him. \u201cUnsupportable. .\u2009.\u2009. It\u2019s innocuous. Sorry guys, you cannot prove Guccifer is a Russian agent. When the intelligence services use the word \u2018assessment\u201d that means, \u2018We don\u2019t know. We\u2019re guessing.\u2019\u2009\u201d</p> <p> </p> <p> <b> </b> </p> <p>Stone\u2019s been working on the annotation for days. But he keeps having to put it down. He has a book to flog.</p> <div class=\"subhead\">\u2018A tough cookie\u2019</div> <p>The three dozen or so people assembled to meet Roger Stone at the Palm Beach Bookstore \u2014 a snug place on a glitzy avenue at the heart of the presidential weekend-getaway town \u2014 include a man with one leg and wearing a Make America Great Again hat who says he attended 14 Trump rallies during the campaign. Another man has arrived via motorcycle to ask Stone to prevail on President Trump to build a 90-mile automobile bridge to connect Key West and Cuba.</p> <p>\u201cYou\u2019re a tough cookie, Mr. Roger!\u201d the man blurts out during Stone\u2019s book talk.</p> <p>Moments later, a young woman walks up and slips Stone a book to sign.</p> <p>\u201cI\u2019m the mystery texter,\u201d she says.</p> <p>Off to the side, Billy Benson \u2014 a former Democratic political operative whose wife, Candice Cohen, owns the store \u2014 watches with amusement in seersucker pants and a bright lavender blazer that he says he picked up cheap at a secondhand store.</p> <p>\u201cWe hated each other when we lived in D.C.,\u201d Benson says of Stone. \u201cHe was doing tricks. I was doing tricks. We get along great now.\u201d</p> <p>Five young women, including the girlfriend of Stone\u2019s driver, sidle up next to him for a photo. They\u2019re speaking Ukrainian. \u201cIt sounds like Russian, but it\u2019s different,\u201d one says, before another abruptly cuts off the conversation.</p> <p>After his chat, Stone explains to a few attendees that he asked his tailor to make him pants with a 2-inch waistband and wide legs because he wanted to emulate the style once worn by Cab Calloway.</p> <p>Stone doesn\u2019t have time to hang around. A cable film crew is waiting at a nearby hotel.</p> <p>He\u2019s famished when the interview finishes, but being Roger Stone is a 24-hour-a-day proposition, so he\u2019s got the media tagging along for dinner. It\u2019s getting late and a television reporter at the table mentions he\u2019s got an early broadcast in the morning.</p> <p>Stone pulls a black tube from his jacket pocket: Clinique for Men face bronzer. He swears by it.</p> </article> <div class=\"clear\"></div> </div> </div> <div class=\"pb-container\"> <div class=\"moat-trackable pb-f-theme-normal pb-1 pb-feature pb-layout-item
