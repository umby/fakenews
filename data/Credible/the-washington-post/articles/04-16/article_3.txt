zipping around the greens of his private golf club.</p> <p>The day before, the president also passed the morning playing golf at Trump International Golf Club, according to someone who saw him on the course.</p> <p id=\"U120017961326376RF\">And in addressing North Korea\u2019s latest provocation, Trump \u2014 who spent at least a portion of both evenings greeting guests at his private Mar-a-Lago Club in Palm Beach \u2014 deferred to his defense secretary, Jim Mattis, a retired four-star Marine Corps general. </p> <p>\u201cThe president and his military team are aware of North Korea\u2019s <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2017/04/16/trumps-deputy-national-security-adviser-wont-say-whether-shes-being-redeployed-to-singapore/?utm_term=.9a38d8649b68\" shape=\"rect\">Trump\u2019s deputy national security adviser won\u2019t say whether she\u2019s being redeployed to Singapore</a>]</i> </p> <p id=\"U12001796132637MFI\">The president was in touch, however, with Pence, Secretary of State Rex Tillerson and Mattis, with whom he spoke several times between Thursday and Saturday as the situation unfolded, according to a senior administration official.</p> <p>But in the hours after the failed launch, Trump \u2014 who previously has said the United States is willing to intervene on its own if others in the region don\u2019t step up \u2014 allowed his advisers, including McFarland and national security adviser H.R. McMaster, to outline the administration\u2019s approach on the Sunday talk shows.</p> <p>McMaster and McFarland offered a notable contrast to Trump\u2019s previously bellicose statements. Both seemed to be calling for patience and tamped down the possibility of imminent military intervention.</p> <p id=\"U120017961326376eD\">McMaster, appearing on ABC\u2019s \u201cThis Week,\u201d said that the United States is exploring \u201ca range of options\u201d to respond to an increasingly provocative North Korea but that the administration would like \u201cto take action short of armed conflict, so we can avoid the worst.\u201d</p> <p>McFarland, meanwhile, played down the significance of yet another failed North Korean missile launch, saying that the test early Sunday was \u201cnot a surprise.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"!Daily\">\u201cEven in the last year, President Kim of North Korea has launched over 30 missiles,\u201d McFarland said. \u201cMost of them have failed. So it didn\u2019t come as a surprise to us. We were expecting something surrounding the birthday of his grandfather.\u201d </p> <p channel=\"!Daily\">Saturday was the anniversary of the 1912 birth of Kim Il Sung, North Korea\u2019s founder and the current leader\u2019s grandfather.</p> <p id=\"U12001796132637nJI\">McMaster and McFarland also talked up the role China could play in bringing North Korea to heel \u2014 and urged some patience on that front. About 80\u2009percent of North Korea\u2019s trade is with neighboring China, giving the country a good deal of leverage, they suggested.</p> <p id=\"U12001796132637e0E\">McFarland said an array of strategies were discussed when Chinese President Xi Jinping recently visited Trump at Mar-a-Lago.</p> <p>\u201cIt\u2019s like your kids in the back of the car on a long trip saying, \u2018When are we going to get there?\u2019 \u201d McFarland said. \u201cWell, in this case, I think we should give the Chinese president some opportunities and some time, as well as pursuing the economic and diplomatic pressures that we have and that our allies have that we can bring to bear on North Korea.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"!Daily\"> </p> <p channel=\"!Daily\"> </p> <p id=\"U12001796132637hVE\"> <span></span>Trump, who save for muscling Justice Neil M. Gorsuch onto the Supreme Court has no real achievements on Capitol Hill of which to boast, has weathered several national security and military crises fewer than 100 days into his presidency, and seems to enjoy both the process and the projection of strength such crises involve.</p> <p>After Syrian President Bashar al-Assad deployed chemical weapons against his own people, Trump worked with his military advisers and responded swiftly,<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/horrible-pictures-of-suffering-moved-trump-to-action-on-syria/2017/04/07/9aa9fcc8-1bce-11e7-8003-f55b4c1cfae2_story.html?utm_term=.8d4202e9af1c\" shape=\"rect\"> firing 59 Tomahawk cruise missiles</a> at a Syrian airfield.</p> <p channel=\"!Daily\">Aides to the president said he enjoyed the orderly process, overseen by McMaster, which resembled the streamlined operations of a more traditional White House, with Trump requesting a range of responses and each adviser weighing in with their expertise before the president made a final decision.</p> <p id=\"U120017961326370OC\">But Trump seemed determined to avoid conflict of just about any sort this weekend \u2014 North Korea or otherwise. He spent his three days in Palm Beach shuttling between Mar-a-Lago and Trump International Golf Club, spending almost no time outside the cocoon of his two palm-tree-lined properties.</p> <p id=\"U12001796132637vuB\">On Saturday, after golf, the president\u2019s motorcade took a different, longer route than usual to return to his private club, coming back from the north and along the coast. The circuitous trip allowed Trump to avoid the protesters who had gathered along the main thoroughfare just west of Mar-a-Lago to call on him to release his tax returns.</p> <p id=\"U12001796132637PDD\">White House press secretary Sean Spicer said the route was determined by the Secret Service. </p> <p id=\"U120017961326374lB\">Trump, a normally prolific \u2014 and brash \u2014 user of Twitter, also spent the weekend largely quiet on social media; his last tweet heading into the weekend concerned his enthusiasm about the White House Easter Egg Roll on Monday.</p> <p id=\"U12001796132637HJG\">On Sunday morning, Trump briefly took to Twitter, boasting about his electoral college victory in the 2016 presidential election and asking why his tax returns mattered in light of his win. He also extolled his efforts to rebuild the military.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2017/04/16/after-a-day-of-marches-trump-asks-why-people-are-still-talking-about-his-taxes/?utm_term=.d74525b34106\" shape=\"rect\">Trump asks why people are still talking about his taxes a day after protesters asked for his returns</a>]</i> </p> <p id=\"U12001796132637q0E\">He also seemed to admit he was using the Treasury Department\u2019s currency report \u2014 which did not label China a currency manipulator \u2014 as a political bargaining weapon.</p> <p channel=\"!Daily\">During the campaign, Trump repeatedly said he would brand China a currency manipulator on Day One of his administration. On Day 83, he officially backed off that course.</p> <p id=\"U12001796132637mJH\">\u201cWhy would I call China a currency manipulator when they are working with us on the North Korea problem?\u201d he wrote on Twitter on Sunday. \u201cWe will see what happens!\u201d</p> <p id=\"U12001796132637pP\">But then the president retreated back into his calm Palm Beach world. He attended Easter services at Bethesda-by-the-Sea church here with his wife, Melania, son Barron, and daughter from his second marriage, \u00adTiffany, before returning to Mar-a-Lago for an annual brunch and Easter egg hunt with his eldest sons, Don Jr. and Eric, and their families, according to an aide. </p> <p id=\"U12001796132637m4G\">One intrusion from the outside world came from Don Jr., who over the weekend was <a href=\"https://www.instagram.com/p/BS60YoEjM9w/?taken-by=donaldjtrumpjr&amp;hl=en\" shape=\"rect\">sporting a green T-shirt</a> that read \u201cVery Fake News\u201d in block white letters \u2014 a reminder of all those who, the president believes, have treated him so unfairly. </p> <p channel=\"wp.com\"></p> </article> <div class=\"clear\"></div> </div> </div> <div
