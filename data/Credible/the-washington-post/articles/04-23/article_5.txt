Guy. (Marvin Joseph/The Washington Post)</span> </div> <p id=\"U12002053507228H6E\">The moment he emerged onstage in a black jacket and red bow tie, the crowd noise hit near-deafening decibels. A sea of iPhones appeared, everyone stretching and jostling for the best possible photo angle. They cupped their hands to their mouths, screaming his name.</p> <p>\u201cGreetings, fellow citizens,\u201d <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/video/national/bill-nye-at-march-for-science-science-serves-everyone-of-us/2017/04/22/5e42e82e-2760-11e7-928e-3624539060e8_video.html\" shape=\"rect\">Bill Nye said</a> to the thousands huddled beneath umbrellas and hand-lettered signs. \u201cWe are marching today to remind people everywhere, our lawmakers especially, of the significance of science for our health and prosperity.\u201d</p> <p>Near the foot of the stage, a young woman with a bright-green pixie cut shouted: \u201cI love you!\u201d</p> <p>It was a significant moment \u2014 for science, for William Sanford Nye and for the masses who have followed him for decades, from fuzzy TV screens in their middle school classrooms to the grounds of the Washington Monument at Saturday\u2019s <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/speaking-of-science/wp/2017/04/21/the-march-for-science-began-with-this-persons-throwaway-line-on-reddit/?tid=a_inl&amp;utm_term=.9bd65d31ea13\" shape=\"rect\">March for Science</a>. He is beloved by millennials who came of age watching the \u201990s-era PBS series \u201cBill Nye the Science Guy,\u201d a role that made him an icon: half mad professor, half Mr. Rogers, perpetually clad in a pale-blue lab coat and bow tie as he unveiled the science of eroding mountains or orbiting comets with theatrical flourish.</p> <p>More than 20 years later, the 61-year-old still wears the bow ties, and he still punctuates his speech with impassioned catchphrases. (\u201cIt\u2019s not magic, it\u2019s science!\u201d is his new favorite.) But now his disheveled locks and vaguely Vulcan eyebrows are streaked with gray, and his persona has assumed a new edge. He has become more than the zany educator-entertainer who charmed kids with cartoonish sound effects. He is an activist for science, leading those now-grownups into political battle.</p> <p>Of all the roles he has played, this is the one he was preparing for all along.</p> <p> </p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"segments\" data-duration=\"86872\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Bill Nye on the March for Science: 'There's been a movement to set science aside.'\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58fa439fe4b07c67ff08857c\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/04-21-2017/t_1492796013937_name_20170421_billnye_wapo.jpg\" data-uuid=\"5e5809e0-26b9-11e7-928e-3624539060e8\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"cIlbmu8rRzM\"> <script src=\"https://d1pz6dax0t5mop.cloudfront.net/sandbox/powaBoot.js\"></script> <script src=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-stat/posttv/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">(The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p></p> <p> <b>\u201cI did imagine</b> it could come to this,\u201d Nye said Friday, during a visit to The Washington Post the day before the march.</p> <p>By \u201cthis,\u201d he meant the legions of scientists, doctors, engineers and concerned members of the public taking to the streets of Washington and more than 600 cities worldwide. Their demonstration was a response to the rise of anti-scientific notions \u2014 the anti-vaccination movement and climate-change denial in particular \u2014 and a retort to the Trump administration, which has <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/national/health-science/trumps-budget-would-slash-scientific-and-medical-research/2017/03/15/d3261f98-0998-11e7-a15f-a58d4a988474_story.html?utm_term=.08d209424551\" shape=\"rect\">proposed deep budget cuts</a> for the Environmental Protection Agency and the National Institutes of Health.</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/national/health-science/big-turnout-expected-for-march-for-science-in-dc/2017/04/21/67cf7f90-237f-11e7-bb9d-8cd6118e1409_story.html?utm_term=.a170ade18fec\" shape=\"rect\">Why people are marching for science: \u2018There is no Planet B\u2019</a>]</i> </p> <p>In the weeks before the march, many called this kind of mass protest from the scientific community <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/energy-environment/wp/2017/04/22/historians-say-the-march-for-science-is-pretty-unprecedented/?utm_term=.9fa13072e289\" shape=\"rect\">unprecedented</a>. But Nye was not surprised.</p> <p>The current \u201canti-science thing,\u201d he said, had been on the rise for decades. \u201cPeople were denying pollution in 1970, saying it\u2019s a-okay.\u201d</p> <p>He took note of the early warning signs as a young man in Seattle, where he got his start in broadcasting with a local sketch-comedy show. He also was volunteering on weekends at the Pacific Science Center and with the Big Brothers/Big Sisters program.</p> <p>\u201cI realized that kids are the future,\u201d he said. \u201cThe reason I made the \u2018Science Guy\u2019 show was quite deliberate. If we can get young people excited about science, then we have a shot. I knew I was fighting the fight.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/video/local/bill-nye-on-the-march-for-science-theres-been-a-movement-to-set-science-aside/2017/04/21/5e5809e0-26b9-11e7-928e-3624539060e8_video.html\" shape=\"rect\">Bill Nye on the March for Science: \u2018There\u2019s been a movement to set science aside.\u2019</a>]</i> </p> <p>The fight is political, but not <i>partisan</i>, he emphasizes. Still, he has drawn his share of partisan critics. Some, like Sarah Palin, have questioned whether Nye is actually qualified to speak on behalf of science: \u201cBill Nye is as much a scientist as I am,\u201d she once declared.</p> <p>Nye chuckles in response: \u201cWell, Ms. Palin, you\u2019re wrong.\u201d</p> <p>To be fair: He did make his name as an entertainer. \u201cI\u2019m not a <i>research</i> scientist,\u201d he acknowledged, like his good friend and fellow science celebrity Neil deGrasse Tyson, who climbed the academic ranks as an astrophysicist. Nye earned his bachelor\u2019s in mechanical engineering at Cornell then went to work for Boeing. His fancier science credentials \u2014 designing an interplanetary sundial used by NASA and becoming chief executive of <a href=\"http://planetary.org/\" shape=\"rect\">the Planetary Society</a>, a space-advocacy group co-founded by Carl Sagan \u2014 came after his TV fame.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-photo inline-photo-double\"> <div class=\"left\" style=\"width:49%\"> <a name=\"\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_742w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/23/Style/Images/BILLNYE012.JPG?uuid=6p0tJieWEeeSjjYkU5Bg6A\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_240w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/23/Style/Images/BILLNYE012.JPG?uuid=6p0tJieWEeeSjjYkU5Bg6A\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/23/Style/Images/BILLNYE012.JPG?uuid=6p0tJieWEeeSjjYkU5Bg6A\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_240w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/23/Style/Images/BILLNYE012.JPG?uuid=6p0tJieWEeeSjjYkU5Bg6A\"/><br/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Nye takes a selfie with a fan backstage before addressing the rally. (Bill O'Leary/The Washington Post)</span> </div> <div class=\"right\" style=\"width:49%\"> <a name=\"\"></a> <img class=\"unprocessed\" data-hi-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_742w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/23/Interactivity/Images/crop_freeBILLNYE002.JPG?uuid=5Kmk8ChDEeeQgfVAX1bT5A\" data-low-res-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_240w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/23/Interactivity/Images/crop_freeBILLNYE002.JPG?uuid=5Kmk8ChDEeeQgfVAX1bT5A\" data-raw-src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rw/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/23/Interactivity/Images/crop_freeBILLNYE002.JPG?uuid=5Kmk8ChDEeeQgfVAX1bT5A\" src=\"https://img.washingtonpost.com/rf/image_240w/2010-2019/WashingtonPost/2017/04/23/Interactivity/Images/crop_freeBILLNYE002.JPG?uuid=5Kmk8ChDEeeQgfVAX1bT5A\"/><br/> <span class=\"pb-caption\">Nye, the self-styled \u201cScience Guy,\u201d speaks at the March for Science. (Bill O'Leary/The Washington Post)</span> </div> <div class=\"clear\"></div> </div> <p>Still, his education was grounded in the scientific method. And, he argues, it doesn\u2019t take a paleontologist to defend evolution or a meteorologist to comprehend the perils of climate change.</p> <p>\u201cWhen you become scientifically literate, I claim, you become an environmentalist,\u201d he said. \u201cSomewhere along the way, there has developed this idea that if you believe something hard enough, it\u2019s as true as things discovered through the process of science. And I will say that\u2019s objectively wrong.\u201d</p> <p>And so, at a moment when science is at the forefront of public discussion, Nye has found himself in the spotlight once again \u2014 though in truth, he has never quite left it. Since \u201cScience Guy\u201d ended its five-year run in 1998, Nye has hosted other science-education programs, sold out lecture halls and written <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/national/health-science/bill-nye-the-science-guy-why-evolution-is-right-and-creationism-is-wrong/2014/11/10/94046908-647e-11e4-bb14-4cfea1e742d5_story.html?utm_term=.398720de6ef9\" shape=\"rect\">best-selling books</a>. He also has racked up pop-culture cred, with appearances on \u201cDancing with the Stars,\u201d \u201cThe Big Bang Theory\u201d and \u201cInside Amy Schumer.\u201d He debuted a new 13-episode Netflix series Friday, \u201cBill Nye Saves the World,\u201d with a more urgent tone, and aimed at a broader audience, than his public TV days.</p> <p>His fans include former president Barack Obama, who invited Nye to visit the Everglades with him on Earth Day 2015, and more than 4 million Twitter followers. But none are so devout as the 20- and 30-somethings who mob him everywhere he goes. (He happily obliges the selfie-seekers, always reminding them: \u201cLook at the lens, not at yourself!\u201d)</p> <p>Nye isn\u2019t married, and he never had children of his own \u2014 a neurological illness runs in his family, which \u201creally affected my life choices,\u201d he said. So he focused on other people\u2019s children instead, determined to pass his own passion for science on to them. The little ones who watched his show became the young adults who would march beside him.</p> <p>\u201cThe scope of it is really astonishing,\u201d he said, with more awe than swagger. \u201cThere are millions of kids who watched the show. I\u2019m very proud of that.\u201d He smiled slightly. \u201cBut I don\u2019t think I quite <i>get </i>it.\u201d</p> <p> </p> <p></p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-gallery-embedded\"> <link href=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/pb/gr/c/ssiSingle/rw315e1JqY9Wfq/css/b0fa517a2c.css?_=0c4a1\" rel=\"stylesheet\"/> <link href=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/pb/gr/p/ssiSingle/rw315e1JqY9Wfq/style.css?_=36b0c\" rel=\"stylesheet\"/> <div class=\"pb-container\"> <div class=\"pb-feature-ssi-single pb-f-theme-normal pb-3 pb-feature pb-layout-item pb-f-gallery-gallery\" data-pb-feature-config=\"gallery/gallery\" id=\"f07JIBhJqY9Wfq\"> <div class=\"wp-volt-gal unprocessed wp-volt-gal-p-end-circular wp-volt-gal-promo-stub wp-volt-gal-on-promo-slide wp-volt-gal-embed-promo wp-volt-gal-embed-promo-hide\" data-blurb=\"Thousands of people gathered on the grounds of the Washington Monument for a rally that culminated in a march to the foot of Capitol Hill.
