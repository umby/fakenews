the federal judiciary Tuesday as the Senate hearings on his Supreme Court nomination turned on the search for his judicial philosophy and what one senator called \u201cthe elephant in the room\u201d \u2014 President Trump.</p> <p>From the first question from a friendly Republican to a grilling by a Democrat hours later, Gorsuch was called upon on the second day of what is expected to be four days of hearings to assert his impartiality and reassure senators that he would not be swayed by political pressure if he wins confirmation, which appeared even more likely after his marathon session.</p> <p>Gorsuch reiterated in public what he had told many senators in private \u2014 that he is offended by attacks like the ones leveled by President Trump against federal judges who have ruled in the past year in cases involving him.\u00a0\u00a0</p> <p>\u201cWhen anyone criticizes the honesty or the integrity or the motives of a federal judge, I find that disheartening. I find that demoralizing \u2014 because I know the truth,\u201d Gorsuch told Sen. Richard Blumenthal (D-Conn.).</p> <p>\u201cAnyone including the president of the United States?\u201d Blumenthal asked, who had made the elephant-in-the-room comment.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"wirereuters\" data-duration=\"239772\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Gorsuch: Attacks from 'anyone' on a judge's motivation are 'disheartening'\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58d1aa54e4b059a9e4281c33\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/03-21-2017/t_1490135747107_name_gorsuch33.jpg\" data-uuid=\"78ea55b0-0e86-11e7-aa57-2ca1b05c41b8\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"E2_4KoK70k0\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\"> (Video: Reuters)</span> </div> </div> <p>\u00a0\u201cAnyone is anyone,\u201d Gorsuch said.\u00a0</p> <p>Gorsuch declined, however, to comment specifically on Trump\u2019s various criticisms of federal judges, including an Indiana-born judge of Mexican descent who handled a federal lawsuit involving an online university bearing Trump\u2019s name and the \u201cso-called\u201d judge who ruled against the president\u2019s first attempt to ban travelers from Muslim-dominant countries from entering the United States.\u00a0</p> <p>\u201cI\u2019ve gone as far as I can go ethically,\u201d Gorsuch told Blumenthal.</p> <p>It was a dramatic moment in a day that for the most part lacked color. Gorsuch refused to be pinned down on most of the issues that Democrats raised: his allegiance to <i>Roe v. Wade</i>, his views on money in politics, the reach of the Second Amendment.</p> <p>He portrayed what Democrats saw as controversial rulings in his 10 years on the U.S. Court of Appeals for the 10th Circuit in Denver as authentic attempts to interpret the laws that Congress writes.</p> <p>\u201cIf we got it wrong, I\u2019m very sorry, but we did our level best,\u201d he said about a decision criticized by Sen. Richard J. Durbin (D-Ill.), but added: \u201cIt was affirmed by the Supreme Court.\u201d</p> <p>Sen. Al Franken (D-Minn.) questioned Gorsuch\u2019s ruling in what has become a celebrated case of a trucker who was fired after unhitching his trailer in subzero weather and driving away in search of warmth and safety. Gorsuch was the lone dissenter in saying a federal law did not protect the driver, but Franken said the judge could have ruled that a strict interpretation of the law would lead to an absurd Franken said.</p> <p>Republican senators did little more than set up Gorsuch, 49, to display an encyclopedic knowledge of the Constitution and Supreme Court precedent, and to allow him to stress his roots as an outdoorsy Westerner.</p> <p>\u201cWhat\u2019s the largest trout you\u2019ve ever caught?\u201d asked Sen. Jeff Flake (R-Ariz.).</p> <p>Gorsuch will be at the witness table again Wednesday as well as the fourth and final day of hearings scheduled for Thursday.</p> <p> Gorsuch seemed happy at the outset of the hearing to take what even he called the \u201csoftball\u201d question offered by Senate Judiciary Committee Chairman Charles E. Grassley (R-Iowa) about whether he would have any trouble ruling against Trump, the man who nominated him.</p> <p>\u201cI have no difficulty ruling against or for any party other than based on what the law and the facts of a particular case require,\u201d Gorsuch told the panel. \u201cAnd I\u2019m heartened by the support I have received from people who recognize that there\u2019s no such thing as a Republican judge or a Democratic judge \u2014 we just have judges in this country.</p> <p>\u201cMy personal views .\u2009.\u2009. I leave those at home,\u201d he added later.</p> <p>The Columbia-Oxford-Harvard graduate employed a homespun tone \u2014 \u201cgosh,\u201d \u201cgolly\u201d and \u201cnope\u201d punctuated his answers. Corny dad jokes fell flat, especially with the Democratic senators.</p> <p>They pressed him on abortion, gun rights, privacy and the protracted 2000 presidential campaign recount. As other Supreme Court nominees have, Gorsuch explained that it would be improper to give his views on cases that might come before him or to grade decisions made in the past.</p> <p>He had a tense encounter with Sen. Sheldon Whitehouse (D-R.I.), who sparred with him on issues of campaign finance and \u201cdark money,\u201d including a $10\u00a0million campaign by the group Judicial Crisis Network to advocate for Gorsuch\u2019s confirmation.</p> <p>Whitehouse said the group\u2019s donors do not have to be disclosed, and he wondered what they saw in Gorsuch that would warrant such an expenditure.</p> <p>\u201cYou\u2019d have to ask them,\u201d Gorsuch said.</p> <p>\u201cI can\u2019t because I don\u2019t know who they are,\u201d Whitehouse shot back.</p> <p>Democrats questioned him about his work at former president George W. Bush\u2019s Justice Department and whether he\u2019d rule against Trump\u2019s travel ban.</p> <p>Gorsuch declined to express his views on Trump\u2019s move to ban travelers from several Muslim-majority countries because \u201cthat\u2019s an issue that is currently being litigated actively.\u201d<br/> </p> <p>When Sen. Patrick J. Leahy (D-Vt.) mentioned that a Republican lawmaker recently suggested that Gorsuch would uphold Trump\u2019s ban if it came before the court, Gorsuch snapped: \u201cSenator, he has no idea how I\u2019d rule in that case.\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <i>[<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/gorsuch-promises-independence-from-trump--and-steals-democrats-line-of-attack/2017/03/21/d71dad30-0e45-11e7-9d5a-a83e627dc120_story.html?utm_term=.d4295c4c6156\" shape=\"rect\">Gorsuch promises independence from Trump \u2014 and steals Democrats\u2019 line of attack</a>]</i> </p> <p>Other senators quizzed Gorsuch about several of Trump\u2019s past statements. During the presidential campaign last year, Trump said that he would nominate people to the Supreme Court who would overrule <i>Roe v. Wade</i> and return decisions on abortion to the states.</p> <p>Sen. Lindsey O. Graham (R-S.C.) asked Gorsuch whether Trump had asked him to do that during his interview before his nomination.\u00a0</p> <p>\u201cSenator, I would have walked out the door,\u201d Gorsuch replied.\u00a0In questioning later, Gorsuch said Trump did mention abortion being a \u201cdivisive\u201d issue but then moved to other topics.</p> <p>It was at least the second time senators had pressed Gorsuch on what Trump had said he was looking for in a Supreme Court justice. Gorsuch said he does not believe in litmus tests and was never questioned about them.\u00a0</p> <p>Each senator was allotted up to 30 minutes to question Gorsuch during the first round of questions. A second round, scheduled to begin Wednesday morning, gives senators an additional 20 minutes to quiz the nominee.\u00a0</p> <p>The committee\u2019s top Democrat, Sen. Dianne Feinstein (Calif.), asked about Gorsuch\u2019s work on issues involving enhanced interrogation of suspected terrorist detainees while he served in Bush\u2019s Justice Department.</p> <p>Even though the issue has been in the news during the past week, Gorsuch said he did not remember a document released last week in which he was preparing talking points for the then-attorney general. \u201cYes,\u201d is handwritten next to a typed question: \u201cHave the aggressive interrogation techniques employed by the Admin yielded any valuable intelligence?\u201d</p> <p channel=\"wp.com\" class=\"interstitial-link\"> <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/national/senate-democrats-focus-on-gorsuchs-defense-of-bush-era-terrorism-policies/2017/03/15/3fced3de-0998-11e7-93dc-00f9bdd74ed1_story.html?utm_term=.9158746d2b44\" shape=\"rect\"> <i>[Senate Democrats want more information about Gorsuch\u2019s role at DOJ]</i> </a> </p> <p>Feinstein said she would supply Gorsuch with the documents for future questioning. In general, Gorsuch portrayed himself as a facilitator rather than a policymaker during his 14\u00a0months at the Justice Department in 2005 and 2006.</p> <p>\u201cI was a lawyer for a client,\u201d he said.</p> <p channel=\"!Daily/EZ_DAILY-01\">Feinstein asked about Gorsuch\u2019s role in designing a signing statement for Bush on a detainee treatment law; she characterized it as indicating that the president did not feel bound by the law he had just signed.</p> <p channel=\"!Daily/EZ_DAILY-01\">\u201cI certainly never would have counseled anyone not to obey the law,\u201d Gorsuch responded.</p> <p>Gorsuch <span>also </span>forcefully rejected claims by one of his former law school students that he had suggested that women take advantage of maternity leave policies by not telling the truth in job interviews about their plans to have families. <span>Democrats had seized on the accusations when they surfaced Sunday and vowed to ask Gorsuch about them.</span> </p> <p>When Durbin asked about the topic, Gorsuch explained that he has taught ethics classes at the University of Colorado Law School for several years. Based on his years of teaching young law students,\u00a0he said that employers in the corporate world, particularly law firms, continue to treat women poorly and often ask inappropriate questions in job interviews that are used to weed out female applicants who plan to have children.</p> <p>Republicans intend to move quickly on confirming Gorsuch. Those on the Judiciary Committee hope to refer him to the full Senate on April 3 so that he can be confirmed before Easter.</p> <p>But\u00a0Senate Minority Leader Charles E. Schumer (D-N.Y.) warned Republicans on Tuesday that his party would attempt to slow down consideration of Gorsuch because Republicans last year blocked then-President Barack Obama\u2019s attempts to fill the vacancy created by Justice Antonin Scalia\u2019s death, and because Trump\u2019s presidential campaign is the subject of an ongoing FBI investigation.</p> <p channel=\"!Daily\">Schumer said it seemed \u201cunseemly to be moving forward so fast on confirming a Supreme Court justice with a lifetime appointment\u201d due to the looming FBI investigation, which could potentially last for months or years.</p> <p channel=\"!Daily\">\u201cYou can bet that if the shoe was on the other foot \u2014 and a Democratic president was under investigation by the FBI \u2014 that Republicans would be howling at the moon about filling a Supreme Court seat in such circumstances,\u201d Schumer added.\u00a0</p> <p class=\"trailer\"> <p>Sean Sullivan contributed to this report.</p> </p> <p Republican to a grilling by a Democrat hours later, Gorsuch was called upon on the second day of what is expected to be four days of hearings to assert his impartiality and reassure senators that he would not be swayed by political pressure if he wins confirmation, which appeared even more likely after his marathon session.\n\nGorsuch reiterated in public what he had told many senators in private \u2014 that he is offended by attacks like the ones leveled by President Trump against federal judges who have ruled in the past year in cases involving him.\n\n\u201cWhen anyone criticizes the honesty or the integrity or the motives of a federal judge, I find that disheartening. I find that demoralizing \u2014 because I know the truth,\u201d Gorsuch told Sen. Richard Blumenthal (D-Conn.).\n\n\u201cAnyone including the president of the United States?\u201d Blumenthal asked, who had made the elephant-in-the-room comment.\n\n(Video: Reuters)\n\n\u201cAnyone is anyone,\u201d Gorsuch said.\n\nGorsuch declined, however, to comment specifically on Trump\u2019s various criticisms of federal judges, including an Indiana-born judge of Mexican descent who handled a federal lawsuit involving an online university bearing Trump\u2019s name and the \u201cso-called\u201d judge who ruled against the president\u2019s first attempt to ban travelers from Muslim-dominant countries from entering the United States.\n\n\u201cI\u2019ve gone as far as I can go ethically,\u201d Gorsuch told Blumenthal.\n\nIt was a dramatic moment in a day that for the most part lacked color. Gorsuch refused to be pinned down on most of the issues that Democrats raised: his allegiance to Roe v. Wade, his views on money in politics, the reach of the Second Amendment.\n\nHe portrayed what Democrats saw as controversial rulings in his 10 years on the U.S. Court of Appeals for the 10th Circuit in Denver as authentic attempts to interpret the laws that Congress writes.\n\n\u201cIf we got it wrong, I\u2019m very sorry, but we did our level best,\u201d he said about a decision criticized by Sen. Al Franken (D-Minn.) questioned Gorsuch\u2019s ruling in what has become a celebrated case of a trucker who was fired after unhitching his trailer in subzero weather and driving away in search of warmth and safety. Gorsuch said he does not believe in litmus tests and was never questioned about them.\n\nEach senator was allotted up to 30 minutes to question Gorsuch during the first round of questions. A second round, scheduled to begin Wednesday morning, gives senators an additional 20 minutes to quiz the nominee.\n\nThe committee\u2019s top Democrat, Sen.
