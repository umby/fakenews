itemprop=\"articleBody\"> <p> <span class=\"dateline\">PALM BEACH, Fla. \u2014</span> President Trump on Saturday praised the U.S. military for carrying out the missile attack on a Syrian airfield and struck back at mounting questions over whether it would help achieve a momentum shift in Syria\u2019s bloody civil war.</p> <p>In <a href=\"https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/850785347038576640\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"twitter.com\">an afternoon tweet</a>, Trump defended the operation against criticism from some members of Congress and military analysts that the nighttime volley of 59 Tomahawk cruise missiles two days earlier did not target the runways at the Shayrat air base in eastern Syria.</p> <p>Administration officials have said the attack successfully destroyed refueling stations, hangars and some planes, effectively making the base inoperable.</p> <p>\u201cThe reason you don\u2019t generally hit runways is that they are easy and inexpensive to quickly fix (fill in and top)!\u201d Trump wrote on Twitter from Mar-a-Lago after playing a round at the nearby Trump International Golf Club. The president is spending the weekend here after completing a two-day summit at his winter estate with Chinese President Xi Jinping. </p> <p>In an earlier message, Trump offered: \u201cCongratulations to our great military men and women for representing the United States, and the world, so well in the Syria attack.\u201d</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"segments\" data-duration=\"124266\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Four things to know about the U.S. missile strike against Syria\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58e7fa1fe4b002d8014a3507\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/04-07-2017/t_1491598860751_name_syria_strikes.jpg\" data-uuid=\"f8346a8c-1bd2-11e7-8598-9a99da559f9e\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"BuVO60qdcIk\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\"> (Amanda Erickson,Jason Aldag/The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p> The White House has sought to cast the mission \u2014 which came in response to evidence that Syrian President Bashar al-Assad\u2019s regime had carried out an attack on civilians with the nerve agent sarin \u2014 as <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/american-strikes-against-syria-prompt-both-praise-and-condemnation/2017/04/07/df58e194-1bb1-11e7-855e-4824bbb5d748_story.html?hpid=hp_hp-top-table-main_ussyria-820pm%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.c9e6f2eeccf0\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">a major success</a> in putting Assad on notice that he can no longer use such weapons without consequences. Officials announced Saturday that Trump had spoken with King Salman of Saudi Arabia, who offered support for his decision.</p> <p>But Saturday brought fresh reminders that a single U.S. attack would hardly dissuade Assad from his brutal campaign to crush a six-year rebellion that has claimed hundreds of thousands of lives. Residents in the northwestern town of Khan Sheikhoun, where at least 86 people had been killed in the sarin attack, reported that <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/warplanes-return-to-syrian-town-devastated-by-chemical-attack/2017/04/08/38a5d8cc-1bdc-11e7-8598-9a99da559f9e_story.html?hpid=hp_hp-top-table-main_syria-845am%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.0fd405f5338b\" shape=\"rect\" title=\"www.washingtonpost.com\">Syrian warplanes had returned and dropped new conventional bombs</a>. </p> <p>Since a U.S. Navy destroyer launched the missiles early Friday in Syria, the Trump administration has struggled to explain how the attack \u2014 which came four years after President Barack Obama chose not to strike Assad unilaterally after a similar use of chemical weapons \u2014 fits into its broader policy on Syria and the Middle East.</p> <p>Trump aides said that they could not unequivocally rule out future strikes against Assad\u2019s forces, but they cautioned that the president\u2019s decision did not signal a broader ramping up of U.S. military engagement on the ground. </p> <p>In a letter to Congress on Saturday, Trump said his aim was to \u201cdegrade the Syrian military\u2019s ability to conduct further chemical weapons attacks and to dissuade the Syrian regime from using or proliferating chemical weapons, thereby promoting the stability of the region and averting a worsening of the region\u2019s current humanitarian catastrophe.\u201d</p> <p>Senior administration officials have acknowledged that the targeted operation did not eliminate Assad\u2019s ability to carry out chemical attacks. And Trump, who has attempted to enact a ban on Syrians and those in five other majority-Muslim nations from entering the United States, has not indicated that he is willing to accept more Syrians who are fleeing violence. </p> <p>Meanwhile, the administration is nearing completion of a review of long-term strategy to combat the Islamic State in Syria and Iraq, which remains the priority over removing Assad from power. A fully developed proposal is expected to be delivered to Trump\u2019s desk in the near future, a senior administration official said.</p><div class=\"inline-content inline-gallery-embedded\"> <link aides said that Trump, who had campaigned generally on a noninterventionist platform, was moved to act after aides on Tuesday delivered a detailed assessment of the chemical attack and <a Obama aides said at the time that the president wanted broad political and public support before acting after years of U.S. military conflicts in the Middle East and Central Asia. But lawmakers voted against the authorization. </p> <p>On Capitol Hill, reaction to Trump\u2019s action has been mixed, with Republican leaders endorsing the president\u2019s belief he did not need congressional approval to act. </p> <p>But some rank-and-file GOP members, along with many Democrats, have criticized Trump for acting impulsively and betraying his own past opposition to U.S. intervention in Syria. </p> <p>Analysts questioned whether the Trump administration, in its rapid deliberations over less than three days, had fully considered how to deal with the unknown consequences of the missile attack. </p> <p>\u201cI do not see any grounds for optimism and worry that expectations will be disappointed,\u201d said Tamara Cofman Wittes, a former deputy assistant secretary for Near East affairs at the State Department under Obama. \u201cI worry that Assad could escalate.
