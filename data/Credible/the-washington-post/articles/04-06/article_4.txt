statement on April 6 after U.S. forces launched approximately 50 cruise missiles at a Syrian military airfield late Thursday. The Trump administration authorized the attack in retaliation after a chemical attack against civilians. (The Washington Post)</span> </div> </div> <p>To hear Sen. Marco Rubio (R-Fla.) and other top Senate Republicans review President Trump's two very different reactions to a\u00a0deadly chemical attack in Syria this week is to hear them talk about two completely\u00a0different presidents.</p> <p>The noninterventionist president\u00a0<a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2017/04/05/trumps-handling-of-syria-has-some-senate-republicans-very-concerned/\">they despised</a>. The first president <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/trump-weighing-military-options-following-chemical-weapons-attack-in-syria/2017/04/06/0c59603a-1ae8-11e7-9887-1a5314b56a08_story.html?hpid=hp_rhp-banner-main_syria-315pm%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&amp;utm_term=.e33c017f9877\">to launch direct military strikes</a> into Syria during its civil war \u2014 now this is a president they can get behind.</p> <p>\u201cTonight's actions show the days of being able to attack with impunity when it comes to Assad are over,\" Rubio said to\u00a0CNN's Anderson Cooper.</p> <p>A few days ago, Trump's administration seemed content to let Syrian President Bashar al-Assad stay in power. Days before the chemical attack, Secretary of State Rex Tillerson said Assad's fate is in the hands of his people. And on Wednesday, Rubio accused Tillerson of providing the \u201cincentive\u201d for Assad to allegedly launch a chemical weapon attack on his own people.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-video\"> <div class=\"posttv-video-embed wpv-player\" data-aspect-ratio=\"0.5625\" data-category-id=\"other\" data-duration=\"34112\" data-emoji=\"0\" data-headline=\"Video shows U.S. strikes in Syria\" data-is-truth-teller=\"0\" data-max-height=\"-1\" data-max-width=\"-1\" data-object-id=\"58e6fcc2e4b002d80149ce51\" data-show-caption=\"1\" data-show-endscreen=\"1\" data-show-posttv-overlay=\"1\" data-show-promo=\"http://s3.amazonaws.com/posttv-thumbnails-prod/04-07-2017/t_1491533193376_name_Screen_Shot_2017_04_06_at_10_46_17_PM.png\" data-uuid=\"f43a39d0-1b3b-11e7-8598-9a99da559f9e\" data-variants=\"0\" data-video-360=\"0\" data-youtube-id=\"Dm0CZYHC7N0\"> <script src=\"//js.washingtonpost.com/video/resources/env/WapoVideo/dist/WapoVideoEmbed.min.js\"></script> </div> <div class=\"inline-video-caption\"> <span class=\"pb-caption\">The Department of Defense released video of the U.S. military launching cruise missiles in Syria after President Trump ordered the strike on April 6. (Department of Defense)</span> </div> </div> <p>On Thursday night, the president <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/trump-weighing-military-options-following-chemical-weapons-attack-in-syria/2017/04/06/0c59603a-1ae8-11e7-9887-1a5314b56a08_story.html?utm_term=.92f0c8d4ed30\">launched 50 missiles</a> at Assad's government. \u201cIt is in this vital national security interest of the United States to prevent and deter the spread and use of deadly chemical weapons,\u201d Trump said in a somber statement from his Mar-a-Lago estate\u00a0in Florida.</p> <p>This wasn't just a notable shift for a president attracted to the idea of stepping America back from\u00a0global affairs. This would be a major moment\u00a0for any U.S. president. Thursday's missiles launched at a Syrian military airfield was the first direct American military assault on Syria's government in that country's six-year-long civil war.</p> <p>And senior Republicans who have been Trump antagonists on foreign policy loved it.</p> <p>\u201cUnlike the previous administration, President Trump confronted a pivotal moment in Syria and took action,\u201d Sens. John McCain (R-Ariz.) and Lindsey O. Graham (R-S.C.) said in a joint statement.</p> <p>Even top Senate Democrats weren't immediately critical. Here's a statement from Sen. Richard\u00a0J. Durbin (Ill.), the No. 2 Senate Democrat: \u201cMy preliminary briefing by the White House indicated this was a measured response to the Syrian nerve gas atrocity.\u201d</p> <p>Late Thursday night, the ultimate validation came via statement from Sen. Bob Corker (R-Tenn.), who chairs the Senate Foreign Relations Committee: \u201cI applaud President Trump for taking decisive action following the latest chemical weapons attack. It is critical that Assad knows he will no longer enjoy impunity for his horrific crimes against his own citizens, and this proportional step was appropriate.\u201d</p> <p>It's hard to overstate how totally, completely, utterly different these Republicans' reactions to Trump's Syria policy are from earlier this week.</p> <p>On CNN on Wednesday morning, McCain,\u00a0chairman of the Senate Armed Services Committee and a chief Trump antagonist, characterized Tillerson's comment that Assad's fate is in the hands of Syrians as \u201cone of the more incredible statements I've ever heard.\u201d</p> <p>Sen. Rob Portman (R-Ohio), also a member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee, issued a statement in response to the chemical attack it seems he wished Trump had delivered. In it, a not-so-subtle prod to Trump to change his ways: \u201cWe cannot afford to make the same mistake twice.\u201d</p> <p>It's possible that Senate Republicans' loud, direct criticism of Trump put him in a\u00a0tough place \u2014 or at the very least underscored the ocean between him and the core of his party on\u00a0foreign policy.</p> <p>As <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/trump-and-his-america-first-philosophy-face-first-moral-quandary-in-syria/2017/04/05/ec854e20-1a21-11e7-bcc2-7d1a0973e7b2_story.html\">The Washington Post's Greg Jaffe wrote</a>\u00a0of Trump's worldview up until Thursday night: \u201cThe Syrian chemical weapons attack poses a particular problem for Trump\u2019s foreign policy philosophy. The attack by Assad\u2019s forces offends America\u2019s values and it violates long-standing international norms of behavior, but it does not present an immediate threat to America\u2019s security or its economic interests. In an 'America First' world, it is an atrocity, but hardly a call to action for the United States and its allies.\u201d</p> <p>By Wednesday afternoon, McCain and Rubio had fired their barbs, and Trump said vaguely in a news conference that his attitude toward Syria has \u201cchanged.\u201d Until he launched military strikes, we weren't really sure what the president meant by that.</p> <p>Now, top Senate Republicans concerned about the death, destruction and instability that a Syrian president unanswerable to the United States has caused seem greatly relieved he's come to their side.</p> <p>Of course, Trump's sudden about-face on Syrian policy won't bring him closer with everyone in his party. Sen. Rand Paul (R-Ky.), whose foreign policy tends to line up with the libertarian, noninterventionist sect, reminded Trump of the fact that Congress, not the White House, has the power to authorize military force.</p> <div class=\"inline-content inline-html\"> <blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"500\"> <p dir=\"ltr\" lang=\"en\">The President needs Congressional authorization for military action as required by the Constitution.</p> <p>\u2014 Senator Rand Paul (@RandPaul) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/RandPaul/status/850161531094523906\">April 7, 2017</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async=\"\" charset=\"utf-8\" src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\"></script></p> </div> <p>And Sen. Tim Kaine (D-Va.), who had made a name for himself in Congress demanding that the Obama and Trump administrations come to it for approval of military force, called the military strike \"unlawful\": \"I voted for military action against Syria in 2013 when Donald Trump was advocating that America turn its back on Assad's atrocities. Congress will work with the President, but this failure to seek Congressional approval is unlawful.\"</p> <p>That's a problem Trump will have to face in the coming days \u2014 one that his predecessor <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2015/12/07/3-reasons-congress-wont-authorize-obamas-use-of-force-against-the-islamic-state/?utm_term=.367b37ffcef3\">never quite solved</a>.
