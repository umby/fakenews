#ACSSanFran</a></h1><p class=\"wpn-byline\"><time datetime=\"2017-04-03T09:35:59+01:00\" pubdate><span class=\"published\"><abbr class=\"value\" title=\"2017-04-03\">03 Apr 2017 </abbr>|<abbr class=\"value\" title=\"09:35:59+0100\"> 09:35 BST</abbr></span></time><span class=\"divider\"> | </span>Posted by <span class=\"author vcard\"><a class=\"url fn n\" href=\"/stepwise/author/bronwen-dekker\" title=\"Bronwen Dekker\">Bronwen Dekker</a></span><span class=\"divider\"> | </span><span class=\"category\"><span class=\"before\">Category: </span><a href=\"/stepwise/category/there-and-back-again\" rel=\"tag\">There and Back Again</a></span> </p><!--  before_entry  -->\r\n\t\t\t\t</header>\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t<section>\r\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"wpn-entry-content content\">\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<p>The \u201cNational Fresenius Award Symposium\u201d celebrates researchers that have made amazing early career advances; this year the award went to <a href=\"http://www-chem.ucsd.edu/faculty/profiles/devaraj_neal_k.html\">Neal K. Devaraj</a>, and this session was assembled in his honour.</p>\n<p>The session started with a talk by <a href=\"http://chenowethgroup.chem.upenn.edu/\">David Chenowerth</a> on making synthetic collagen. Collagen is a peptide triple helix made of peptide chains of the general form: [Gxx]3. His group has been doing experiments replacing carbon atoms with nitrogen \u2013 e.g. <a href=\"http://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/jacs.6b03823\">using aza-glycine instead of glycine in one (or more) of the repeating triplets Proline-Hydroxyproline-Glycine</a>\u00a0\u2013 which has remarkable effects on the stability of the resulting triple helices.</p>\n<p>After a fascinating talk from <a href=\"http://www.bioscience.utah.edu/faculty/biological-chemistry-faculty/poulter/poulter.php\">Dale Poulter</a> about exploiting the versatility of farnesyl transferase for the preparation of protein microarrays, there were three talks about lipid bilayers.</p>\n<p><a href=\"https://web.stanford.edu/group/boxer/sboxer.html\">Steven Boxer</a>\u00a0spoke about recent work on tethered lipid bilayers &#8211; using a really clever approach using lipid-DNA conjugates with\u00a0complementary DNA strands &#8211; which has been applied to looking at <a href=\"http://www.cell.com/biophysj/abstract/S0006-3495(16)30395-2\">membrane fusion of enveloped viruses</a>.</p>\n<p>For imaging organelles, you could label either the membrane proteins or the lipids. An advantage of labelling the lipids is that there are many more lipid molecules than proteins in the membrane, so if you choose a photostable fluorophore that blinks spontaneously it should be possible to image for longer time periods before you get photobleaching. <a href=\"http://schepartzlab.yale.edu/\">Alanna Schepartz </a>spoke about work using an environmentally sensitive (fluorescesces more at low pH) fluorophore (Sir-Tz) to image things like <a href=\"http://www.nature.com/articles/ncomms10778#content\">Golgi and ER using STED</a>.\u00a0 The Supplementary Movies are remarkable both for the resolution, and for the length of time of the imaging experiment.</p>\n<p>The last talk was by Neal Devaraj who spoke about his work on in situ synthesis membrane synthesis. So what does this mean? He creates <a href=\"https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3262119/figure/F1/\">clickable phospholipid precursors</a>, sets up the reaction and watches how new lipid vesicles form over time. To solve the problem of catalyst dilution over time, they developed a really neat system for continual synthesis of a catalytic membrane.</p>\n<p><img src=\"http://www.pnas.org/content/112/27/8187/F1.large.jpg\" alt=\"\" /></p>\n<p>Image taken from: 10.1073/pnas.1506704112, Hardy et al. (2015) PNAS 112\u00a0 pp 8187-8192</p>\n<p>They have also done some exciting work towards <a href=\"http://www.pnas.org/content/113/31/8589.long\">synthetic re-modelling of lipid bilayers using a reversible covalent coupling</a>. Remarkably it is possible to see the formation of microdomains!</p>\n<p><img src=\"http://www.pnas.org/content/113/31/8589/F1.large.jpg\" alt=\"\" /></p>\n<p>Image taken from: doi: 10.1073/pnas.1605541113, Brea et al.
