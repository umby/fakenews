ACT for America, a self-described \u201cnational security organization,\u201d and the Council on American-Islamic Relations, a Muslim civil rights and advocacy group, feuding over a chapter on Islam.</p><p>The ACT for America Space Coast chapter says the Pearson World History textbook paints Islam in an inaccurate, positive light. CAIR-Florida says the allegations are part of ACT\u2019s Islamaphobic agenda. The Southern Poverty Law Center recently designated ACT as a hate group, classifying it alongside organizations such as the Ku Klux Klan.</p><p>At this point, Superintendent Desmond Blackburn does not foresee revising the materials.</p><p>\u201cI do believe that we take ourselves down a slippery road, perhaps a slippery road backwards when we censor or manipulate the materials,\u201d he said.</p><p><a href=\"/story/news/2014/01/21/textbook-committee-to-offer-solutions-/4689677/\">2013 debate: Textbook committee offers solutions</a></p><p>The debate started when two Brevard residents signed up for public comment at a school board meeting on March 14. Doug Murphy and Lee Boyland said although the Pearson World History textbook is \u201cmuch improved\u201d from the last edition, there are still some areas that ignore the \u201ctrue history\u201d of Islam.</p><div class=\"partner-placement partner-spike\"></div><p>They pointed to passages about women\u2019s rights and Mohammed\u2019s life that they claim inaccurately portray Islam.</p><p>Murphy cited passages from the Quran that state a man can have four wives, but a woman can only have one husband, and that a man can divorce his wife by making a declaration, but a woman must have his consent, among other excerpts. The textbook, however, says \u201cIslam extended rights and protection to women by affirming the spiritual equality of all Muslims,\u201d he read at the meeting.</p><div class=\"partner-placement partner-outstream\"></div><p>\u201cDoes this sound like social justice?\u201d he asked the board. \u201cThis is political correctness. Not at the expense of the truth, please, our young adults deserve the truth.\u201d</p><p>Boyland, who sat on the committee to review this textbook in 2013, took issue with the positive portrayal of Mohammed.</p><p>More residents \u2014 as well as the local chapters of ACT and CAIR \u2014 came ready to speak at the next meeting.</p><p>Muslim students who attend Brevard high schools said the sentences in question had been taken out of context and that \u201can alteration in the history textbook would not be for historical purposes, but for alternate reasons.\u201d</p><p>One student, a freshman from Melbourne High, said Murphy\u2019s claims were \u201cpatently false.\u201d</p><p>\u201cTake me as an example,\u201d she said. \u201cI\u2019m an IB student, I\u2019m on two soccer teams, I play viola for my high school\u2019s orchestra, and guess what, I\u2019m a Muslim woman. Does that sound like I practice a religion that oppresses women or degrades them from the rest of society? Of course not, and for anyone to say that it does is simply out of ignorance.\u201d</p><p>ACT Space Coast chapter president Roger Gangitano argued that by omitting details about the \u201chorrific acts\u201d Mohammed committed, and only presenting positive aspects from his life, students are getting a flawed education.</p><p>\u201cIf we change history and we omit the facts that are insulting to some people, then we wouldn\u2019t have described Hitler the way that we did,\u201d he said. \u201cWe would have only have addressed the issue and his life prior to the Third Reich and that, of course, would be wrong.\u201d</p><p>Rasha Mubarak, Orlando Regional Coordinator of CAIR-Florida, said ACT\u2019s only mission in this textbook battle is to spread anti-Islamic rhetoric.</p><p>\u201cThey are not concerned with how well Brevard County\u2019s students perform nor about their futures,\u201d said Mubarak. \u201cThey are using our students for their one intention and that is to push their anti-Islamic agenda.\u201d</p><p>She called ACT a \u201chate group\u201d with zero credibility whose purpose is to discriminate. Mubarak\u2019s fiery speech elicited a loud \u201cboo\u201d from the board room audience.</p><p>Gangitano countered, \u201cI can\u2019t tell you how infuriating it is to have someone from an organization that was founded by terrorists call me a hate group.\u201d He has recently <a href=\"/story/news/2017/03/14/viera-group-disputes-anti-muslim-designation/98917218/\">disputed the Southern Poverty Law Center\u2019s classification</a>.</p><p>One speaker questioned the legitimacy of Islam completely and said it was \u201cpreposterous\u201d to call Mohammed a prophet, as he is identified in the textbook, because parts of the Quran conflict with the teachings of the Bible.</p><p>\u201cIslam is not a religion, so if you discriminate against quote-unquote Islam, you\u2019re not discriminating against a religion, you\u2019re discriminating against a secular group who claims to be a religion,\u201d said Mike Bizzaro.</p><p>The Brevard School Board was caught in the middle of an identical debate in 2013. It drew the attention of national media, including <a href=\"http://radio.foxnews.com/toddstarnes/top-stories/does-high-school-textbook-have-islamic-bias.html\">FOX News</a> and the <a href=\"http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/07/30/ritch-workman-islam-textbook_n_3678927.html\">Huffington Post</a>, and prompted the board to task a committee with putting together a supplement to the chapters on Islam.</p><p>Board member Andy Ziegler, who sat on the board in 2013, was happy to hear the book had improved since the 2007 edition. When he spoke to FLORIDA TODAY, he wasn\u2019t sure whether ACT\u2019s problems with the text needed to be addressed.</p><p>\u201cI\u2019ve got to imagine that every history book has some omissions in them or the books would be so thick you couldn\u2019t carry them around,\u201d he said. \u201cWe could probably find problems with every textbook, so how big a magnifying glass do we use to try to find problems and fix them?\u201d</p><p>Board members assured speakers concerned about the textbook that it is simply one resource available to students.</p><p>\u201cI will tell you that a teacher who teaches out of a book in our county is not a very good teacher. They use it as a resource,\u201d said Matt Susin, who taught World History before being elected to the board in November.</p><p>Because the board voted to move forward with the book as is, parents now have until April 29 to file a petition.
