Wochit\n\nFrank Lagano (Photo: NorthJersey.com)\n\nTen years ago, a reputed Lucchese family soldier from Tenafly was gunned down as he stepped out of his BMW at the Seville Diner in East Brunswick.\n\nThe still-unsolved murder is the flashpoint in a bizarre and complex tale that involves a high profile but troubled mob bust, multiple lawsuits and allegations of ties between the mobster and one of Bergen County\u2019s most renowned law enforcement officers.\n\nIt\u2019s a story that continues to this day.\n\nCourt records now show that in the weeks following the fatal shooting of Frank Lagano, a state investigator penned a lengthy memo describing a business and personal relationship between the alleged mobster and Michael Mordaga, the onetime chief of detectives in the Bergen County Prosecutor\u2019s Office.\n\nAnd it wasn\u2019t the first time.\n\nTHE PLAYERS:Cast of characters in Lagano murder saga\n\nTIMELINE:Operation Jersey Boyz gambling case\n\nPARAMUS:$1.3 million house was dream for former chief of detectives\n\nAs far back as February 2005 \u2013 long before Lagano was killed \u2013 the same investigator wrote to a higher-up in the Attorney General\u2019s office about a possible connection between Lagano and Mordaga.\n\nThe two memos written by now-deceased state investigator James Sweeney are part of the growing cache of court filings in an ongoing federal lawsuit brought by the dead man\u2019s estate, with a shocking allegation: Mordaga and others in the Bergen County Prosecutor\u2019s Office caused Lagano\u2019s execution-style killing by intentionally blowing his cover as a state informant.\n\nMichael Mordaga (Photo: Viorel Florescu/NorthJersey.com)\n\nThese records give new insight into the long-running case, providing support for Sweeney\u2019s claims that he reported allegations of police corruption in Bergen County to his bosses. And they underscore lingering questions about the response to those claims by the Attorney General\u2019s office.\n\nBut Sweeney, like so much else in this saga, is not without controversy.\n\nFormer Bergen County Prosecutor John Molinelli has called him a \u201crogue law enforcement officer\u201d whose claims have no merit. Indeed, Sweeney\u2019s memos to the state followed revelations that he was picked up on a wiretap talking to a source in a case involving Bergen County.\n\nMordaga, meanwhile, has long denied any relationship with Lagano and, when the estate\u2019s suit was filed in 2012, its allegations were strongly denied by both the former chief and former prosecutor.\n\nEven so, reporting by The Record and NorthJersey.com has shown links between the former chief and the mobster, including multiple phone calls \u2013 including three to his home and cell on Christmas Eve of 2003.\n\nAn accountant who did work for the men has also described them as friends and said he once dined with them.\n\nMoreover, the estate has claimed that, as part of a business arrangement involving Lagano, the former chief made referrals to a personal injury attorney for \u201ckickbacks.\u201d The Record has found that Mordaga had a role in bringing that same attorney the case of a man injured in a Teterboro jet crash. One of the Sweeney memos describes an arrangement with an attorney as well as a Teterboro lawsuit.\n\nNow, a decade after Lagano\u2019s slaying, the lawsuit brought by his heirs inches forward with no resolution in sight.\n\nThe state has tried to get the case thrown out and to have documents that suggest Mordaga and Lagano had a business arrangement with the attorney placed under judicial seal.\n\nThe Attorney General\u2019s office has taken its own lawyers off the case and hired private law firms \u2013 at taxpayer expense \u2013 to defend both Mordaga and the prosecutor\u2019s office.\n\nALLEGED MISCONDUCT:Suspended Hackensack cop\u2019s lawyers seek review of Mordaga\n\nMICHAEL MORDAGA:Top Bergen County detective moonlighted for Hackensack University Medical Center\n\nThe Decotiis, Fitzpatrick & Cole law firm of Teaneck has been tapped to represent the former chief and the firm of Schenck, Price, Smith & King has signed on as counsel for the prosecutor\u2019s office. The hourly rate for partners from either firm on the case is $200, a state spokesman said.\n\nThe Decotiis firm declined comment, saying it \u201cdoes not publicly discuss its clients or the details of matters that are in litigation.\u201d The attorney for the prosecutor\u2019s office did not return calls. Neither did Mordaga or Molinelli.\n\nThe Attorney General\u2019s office, meanwhile, won\u2019t say if it has ever investigated allegations that Mordaga had a relationship with Lagano.\n\nThe suit has more recently been marked by legal maneuvering in and out of federal court over records, confidentiality, depositions and more.\n\nA battle is also shaping up between in-state Appellate Court, where the Attorney General is trying to block an order to unseal records from \u201cOperation Jersey Boyz,\u201d a high-profile but troubled 2004 organized-crime gambling bust that\u2019s at the heart of the Lagano family\u2019s claims of police corruption. Lagano and dozens of other alleged mobsters were arrested in the bust, led by the Bergen County Prosecutor\u2019s Office and state police.\n\nMolinelli has said that the true motive behind the estate\u2019s litigation is the return of more than $264,000 seized in that bust by the Prosecutor\u2019s Office, the subject of a separate legal action.\n\nAmid all the legal wrangling, Mordaga put his $1.3 million Paramus home and another property in an irrevocable living trust last year. The luxury home was raised twice in Sweeney\u2019s communications to his bosses.\n\nBuried within the federal court file, concerns raised by Sweeney more than a decade ago have resurfaced and come into sharp relief. The investigator died in 2011, before a lawsuit that he filed the previous year \u2013 one that claimed he was fired for pursuing questions surrounding Lagano\u2019s arrest and murder \u2013 could be resolved. Sweeney\u2019s allegations of corruption and a cover-up form the backbone of the estate\u2019s arguments.\n\n\u201cA review of this report\u2019s contents provides a possible motive for Langano\u2019s [sic] death, if allegations are pursued and proven,\u201d Sweeney wrote at the end of his 2007 memo.\n\nJames Sweeney\n\nSweeney\u2019s place in the matter is complicated. A former Marine, he spent much of his more than four decades in law enforcement as an organized-crime investigator for various state agencies.\n\nJames Sweeney (Photo: submitted to The Record)\n\nHe was overheard on a wiretap in the Jersey Boyz case talking to one of his sources \u2013 who turned out to be a target of the probe - telling him that another agency had been asking about him.\n\nNo one alerted Sweeney\u2019s agency \u2013 the state Division of Criminal Justice \u2013 or more importantly, the judge overseeing the wiretaps.\n\nIt is unclear what Sweeney was talking to his confidential informant about from available documents. But records show the judge was disturbed not by the substance of the conversation, but because the prosecutor's office continued to seek wiretaps without telling her an informant with another agency was a suspect in the case.\n\nIt was a serious misstep that led the Jersey Boyz case to unravel. Evidence was thrown out, cases dismissed and plea bargains struck. In the end, no one was sentenced to jail.\n\nIn the turmoil that followed, Sweeney claimed his source introduced him to Lagano \u2013 and at least some of what Lagano told Sweeney was put in writing by the investigator, whose records were subpoenaed by the estate from his former attorney.\n\nThe two memos are significantly redacted and Mordaga and Lagano\u2019s names are consistently misspelled. But the paperwork provides a window into the information flowing behind the scenes.\n\nIn the earlier memo, dated February 2005, a connection between Mordaga and Lagano is raised toward the end of a five-page report focused on the fallout in the Jersey Boyz case and addressed to the deputy chief of the organized crime bureau.\n\nIt says simply that Mordaga and Lagano are friends.\n\n\u201cAccording to source both visit each others homes, have dinner together, and socialize on a regular basis,\u201d he wrote.\n\nOther references to Mordaga in the same memo include \u201callegations obtained\u201d that he\u2019s building a \u201cmulti-million dollar home in Paramus. Contributions allegedly being made by prominent Bergen County builders.\u201d\n\nA view of the property in Paramus built by Michael Mordaga, former chief of detectives in the Bergen County Prosecutor's Office. (Photo: Staff)\n\nIn May 2006 court records show Sweeney wrote an email to the head of the Division of Criminal Justice \u2013 subject line \u201ccorruption\u201d \u2013 and asked to make him aware of \u201cpresent situations confronting the Organized Crime Bureau.\u201d\n\nA handwritten notation says \u201cnever answered.\u201d\n\nLagano's execution-style murder\n\nOn April 12, 2007 Lagano was shot in the head outside the Seville Diner, which he co-owned.\n\nAn email Sweeney sent to higher-ups five days later indicates there was talk of the killing in the Attorney General\u2019s office. The email, with Lagano in the subject line, begins by referencing \u201cyesterdays discussion pertaining to this case.\u201d It details Lagano\u2019s financial interest in the diner and notes Sweeney\u2019s attempt to gain access to \u201ca list of monies\u201d owed the dead mobster.\n\nEast Brunswick police block off the Seville Diner along Rt 18 South in East Brunswick where a man was found dead outside of his car in the parking lot. (Photo: File Photo/Gannett)\n\n\u201cAdditional data deemed sensative [sic] also obtained concerning several Bergen County Attorney\u2019s (sic) who were associated with Langano [sic] and Mike M,\u201d it concludes.\n\nA handwritten note on that email reads \u201cinstructed to end investigation and deny he was source. No help to Middlesex County.\u201d\n\nThat May, Sweeney wrote a lengthymemorandum to the division\u2019s chief investigator labelled \u201cCONFIDENTIAL.\u201d The subject line again was Lagano\u2019s homicide and included a case number.\n\nIt said Lagano was \u201cinfuriated\u201d to discover $25,000 in cash and other items went missing when his home and safe deposit box were searched during his arrest in the Jersey Boyz case. Lagano, it says, was brought to Mordaga\u2019s office after being processed and the chief gave him the business card of an attorney who would make \u201c90% of his problems go away.\u201d\n\nAccording to Lagano, Sweeney wrote, his relationship with Mordaga \u201cbecame very strained after his arrest.\u201d\n\n\u201cHis friendship in the past included the lending of money, social visits to each others homes, vacationing in Florida\u201d Sweeney wrote. Again, the chief\u2019s house is raised, this time asserting that Lagano said he provided him \u201cwith the construction plans of his family room in order for Mordagna [sic] to duplicate it in his new Paramus home.\u201d\n\n\"Business ventures\"\n\nThe 2007 memo also alleges the two men entered into \u201cbusiness ventures.\u201d It goes on to describe an arrangement between Mordaga, Lagano, and an attorney whose name is redacted. Sweeney writes that the attorney borrowed money from Lagano for \u201cthe purpose of paying for trial preparation expenses.\u201d\n\nLagano, according to the memo, doubled his money when a case settled with \u201ca percentage of that figure\u201d going to Mordaga.\n\nWhen one client got an $11 million award, for example, Lagano, Mordaga and a private investigator were each to get a percentage of the attorney\u2019s earnings, according to the memo.\n\nThe memo says the attorney also asked Lagano for $100,000 \u201cfor expenses concerning a lawsuit in Teterboro.\u201d\n\nSweeney does not write in the memo why Mordaga would share in case proceeds. But the Lagano estate alleged in court papers last year that the former chief made \u201cillegal\u201d referrals to personal injury attorney Richard J. Weiner for \u201ckickbacks\u201d on multimillion-dollar cases. It filed business records \u2014 apparently from Weiner\u2019s defunct firm \u2014 in support of its claims. In a deposition, the firm\u2019s comptroller acknowledged his handwriting on three documents and testified that Mordaga referred \u201ca couple of cases\u201d to Weiner.\n\nOne of the cases the estate cites involved a man injured in a 2005 corporate jet crash at Teterboro Airport, James Dinnall.\n\nA plane crashed into the Strawberry clothing factory after takeoff from Teterboro Airport on Feb. 2, 2005. (Photo: Chris Pedota/NorthJersey.com)\n\nMordaga was involved in bringing that case to Weiner, who died in 2015, interviews and phone records show.\n\nOn the day of the accident, Mordaga visited the injured man\u2019s family at the hospital and subsequently recommended that they hire Weiner, the man\u2019s daughter, Diane Dinnall, said in an interview.\n\nAt some point, she said, Mordaga suggested Weiner.\n\n\u201cHe recommended him,\u201d she said. \u201cI can\u2019t say if it was the same day of the accident but he recommended him.\u201d\n\nA car on the eastbound lane of Rt. 46, where a man was injured by a plane that eventually crashed into the Strawberry clothing factory after takeoff from Teterboro Airport on Feb. 2, 2005. (Photo: Chris Pedota/NorthJersey.com)\n\nPhone records of Mordaga\u2019s county-issued cellphone indicate contact with Weiner \u2014 two calls on the day of the crash and 10 more by the time the lawsuit was filed on the victim\u2019s behalf less than three months later. The records also show a call to the victim\u2019s daughter on the evening of the crash, and four more in subsequent weeks.\n\nThe Sweeney memo goes on to say that shortly before Lagano\u2019s killing, the attorney sent him a $60,000 check as partial payment on a loan but stopped payment on it after learning he died.\n\nThe estate\u2019s court filings include a copy of a $60,000 check from Weiner to Lagano dated the day before his death.\n\nThe Sweeney memo omits more than a dozen pages immediately after the mention of the canceled check; sources say the omission is a transcript of a recorded conversation between Lagano\u2019s son and Weiner.\n\nThe alleged business relationship is key to the estate\u2019s claims.\n\n\u201cMordaga, in addition to owing Lagano money, also feared his lawsuit referral scheme would be exposed because Lagano was providing intelligence to Sweeney,\u201d the estate claims in one filing.\n\nSweeney\u2019s 2007 memo indicates Mordaga was aware that he and Lagano were talking. It recounts how Lagano was allegedly approached by Mordaga at a restaurant and told he could have half his money returned - and serve no jail time - if he used \u201chis suggested counsel.\u201d\n\nWhen Lagano left, Mordaga followed him to another establishment and allegedly said \u201cdon\u2019t count on Sweeney helping you, he\u2019s going to jail.\u201d\n\nMordaga and Lagano\n\nMordaga retired from the prosecutor\u2019s office in 2007, in the months after Lagano\u2019s death, and went on to become civilian police director in Hackensack. He left that post last year.\n\nThough he has rarely spoken about the case, court filings show he denied having a business relationship or \u201carrangement\u201d with Weiner, or receiving any money from him \u2013 including related to the Teterboro case.\n\nCourt records show, too, that Mordaga explained his interactions with Lagano, who he said \u201ccontacted me at some point\u201d but he did not remember \u201call the specifics of those contacts.\u201d\n\nHACKENSACK:City won\u2019t replace Mordaga with another civilian police director\n\nPAYOUT:$63,000 payout to Mordaga for unused sick
