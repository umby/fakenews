Situation\" Sorrentino\u2019s legal problems just got worse, as a federal grand jury Friday returned a new indictment containing additional charges of tax evasion and other federal crimes.</p><p>Sorrentino, 35, of Manalapan, and his brother, Marc Sorrentino, were first brought up on tax evasion and fraud charges in 2014, and now are both named in a new indictment alleging tax evasion, financial structuring and falsifying records, said acting U.S. Attorney William E. Fitzpatrick.<br><br></p><p><span class=\"exclude-from-newsgate\"><strong>MORE:<a href=\"/story/news/crime/jersey-mayhem/2015/12/16/accountant-admits-helping-situation-scam-irs/77435632/\">\u00a0Accountant admits helping 'The Situation' scam IRS</a></strong></span><br><br>\r\nMichael Sorrentino coined the nickname \u201cThe Situation\u2019\u2019 while starring in the MTV reality show \u201cJersey Shore,\u2019\u2019 which followed the exploits of hard-partying 20-somethings in Seaside Heights.</p><div class=\"partner-placement partner-spike\"></div><p>The new indictment said Michael and Marc Sorrentino created various companies, such as MPS Entertainment LLC and Situation Nation, Inc. to exploit Michael\u2019s celebrity status.</p><p>The new indictment repeats the allegations made in 2014 that Michael Sorrentino and his brother conspired with each other to defraud the United States by failing to pay all of the taxes owed on about $8.9 million that Michael Sorrentino made between 2010 and 2012.</p><div class=\"partner-outstream\"></div><p>In addition, the new indictment charges Michael Sorrentino with structuring funds to evade currency transaction reports. And it charges Marc Sorrentino with falsifying records to obstruct a grand jury investigation.</p><p>Michael Sorrentino and his brother were originally scheduled to stand trial on the tax evasion charges in 2015, but the trial was postponed because the reality television star failed to pay his attorney, who then withdrew from the case. The trial had not been re-scheduled.</p><p>Michael Sorrentino reportedly earned $150,000 an episode for the six-season MTV reality series \u201cJersey Shore,\u2019\u2019 which was filmed primarily in Seaside Heights.</p><p>He and his brother also opened Boca Tanning Clubs in the area, but the tanning salon operated by Michael Sorrentino closed in 2014, and Marc Sorrentino\u2019s tanning salon in Manalapan closed the following year.<br></p><p><span class=\"exclude-from-newsgate\"><strong>MORE:<a href=\"/story/money/business/main-street/2015/11/02/manalapans-boca-tanning-sorrentino-fame-closes/74993630/\">\u00a0Manalapan's Boca Tanning of Sorrentino fame closes</a></strong></span></p><p><br>\r\nIn the superceding indictment, the brothers are charged with falsifying tax returns by understating gross receipts, claiming fraudulent business deductions, and disguising and underreporting income. The indictment alleges they commingled funds among business and personal bank accounts and used money from business accounts to pay for personal items, such as high-end luxury vehicles and clothing.</p><p>The superceding indictment alleges that Michael Sorrentino made multiple cash deposits on the same day in amounts less than $10,000 to different bank accounts to evade the banks\u2019 requirements to report cash deposits exceeding $10,000 to the U.S. Treasury.</p><p>In addition, the new indictment alleges that Marc Sorrentino, after being served grand jury subpoenas for the books and records of MPS Entertainment LLC and Situation Nation Inc., altered the books by reclassifying taxable payments to himself as non-taxable payments and legitimate business deductions.</p><p>The Sorrentino brothers face arraignment on the new charges on April 17 before U.S. District Judge Susan D. Wigenton in federal court in Newark.<br><br>\r\nIf convicted, each brother faces a maximum of five years in prison on the conspiracy charge and three years in prison for each of multiple counts of aiding in the preparation of false tax returns. Michael Sorrentino would face a maximum of 10 years in prison for each of multiple counts of financial structuring, and five years in prison for tax evasion. Marc Sorrentino would face a maximum sentence of 20 years in prison if convicted of obstruction. They brothers also would face supervised release and be required to pay restitution and fines.</p><p><em>Kathleen Hopkins: 732-643-4202; Khopkins@app.com</em></p><div (Photo: ASSOCIATED PRESS)\n\nNEWARK - \"Jersey Shore\" reality star Michael \"The Situation\" Sorrentino\u2019s legal problems just got worse, as a federal grand jury Friday returned a new indictment containing additional charges of tax evasion and other federal crimes.\n\nSorrentino, 35, of Manalapan, and his brother, Marc Sorrentino, were first brought up on tax evasion and fraud charges in 2014, and now are both named in a new indictment alleging tax evasion, financial structuring and falsifying records, said acting U.S. Attorney William E. Fitzpatrick.\n\n\n\n\n\nMORE: Accountant admits helping 'The Situation' scam IRS\n\n\n\nMichael Sorrentino coined the nickname \u201cThe Situation\u2019\u2019 while starring in the MTV reality show \u201cJersey Shore,\u2019\u2019 which followed the exploits of hard-partying 20-somethings in Seaside Heights.\n\nThe new indictment said Michael and Marc Sorrentino created various companies, such as MPS Entertainment LLC and Situation Nation, Inc. to exploit Michael\u2019s celebrity status.\n\nThe new indictment repeats the allegations made in 2014 that Michael Sorrentino and his brother conspired with each other to defraud the United States by failing to pay all of the taxes owed on about $8.9 million that Michael Sorrentino made between 2010 and 2012.\n\nIn addition, the new indictment charges Michael Sorrentino with structuring funds to evade currency transaction reports. And it charges Marc Sorrentino with falsifying records to obstruct a grand jury investigation.\n\nMichael Sorrentino and his brother were originally scheduled to stand trial on the tax evasion charges in 2015, but the trial was postponed because the reality television star failed to pay his attorney, who then withdrew from the case. The trial had not been re-scheduled.\n\nMichael Sorrentino reportedly earned $150,000 an episode for the six-season MTV reality series \u201cJersey Shore,\u2019\u2019 which was filmed primarily in Seaside Heights.\n\nHe and his brother also opened Boca Tanning Clubs in the area, but the tanning salon operated by Michael Sorrentino closed in 2014, and Marc Sorrentino\u2019s tanning salon in Manalapan closed the following year.\n\n\n\nMORE: Manalapan's Boca Tanning of Sorrentino fame closes\n\n\n\nIn the superceding indictment, the brothers are charged with falsifying tax returns by understating gross receipts, claiming fraudulent business deductions, and disguising and underreporting income. The indictment alleges they commingled funds among business and personal bank accounts and used money from business accounts to pay for personal items, such as high-end luxury vehicles and clothing.\n\nThe superceding indictment alleges that Michael Sorrentino made multiple cash deposits on the same day in amounts less than $10,000 to different bank accounts to evade the banks\u2019 requirements to report cash deposits exceeding $10,000 to the U.S. Treasury.\n\nIn addition, the new indictment alleges that Marc Sorrentino, after being served grand jury subpoenas for the books and records of MPS Entertainment LLC and Situation Nation Inc., altered the books by reclassifying taxable payments to himself as non-taxable payments and legitimate business deductions.\n\nThe Sorrentino brothers face arraignment on the new charges on April 17 before U.S. District Judge Susan D. Wigenton in federal court in Newark.\n\n\n\nIf convicted, each brother faces a maximum of five years in prison on the conspiracy charge and three years in prison for each of multiple counts of aiding in the preparation of false tax returns. Michael Sorrentino would face a maximum of 10 years in prison for each of multiple counts of financial structuring, and five years in prison for tax evasion. Marc Sorrentino would face a maximum sentence of 20 years in prison if convicted of obstruction.
