the sort of athletic gifts that would lead one day to <em>SportsCente</em>r highlights and multi-million-dollar contracts to play for the darlings of his region, the New England Patriots.</p><p>But the boy from Bristol also harbored demons of the sort that would one day lead him to drugs, accusations of cold-blooded murder and, finally, his own death. Authorities said he <a href=\"https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/nfl/2017/04/19/aaron-hernandez-dead-after-being-found-hanged-in-prison-cell/100638226/\">hanged himself with a bed sheet early Wednesday</a> in the prison cell where he was serving a life sentence without possibility of parole. He was 27 and had lived an operatic hybrid of American dream and ghastly nightmare. His death came the same day his former team visited the White House to celebrate their Super Bowl victory two months ago.</p><p>Hernandez caught a touchdown pass in the Super Bowl in early 2012 \u2013\u00a0and the next year found himself accused of killing a semipro football player, Odin Lloyd, who was dating the sister of Hernandez\u2019s fianc\u00e9e. Motive remains unclear, though it may have been that Lloyd had some knowledge of Hernandez\u2019s link to a double homicide in the summer of 2012.</p><p>Last week, Hernandez was acquitted of the most serious charges in that case, which would have only added to his life sentence because Massachusetts does not have the death penalty. Answers are ultimately unknowable but clues abound, beginning with the unexpected death of his father in 2006 when Hernandez was 16.</p></p><p>Dennis Hernandez was a high school custodian who died of complications arising from routine hernia surgery. The aftermath \u201cwas a mess with a lot of family issues, fighting and disagreements,\u201d <a href=\"https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/nfl/2017/04/19/aaron-hernandez-father-tattoos-death/100651348/\">Hernandez told USA TODAY Sports in 2009</a>, when he was playing at Florida.</p><p>\u201cI didn\u2019t know what to do for him,\u201d his mother Terri said in the same story. \u201cHe would rebel. The shock of losing his dad, there was so much anger.\u201d</p><h3 class=\"presto-h3\"><strong>Trouble 'finds this guy'</strong></h3><p>Hernandez was an honor student at Bristol Central High School, though Doug Pina, his high school coach, told the <em>Hartford Courant</em> in 2010 that he\u2019d personally \u201calways had concerns\u201d about him.</p><p>Hernandez had originally planned to play college football for Connecticut, where his father and older brother played, but changed his mind and chose Florida. Before his first college game, Hernandez, then 17, punched a restaurant manager in the side of the head, busting his eardrum in a dispute over the bill. The case apparently settled out of court.</p><p>Hernandez also was among several Gators players interviewed by police after a shooting at a nightclub that left two men wounded. No one was ever charged.</p><p>Hernandez won a national championship and the John Mackey Award for the nation\u2019s best tight end during his time at Florida. Hernandez\u2019s cousin Tanya married Jeffrey Cummings in 2005; they had a son together and got divorced. Then, in 2009, Cummings married Hernandez\u2019s mother on a trip to Las Vegas. Cummings would wind up in jail and they would divorce.</p><p>Hernandez married Shayanna Jenkins in 2012 in the same month their daughter was born.
