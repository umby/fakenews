He could continue moving the party to the center or tack back to the left. The second option would have focused on government programs, like expanding Medicare to start at age 55. But Obama and his team thought a plan that mixed government and markets \u2014 farther to the right of Clinton\u2019s \u2014 could cover millions of people and had a realistic chance of passing.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-6\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-6 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-5\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"329\" data-total-count=\"3425\" id=\"story-continues-5\">They embarked on a bipartisan approach. They borrowed from Mitt Romney\u2019s plan in Massachusetts, <a href=\"https://www.theatlantic.com/politics/archive/2015/07/the-real-story-of-obamacares-birth/397742/\">gave a big role</a> to a bipartisan Senate working group, incorporated conservative ideas and won initial support from some Republicans. The bill also won over groups that had long blocked reform, like the American Medical Association.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"416\" data-total-count=\"3841\">But congressional Republicans ultimately decided that opposing any bill, regardless of its substance, was in their political interest. The consultant Frank Luntz wrote <a href=\"https://thinkprogress.org/deconstructing-frank-luntzs-obstructionist-health-care-reform-memo-83e78d209e86#.ohafs0uq3\">an influential memo</a> in 2009 advising Republicans to talk positively about \u201creform\u201d while also opposing actual solutions. McConnell, the Senate leader, <a href=\"http://www.nytimes.com/2010/03/17/us/politics/17mcconnell.html?pagewanted=all\">persuaded</a> his colleagues that they could make Obama look bad by denying him bipartisan cover.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-8\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-8 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-6\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"222\" data-total-count=\"4063\" id=\"story-continues-6\">At that point, Obama faced a second choice \u2013 between forging ahead with a substantively bipartisan bill and forgetting about covering the uninsured. The kumbaya plan for which pundits now wax nostalgic was not an option.</p><div id=\"story-ad-2\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent \">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-7\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"376\" data-total-count=\"4439\" id=\"story-continues-7\">The reason is simple enough: Obamacare <em>is</em> the bipartisan version of health reform. It accomplishes a liberal end through conservative means and is much closer to the plan conservatives favored a few decades ago than the one liberals did. \u201cIt was the ultimate troll,\u201d as Michael Anne Kyle of Harvard Business School <a href=\"https://twitter.com/michaelannica/status/834908950785032195?refsrc=email&amp;s=11\">put it</a>, \u201cfor Obama to pass Republican health reform.\u201d</p><button class=\"button comments-button  theme-speech-bubble-large\" data-skip-to-para-id=\"\">\n</button>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"520\" data-total-count=\"4959\">Today\u2019s Republican Party has moved so far to the right that it no longer supports any plan that covers the uninsured. Of course, Republican leaders are not willing to say as much, because they know how unpopular that position is. Having run out of political ground, Ryan, McConnell and Trump have had to invent the notion of a socialistic Obamacare that they will repeal and replace with \u2026 something great! This morning they were also left to pretend that the Budget Office report was something less than a disaster.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-10\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-10 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-8\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"188\" data-total-count=\"5147\" id=\"story-continues-8\">Their approach to Obamacare has worked quite nicely for them, until now. Lying can be an effective political tactic. Believing your own alternative facts, however, is usually not so smart.</p><footer class=\"story-footer story-content\">\n    <div class=\"story-meta\">\n                        <div class=\"story-info\"><p><em>You can join me on <a href=\"https://twitter.com/DLeonhardt\" title=\"Ross Douthat on Twitter\">Twitter (@DLeonhardt)</a> and <a href=\"https://www.facebook.com/DavidRLeonhardt/\">Facebook</a>.
