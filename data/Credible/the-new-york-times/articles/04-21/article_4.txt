Trump built his entire reputation not as the champion of the common man, but by curating his image as a crude effigy of the cultural elite.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"136\" data-total-count=\"4097\">He accrued his wealth by selling hollow dreams of high society to people who wanted to flaunt their money or pretend that they had some.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"279\" data-total-count=\"4376\">Put another way, Trump\u2019s brand is built on exclusivity, not inclusivity. It is about the separate, vaulted position of luxury, above and beyond the ability for it to be accessed by the common. It is all about the bourgeois and has absolutely nothing to do with the blue collar.</p><button class=\"button comments-button  theme-speech-bubble-large\" data-skip-to-para-id=\"\">\n</button>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"153\" data-total-count=\"4529\">And yet somehow, it was the blue collar that bought his bill of goods. People saw uncouth and thought unconventional; they saw raffish and thought rebel.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"153\" data-total-count=\"4682\">They projected principle and commitment onto a person anathema to both.
