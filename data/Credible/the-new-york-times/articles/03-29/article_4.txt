There weren\u2019t many women in that room because the Freedom Caucus appears to be almost entirely <a href=\"http://www.cnn.com/2017/03/23/politics/mike-pence-patty-murray-photo/\">male</a>. Women make up less than nine percent of congressional Republicans. President Trump\u2019s <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/01/13/us/politics/trump-cabinet-women-minorities.html\">cabinet</a> is the most white and male in 35 years. Among his top staff members, men <a href=\"http://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/2017/02/20/men-outnumber-women-trump-aide-white-house/97913112/\">outnumber</a> women two to one. Republicans bank on a white male voter base that is shrinking demographically, yet they are making no real effort to broaden their appeal, perhaps counting on <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2016/09/08/opinion/voter-suppression-in-north-carolina.html\">voter suppression</a> to make it harder for likely Democratic voters to cast their ballots, and simultaneously throwing red meat to the men they need to turn out to carry them to victory.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"308\" data-total-count=\"4936\">That\u2019s why Mr. Trump\u2019s infamous lewd comments about his conduct with women weren\u2019t actually a liability. The people those comments offended weren\u2019t going to vote for him in the first place, and the people most drawn to Mr. Trump liked him because he\u2019s an unrepentant chauvinist, not in spite of it.</p><div id=\"story-ad--aggro-6\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-6 aggro-only\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-5\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"318\" data-total-count=\"5254\" id=\"story-continues-5\">The Trump team is well aware of this dynamic, which is why it doesn\u2019t spend much time worrying about even putting forward a facade of diversity. The great America it promised has white men at the top, and that\u2019s the image they\u2019re projecting, figuratively and literally. It\u2019s not an error, it\u2019s the game plan.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"525\" data-total-count=\"5779\">Of course, it might be a losing one \u2014 despite seven years of lead time and a majority in Congress, Republicans couldn\u2019t cobble together a health care bill they could all agree on. That failure came because the men of the Freedom Caucus refused to vote for a bill that didn\u2019t meet every one of their demands, and the demands of theirs that were met were sufficiently alienating to the more moderate members of their party that many of them (including several <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2017/03/24/us/politics/house-vote-republican-health-care-bill.html\">women</a>) probably weren\u2019t going to vote for the bill, either.</p><p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"290\" data-total-count=\"6069\">The incompetence of the boys\u2019 club effectively saved funding for Planned Parenthood and insurance coverage of maternity care. But don\u2019t expect them to learn the lesson that this should be the last female-free image (and female-unfriendly policy) the Republican leadership proudly touts.</p><div id=\"story-ad-2\" class=\"story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-8\">\n    <div class=\"accessibility-ad-header visually-hidden\">\n    <p>Advertisement</p>\n</div>\n<a class=\"visually-hidden skip-to-text-link\" href=\"#story-continues-6\">Continue reading the main story</a>\n</div>\n<p class=\"story-body-text story-content\" data-para-count=\"168\" data-total-count=\"6237\" id=\"story-continues-6\">For liberal women, this latest all-male photo is a visualization of our worst fears realized.
