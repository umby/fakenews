He also has a neat trick or two.\n\nWashing your hair should be as basic as slipping on your favorite bluejeans. After all, the process would seem rote at this point. But if the clients at Christophe Robin\u2019s Paris salon are any indication, there is plenty of confusion.\n\n\u201cThat\u2019s my most asked question at the salon now: \u2018How do I wash my hair?\u2019\u201d said Mr. Robin, the star colorist who tends the locks of Catherine Deneuve and Tilda Swinton. Part of the problem is the baffling messaging about shampooing: Women have been told they are both overwashing and underwashing.\n\n\u201cA lot of this is also from all the products we have out there now,\u201d Mr. Robin said. \u201cAnd women are so often in a rush. You have to cleanse properly. You have to take time to make sure you rinse all the products out.\u201d\n\nHere, Mr. Robin offers a tutorial on the right way to shampoo. He also has some tips for maximizing shine and volume, all without styling products:\n\nInteractive\n\nStep 1: First detangle hair with a quality brush. Mr. Robin recommends starting at the ends. Then double back to the roots to finish the process. \u201cYou detangle first so that way you don\u2019t have to detangle hair after, when it\u2019s wet,\u201d he said. \u201cYou never should use a comb on hair that is wet. It\u2019s very stressing on the hair.\u201d\n\nInteractive\n\nStep 2: Before shampooing, apply a vegetable-derived oil to the ends of hair and brush to disperse. Mr. Robin uses themoisturizing hair oil with lavender($44) from his namesake line, but he said pure almond oil or argan oil would do as well. Ideally, you would leave the oil on overnight, but even a good 15 minutes will be beneficial. Then you can skip the conditioner.\n\n\u201cI don\u2019t love conditioners,\u201d Mr. Robin said. \u201cThey can weigh your hair down.\u201d\n\nInteractive\n\nStep 3: Wash with the cleanser best suited to your hair type. \u201cThe problem is, some women are not choosing the right shampoo for their hair,\u201d he said. Considering the sulfate-free trend in hair care lately, he noted that colored hair should never be treated with sulfate products but that sometimes a sulfate shampoo, which has a stronger detergent, is appropriate \u201cfor someone who has virgin hair and greasy roots.\u201d\n\nCurlier hair, he explained, tends to be drier and can benefit from co-washing \u2014 that is, washing with a nonfoaming, conditioning cleanser as a substitute for shampoo. Mr. Robin released one of the first luxury brand co-washes some 20 years ago, his cleansing mask with lemon ($49).\n\nMr. Robin said most women used too much product: \u201cYou should use only about a teaspoon and then you emulsify that with water.\u201d Lather only the roots of your hair with your fingertips (not your nails), avoiding the ends. To increase volume and circulation, shampoo with your head upside down.\n\nInteractive\n\nStep 4: Rinse thoroughly. \u201cThis is actually a big problem because people are not taking enough time to rinse out all the product,\u201d Mr. Robin said. \u201cHair should be squeaky clean.\u201d\n\n\n\nInteractive\n\nStep 5: If you decide to add a conditioner, apply only to the ends. Mr. Robin recommends a deep conditioning mask once a week to address various hair issues, including brittleness or brassy color.\n\nInteractive\n\nStep 6: Don\u2019t rub hair dry. Mr. Robin demonstrated a Moroccan tip he learned to detangle and lift the roots: \u201cFlip your head upside down and run a towel over it from both sides quickly,\u201d he said. The towel\u2019s flicking motion will also remove excess water.\n\nBonus Tip: Mr. Robin acknowledges that his washing process may take more time than the usual regimens, but that if done correctly, the method can make each shampoo last longer. \u201cMost women should not be washing more than twice a week,\u201d he said.\n\nThat is not to say that dry shampoo is the answer for the other days. \u201cDry shampoo should only give you maybe one night or one day extra,\u201d he said. Instead, especially for active women, a few spritzes of a vinegar solution at the roots will remove oil. (Just add five drops of apple cider vinegar to five ounces of water in a spray bottle.)\n\nMr. Robin added, \u201cUnlike dry shampoo, there\u2019s no residue, and the vinegar is a wonderful tonic for the scalp.\u201d\n\nClick here to see the full \u201chow to wash your hair\u201d tutorial video.\n\nContinue following our fashion and lifestyle coverage on Facebook (Styles and Modern Love), Twitter (Styles, Fashion and Weddings) and Instagram.", "images": ["https://static01.nyt.com/images/2017/04/21/fashion/21-christophe-robin/21-christophe-robin-articleLarge.jpg", "https://static01.nyt.com/images/2017/04/21/fashion/21-christophe-robin/21-christophe-robin-facebookJumbo-v2.jpg"], "videos": []}
