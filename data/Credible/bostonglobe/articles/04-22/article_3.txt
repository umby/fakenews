The organization has become a major source of funding for Brady&rsquo;s own charitable goals.</p><p>\n<b class=\"b\"></b>Since 2011, while Brady has served as the face of its signature Massachusetts fund-raiser and helped it raise nearly $20 million, Best Buddies has paid $2.75 million to Brady&rsquo;s own charitable trust and has pledged to grant the organization an additional $500,000 in 2017 &mdash; a total of $3.25 million.</p><div class=\"inline-newsletter\" data-exact-target-ID=\"20233025\" data-mailing-list-name=\"Sports Headlines\" data-is-amp=\"\" data-section=\"/sports\">\n  <div class=\"inline-newsletter__network-error\"></div>\n  <div class=\"inline-newsletter__copy\">\n    <div class=\"inline-newsletter__title\">\n      Get <span class=\"inline-newsletter__bold\">Sports Headlines</span> in your inbox:\n    </div>\n    <div class=\"inline-newsletter__description\">\n        The Globe's most recent sports headlines delivered to your inbox every morning.</div>\n    </div>\n  <form class=\"inline-newsletter__content\" method=\"post\">\n    <div class=\"inline-newsletter__error-text\"></div>\n    <input placeholder=\"Enter email address\" type=\"text\" id=\"inline-newsletter-email-input\" class=\"inline-newsletter__input\">\n\n    <div class=\"inline-newsletter__button-container\">\n      <button class=\"inline-newsletter__button\">\n        <span class=\"inline-newsletter__button-text\">Sign Up</span>\n      </button>\n      <div class=\"inline-newsletter__spinner\"></div>\n    </div>\n  </form>\n  <div class=\"inline-newsletter__thanks\">\n    Thank you for signing up!\n    <a href=\"/newsletters?p1=Article_inline-newsletterWidget_ThankYou_Link\" class=\"inline-newsletter__link\">Sign up for more newsletters here</a>\n    </div>\n</div>\n\n<p>The payments, made at Brady&rsquo;s request, support his Boston-based Change the World Foundation Trust, whose giving has focused almost entirely on causes tied to Brady&rsquo;s personal interests, including his high school alma mater, his children&rsquo;s private schools, and charities operated by his football friends. Aiding those with disabilities, the focus of Best Buddies, has been a lower priority.</p><!-- total:0 -->\n\n<p>Independent charity monitors said there appears to be nothing illegal about the arrangement &mdash; charities can and do donate to other charities. But they questioned why someone of Brady&rsquo;s wealth has taken large sums for his foundation from another charity that has its own priorities and needs. The donations from Best Buddies have been by far the Brady organization&rsquo;s largest source of funds.</p><p>Brady &ldquo;really can&rsquo;t take credit for being a great philanthropist when he is using other people&rsquo;s money to help his own organizations,&rsquo;&rsquo; said Daniel Borochoff, president of Charity Watch, a Chicago-based nonprofit that evaluates philanthropies and advocates for consumers. &ldquo;It&rsquo;s certainly not pure altruism.&rsquo;&rsquo;</p><!--  working ad -->\n<p class=\"skip-nav article-more\">\n\t<a>Advertisement</a>\n\t<!-- <a href=\"#skip-target2\">Continue reading below</a> -->\n</p>\n<!-- adLayerPath: /Boston/Production/BostonGlobe.com/WebPages/Homepage/homepage.ad-layer.story-asset.dwp -->\n<div class=\"ad aside\" id=\"ad_bigbox2\"></div>  <a id=\"skip-target2\"></a>\n\n<p>\n<b class=\"b\"></b>Charitable giving experts also expressed concern that Best Buddies and Brady have given the public and other Best Buddies donors and participants only limited information about the financial arrangement, and they wondered if Best Buddies should properly report the annual payments as fund-raising expenses rather than charitable gifts.</p><p>\n<b class=\"b\"></b><b class=\"b\"></b>The arrangement was forged by Brady and his friend, Anthony K. Shriver, the founder and chairman of Best Buddies, a public charity that solicits money nationwide.</p><div class=\"wide\">\n<img alt=\"Tom Brady (left) and Anthony K. Shriver (right), with Chris Harrington, at a Best Buddies event in Boston.\" data-fullsrc=\"//c.o0bg.com/rf/image_1920w/Boston/2011-2020/2017/04/23/BostonGlobe.com/Metro/Images/01272012_27namesbrady1-8166589.jpg\" src=\"//c.o0bg.com/rf/image_r/Boston/2011-2020/2017/04/23/BostonGlobe.com/Metro/Images/01272012_27namesbrady1-8166589.r.jpg\"><div class=\"figcaption\">\n<p class=\"credit\">Jay Connor</p>\n<p>Tom Brady (left) and Anthony K. Shriver (right), with Chris Harrington, at a Best Buddies event in Boston.</p>\n</div>\n</div><p>Shriver said in an interview that Brady had volunteered his time and image to Best Buddies for many years before he told Shriver he was becoming very busy and wanted to focus on his own charitable foundation.</p><p>Shriver quoted Brady as saying, some time before the payments began in 2011: &ldquo;When I come to Best Buddies events, it&rsquo;s all about Best Buddies, which is the way I want to keep it while I develop my interests. Would you support helping me pursue my things while I continue to put a lot of energy into your thing?&rsquo;&rsquo;</p><div class=\"aside nr-graphic\">\n<img alt=\"\n\" data-fullsrc=\"//c.o0bg.com/rf/image_371w/Boston/2011-2020/2017/04/23/BostonGlobe.com/Metro/Images/bradybestbuddiesnumbers.jpg\" src=\"//c.o0bg.com/rf/image_r/Boston/2011-2020/2017/04/23/BostonGlobe.com/Metro/Images/bradybestbuddiesnumbers.r.jpg\"><div class=\"figcaption\">\n<p class=\"credit\">\n\n</p>\n<p>\n\n</p>\n</div>\n</div><p>Shriver said they agreed Best Buddies would grant Brady&rsquo;s charitable trust $250,000 in 2011. Every year since, they have decided the figure would be $500,000, and Shriver said he expects Best Buddies to continue giving $500,000 annually to Brady&rsquo;s trust. They have no contract.</p><p>&ldquo;I think it has been a smart move, because we have been able to provide services to tens of thousands of people because of Tom, while keeping him engaged and helping him pursue some of his own interests,&rsquo;&rsquo; Shriver said. &ldquo;It has been super beneficial to us and him.&rsquo;&rsquo;</p><p>Shriver said Brady was not available to comment. Shortly after inquiries from the Globe earlier this month, Best Buddies issued a news release crediting Brady with helping to raise $46.5 million since he began supporting the organization as a volunteer in 2001.</p><p>The release quoted Brady as describing his affiliation with Best Buddies as &ldquo;one of my greatest honors.&rsquo;&rsquo;</p><p>&ldquo;This organization has become a part of my life,&rsquo;&rsquo; Brady said in the release. &ldquo;I love meeting the inspiring buddies at events, seeing their smiles, and hearing them talk about how Best Buddies has changed their lives for the better by helping them make friends at school and find meaningful jobs in their local communities. Best Buddies&rsquo; mission is a simple one, but the impact is incredibly powerful. I am so grateful to be a part of this global movement.&rdquo;</p><p>Shriver, a nephew of President John F. Kennedy, founded Best Buddies in 1989 to advance a cause that his mother, Eunice Kennedy Shriver, the slain president&rsquo;s sister, began to champion in the 1960s.</p><p>Many luminaries have supported Best Buddies through the years, among them current &ldquo;celebrity ambassadors&rsquo;&rsquo; who include Brady, Sugar Ray Leonard, Kyrie Irving, Carl Lewis, Ron Howard, Rob Lowe, Emmitt Smith, and Cindy Crawford.</p><p>Yet only one celebrity other than Brady has received compensation for his charitable foundation from Best Buddies, Shriver said. The other was Kevin Spacey, whose personal charity received a one-time grant of $200,000 in 2015.</p><p>Charity watchdogs said it is not unusual for nonprofits to pay celebrities to help raise money, and they did not dispute that Best Buddies has realized an enviable return on its relationship with Brady. Shriver credited Brady with raising a sizable portion of the $19.7 million that Best Buddies has garnered from its annual Hyannis Port Challenge since the payments to Brady&rsquo;s trust began in 2011.</p><p>But nonprofit watchdogs said the arrangement could diminish Brady&rsquo;s charitable image.</p><p>&ldquo;It&rsquo;s not all bad, because he is helping an important cause,&rsquo;&rsquo; Borochoff said. &ldquo;But he could help Best Buddies to a much greater extent if he didn&rsquo;t take their money&rsquo;&rsquo; for his own charitable trust.</p><p>Brady launched Change the World in 2005, after he signed his first big deal with the Patriots: a six-year, $60 million contract. He has since amassed personal wealth estimated at $180 million, while the net worth of his wife, Gisele Bundchen, is about $360 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth, a website that tracks such things.</p><p>\n<b class=\"b\"></b>Brady, the sole trustee of his private charity, initially funded the nonprofit with a donation of $490,000 in 2005. Since then he has not made a significant financial contribution, according to tax reports through 2015, the most recently available.</p><p>Instead, Best Buddies has almost entirely funded Brady&rsquo;s foundation. Through 2015, nearly 98 percent of Change the World&rsquo;s total contributions of $2.3 million since Brady&rsquo;s initial donation were $2.25 million in grants from Best Buddies. Including Brady&rsquo;s donation, the percentage is about 80%.</p><p>And where did the money go? From 2011 through to 2015, Change the World gave grants of $55,000 to Best Buddies, after donating a total of $80,000 over the previous three years.</p><p>Otherwise, from 2011 through 2015, Change the World distributed nearly $1 million to more than 30 nonprofits. The largest beneficiary was Brady&rsquo;s alma mater, Junipero Serra High School, in San Mateo, Calif., at $250,000.</p><p>The next largest grants, $100,000 each, went to Santa Monica Catholic Community, where Brady and Gisele Bundchen were married in 2009, and the TB12 Foundation, a charity Brady formed in 2015 to support clients of his Foxborough-based TB12 fitness and nutrition company.</p><p>Ohio State University received a $50,000 donation in honor of Ed Razek, a former Victoria&rsquo;s Secret executive who is said to have helped launch Brady&rsquo;s romance with Bundchen by setting them up in 2006.</p><p>Others getting $50,000 were Brady&rsquo;s college alma mater, the University of Michigan, for cancer research; as well as Dana-Farber Cancer Center, Mission of Hope Haiti, and the Navy SEAL Foundation.</p><p>A charity honoring Tom Martinez, Brady&rsquo;s longtime personal coach who died in 2012, received $37,800. And $30,000 went to a nursing facility in Minnesota that cared for Brady&rsquo;s grandfather before he died in 2016.</p><p>In addition, Change the World donated $10,000 to Saint David&rsquo;s School in New York City and $20,000 to the Apple Orchard School in Brookline, both of which Brady&rsquo;s children have attended.</p><p>Nonprofits created by Brady&rsquo;s former teammates also benefited, with grants of $25,000 going to Vince Wilfork&rsquo;s charity, $10,000 to Deion Branch&rsquo;s, and $5,000 to Wes Welker&rsquo;s.</p><p>The entire arrangement has stirred concerns among some nonprofit watchdogs. Marc Pollick, who for 20 years has advised charity-minded celebrities through his Giving Back Fund, began consulting last year for the NFL Players Association. Because Brady is an NFLPA member, Pollick would not comment directly on the quarterback or Best Buddies.</p><p>But he said he would counsel any celebrity against participating in such an arrangement.</p><p>&ldquo;I would say, &lsquo;Absolutely not,&rsquo;&thinsp;&rdquo; Pollick said. &ldquo;It&rsquo;s not good use of charitable money, and it&rsquo;s bad for your reputation.&rsquo;&rsquo;</p><p>Best Buddies has spent more than $200 million developing a volunteer network aimed at ending the social, physical, and economic isolation often experienced by those living with disabilities. Volunteers help them form friendships with peers, find jobs, and live independently.</p><p>Shriver said he was not concerned that Brady&rsquo;s trust has dedicated relatively little of its Best Buddies money to Shriver&rsquo;s cause. Change the World ended 2015 with net assets of $1.7 million.</p><p>&ldquo;I&rsquo;m comfortable supporting things that Tom has developed an interest in,&rsquo;&rsquo; Shriver said. &ldquo;He has done a lot for us, and I think we get more out of it than Change the World gets from us.&rsquo;&rsquo;</p><p>Nonprofit watchdogs said the public would be better served if their financial relationship were clearly disclosed. There is no mention of the arrangement in Best Buddies promotional material.</p><p>Best Buddies did recently describe the payment arrangment with Brady in a press release. Before that, a prospective donor, short of directly asking Best Buddies about the connection, would have needed to visit the charity&rsquo;s website, download its tax return, and scroll through dozens of pages to find listed a $500,000 grant to the Change the World Foundation.</p><p>Even then, there is no mention of Brady. And Googling &ldquo;Change the World Foundation&rsquo;&rsquo; does not readily reveal a connection to Brady. By further searching Change the World&rsquo;s state or federal filings, a prospective donor could discover that the nonprofit&rsquo;s trustee is Thomas E. Brady Jr.</p><p>Borochoff said charities are not required to publicize their compensation to celebrities, although failing to do so may not reflect the highest standards of philanthropy.</p><p>&ldquo;Sometimes the compensation is hard to find, and a celebrity gets credit for caring about something when the reason he actually cares about it is because he or his foundation is getting paid,&rsquo;&rsquo; Borochoff said.</p><p>Shriver said he and Brady agreed that fully disclosing the arrangement might prove distracting, especially during the Hyannis Port Challenge. This year&rsquo;s sold-out event, whose highlight is a 100-mile fund-raising bike ride from Boston to Hyannis Port, is scheduled for the first weekend in June.</p><p>&ldquo;We decided it was really best to make the weekend all about Best Buddies and not confuse the message,&rsquo;&rsquo; Shriver said.</p><p>The Hyannis Port event typically kicks off on Friday with Brady hosting a celebrity football game at Harvard Stadium. On Saturday, Brady and Shriver host a VIP lunch for donors at the home of President Kennedy&rsquo;s parents at the family&rsquo;s Hyannis Port compound.</p><p>Brady then takes short tandem rides with participating cyclists, helps distribute awards during a lobster and clam bake, and attends a VIP reception at Shriver&rsquo;s home in the Kennedy compound.</p><p>Supporters who commit to raising $50,000 receive a spot on Brady&rsquo;s team at Harvard Stadium, two photos with him, five VIP tickets to the weekend events, and an official football and jersey, each autographed by the five-time Super Bowl champion.</p><p>Best Buddies gets generally high marks from consumer groups, including three of four stars from Charity Navigator, an organization that says it provides prospective donors with unbiased evaluations of nonprofits they may wish to support.</p><p>But several nonprofit monitors said Best Buddies appears to be falling short by not reporting the payments to Brady&rsquo;s foundation on its state and federal tax documents as fund-raising expenses.</p><p>&ldquo;It looks sloppy,&rsquo;&rsquo; said Art Taylor, president of the Better Business Bureau Wise Giving Alliance. &ldquo;The charity might want to clean it up a little bit and say Brady is providing this fund-raising service to us, thus we are compensating him in this way.&rsquo;&rsquo;</p><p>Shriver disagreed, asserting that the Change the World grants are appropriate honorariums because Brady provides Best Buddies much more than fund-raising services.</p><p>&ldquo;He&rsquo;s a global ambassador for the movement,&rsquo;&rsquo; Shriver said.</p><p>Shriver said Brady appears at Best Buddies events across the country. Next year, his duties will include attending the Masters golf tournament for a venture involving Best Buddies and one of its sponsors, Chevron.\n</p><p>Shriver asserted that wealthy public figures such as Brady are sometimes unfairly criticized for the charitable and fund-raising practices.</p><p>He cited members of the Kennedy family who have been chided for raising election funds despite their personal fortunes.</p><p>&ldquo;When you collaborate, good can come of it,&rsquo;&rsquo; Shriver said. &ldquo;At the end of the day, Best Buddies and Tom are helping more people, and it has nothing to do with how much money Tom or I have personally.&rsquo;&rsquo;</p><!----><p></p><i>Bob Hohler can be reached at <a class=\"a\" href=\"mailto:robert.hohler@globe.com\">robert.hohler@globe.com</a>.</i><!-- inside story_update.jpt -->\n</div><!-- end article-text -->\n\t\t\t\t\t<!-- Story tools -->\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<!-- for comments -->\n\t\t\t\t\t<footer class=\"article-footer\" role=\"contentinfo\">\n<script>var uuid = '0cfbc01e-27a4-11e7-b6a3-be7e21d7ddf9';</script>\n<div id=\"comments\">\n\t<div id=\"comments-content\" class=\"ugc-comments comments\">\n\t\t<div class=\"loading-message\" id=\"loading-comments\">\n\t\t\tLoading comments...\n\t\t\t </div>\n\t</div>\n</div>\n<div id=\"newcomments\"></div>\n</footer>\n</div><!-- ends article-body -->\n\t\t\t\t<!-- place for ads and trending -->\n\t\t\t\t<nav id=\"section-nav\" class=\"section-nav trending-nav\" data-sectionname=\"/sports\">\n     <h3>Top 10 Trending Articles</h3>\n      <nav>\n        <ul class=\"bg-tabs-sidebar bg-33-33-33\">\n          <li><a class=\"here js-show-tab\" href=\"#most-viewed\">Most Viewed</a></li>\n          <li><a class=\"js-show-tab\" href=\"#most-commented\">Most Commented</a></li>\n          <li><a class=\"js-show-tab\" href=\"#most-shared\">Most Shared</a></li>\n        </ul>\n      </nav>\n\n      </nav>\n</article>\n\t\t</main>\n\t\t<!-- Page footer -->\n<div class=\"subscription-banner\">\n\t<a class=\"subscription-banner__link\" href=\"http://subscribe.bostonglobe.com/B0565?p1=BGFooter_DigitalSubscription_Banner\">\n\t\t<span class=\"subscription-banner__title\">Real journalists. The organization has become a major source of funding for Brady\u2019s own charitable goals.\n\nThe legend of Tom Brady has grown thanks to charming images of his celebrated appearances for Best Buddies International, a nonprofit dedicated to helping intellectually and developmentally disabled people.\n\nSmiling children, adoring spectators, and a beaming Brady embracing those with disabilities: No other undertaking in his 17 years in Boston has more vividly portrayed theNew England Patriots star as a fabulously wealthy sports figure committed to charity.\n\nAdvertisement\n\nYet Brady\u2019s relationship with Best Buddies has changed in recent years. The organization has become a major source of funding for Brady\u2019s own charitable goals.\n\nSince 2011, while Brady has served as the face of its signature Massachusetts fund-raiser and helped it raise nearly $20 million, Best Buddies has paid $2.75 million to Brady\u2019s own charitable trust and has pledged to grant the organization an additional $500,000 in 2017 \u2014 a total of $3.25 million.\n\nGet Sports Headlines in your inbox: The Globe's most recent sports headlines delivered to your inbox every morning. Sign Up Thank you for signing up! Sign up for more newsletters here\n\nThe payments, made at Brady\u2019s request, support his Boston-based Change the World Foundation Trust, whose giving has focused almost entirely on causes tied to Brady\u2019s personal interests, including his high school alma mater, his children\u2019s private schools, and charities operated by his football friends. Aiding those with disabilities, the focus of Best Buddies, has been a lower priority.\n\nIndependent charity monitors said there appears to be nothing illegal about the arrangement \u2014 charities can and do donate to other charities. But they questioned why someone of Brady\u2019s wealth has taken large sums for his foundation from another charity that has its own priorities and needs. The donations from Best Buddies have been by far the Brady organization\u2019s largest source of funds.\n\nBrady \u201creally can\u2019t take credit for being a great philanthropist when he is using other people\u2019s money to help his own organizations,\u2019\u2019 said Daniel Borochoff, president of Charity Watch, a Chicago-based nonprofit that evaluates philanthropies and advocates for consumers. \u201cIt\u2019s certainly not pure altruism.\u2019\u2019\n\nAdvertisement\n\nCharitable giving experts also expressed concern that Best Buddies and Brady have given the public and other Best Buddies donors and participants only limited information about the financial arrangement, and they wondered if Best Buddies should properly report the annual payments as fund-raising expenses rather than charitable gifts.\n\nThe arrangement was forged by Brady and his friend, Anthony K. Shriver, the founder and chairman of Best Buddies, a public charity that solicits money nationwide.\n\nJay Connor Tom Brady (left) and Anthony K. Shriver (right), with Chris Harrington, at a Best Buddies event in Boston.\n\nShriver said in an interview that Brady had volunteered his time and image to Best Buddies for many years before he told Shriver he was becoming very busy and wanted to focus on his own charitable foundation.\n\nShriver quoted Brady as saying, some time before the payments began in 2011: \u201cWhen I come to Best Buddies events, it\u2019s all about Best Buddies, which is the way I want to keep it while I develop my interests. Would you support helping me pursue my things while I continue to put a lot of energy into your thing?\u2019\u2019\n\nShriver said they agreed Best Buddies would grant Brady\u2019s charitable trust $250,000 in 2011. Every year since, they have decided the figure would be $500,000, and Shriver said he expects Best Buddies to continue giving $500,000 annually to Brady\u2019s trust. They have no contract.\n\n\u201cI think it has been a smart move, because we have been able to provide services to tens of thousands of people because of Tom, while keeping him engaged and helping him pursue some of his own interests,\u2019\u2019 Shriver said. \u201cIt has been super beneficial to us and him.\u2019\u2019\n\nShriver said Brady was not available to comment. Shortly after inquiries from the Globe earlier this month, Best Buddies issued a news release crediting Brady with helping to raise $46.5 million since he began supporting the organization as a volunteer in 2001.\n\nThe release quoted Brady as describing his affiliation with Best Buddies as \u201cone of my greatest honors.\u2019\u2019\n\n\u201cThis organization has become a part of my life,\u2019\u2019 Brady said in the release. \u201cI love meeting the inspiring buddies at events, seeing their smiles, and hearing them talk about how Best Buddies has changed their lives for the better by helping them make friends at school and find meaningful jobs in their local communities. Best Buddies\u2019 mission is a simple one, but the impact is incredibly powerful. I am so grateful to be a part of this global movement.\u201d\n\nShriver, a nephew of President John F. Kennedy, founded Best Buddies in 1989 to advance a cause that his mother, Eunice Kennedy Shriver, the slain president\u2019s sister, began to champion in the 1960s.\n\nMany luminaries have supported Best Buddies through the years, among them current \u201ccelebrity ambassadors\u2019\u2019 who include Brady, Sugar Ray Leonard, Kyrie Irving, Carl Lewis, Ron Howard, Rob Lowe, Emmitt Smith, and Cindy Crawford.\n\nYet only one celebrity other than Brady has received compensation for his charitable foundation from Best Buddies, Shriver said. The other was Kevin Spacey, whose personal charity received a one-time grant of $200,000 in 2015.\n\nCharity watchdogs said it is not unusual for nonprofits to pay celebrities to help raise money, and they did not dispute that Best Buddies has realized an enviable return on its relationship with Brady. Shriver credited Brady with raising a sizable portion of the $19.7 million that Best Buddies has garnered from its annual Hyannis Port Challenge since the payments to Brady\u2019s trust began in 2011.\n\nBut nonprofit watchdogs said the arrangement could diminish Brady\u2019s charitable image.\n\n\u201cIt\u2019s not all bad, because he is helping an important cause,\u2019\u2019 Borochoff said. \u201cBut he could help Best Buddies to a much greater extent if he didn\u2019t take their money\u2019\u2019 for his own charitable trust.\n\nBrady launched Change the World in 2005, after he signed his first big deal with the Patriots: a six-year, $60 million contract. He has since amassed personal wealth estimated at $180 million, while the net worth of his wife, Gisele Bundchen, is about $360 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth, a website that tracks such things.\n\nBrady, the sole trustee of his private charity, initially funded the nonprofit with a donation of $490,000 in 2005. Since then he has not made a significant financial contribution, according to tax reports through 2015, the most recently available.\n\nInstead, Best Buddies has almost entirely funded Brady\u2019s foundation. Through 2015, nearly 98 percent of Change the World\u2019s total contributions of $2.3 million since Brady\u2019s initial donation were $2.25 million in grants from Best Buddies. Including Brady\u2019s donation, the percentage is about 80%.\n\nAnd where did the money go? From 2011 through to 2015, Change the World gave grants of $55,000 to Best Buddies, after donating a total of $80,000 over the previous three years.\n\nOtherwise, from 2011 through 2015, Change the World distributed nearly $1 million to more than 30 nonprofits. The largest beneficiary was Brady\u2019s alma mater, Junipero Serra High School, in San Mateo, Calif., at $250,000.\n\nThe next largest grants, $100,000 each, went to Santa Monica Catholic Community, where Brady and Gisele Bundchen were married in 2009, and the TB12 Foundation, a charity Brady formed in 2015 to support clients of his Foxborough-based TB12 fitness and nutrition company.\n\nOhio State University received a $50,000 donation in honor of Ed Razek, a former Victoria\u2019s Secret executive who is said to have helped launch Brady\u2019s romance with Bundchen by setting them up in 2006.\n\nOthers getting $50,000 were Brady\u2019s college alma mater, the University of Michigan, for cancer research; as well as Dana-Farber Cancer Center, Mission of Hope Haiti, and the Navy SEAL Foundation.\n\nA charity honoring Tom Martinez, Brady\u2019s longtime personal coach who died in 2012, received $37,800. And $30,000 went to a nursing facility in Minnesota that cared for Brady\u2019s grandfather before he died in 2016.\n\nIn addition, Change the World donated $10,000 to Saint David\u2019s School in New York City and $20,000 to the Apple Orchard School in Brookline, both of which Brady\u2019s children have attended.\n\nNonprofits created by Brady\u2019s former teammates also benefited, with grants of $25,000 going to Vince Wilfork\u2019s charity, $10,000 to Deion Branch\u2019s, and $5,000 to Wes Welker\u2019s.\n\nThe entire arrangement has stirred concerns among some nonprofit watchdogs. Marc Pollick, who for 20 years has advised charity-minded celebrities through his Giving Back Fund, began consulting last year for the NFL Players Association. Because Brady is an NFLPA member, Pollick would not comment directly on the quarterback or Best Buddies.\n\nBut he said he would counsel any celebrity against participating in such an arrangement.\n\n\u201cI would say, \u2018Absolutely not,\u2019 \u201d Pollick said. \u201cIt\u2019s not good use of charitable money, and it\u2019s bad for your reputation.\u2019\u2019\n\nBest Buddies has spent more than $200 million developing a volunteer network aimed at ending the social, physical, and economic isolation often experienced by those living with disabilities. Volunteers help them form friendships with peers, find jobs, and live independently.\n\nShriver said he was not concerned that Brady\u2019s trust has dedicated relatively little of its Best Buddies money to Shriver\u2019s cause. Change the World ended 2015 with net assets of $1.7 million.\n\n\u201cI\u2019m comfortable supporting things that Tom has developed an interest in,\u2019\u2019 Shriver said. \u201cHe has done a lot for us, and I think we get more out of it than Change the World gets from us.\u2019\u2019\n\nNonprofit watchdogs said the public would be better served if their financial relationship were clearly disclosed. There is no mention of the arrangement in Best Buddies promotional material.\n\nBest Buddies did recently describe the payment arrangment with Brady in a press release. Before that, a prospective donor, short of directly asking Best Buddies about the connection, would have needed to visit the charity\u2019s website, download its tax return, and scroll through dozens of pages to find listed a $500,000 grant to the Change the World Foundation.\n\nEven then, there is no mention of Brady. And Googling \u201cChange the World Foundation\u2019\u2019 does not readily reveal a connection to Brady. By further searching Change the World\u2019s state or federal filings, a prospective donor could discover that the nonprofit\u2019s trustee is Thomas E. Brady Jr.\n\nBorochoff said charities are not required to publicize their compensation to celebrities, although failing to do so may not reflect the highest standards of philanthropy.\n\n\u201cSometimes the compensation is hard to find, and a celebrity gets credit for caring about something when the reason he actually cares about it is because he or his foundation is getting paid,\u2019\u2019 Borochoff said.\n\nShriver said he and Brady agreed that fully disclosing the arrangement might prove distracting, especially during the Hyannis Port Challenge. This year\u2019s sold-out event, whose highlight is a 100-mile fund-raising bike ride from Boston to Hyannis Port, is scheduled for the first weekend in June.\n\n\u201cWe decided it was really best to make the weekend all about Best Buddies and not confuse the message,\u2019\u2019 Shriver said.\n\nThe Hyannis Port event typically kicks off on Friday with Brady hosting a celebrity football game at Harvard Stadium. On Saturday, Brady and Shriver host a VIP lunch for donors at the home of President Kennedy\u2019s parents at the family\u2019s Hyannis Port compound.\n\nBrady then takes short tandem rides with participating cyclists, helps distribute awards during a lobster and clam bake, and attends a VIP reception at Shriver\u2019s home in the Kennedy compound.\n\nSupporters who commit to raising $50,000 receive a spot on Brady\u2019s team at Harvard Stadium, two photos with him, five VIP tickets to the weekend events, and an official football and jersey, each autographed by the five-time Super Bowl champion.\n\nBest Buddies gets generally high marks from consumer groups, including three of four stars from Charity Navigator, an organization that says it provides prospective donors with unbiased evaluations of nonprofits they may wish to support.\n\nBut several nonprofit monitors said Best Buddies appears to be falling short by not reporting the payments to Brady\u2019s foundation on its state and federal tax documents as fund-raising expenses.\n\n\u201cIt looks sloppy,\u2019\u2019 said Art Taylor, president of the Better Business Bureau Wise Giving Alliance. \u201cThe charity might want to clean it up a little bit and say Brady is providing this fund-raising service to us, thus we are compensating him in this way.\u2019\u2019\n\nShriver disagreed, asserting that the Change the World grants are appropriate honorariums because Brady provides Best Buddies much more than fund-raising services.\n\n\u201cHe\u2019s a global ambassador for the movement,\u2019\u2019 Shriver said.\n\nShriver said Brady appears at Best Buddies events across the country. Next year, his duties will include attending the Masters golf tournament for a venture involving Best Buddies and one of its sponsors, Chevron.\n\nShriver asserted that wealthy public figures such as Brady are sometimes unfairly criticized for the charitable and fund-raising practices.\n\nHe cited members of the Kennedy family who have been chided for raising election funds despite their personal fortunes.\n\n\u201cWhen you collaborate, good can come of it,\u2019\u2019 Shriver said. \u201cAt the end of the day, Best Buddies and Tom are helping more people, and it has nothing to do with how
