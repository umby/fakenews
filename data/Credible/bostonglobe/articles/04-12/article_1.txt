Sometimes, the split is amicable, but often, as in the case of guitarist<b class=\"b\"> J. Geils </b>and <b class=\"b\">Peter Wolf</b>, frontman for the J. Geils Band, it isn&rsquo;t.</p><p class=\"skip-nav article-more\">\n\t<a>Advertisement</a>\n\t</p>\n\n<div class=\"ad aside\" id=\"ad_bigbox1\"></div>  <a id=\"skip-target1\"></a>\n\n<p>Geils, who was <a class=\"a\" href=\"http://www.bostonglobe.com/metro/2017/04/11/guitarist-geils-found-dead-groton-home/zSjOaYn4BN5LMxNEms2f2J/story.html\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\">found dead Tuesday</a> in his Groton home, had been estranged from Wolf and the rest of the band that bears his name for several years. The 71-year-old Geils hadn&rsquo;t performed alongside Wolf since 2011, when <a class=\"a\" href=\"https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/music/2012/08/10/eve-fall-tour-geils-band-poised-for-bitter-legal-battle-over-who-owns-name/i6LX4L8QvYVybZ3dmCgzIM/story.html\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\">it was discovered </a>that the guitarist had trademarked the name &ldquo;J. Geils Band&rdquo; without telling his bandmates. </p><p>The bold move, intended to give Geils ownership and control of the band&rsquo;s name, touched off a bitter legal battle that was eventually settled, but it abruptly ended his tenure in a group known for its ferocious live shows, whether playing in a sweaty club in the &rsquo;70s or opening for the Rolling Stones in a stadium in the &rsquo;80s.  </p><div class=\"inline-newsletter\" data-exact-target-ID=\"20233021\" data-mailing-list-name=\"The Weekender\" data-is-amp=\"\" data-section=\"/lifestyle/names\">\n  <div Geils found dead in Groton </h4><!-- story-title  -->\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<p class=\"bg-related__description\">Geils, born John Warren Geils, formed the popular Boston-area band in the late 1960s. He was 71.</p>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t</div>\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t</div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t</a>\n\t\t\t\t\t</aside>\n\t<p><a id=\"skip-target-related-box\"></a></p>\n\n<p>The list of bands who&rsquo;ve endured bad break-ups &mdash; so bad members can&rsquo;t be in the same room with one another &mdash; is long and includes the Kinks, Beach Boys, the Eagles, Talking Heads, newly-inducted Rock and Roll Hall of Famers Journey, Van Halen, and Oasis, to name just a few.</p><p>&ldquo;It&rsquo;s true what (<b class=\"b\">George</b>) <b class=\"b\">Bizet</b> said, &lsquo;Ah, music. What a beautiful art. But what a wretched profession,&rsquo; &rdquo; says <b class=\"b\">Stephen Davis</b>, a rock &rsquo;n&rsquo; roll historian who&rsquo;s written books about several famously fractious bands, including Fleetwood Mac, the Band, and Guns N&rsquo; Roses. </p><!--  working ad -->\n<p class=\"skip-nav article-more\">\n\t<a>Advertisement</a>\n\t<!-- <a href=\"#skip-target2\">Continue reading below</a> -->\n</p>\n<!-- adLayerPath: /Boston/Production/BostonGlobe.com/WebPages/Homepage/homepage.ad-layer.story-asset.dwp -->\n<div class=\"ad aside\" id=\"ad_bigbox2\"></div>  <a id=\"skip-target2\"></a>\n\n<p>&ldquo;It&rsquo;s a real problem for musicians,&rdquo; says Davis, who lives in Milton. &ldquo;They&rsquo;re condemned to repetition, doing the same thing with the same people night after night.&rdquo;</p><p>The J. Geils Band, which Geils formed (without Wolf) in the late &rsquo;60s, enjoyed its greatest success with the release of 1981&rsquo;s &ldquo;Freeze-Frame,&rdquo; an album that yielded two enormous hits, &ldquo;<a class=\"a\" href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BqDjMZKf-wg\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\">Centerfold</a>&rdquo; and the <a class=\"a\" href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wHo43B6nu60\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\">title track</a>. But even during good times, there was tension. The band was featured on the cover of Rolling Stone<a class=\"a\" href=\"http://i.ebayimg.com/images/g/ksoAAOSw65FXrz~G/s-l300.jpg\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\"> in 1973</a> and again<a class=\"a\" href=\"https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/236x/93/97/20/9397207a8a11b69a7e016248179be149.jpg\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\"> in 1982</a>, and on both occasions only Wolf was pictured, which could not have pleased the band&rsquo;s other principal songwriter, keyboardist<b class=\"b\"> Seth Justman</b>, not to mention Geils, harmonica player <b class=\"b\">Magic Dick</b>, bassist <b class=\"b\">Danny Klein</b>, or drummer <b class=\"b\">Stephen Bladd</b>. </p><p>&ldquo;Between, say, 1969 and 1973, J. Geils had been like Boston&rsquo;s house band,&rdquo; says Davis, who was a student at Boston University at the time. &ldquo;They were the only band in Boston that was any good. Wolf was great and no one could touch the band. They were like the American Rolling Stones.&rdquo;</p><p>Their eventual break-up, in 1985, is generally attributed to disagreements between Wolf and Justman, but Geils, in a 2012 lawsuit against his bandmates, revealed that he also had been unhappy. In the suit, Geils claimed he&rsquo;d signed, &ldquo;under tremendous duress and pressure,&rdquo; a document in 1982 that stipulated that if he ever stopped performing with the band, he couldn&rsquo;t use the name &ldquo;J. Geils&rdquo; or &ldquo;Geils&rdquo; in connection with any commercial venture. (He could, though, play as &ldquo;John Geils&rdquo; or &ldquo;John Geils Jr.&rdquo;) </p><p>To the delight of their fans, the J. Geils Band reunited periodically over the years, including concerts in 1999, 2005, 2009, and a <a class=\"a\" href=\"http://archive.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2010/08/15/boston_rock_twin_bill_thrills_fenway_crowd/\" shape=\"rect\" target=\"_blank\">memorable performance</a> in 2010 at Fenway Park, co-headlining with Aerosmith. But it was clear by then that Geils, whose musical tastes ran more toward jazz and blues, no longer wanted to match Wolf&rsquo;s manic, high-energy style on stage. (For the concert at Fenway, Geils asked to sit on a stool, a request vetoed by the band.) That, combined with bitterness on both sides over use of the band name, led to Geils&rsquo;s departure. </p><p>When the J. Geils Band hit the road in 2012, its namesake had been replaced on guitar by <b class=\"b\">Duke Levine</b>, and Geils never played with the band again. </p><p>Here then is a clip of the J. Geils Band, in peak form, performing at Holy Cross in 1972.</p><p><iframe width=\"100%\" height=\"450\" src=\"https://www.youtube.com/embed/Mq79uJ58eAM\" frameborder=\"0\" allowfullscreen></iframe></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><p></p><!-- inside story_update.jpt -->\n</div><!-- end article-text -->\n\t\t\t\t\t<!-- Story tools -->\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<!-- for comments -->\n\t\t\t\t\t<footer class=\"article-footer\" role=\"contentinfo\">\n<script>var uuid = 'b73e6c3a-1fba-11e7-9184-561cfe15bb0b';</script>\n<div id=\"comments\">\n\t<div id=\"comments-content\" class=\"ugc-comments comments\">\n\t\t<div class=\"loading-message\" id=\"loading-comments\">\n\t\t\tLoading comments...\n\t\t\t </div>\n\t</div>\n</div>\n<div id=\"newcomments\"></div>\n</footer>\n</div><!-- ends article-body -->\n\t\t\t\t<!-- place for ads and trending -->\n\t\t\t\t<nav id=\"section-nav\" class=\"section-nav trending-nav\" data-sectionname=\"/lifestyle/names\">\n     <h3>Top 10 Trending Articles</h3>\n      <nav>\n        <ul class=\"bg-tabs-sidebar bg-33-33-33\">\n          <li><a class=\"here js-show-tab\" href=\"#most-viewed\">Most Viewed</a></li>\n          <li><a class=\"js-show-tab\" href=\"#most-commented\">Most Commented</a></li>\n          <li><a class=\"js-show-tab\" href=\"#most-shared\">Most Shared</a></li>\n        </ul>\n      </nav>\n\n      </nav>\n</article>\n\t\t</main>\n\t\t<!-- Page footer -->\n<div class=\"subscription-banner\">\n\t<a class=\"subscription-banner__link\" href=\"http://subscribe.bostonglobe.com/B0565?p1=BGFooter_DigitalSubscription_Banner\">\n\t\t<span class=\"subscription-banner__title\">Real journalists.
