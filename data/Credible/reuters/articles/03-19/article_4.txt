REUTERS/Yuri Gripas\n\nWASHINGTON U.S. lawmakers from both parties said on Sunday they had seen no proof to support the claim by Republican President Donald Trump that his predecessor Barack Obama had wiretapped him last year, adding pressure on Trump to explain or back off his repeated assertion.\n\nSeveral Republicans last week urged Trump to apologize for the allegations he made in a series of tweets on March 4. The maelstrom also caused tension with key U.S. allies and threatens to distract Republicans from campaign promises on health care and taxes.\n\n\"I don't know the basis for President Trump's assertion,\" U.S. Senator Susan Collins, a Republican, said on NBC's \"Meet the Press.\" \"I do believe he owes us that explanation.\"\n\nCollins said she supported Trump as president, but she wouldn't side with him if he \"misstated what the facts are.\"\n\nFBI Director James Comey is expected to be asked about Trump's claims when he testifies at a rare public hearing on Monday about alleged Russian meddling in the 2016 presidential election. Russia has denied the assertion it was involved in hacked emails and other attempts to influence the race.\n\nRepresentative Adam Schiff, the top Democrat on the House Intelligence Committee holding the hearing, called Trump's claims \"patently false\" and said he expected Comey to say as much on Monday.\n\nThe Justice Department on Friday delivered documents to congressional committees to help clear up whether the Obama administration spied on Trump.\n\nRepublican Representative Devin Nunes, who leads the House intelligence panel, said after receiving the material, he saw no evidence of wiretapping.\n\nBut Nunes, who served on Trump's transition team, joined the White House in seeking to shift attention away from the controversies by calling for investigations of leaks to the news media.\n\nNunes said on \"Fox News Sunday\" that leaks to reporters about former Trump national security adviser Michael Flynn were criminal and that his panel was probing whether other names were leaked.\n\nTrump has been dogged by allegations that his associates had ties to Russian officials. He fired Flynn last month after reports he had discussed sanctions with Russia's ambassador before Trump took office, without telling other White House officials.\n\n\"The one crime we know that's been committed is that one: the leaking of someone's name,\" Nunes said. \"Were there any other names that were ... leaked out?\"\n\nNunes also said ahead of Monday's hearing he had seen \"no evidence\" of collusion between Russia and Trump's team. But Schiff, the panel's top Democrat, said there was enough \"circumstantial evidence\" that he still had questions.\n\nNOT BACKING DOWN\n\nMeanwhile, the White House has not backed down on Trump's surveillance claims.\n\nThe administration was forced to reassure key ally Britain after White House press secretary Sean Spicer repeated a Fox News analyst's claim that a British intelligence agency helped Obama wiretap Trump. The British government strongly denied it.\n\nThe issue led to an awkward moment on Friday at a joint press conference with visiting German Chancellor Angela Merkel when Trump was asked about the wiretap claims by a German reporter.\n\nTrump said he and Merkel had \"something in common,\" apparently referring to reports during the Obama administration that Merkel's phone was bugged. The quip left the German leader looking bewildered.\n\nSenior Republican Representative Tom Cole told reporters on Friday that Trump owed Obama an apology. Representatives Charlie Dent and Will Hurd, also Republicans, made similar comments.\n\n\"I see no indication that that's true,\" Cole said of the wiretapping charge.\n\nUnless Trump produces convincing proof, Cole added, \"President Obama is owed an apology.\"\n\n(Reporting By Emily Stephenson and Caren Bohan; Editing by Phil Berlowitz and Jeffrey Benkoe)", "images": ["http://s4.reutersmedia.net/resources_v2/images/r-generic-hdr.png", "http://s4.reutersmedia.net/resources/r/?m=02&d=20170319&t=2&i=1177171313&w=&fh=545px&fw=&ll=&pl=&sq=&r=LYNXMPED2I0HZ"], "videos": []}
