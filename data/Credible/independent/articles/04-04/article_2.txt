<p>A campaign to persuade the Government to abandon its target to reduce net migration below 100,000 a year is launched today by <em>The Independent</em> and the Open Britain group.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cDrop the Target\u201d has already won the backing of MPs across the political spectrum who believe the goal will never be achieved \u2013\u00a0even after Britain leaves the EU \u2013 and that doing so would damage the UK economy and leave the vulnerable worse off. Latest figures show net migration running at 273,000 a year.\u00a0</p>\n\n<p>The promise to cut migration to \u201ctens of thousands\u201d was made by David Cameron at the 2010 general election and repeated in the 2015 Conservative manifesto. Although Theresa May described it as an \u201cambition\u201d rather than a firm target when she was Home Secretary, she has stuck to it since becoming Prime Minister.</p>\n\n<div class=\"inline-block inline-block-related-single inline-block-right\" data-nid=\"/voices/campaigns/dropthetarget/drop-the-target-petition-sign-immigration-net-migration-cap-theresa-may-eu-open-borders-a7653906.html\"><a href=\"/voices/campaigns/dropthetarget/drop-the-target-petition-sign-immigration-net-migration-cap-theresa-may-eu-open-borders-a7653906.html\"><img src=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/s3/files/styles/readmore_card/public/thumbnails/image/2017/03/31/18/untitled-4.jpg\" /><div class=\"capsules\">\n<ul><li>\n\t<div class=\"label context-capsule\">Read more</div>\n\t</li>\n</ul></div>\n\n<div class=\"content\">\n<h2>Drop the Target: sign our petition on the immigration cap now</h2>\n</div>\n</a></div>\n\n<p><a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/voices/letters/drop-the-target-brexit-immigration-reduction-questions-to-answer-a7664221.html\">In an open letter</a>, MPs warn that the target is \u201ceconomically damaging\u201d because the UK needs migrant labour and could be \u201csocially divisive\u201d because it is based on the false premise that migrants are a \u201cnegative\u201d for the country.</p>\n\n<p>Open Britain, the successor to the Remain campaign in last year\u2019s referendum which now urges a soft Brexit, and <em>The Independent</em>,<a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/voices/campaigns/dropthetarget/drop-the-target-petition-sign-immigration-net-migration-cap-theresa-may-eu-open-borders-a7653906.html\"> are launching a petition</a>\u00a0to rally public support for ditching the target and to highlight the positive contribution made by immigrants, often vilified in populist politics. The campaign has been endorsed by the Royal College of Midwives, the Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development (CIPD) and the Migration Matters Trust.\u00a0</p>\n\n<p>In the letter, three former ministers warn the Government that the target is at odds with recent statements by Cabinet ministers suggesting that workers from EU countries will still be needed after Brexit in sectors such as hospitality, financial services, farming, and construction.</p>\n\n<div class=\"relatedlinkslist inline-block-related-list inline-block-left\">\n<h2 class=\"box-title\">Read more</h2>\n\n<div class=\"relatedlinkslist-content\" style=\"min-height: 10px;\">\n<ul class=\"related\"><li><a data-author=\"Editorial...\" data-nid=\"7664206\" data-publish_date=\"03-04-2017\" href=\"/voices/editorials/the-independent-joins-open-britain-in-launching-a-cross-party-campaign-to-drop-the-migration-target-a7664206.html\">The Independent announces its new campaign</a></li>\n\t<li><a data-author=\"Anna Soubry...\" data-nid=\"7664146\" data-publish_date=\"03-04-2017\" href=\"/voices/drop-the-target-brexit-immigration-reduction-whats-best-for-the-uk-a7664146.html\">We need an immigration policy based on what's best for the UK</a></li>\n\t<li><a data-author=\"Cathy Warwick...\" data-nid=\"7664231\" data-publish_date=\"03-04-2017\" href=\"/voices/if-we-keep-the-target-on-migration-we-contribute-to-the-demise-of-the-nhs-a7664231.html\">If we keep the target on migration, we contribute to the NHS's demise</a></li>\n\t<li><a data-author=\"Anna Soubry...\" data-nid=\"7664146\" data-publish_date=\"03-04-2017\" href=\"/voices/drop-the-target-brexit-immigration-reduction-whats-best-for-the-uk-a7664146.html\">We need an immigration policy based on what's best for the UK</a></li>\n</ul></div>\n</div>\n\n<p>There are also fears that frontline workers in social care and the NHS \u2013 looking after some of the most vulnerable members of our society \u2013 will be hit by the cap.</p>\n\n<p>Conservative MP Anna Soubry, Labour\u2019s Pat McFadden and Norman Lamb from the Liberal Democrats argue that \u201cEU workers are indispensable to the UK workforce\u201d and that it would be \u201cdifficult and damaging\u201d to make huge reductions to EU migration after Brexit. They warn that such cuts would fall on migrants in non-protected areas such as manufacturing, energy, science, communications, education and defence. \u201cSuch a reduction\u2026would deny businesses the skills and they need and exposes the target as unachievable,\u201d they say.</p>\n\n<p>Data from the Office for National Statistics shows that 1.3 million EU nationals are employed in these non-protected sectors, compared to 699,000 in the areas ministers suggest would be protected.</p>\n\n<p>The three MPs argue that the Government\u2019s goal is incompatible with its recent White Paper on Brexit, which said the country must recognise the valuable contribution of migrants to society and welcome \u201cthose with the skills and expertise to make our nation better still\u201d.</p>\n\n<div class=\"inline-block inline-block-related-single inline-block-left\" data-nid=\"/voices/letters/drop-the-target-brexit-immigration-reduction-questions-to-answer-a7664221.html\"><a href=\"/voices/letters/drop-the-target-brexit-immigration-reduction-questions-to-answer-a7664221.html\"><img src=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/s3/files/styles/readmore_card/public/thumbnails/image/2010/06/27/00/401970.bin\" /><div class=\"capsules\">\n<ul><li>\n\t<div class=\"label context-capsule\">Read more</div>\n\t</li>\n</ul></div>\n\n<div class=\"content\">\n<h2>The Government has questions to answer about its immigration target</h2>\n</div>\n</a></div>\n\n<p>Challenging ministers to justify the target, the three MPs pose 10 key questions \u2013 including whether the Government will aim for about 50,000 EU and 50,000 non-EU migrants in order to achieve its goal and asking what estimates of skills shortages in specific sectors have been made.</p>\n\n<p>The Government is under pressure to allow the NHS and social care, which employ 140,000 EU workers, to continue to be able to recruit them after Brexit amid signs that EU nationals are leaving the NHS. EU workers make up nearly a quarter of the three million jobs in restaurants, hotels and tourism. The British Hospitality Association has warned of a shortfall of 60,000 workers a year and that it could take 10 years to train enough British workers.</p>\n\n<p>Ms May is expected to retain the target when the Government draws up new rules to replace free movement for EU citizens. Her allies say she regards it as \u201cunfinished business\u201d from her six years at the Home Office. They say dropping the goal would ignore a key lesson from the EU referendum \u2013 that the public wants the Government to regain control of migration. However, some ministers admit privately that the target is highly unlikely to be achieved and is being kept mainly for symbolic reasons. Several Cabinet ministers are urging Mrs May to exclude foreign students from the target, which would make it easier to hit. But she believes the move would be seen as \u201cfiddling the figures\u201d.</p>\n\n<p>Responding to the campaign\u2019s launch, the Home Office said: \u201cWe want to see net migration fall to sustainable levels \u2013 the tens of thousands. The UK needs a fair and controlled immigration policy and that is exactly what this Government will deliver.\u201d</p>\n\n<p>But Ben Wilmott, head of public policy at the CIPD, warned that the idea of an optimal level of net migration is a \u201ctotal myth\u201d.
