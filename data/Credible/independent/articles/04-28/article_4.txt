class=\"main-content-column\">\n      <div class=\"text-wrapper\" itemprop=\"articleBody\">\n        <p>Mental health professionals fear reprisals if they say publicly that Donald Trump may be psychologically unwell, according to a therapist who believes \u201chalf of America\u201d is suffering panic attacks because of the President\u2019s actions.</p>\n\n<p>Psychiatrists are afraid they will be sued or reported to professional regulators if they say Mr Trump is suffering a mental illness, Dr John Gartner told <em>The Independent</em>, adding he had received \u201ca lot of very crude hate mail\u201d after speaking out.</p>\n\n<p>Last week he and a few others sidestepped the longstanding Goldwater Rule\u2014designed to discourage therapists from attempting to diagnose public figures without having examined them\u2014by calling for a psychological evaluation of the 45<sup>th</sup> President over what they said were \u201cimpulsive\u201d acts, like bombing a Syrian airfield after a suspected chemical weapons attack by the Assad government.</p>\n\n<div class=\"inline-block inline-block-related-single inline-block-left\" class=\"label context-capsule\">Read more</div>\n\t</li>\n</ul></div>\n\n<div class=\"content\">\n<h2>Being President not as easy as I thought, Trump admits</h2>\n</div>\n</a></div>\n\n<p>They were Complaints could be made against their licence. There's a fear of it. Losing your licence is the worst thing that could happen to you. It's enough to make many back off.\u201d</p>\n\n<p>The Baltimore, Maryland, practitioner said he believed the American Psychiatric Association (APA), which backs the rule in part because it thinks breaking it could damage patients\u2019 confidence in therapists, would be on the wrong side of history as he compared Mr Trump\u2019s behaviour to that of Adolf Hitler.</p>\n\n<p>He said: \u201cI don\u2019t think it would damage [patients\u2019] confidence in their doctors. When Hitler was rising no German psychiatrists spoke out against him, not one.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cWe don\u2019t look upon that now as having been the height of professional ethics. This is the rise of a dangerously mentally ill President. Someone who has a malignant narcissism that makes him a threat.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cI think history will judge the position of the APA very harshly. I\u2019m not saying he\u2019s as bad as Hitler and Stalin, what I\u2019m saying is he\u2019s a member of the same diagnostic group.\u201d</p>\n\n<p>At a panel organised by Yale University lecturer Dr Bandy Lee last week, Dr Gartner employed vivid imagery to illustrate his fears. He said: \u201cI keep being haunted by a fantasy\u2014my adult grandchildren and I are huddled around a garbage can fire, wearing ragged gloves without fingers, slapping our hands together over the flames trying to keep warm.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cAlong with other survivors of World War Three, we\u2019re living in a refuge camp in Idaho, one of the last habitable places in America, so remote it was never targeted.\u201d</p>\n\n<p>The APA said last year, in a blog post specifically addressing the presidential election, that speaking in this way was \u201cpotentially stigmatising\u201d for people with mental health problems.</p>\n\n<p>And JR Romano, the Connecticut Republican party chairman, accused the Yale panellists of \u201cthrowing ethical standards out the window because they cannot accept the election results\u201d.</p>\n\n<p>In February another psychiatrist, Dr Allen Frances, spoke out against \u201cpsychiatric name-calling\u201d which he said was \u201ca misguided way of countering Mr Trump\u2019s attack on democracy\u201d.</p>\n\n<p>Dr Frances, of Duke University Medical College, headed a taskforce that literally wrote the book on diagnosing mental illness in 1994.
