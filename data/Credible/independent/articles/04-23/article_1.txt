far-right Front National (FN) had secured\u00a021.6 per cent of the vote. She will face centrist\u00a0<a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/europe/french-election-latest-emmanuel-macron-who-is-france-en-marche-marine-le-pen-a7697951.html\">Emmanuel Macron</a>, the\u00a0former Socialist minister who set up his own party to run for president.\u00a0He won the first round with about\u00a023.8 per cent.</p>\n\n<p>Mr Macron\u00a0is now the clear favourite to triumph in the second,\u00a0and final,\u00a0round in two weeks, according to polls.</p>\n\n<div class=\"inline-block inline-block-related-single inline-block-left\" data-nid=\"/news/world/europe/marine-le-pen-national-front-french-election-who-is-a7698051.html\"><a href=\"/news/world/europe/marine-le-pen-national-front-french-election-who-is-a7698051.html\"><img src=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/s3/files/styles/readmore_card/public/thumbnails/image/2017/04/23/16/marine-lepen.jpg\" /><div class=\"capsules\">\n<ul><li>\n\t<div class=\"label context-capsule\">Read more</div>\n\t</li>\n</ul></div>\n\n<div class=\"content\">\n<h2>Who is Marine Le Pen and what happens if the Front National wins?</h2>\n</div>\n</a></div>\n\n<p>The vote\u00a0marks the end of a political era with the two major\u00a0parties, the Socialists\u00a0and\u00a0centre-right Les Republicains,\u00a0which have traditionally been the pillars of France's political arena, both being eliminated in the first round.</p>\n\n<p>Conservative Francois Fillon\u00a0was on just under 20 per cent of the vote, marginally ahead of\u00a0far-left candidate Jean-Luc Melenchon\u00a0on 19.5 per cent. Palace had\u00a0been overcome.\u00a0</p>\n\n<p>She hailed the results as \u201chistoric\u201d and warned \u201cthe survival of French civilisation\u201d was now at stake.\u00a0</p>\n\n<p>European
