<div class=\"text-wrapper\" itemprop=\"articleBody\">\n        <p>Mourners in Republican West Belfast have gathered for a candle lit vigil in memory of <a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/home-news/martin-mcguinness-dead-former-northern-ireland-deputy-first-minister-dies-aged-66-a7640661.html\">Martin McGuinness</a>.</p>\n\n<p>At least a thousand people gathered in the Andersonstown area of the city to pay tribute to the politician, bringing West Belfast to a standstill as mourners overflowed into nearby streets and parks.</p>\n\n<p>The former IRA commander, who renounced violence and became one of the leading figures in the Northern Irish peace process, died in the early hours of Tuesday morning aged 66. The Sinn Fein politician had been suffering from a genetic condition for several months and had resigned from front line politics in January after appearing to be increasingly frail.</p>\n\n<div class=\"inline-block inline-block-related-single inline-block-left\" data-nid=\"/news/obituaries/martin-mcguinness-obituary-death-ira-sinn-fein-life-career-a7619391.html\"><a href=\"/news/obituaries/martin-mcguinness-obituary-death-ira-sinn-fein-life-career-a7619391.html\"><img src=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/s3/files/styles/readmore_card/public/thumbnails/image/2017/03/21/11/martin-mcguinnes-life-17.jpg\" /><div class=\"capsules\">\n<ul><li>\n\t<div class=\"label context-capsule\">Read more</div>\n\t</li>\n</ul></div>\n\n<div class=\"content\">\n<h2>Martin McGuinness: One of the most dangerous enemies the UK ever had</h2>\n</div>\n</a></div>\n\n<p>West Belfast has long been known as the most Republican part of the city and is a stronghold of support for Sinn Fein.</p>\n\n<p>The vigil's location perhaps embodies the journey Northern Ireland has undergone through the peace process. The site was once where security forces spent much of the Troubles stationed as they sought intelligence on local Republicans.</p>\n\n<p>Since peace came to Northern Ireland, the barracks has been demolished and now stands as a grassy park for local children to play, a fitting venue which in many ways mirrors the transition from conflict to peace.</p>\n\n<p>As mourners gathered in swathes, the park soon over flowed and spilt out into nearby roads, blocking off this corner of the city to traffic while the vigil was underway.</p>\n\n<div class=\"dnd-widget-wrapper context-sdl_editor_representation type-image\"><div class=\"dnd-atom-rendered\"><!-- scald=5329866:sdl_editor_representation --><div class=\"image\"><img src=\"https://static.independent.co.uk/s3fs-public/styles/story_medium/public/thumbnails/image/2017/03/21/20/martin-mcguinness.jpg\" width=\"564\" height=\"423\" alt=\"martin-mcguinness.jpg\" title=\"martin-mcguinness.jpg\" /></div><!-- END scald=5329866 --></div><div class=\"dnd-caption-wrapper\">A woman touches the coffin of Martin <span class=\"scayt-misspell-word\" data-scayt-lang=\"en_GB\" data-scayt-word=\"McGuinness\">McGuinness</span> as it is carried through the streets of <span class=\"scayt-misspell-word\" data-scayt-lang=\"en_GB\" data-scayt-word=\"Derry\">Derry</span>, Northern Ireland (Reuters)</div></div>\n\n<p>The park was ablaze with both the lit candles of some mourners, while the bright mobile phone screens of younger crowd members added to the glow.</p>\n\n<p>A local Sinn Fein organiser addressed the crowd, celebrating Mr McGuinness' legacy as a Republican icon. She said: \"Martin was a dear friend and a dear comrade to the Republican struggle, but also to the community right across Belfast and across Ireland. His loss will be especially felt in the Republican family.\"</p>\n\n<p>During the vigil another Sinn Fein activist lead the crowd in singing a number of Irish Rebel songs including a rendition of 'The Bold Fenian Men' in the Irish language, as well as 'I wish I was in Derry' in tribute to Mr McGuinness' home city.</p>\n\n<p>A local priest also led mourners in a decade of the rosary, in both the Irish and English languages.</p>\n\n<p>Mr McGuinness was a controversial figure throughout his life. He has openly admitted to joining the terrorist group the IRA, while he was living in Derry during the conflict.</p>\n\n<p>He has since renounced violence and has been praised for entering
