\u00a3200m a year hit to charities unless the Government steps in to plug the gap, a parliamentary committee warns today.</p>\n\n<p>Small charities will be punished hardest by the loss of vital grants, mostly from the European Social Fund (ESF), peers suggest.</p>\n\n<p>Their report highlights fears that medical research charities \u2013 for conditions including cancer, heart disease, bone disease and obesity \u2013 will lose \u201cconsiderable research funding and opportunities for collaboration\u201d.</p>\n\n<div class=\"inline-block inline-block-related-single inline-block-left\" ministers to publish a full assessment of the likely financial blow by the end of this year.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cThis should include the impact of loss of funding as well as on research collaboration,\u201d the House of Lords Committee on Charities said.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cThe anticipated loss of funding when the UK leaves the European Union will impact on smaller charities the most, yet it is these that are already under the most significant funding pressures,\u201d it added.</p>\n\n<p>However, the Government has given no guarantee that grants from the ESF and other EU investment programmes will be picked up by the British taxpayer after 2020.</p>\n\n<p>Even before then, only schemes which ministers believe offer proper value for\u00a0money will continue to receive funding after Brexit, the Treasury said.</p>\n\n<p>In evidence to the committee, the Brain Tumour Charity told of its \u201cconcern that the UK Government would not be able to guarantee the level of funding currently leveraged from the EU\u201d.</p>\n\n<p>The Royal Mencap Society described the ESF as a \u201cmajor source of revenue\u201d\u00a0to help raise living standards and provide job opportunities for young people and the long-term unemployed.</p>\n\n<p>And the British Heart Foundation pointed out that the UK received the second highest sum of all EU states from Horizon 2020, the research and innovation programme.</p>\n\n<p>When questioned by the peers, Rob Wilson, the civil society minister, gave no guarantee to charities but insisted he was \u201clistening to their concerns\u201d.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cWe recognise that charities will be affected by exiting the EU and there are a broad range of implications,\u201d he admitted. Projects showing \u201cstrong value for money\u201d would still be funded.</p>\n\n<p>Elsewhere, the committee also said it had \u201cgrave concerns\u201d about the Charity Commission\u2019s proposal to charge charities an annual registration fee.</p>\n\n<p>And it criticised the Government for causing \u201cunnecessary concern\u201d with an \u201canti-advocacy\u201d clause in 2014 laws which \u201cthreatened the vital advocacy role of charities\u201d.</p>\n\n<div class=\"relatedlinkslist inline-block-related-list inline-block-left\">\n<h2 class=\"box-title\">Read more</h2>\n\n<div class=\"relatedlinkslist-content\" style=\"min-height: 10px;\">\n<ul class=\"related\"><li><a data-author=\"Niamh McIntyre...\" data-nid=\"7649381\" data-publish_date=\"25-03-2017\" href=\"/news/business/news/cadbury-brexit-chocolate-smaller-eu-latest-a7649381.html\">Cadbury warns chocolate bars could get smaller after Brexit</a></li>\n\t<li><a data-author=\"Jon Stone...\" data-nid=\"7648881\" data-publish_date=\"24-03-2017\" href=\"/news/uk/politics/march-for-europe-brexit-protest-theresa-may-speakers-time-tim-farron-a7648881.html\">Theresa May to be accused of 'dividing the country' at Brexit protest</a></li>\n\t<li><a data-author=\"Andrew Woodcock...\" data-nid=\"7647316\" data-publish_date=\"24-03-2017\" href=\"/news/uk/politics/brexit-latest-news-jean-claude-juncker-failure-tragedy-eu-european-commission-president-a7647316.html\">Brexit \u2018a failure and a tragedy\u2019, warns Jean-Claude Juncker</a></li>\n\t<li><a data-author=\"Ben Chu...\" data-nid=\"7646471\" data-publish_date=\"23-03-2017\" href=\"/news/business/news/brexit-anti-corruption-oecd-a7646471.html\">Brexit threat to anti-corruption fight warns OECD</a></li>\n</ul></div>\n</div>\n\n<p>Baroness Pitkeathley, the committee\u2019s chairwoman, said: \u201cCharities are the lifeblood of society.
