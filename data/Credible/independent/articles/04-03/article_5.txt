\n  \n  <div class=\"article-wrapper clearfix\">\n    <div class=\"main-content-column\">\n      <div class=\"text-wrapper\" itemprop=\"articleBody\">\n        <p>British people who oppose further immigration to the UK are less happy than those who welcome it,<span style=\"line-height: 20.8px;\">\u00a0</span><span style=\"line-height: 20.8px;\">and politicians are part of the reason for this,</span><span style=\"line-height: 1.6;\">\u00a0research shows.</span></p>\n\n<p>Those who say they would allow \u201cmany\u201d immigrants to enter are around eight\u00a0per cent happier than those who want none to enter, according to a study, prompting researchers to conclude that anti-immigrant discourse in politics is \u201ccontributing to undermining the subjective well-being of the natives themselves\u201d.</p>\n\n<p><span style=\"line-height: 20.8px;\">Dr David\u00a0</span>Bartram<span style=\"line-height: 20.8px;\">, lecturer at the University of Leicester who carried out the research, drew on a</span>n analysis of data from the European Social Survey on 5,995 people in the UK to correlate their opinion on immigration with how happy they described themselves on a scale of 0-10.</p>\n\n<div class=\"relatedlinkslist inline-block-related-list inline-block-left\">\n<h2 class=\"box-title\">Read more</h2>\n\n<div class=\"relatedlinkslist-content\" style=\"min-height: 10px;\">\n<ul class=\"related\"><li><a data-author=\"Anna Soubry...\" data-nid=\"7664146\" data-publish_date=\"03-04-2017\" href=\"/voices/drop-the-target-brexit-immigration-reduction-whats-best-for-the-uk-a7664146.html\">We need an immigration policy based on what's best for the UK</a></li>\n\t<li><a data-author=\"Empty\" data-nid=\"7655511\" data-publish_date=\"29-03-2017\" href=\"/indy100/article/immigration-map-jakub-marian-europe-un-population-data-7655511\">These maps will change how you think about per cent difference, the findings showed.</p>\n\n<p>The survey found that six per cent would allow \u201cmany\u201d immigrants to enter the UK, 34 per cent would allow \u201csome\u201d, 35 per cent would allow \u201ca few\u201d and 25 per cent would allow \u201cnone\u201d.\u00a0</p>\n\n<p>The effect was strongest among those who were out of work because of sickness or disability, and those who had been unemployed in the past for three months or more. In these groups, those who would allow many immigrants to enter scored 7.07, and those who wanted none to enter scored 6.19.</p>\n\n<p>When people considered immigration from people of the same ethnic group, these percentages changed, with 10 per cent saying they would allow many immigrants to enter the UK, while 48 per cent would allow some, 30 per cent would allow a few and 12 per cent would allow none.</p>\n\n<p>Speaking at the British Sociological Association\u2019s annual conference in Manchester, Dr Bartram said: \u201cFor the most part, immigration is not a threat to the employment or wages of natives. Economic research on that topic finds that for the economy as a whole, immigration enhances the economic situation of natives \u2013 it expands job opportunities and doesn\u2019t undermine wages. \u00a0</p>\n\n<p>\u201cInstead it\u2019s the beliefs themselves that people have about immigrants, the way people think about immigrants \u2013 they\u2019re not \u2018part of us\u2019 \u2013 that makes them unhappy about immigrants, and indeed perhaps less happy in general.\u00a0</p>\n\n<div class=\"inline-block inline-block-related-single inline-block-left\" data-nid=\"/voices/drop-the-target-brexit-immigration-reduction-whats-best-for-the-uk-a7664146.html\"><a href=\"/voices/drop-the-target-brexit-immigration-reduction-whats-best-for-the-uk-a7664146.html\"><img src=\"https://static.independent.co.uk/s3fs-public/styles/readmore_card/public/thumbnails/image/2015/09/14/23/55-anna-soubry-rex.jpg\" /><div class=\"capsules\">\n<ul><li>\n\t<div class=\"label context-capsule\">Read more</div>\n\t</li>\n</ul></div>\n\n<div class=\"content\">\n<h2>We need an immigration policy based on what's best for the UK</h2>\n</div>\n</a></div>\n\n<p>\u201cThe fall of eight\u00a0per cent in happiness is significant \u2013 equivalent to the gap between the average level of happiness of people earning \u00a350,000 and those earning \u00a320,000 a year, for instance.\u201d</p>\n\n<p>Dr Bartram added that political messages play a key role in promoting such discourse among the population, and that it was \u201cdamaging\u201d those who were influenced by it.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cAnti-immigrant discourses, political messages that highlight and bemoan how different immigrants are, contribute to undermining the subjective well-being of the natives themselves,\u201d he added.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cWe would likely see a significant benefit if politicians stopped talking about immigration and immigrants in the way many of them currently do. The current discourse is damaging to natives, and recognition of this idea could amount to reason for reflection.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cPerhaps this research could persuade politicians to reconsider the way they think and speak about immigrants, but I\u2019m not going to hold my breath.</p>\n\n<p>\u201cIt might seem that I\u2019m telling a very pessimistic story about human nature \u2013 the notion that there\u2019s a deeply\u00a0rooted tendency to be suspicious of something that seems unfamiliar and thus a corresponding tendency to distrust and dislike foreigners. \u00a0</p>\n\n<p>\u201cBut in fact we have plenty of evidence indicating that this way of engaging with foreigners can be unlearned \u2013 or at least that a new way of thinking about foreigners can be taught to the next generation.
