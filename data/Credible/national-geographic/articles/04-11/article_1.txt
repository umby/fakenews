From the MIT facility, Fish was constantly monitoring the weather at each site on one screen and communicating with astronomers on another.\n\n\u201cIt\u2019s a heartbreaker if you fire off a night and [bad] weather closes in,\u201d or if observations are cancelled on what turns out be a good night, says Shep Doeleman, director of the Event Horizon Telescope at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics in Cambridge, Massachusetts.\n\nHoping for Peanuts\n\nNow that all five days\u2019 worth of observations have been completed, astronomers have a long wait\u2014and months of analysis\u2014to find out if they\u2019ve produced a black hole portrait.\n\nEach observatory records so much data that it can\u2019t be transmitted electronically. Instead, the information from all the telescopes\u2014equivalent to the storage capacity of ten thousand laptops\u2014has been recorded on 1,024 hard drives. The drives must be mailed to the Event Horizon Telescope\u2019s processing centers at MIT Haystack and the Max Planck Institute for Radio Astronomy in Bonn, Germany.\n\nThe hard drives from the South Pole telescope can\u2019t be flown out until the end of the winter season there at the end of October.\n\nOnce the data reach each processing center, a stack of servers will perform the all-important task of combining the time-stamped signals from the eight observatories. Comparing and combining the radio waves must be done with extraordinary care, so that critical information about the size and structure of the event horizon is not lost when they are added together.\n\nThe technique of combining radio waves, known as very long baseline interferometry, is common enough in radio astronomy. But usually the telescopes are not so numerous nor spread out over such a large area.\n\n\u201cWe\u2019re trying to make coherent a network the size of the globe, which is incredible when you think about it,\u201d says Doeleman.\n\nWhat astronomers hope to finally see when they add up all the signals is a halo of light surrounding a dark circle\u2014the shadow of the black hole.
