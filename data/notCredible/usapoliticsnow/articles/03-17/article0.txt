increases for social security recipients. The average monthly payment will increase to $1,360 for single recipients and about $2,260 for married couples.</p>\n<div></div>\n<p>Recipients who want or need to work will be\u00a0able to earn more in 2017. \u00a0Those under 65 can earn up to $16, 920 this year. \u00a0Those who are turning 66 can earn as much as $44, 880, this is up $3,000 from last year.</p>\n<p>In the past, if you worked and received benefits you were penalized if you earned too much. Those penalties are going down this year. $1 in benefits will be withheld for every $2 over the maximum earnings for those under age 65.</p>\n<p>For those who are 66, but not yet full retirement age (66yrs, 2 months), $1 for every $3 earned over the limit will be held back. Once you reach full retirement age, there is no limit on what you can earn.
