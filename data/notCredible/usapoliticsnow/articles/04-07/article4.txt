elite pedophile ring was shot dead in\u00a0Atlanta on Monday.</strong></p>\n<p>Trinh Huynh, 40,\u00a0was working pro bono with abuse victims on several legal cases involving high-profile elite pedophiles\u00a0outside of her regular work at a law firm, and was said to have a \u201c<em>take no prisoners</em>\u201d attitude towards pedophiles, regardless of their status in society.</p>\n<p>The brave attorney\u00a0was executed in the street by multiple gunshots at 7:40am in midtown Atlanta on Monday in what police have described as a \u201c<em>targeted killing</em>\u201c.</p>\n<p>The\u00a0killer was captured on CCTV released by police and\u00a0can be seen \u201c<em>acting calm</em>\u201d before identifying and approaching Huynh,\u00a0shooting her\u00a0in cold blood, and then casually jogging away after the killing.</p>\n<p>Police have now arrested the alleged\u00a0assassin, 39-year-old Raylon Browning, over the shooting, but admit they have yet to connect the dots\u00a0between him and his victim.</p>\n<p>Officers arrested him in nearby Cobb County\u00a0after he was pulled over\u00a0for running a red light by traffic police.\u00a0A police spokesman said Browning was\u00a0\u201c<em>unfazed</em>\u201d by the allegations.</p>\n<p>Those who knew Huynh said she was an amazing woman with a kind heart who \u201c<em>always had a smile on face</em>\u201d despite the harrowing\u00a0nature of her job.</p>\n<p>But according to police, the brave attorney, fighting the elite pedophile ring,\u00a0may have made herself a target.</p>\n<p>\u201c<em>We definitely believe that she was a target</em>,\u201d Lt. Ricardo Vazquez said. \u201c<em>Why she was a target, we do not know that yet.\u201d</em></p>\n<figure style=\"width: 620px\" class=\"wp-caption aligncenter\"><img src=\"http://yournewswire.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/pedophile-ring-victims-lawyer-shot-dead-by-hitman-6417.jpg\" alt=\"elite-pedophile-ring\" width=\"620\" height=\"340\" /><figcaption class=\"wp-caption-text\"><strong><em>Trinh Huynh, left, with CCTV images of her assassin, right.</em></strong></figcaption></figure>\n<p>Deputy Chief Darryl Tolleson said the 39-year-old alleged killer was also wanted on assault charges stemming from a separate incident on Sunday, where he allegedly stabbed two people.</p>\n<p>The officers did not know Browning was a suspect in Huynh\u2019s murder when they arrested him, the Journal-Constitution reports. Police later found what is believed to be the murder weapon in his car.</p>\n<p>The arrest comes after <a href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_GGQIVdroCw\">CCTV footage</a> showing the man \u2013 believed to be Browning \u2013 in the moments directly before and after the shooting on Huynh.</p>\n<p><iframe width=\"740\" height=\"416\" src=\"https://www.youtube.com/embed/_GGQIVdroCw?feature=oembed\" frameborder=\"0\" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>\n<p>The man was spotted walking down Peachtree Place to the junction of Peachtree Road, where he shot Huynh in the back before fleeing the way he came.</p>\n<p>\u201c<em>I look up and see the woman, she just drops in the crosswalk bleeding</em>,\u201d witness Toney Booker <a href=\"http://www.fox5atlanta.com/news/245953972-story\">told</a> Fox 5 Atlanta.</p>\n<p>\u201c<em>She\u2019s clutching her wound trying to keep pressure, and then she went unresponsive, So civilians run out to her and give her chest compression\u2019s to bring her back, while they\u2019re applying pressure.</em>\u201c</p>\n<p>She was pronounced dead later, at Grady Memorial Hospital.</p>\n<p>The killer didn\u2019t talk to Huynh before he killed her \u2013 suggesting that the killing was \u201c<em>targeted</em>\u201d \u2013 police said, earlier this week.</p>\n<p>\u201c<em>We see him following her in another piece of video</em>,\u201d Vazquez said. \u201c<em>There was another person in that intersection and it appears that he specifically targeted her.\u201d</em></p>\n<p>He added that the killer was unusually calm throughout. A connection between the shooter and Huynh hasn\u2019t yet been established, Vazquez said.</p>\n<p>Huynh was as an in-house lawyer at UPS who had previously worked at law firms Powell Goldstein and Alston &amp; Bird, Law.com <a href=\"http://www.law.com/sites/almstaff/2017/04/04/ups-lawyer-former-big-law-associate-killed-in-atlanta-shooting/?slreturn=20170307032123\">reported</a>.</p>\n<p>Outside of work, she traveled extensively and was active in both pro bono legal work and volunteering.</p>\n<p>Huynh was a member of the Georgia Asylum and Immigration Network, which provides free legal services to immigrant victims of human trafficking, domestic violence, sexual assault and other crimes.</p>\n<p>The 40-year-old was born in Saigon, Vietnam, but came to America as a child immigrant.
