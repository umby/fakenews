Paul Ryan as Speaker of the House.</p>\n<p><a href=\"http://www.breitbart.com/big-government/2017/03/24/exclusive-discussion-gop-replacement-paul-ryan-speaker-house-intensifies-white-house-congress/?utm_source=facebook&amp;utm_medium=social\">Via Breitbart:</a>\u00a0Many allies of the president and several White House officials over the weeks since have confirmed to Breitbart News that the president is concerned that Ryan may not have his\u2014or his agenda\u2019s\u2014best interests at heart. Ryan\u2019s failure to deliver the votes on healthcare cement Trump\u2019s skepticism of Ryan, they say.</p>\n<p>\u201cThis is another example of the staff not serving the president well and the weakness of the Paul Ryan speakership,\u201d a source close to President Trump told Breitbart News. \u201cThis calls into question once again the speaker\u2019s commitment to supporting Donald Trump and his agenda.\u201d</p>\n<p>\u201cSpeaker Ryan proved today that he does not have the best interests of the President at heart,\u201d said another source close to the president. \u201cHe sold out the president and showed his word can be taken with a grain of salt. After the election in November, it was widely reported\u00a0that there were enough Republican votes to remove Ryan as Speaker\u2014and the only reason conservatives kept him is that Trump won the election and embraced Ryan. But now Trump may perceive Ryan as a\u00a0burden rather than someone who can help enact his agenda.</p>\n<p>A senior Senate GOP aide questioned whether Ryan has the chops to continue in the position.</p>\n<p>\u201cA tale as old as time, our establishment leadership continue advocating for moderate pieces of legislation after ignoring conservative input,\u201d the Senate aide said. \u201cHow can President Donald Trump trust Speaker Paul Ryan in the future after this failure? If he couldn\u2019t deliver on something so simple as repealing Obamacare, will he be able to deliver on complex pieces of legislation?\u201d</p>\n<p>House Republicans are also questioning whether Ryan can remain as Speaker after this abysmal failure.</p>\n<p>\u201cIf Speaker Ryan cannot pass his RyanCare plan and negotiations had to be taken to the Oval Office by non-leadership members of the conference, it is certainly time to evaluate his effectiveness as the Speaker of the House,\u201d a senior House GOP aide told Breitbart News.\u201d</p>\n<p>If this is true and he was replaced, this failed bill would be the greatest thing that could happen for Trump and the American people. Ryan as a speaker is no better than Pelosi as speaker.</p>\n<p>From the start, Ryan wanted his bill in and refused to work with his own party to realize the changes that would be required for a Yes vote.
