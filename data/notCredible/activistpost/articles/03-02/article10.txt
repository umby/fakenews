So, they can kill.</p>\n<p><a href=\"http://activistpost.net/unexplainable.html\" rel=\"nofollow\"><img class=\"alignright\" src=\"https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/_qLAIskTQXUc/TdShMb4n6MI/AAAAAAAAI3o/d72a9NrD4wE/unexmys.gif\" alt=\"\" /></a>Surprisingly, this is <em>not</em> the first time wolves have been politically sacrificed to appease the blood-lust of a soulless Congress, ignorant US gun nuts, hateful ranchers, and hunters.</p>\n<p>A mere six years ago President Obama foreshadowed his coming two terms of political weakness by sending over 1700 wolves &#8211; and then many more &#8211; to slaughter at the hands hunters and ranchers in Idaho and Montana. Not just for blood. No. Every wolf died for the sake of two congressional votes.</p>\n<p>As previously reported on Jan. 7,2011 in <a href=\"http://www.watchingromeburn.com/news/another-death-nail-democracy-kills-1700-endangered-wolves/\">an article by this author, \u201c</a><a href=\"http://www.watchingromeburn.com/news/another-death-nail-democracy-kills-1700-endangered-wolves/\"><em>Another Death Nail for Democracy Kills 1700 Endangered Wolves</em></a><a href=\"http://www.watchingromeburn.com/news/another-death-nail-democracy-kills-1700-endangered-wolves/\">\u201d&#8230; </a></p>\n<blockquote><p>The dirty deal that will kill 1700 wolves came from the result of a desperate president, hungry to pass any federal budget, and two congressmen who well understood his weakness. Mike Simpson (R-Idaho) were obviously mindful of previous gifts from Obama to their other congressional brethren. These two congressmen played their cards- and their president- once it was obvious that Obama was now &#8220;all in&#8221; on his proposed budget. Only once in recorded US history has any wolf attacked a man. However, due to legislation in the 1800s and 1900s, bounties were introduced that allowed men to literally wipe the North American wolves from the Midwestern landscape. Sadly, by the 1920s the majestic sound of the wolf&#8217;s long howl was no longer heard south of the Canadian border in these parts of the country.</p>\n<p>Similarly, just sixty odd years before this same government, hunters and ranchers had also eradicated the American Indian. For exactly the same reason: to kill.</p>\n<p>Thankfully, legislation to protect and reintroduce the wolf to these areas came in the form of the Endangered Species Act.
