And no one defined it more succinctly than Orwell: \u201cIf you want a vision of the future, imagine a boot stamping on a human face \u2013 forever.\u201d However, if they read carefully enough, they\u2019ll realize that temporary leaders of \u201cBig Brother\u201d merely continue the machinery of the State.\n\nYour computer, smartphone, car, smart TV and appliances, and credit cards didn\u2019t just start spying on you when Donald Trump became president. Moreover, a new approach to immigration signals that even more doors could be opened up for invading the privacy of travelers and residents alike.\n\nAs we learned in 1984, all of the tyrannical measures of control are justified by convincing the people that they\u2019re perpetually fighting a dangerous enemy: \u201cOceania had always been at war with Eastasia.\u201d\n\nTrump uses more colorful language when describing the bogeyman than previous administrations, but make no mistake, it\u2019s the exact same tactic. Finally, Senator Jeff Sessions\u2019 past comments regarding immigration have some activists worried about how he will enforce immigration policy as Attorney General. Each of these issues deserves your time and research so you may develop a more informed opinion regarding Jeff Sessions\u2019 stances. However, I wish to take a moment to focus on Sessions\u2019 views on surveillance and how that view will play into the issues mentioned above. In a new report, the Center for Democracy & Technology focuses on the senator\u2019s voting record and comments on the government\u2019s use of surveillance. His actions and comments should have all lovers of liberty concerned.\n\nWhat\u2019s more, Sessions has vowed to mandate encryption backdoors for \u201cnational security.\u201d During his confirmation hearing he said this about encryption:\n\nEncryption serves many valuable and important purposes. In fact, it is something that will affect all travelers into the U.S. and easily could trickle down into the everyday lives of Americans. According to Section 7 of that order, Homeland Security is directed to implement biometric registration and databasing of all who enter.\n\nWhile that might seem unrelated to encryption, there is an \u201cinterview\u201d stipulation as well that easily could expand what type of data is deemed open for examination. This appears already to be happening, as even U.S.-born Americans have been caught up in the early dragnet. Perhaps the most striking case is that of Sidd Bikkannavar, a NASA scientist who was born in the United States, but was stopped at the border with a demand to unlock his smartphone and give up his social media passwords. There are also various reports coming in from around the country of people being stopped on the street and asked for documentation. All of this is to illustrate that the current administration is clearly interested in further tracking citizens and non-citizens alike \u2013 at the borders, as well as internally \u2013 and employing the latest technology to do it.\n\nThese are fundamental issues for privacy and liberty, and, yet, we are clearly on a path where the government sees privacy and liberty as liabilities rather than guarantees. It\u2019s a good reminder for you to do what you can to limit the government\u2019s ability to spy on you, as well as limit tracking by any other source. Use encrypted chat apps and email services.
