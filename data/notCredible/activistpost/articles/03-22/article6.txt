If they want to push an agenda, you can rest assured that agenda will be present on each of their outlets in some form.</p>\n<h3>The Rulers of the Internet</h3>\n<p>People under 50 get more of their news and information from the web than any other source these days.</p>\n<p>One hundred websites get the majority of the traffic on the Internet, and that traffic is massive. The people behind these websites are major influences in the reality perceived by Americans.</p>\n<p><a href=\"http://activistpost.net/icloak.html\"><img class=\"alignleft\" src=\"https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/-0vH7MlvtVgs/VdOqlEsRfBI/AAAAAAAAB34/21xB26VGJXk/s190-Ic42/icloak250-2.jpg\" alt=\"\" /></a>Think about this:</p>\n<p>Google gets 28 billion visits per month. That means when you look something up on Google, you are provided with the answer Google wants you to have, which may or may not be the actual answer.</p>\n<p>YouTube follows quickly behind with 20.5 billion visits. (<a href=\"http://www.zerohedge.com/news/2017-03-17/visualizing-100-websites-rule-internet\">source</a>) I\u2019m sure by now you have heard that <a href=\"http://daisyluther.com/google-and-facebook-will-be-arbiters-of-truth-in-the-war-on-fake-news/\">YouTube has taken advertising privileges</a> away from a lot of videographers whose points of view are contrary to the agenda they wish to promote. They\u2019re saying that the content is not \u201cadvertiser friendly\u201d \u2013 and obviously, this discourages people who dedicate their days to advancing the truth from doing so. They, too, must make a living, and video editing is a time-consuming process. One well-done 10-minute video can take 8 hours to produce.</p>\n<p>These are the 100 websites that provide the \u201creality\u201d for the majority of the people who rely on the Internet for their news, as per Alexa.com\u2019s traffic reporting system.</p>\n<p style=\"text-align: center;\"><a Unless you\u2019ve been living under a rock, you probably heard about Twitter permanently banning a bunch of popular Alt-Right people, most infamously, Milo Yiannopoulos for his perspective.</p>\n<p>Now, personally, I\u2019m not a fan of the Alt-Right viewpoint. It seems like they\u2019re presenting meanness as \u201ctruth\u201d when they could just as easily state true things without being jerks about it. But that is beside the point. If these people are going to be banned for their unsavory ways of communicating, why does Twitter allow things like <a href=\"http://daisyluther.com/wheres-feminist-outrage-rapemelania-trends-twitter/\">#RapeMelania</a> to trend? If you\u2019re going to start banning offensiveness, shouldn\u2019t it be across the board?</p>\n<p><a href=\"http://activistpost.net/backyardliberty.html\" rel=\"nofollow\"><img class=\"alignright\" src=\"https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-ONE8Lk_RzR8/UjeZnRyZRTI/AAAAAAAAVJw/TMNS4NzHKZo/s220/BL_158220.gif\" alt=\"\" /></a>Then there\u2019s Facebook, who has actually been busted <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2014/06/30/technology/facebook-tinkers-with-users-emotions-in-news-feed-experiment-stirring-outcry.html\">running social experiments on its users by attempting to alter people\u2019s moods</a> with what they show in their timelines. But it doesn\u2019t stop there. Nearly every blogger I know who works in the alternative news genre has been in Facebook jail at least once for presenting an unpopular opinion. I personally got warnings when I posted <a href=\"http://www.theorganicprepper.ca/is-civil-unrest-coming-to-suburbia-rest-assured-the-media-will-lie-about-it-08182016\">this article</a> on my page \u2013 the article was taken down dozens of times and reported as \u201chate speech\u201d when the \u201chate\u201d it reported on was certainly not MY hatred.</p>\n<p>Of course, when you sign up to use any of these social media networks, you agree to their terms and conditions. Go ahead and use them \u2013 I do because they are helpful to my business \u2013 but do so fully aware that what you see presented there reflects the opinions of the management\u00a0and not actual reality.</p>\n<h3>So how do we get the truth?</h3>\n<p>There really aren\u2019t any unbiased sources out there. Having worked as an alternative journalist, I can tell you honestly that no matter how hard you try to be unbiased, your personal feelings sneak in, at least a little bit. Maybe it\u2019s with the background information you provide to support your story because you can see the direction what you are reporting on is going. Maybe it is with a little bit of snarky commentary or a pithy headline.</p>\n<p>Because of this, many people pick the reality in which they want to live. Maybe they select ultra-conservative websites that reflect their religious beliefs. Perhaps they opt for anti-establishment outlets that gleefully point out in a non-partisan fashion that all of the politicians are sell-outs. For others, they want \u201cnews\u201d that supports their warm, fuzzy liberal view of the world.</p>\n<p>It\u2019s pretty easy to get manipulated through the subliminal messages and the predictive programming, but some folks seem to be immune. You really want to be one of those people, and if you aren\u2019t, you can get there.</p>\n<p>I <a href=\"http://daisyluther.com/not-just-a-conspiracy-theory-how-subliminal-messaging-and-predictive-programming-are-really-used/\">wrote about this</a> previously:</p>\n<blockquote><p>What if some people are genetically more susceptible than others? Are we just mutants? Is this why I can explain this stuff until I lose my voice and just get a blank stare from people who think I am the crazy one?</p>\n<p>The vast majority of people in the United States are\u00a0completely accepting of the official message:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>They believe that the government will be there to save them in all circumstances.</li>\n<li>They don\u2019t question or inquire. I wasn\u2019t always the tinfoil-clad blogger you see before you today. When I was in my early 20s, I truly did believe that the government agencies like the <a href=\"http://daisyluther.com/the-epa-theyre-protecting-someone-but-it-isnt-you/\">EPA</a> and the <a href=\"http://daisyluther.com/20-things-the-fda-federal-deception-administration-doesnt-want-you-to-know/\">FDA</a> were looking out for our best interests, that food or medicine wouldn\u2019t be sold if it was harmful, and that schools truly were in the business of educating children. \u00a0Most of us have to shake off our early training at the hands of the education system and the television.</p>\n<p>And sort of like in the movie, <em><a href=\"http://amzn.to/2eg1kyk\">The Matrix</a></em>, if you can get someone to swallow the red pill, they become a lot less willing to believe whatever they\u2019re fed.</p>\n<p><img src=\"https://i2.wp.com/s.quickmeme.com/img/08/08b961a0eacb4fb896b16e34e57427ec89744456a68e48827b5aea1ff8b9c233.jpg\" alt=\"Image result for matrix red pill blue pill meme\" width=\"799\" height=\"378\" /></p>\n<p>Subliminal messaging can change people\u2019s views, can change the way they think, can change what they want to eat, what car they want to buy, maybe even how they vote.
