Forecaster</em></a></p>\n<p>It may have arrived with little fanfare, but Russia&#8217;s SWIFT alternative has, more or less, arrived. Speaking in no uncertain terms at a meeting with Russian President Vladimir Putin late last month, Elvira Nabiullina, the Governor of Russia&#8217;s central bank, <a href=\"https://www.rt.com/business/382017-russia-swift-central-bank/\">stated</a>: &#8220;We have finished working on our own payment system, and if something happens, all operations in SWIFT format will work inside the country. We have created an alternative.&#8221;</p>\n<p><iframe width=\"777\" height=\"437\" src=\"https://www.youtube.com/embed/515wKNvH0pc?feature=oembed\" frameborder=\"0\" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>\n<div class=\"sb-widget clearfix\">\t\t\t<div class=\"textwidget\"><div align=\"left\"><script async src=\"//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js\"></script>\r\n<!-- AP WP Responsive -->\r\n<ins class=\"adsbygoogle\"\r\n     style=\"display:block\"\r\n     data-ad-client=\"ca-pub-1897954795849722\"\r\n     data-ad-slot=\"4254280269\"\r\n     data-ad-format=\"auto\"></ins>\r\n<script>\r\n(adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});\r\n</script></div></div>\n\t\t</div>\n<p>Now this news will be old news to intrepid Corbett Reporteers. My long-term audience will no doubt recall the <a href=\"https://www.corbettreport.com/swift-alternative-fed-audit-good-news-new-world-next-week/\">September 21, 2014 episode</a> of New World Next Week where James Evan Pilato and I covered the Russia/China talks to create both a SWIFT alternative and an independent ratings agency. You&#8217;ll also of course recall my <a href=\"https://www.corbettreport.com/chinas-swift-alternative-and-the-engineered-death-of-the-dollar/\">March 11, 2015 editorial</a> in these very pages where I discussed then-recent reports that China was ready to go live with its own SWIFT alternative, the Cross-Border Inter-Bank Payments System. For those not following along at home, that system did indeed go live in October of that year, but in a &#8220;<a href=\"http://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-china-yuan-payments-idUKKCN0PN0P020150713\">watered down</a>&#8221; form that only accounts for cross-border yuan trade deals, not capital-related transactions.</p>\n<p>But for those who are really lost in the woods, let&#8217;s go back to my &#8220;<a href=\"https://www.corbettreport.com/chinas-swift-alternative-and-the-engineered-death-of-the-dollar/\">China\u2019s SWIFT Alternative and the (Engineered) Death of the Dollar</a>&#8221; editorial to re-establish just what SWIFT is and why alternatives to it are so potentially important. As I wrote at the time:</p>\n<blockquote><p><div class=\"alignright\"><!-- /9716847/AP-Content -->\r\n<div id='div-gpt-ad-1470694951173-5' style='height:250px; width:300px;'>\r\n<script>\r\ngoogletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1470694951173-5'); });\r\n</script>\r\n</div></div>For those who don\u2019t know, SWIFT stands for the Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication and is shorthand for the SWIFTNet Network that is used by over 10,500 financial institutions in 215 countries and territories to transmit financial transaction data around the world. SWIFT does not do any of the clearing or processing for these transactions itself, but instead sends the payment orders that are then settled by correspondent banks of the member institutions. Still, given the system\u2019s near universality in the financial system, it means that virtually every international transaction between banking institutions goes through the SWIFT network.</p></blockquote>\n<p>This is why the SWIFT system is so important to the global economy and why it was a significant hamper to the Iranian economy when 30 Iranian financial institutions (including the central bank) were <a href=\"http://www.swift.com/news/press_releases/SWIFT_disconnect_Iranian_banks\">de-listed from the SWIFT network</a> in 2012. And this is exactly why China and Russia have been so keen on setting up an alternative infrastructure of their own, just in case the &#8220;completely independent&#8221; SWIFT organization acts as a proxy weapon for the US State Department and its pals at some point in the future.</p>\n<p>Now this latest announcement about the Russian SWIFT alternative is not new news, per se. The system was already <a href=\"http://russia-insider.com/en/politics/russias-swift-equivalent-already-use/ri9845\">up and operating</a> on a trial basis among &#8220;several dozen banks&#8221; in 2015. Governor Nabiullina&#8217;s latest pronouncement is more of a reassurance that the Russian economy will be able to continue in the event of anything, including a de-listing from SWIFT. As the latest reports reveal, 330 banks are now on the system. What&#8217;s more, fully 90% of Russia&#8217;s ATMs are now able to accept the Mir payment system, the Russian alternative to Visa and Mastercard.</p>\n<p>Now maybe these stories taken by themselves are no big news. But let&#8217;s look at them in the context of recent events:</p>\n<p>Russia just created its <a href=\"http://tass.com/economy/935934\">first ever representative office of the Bank of Russia abroad</a>&#8230;in Beijing. This move brings Russia one step closer to issuing its own <a href=\"http://www.bondsloans.com/news/article/1302/with-russias-cb-moving-to-china-sovereign-yua\">sovereign yuan-denominated bonds</a>.</p>\n<h4 style=\"text-align: center;\"><a href=\"http://activistpost.net/dollardoomsday.html\" target=\"_blank\">April 21 2017 &#8211; Trigger Event for the US Dollar? (Ad)</a></h4>\n<p>Meanwhile the Industrial and Commercial Bank of China is now <a href=\"http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/business/2017-03/23/content_28651190.htm\">officially acting as a renminbi clearing bank in Russia</a>, which expands local settlement business in direct yuan-ruble trade and follows Russia&#8217;s 2015 inclusion of the renminbi on its official reserve currency list.</p>\n<p align=\"justify\">Are you starting to see the bigger picture? Again, no one of these stories is a silver bullet, but they&#8217;re all related and they all point in the same direction: China and Russia are preparing for the inevitable(?) split with the US-dominated, dollar-denominated, SWIFT-networked global(ist) financial architecture.</p>\n<p>Now there are some caveats to this before we start converting all our cash to yuan and moving to Vladivostok. As it turns out, every single one of these moves are either baby steps, the beginning moves that might one day lead to baby steps, or steps backward disguised as baby steps.</p>\n<p>Like that Chinese SWIFT alternative, the CIPS system. It was originally being touted as a rival to SWIFT. Then, as we&#8217;ve seen, it was &#8220;watered down&#8221; and became something like an adjunct to SWIFT. Then in March 2016 CIPS actually <a href=\"https://www.swift.com/insights/press-releases/swift-and-cips-co_sign-memorandum-of-understanding-on-cross-border-interbank-payment-system-cooperation\">signed a memorandum of understanding</a> with SWIFT to use SWIFT&#8217;s own platform for transmitting its messages. CIPS is now, if not an all-out subsidiary of SWIFT, at least just another one of the 150 or so payment networks that make use of the SWIFT network. So China has actually made no progress whatsoever toward safeguarding itself from a SWIFT de-listing.</p>\n<p>And Russia&#8217;s newly (re)announced SWIFT alternative? It has some considerable limitations of its own. As <a href=\"https://www.rt.com/business/382017-russia-swift-central-bank/\">RT reports</a>, the system &#8220;doesn\u2019t work from 9pm to 5am Moscow time and costs up to five cents per wire transfer, which is regarded expensive.&#8221;</p>\n<p>And Russia&#8217;s Mir payment system? The homegrown Mastercard/Visa alternative that is now readable at 90% of the country&#8217;s ATMs? Well wouldn&#8217;t you know it, a suspiciously well-engineered virus has just infected the country&#8217;s ATM network, causing the machines to release all their large denomination banknotes when a certain code is entered. As Russia Beyond the Headlines <a href=\"http://rbth.com/business/2017/03/20/russian-atms-catch-new-virus_723391\">intriguingly notes</a>: &#8220;The virus does not have a file body, so it is not recognized by antivirus programs. It can therefore exist in the operating memory of an ATM for an unlimited time. It is the first time such a virus has been detected in Russia.&#8221;</p>\n<p>So it&#8217;s not like the path from here to the Sino-Russia (New) New World Order will be all sunshine and rainbows. But who was expecting that?
