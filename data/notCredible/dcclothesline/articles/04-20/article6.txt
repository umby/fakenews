style=\"font-weight: 400;\">35-fold</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> in just the first 13 years, the Taliban had successfully decimated the opium poppy crop in <a style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cIn its early stage, the Kim Jong-un regime declared a war against drugs, getting rid of poppy fields,\u201d</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> Kang 400;\">interview</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> with </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">NPR</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">, Ma Young Ae \u2014 a defector and Her job was was to track down drug smugglers. That sounds like pretty normal law enforcement, except for one difference. <strong>She was supposed to stop small-time Korean drug dealers in order to protect the biggest drug dealer in the country: the North Korean government.</strong></span></i></p>\n<p><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cMa told us <strong>the North Korean government produced opium on a large scale. But it hid its poppy fields from most of the population.</strong> Ma only saw the fields because she was an insider.</span></i></p>\n<p><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cAfter harvesting the fields, the government would put its empty factories to use. The government would turn on its production lines at night and process opium, Ma says. Jong-il\u2019s son and successor instead chose to fight the war on drugs \u2014 until the Chinese Commerce Ministry suspended imports of coal from February through the end of the year, in response to one of Pyongyang\u2019s contentious ballistic missiles tests.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Faced with the rapid loss of hard currency and an uphill battle to fund the regime\u2019s </span><b><i>\u201cThe North is cultivating poppy fields again for drug smuggling as a way to secure funds to manage its regime.\u201d</i></b></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Funding an entire government\u2019s operations from the cultivation and production of opium </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">should</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> be a piece of cake \u2014 should illegal markets fail, America has an insidious obsession with opioids.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Tens of thousands each year die of overdoses from heroin, opioids, and/or their synthetics in the United States, alone \u2014 in large part, courtesy of the pharmaceutical industry\u2019s reckless devotion to painkillers.</span></p>\n<p><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> March 29 the opioid </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cepidemic has by and large been caused by the rise in opioid overdose deaths. First, opioid painkiller overdoses began to rise, as doctors began to fill out a record number of prescriptions for the drugs in an attempt to treat patients\u2019 pain conditions. Then, people hooked on painkillers began to move over to heroin as they or their sources of drugs lost their prescriptions. And recently, more people have begun moving to fentanyl, an opioid that\u2019s even more potent and cheaper than heroin. The result is a deadly epidemic that so far shows no signs of slowing down.\u201d</span></i></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">And how </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">could</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> it slow down? </span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Opioids doled out like candy by doctors and hospitals to those suffering but unaware of the addiction pitfalls inherent in rising tolerance, short-term prescriptions, and \u2014 in particular \u2014 the availability of potent substances like heroin and fentanyl on the black market.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">This isn\u2019t by far purely an issue to be blamed on illegal trade in drugs. </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Media Roots</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u2019 Abby Martin elaborated on the perniciousness of the opioid crisis in 2014, </span><a href=\"http://mediaroots.org/opium-what-afghanistan-is-really-about/\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">stating</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">,</span></p>\n<p><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cIn today\u2019s globalized world of rule-for-profit, one can\u2019t discount the role that multinational corporations play in US foreign policy decisions either.
