class=\"entry-date\">March 5, 2017</span></a> <span class=\"meta-sep\">by</span> <span class=\"author vcard\"><a class=\"url fn n\" href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/author/dr-eowyn/\" title=\"View all posts by Dr. Eowyn\">Dr. Eowyn</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p>A Catholic university in California \u2014 the Jesuit <a href=\"https://scu.edu/news-and-events/campus-calendar/#event_id/24695/view/event/date/20171012\"><span style=\"color: #0000ff;\">Santa Clara University</span></a> \u2014 has invited UK University of Exeter theologian Susannah Cornwall to deliver the Santa Clara Lecture on \u201cGendered Theologies and the Common Good\u201d on October 12, 2017.<span id=\"more-70223\"></span></p>\n<p>The <a href=\"http://liturgy.co.nz/spirituality/reflections_assets/schneiders.pdf\"><span style=\"color: #0000ff;\">Santa Clara Lectures</span></a> are a series of lectures that \u201cbrings to campus leading scholars in theology, offering the University community and the general public an ongoing exposure to debate on the most significant issues of our times. Santa Clara University will publish these lectures and distribute them throughout the United States and internationally.\u201d</p>\n<p>Cornwall describes herself on her blog as specializing in: \u201cResearch and writing in feminist theology, sexuality, gender, embodiment, ethics and other fun things like that.\u201d</p>\n<div id=\"attachment_144116\" class=\"wp-caption aligncenter\" style=\"width: 228px;\" data-shortcode=\"caption\">\n<p><a href=\"https://fellowshipofminds.files.wordpress.com/2017/03/susannah-cornwall.jpg\"><img class=\"size-full wp-image-144116\" theologian</p>\n</div>\n<p>That a Catholic university invited Dr. Cornwall to deliver any lecture, even less the university\u2019s prestigious Santa Clara Lecture, is reprehensible because Cornwall is known for her peculiar notion that Jesus might have been a hermaphrodite, i.e., with both male and female DNA and genitalia.</p>\n<p>As reported by John Bingham for <a href=\"http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/religion/9116974/Jesus-may-have-been-a-hermaphrodite-claims-academic.html\"><span style=\"color: #0000ff;\"><em>The Telegraph</em></span></a>, Cornwall\u2019s claim that it is \u201csimply a best guess\u201d that Jesus was male was published in response to the ongoing debate about women bishops in the Church of England.</p>\n<p>In her paper \u201cIntersex &amp; Ontology, A Response to The Church, Women Bishops and Provision\u201d, she argues that it is not possible to know \u201cwith any certainty\u201d that Jesus did not suffer from an intersex condition, with both male and female organs, and that the fact that Jesus is not recorded to have had children made his gender status \u201ceven more uncertain\u201d. As she put it:</p>\n<blockquote><p>\u201cIt is not possible to assert with any degree of certainty that Jesus was male as we now define maleness. There is no way of knowing for sure that Jesus did not have one of the intersex conditions which would give him a body which appeared externally to be unremarkably male, but which might nonetheless have had some \u2018hidden\u2019 female physical features.\u201d</p></blockquote>\n<p>In arguing that Jesus might have been a hermaphrodite, Cornwall employs a logical fallacy called \u201cthe absence of evidence\u201d. She argues:</p>\n<blockquote><p>\u201cWe cannot know for sure that Jesus was male \u2013 since we do not have a body to examine and analyse \u2013 it can only be that Jesus\u2019 masculine gender role, rather than his male sex, is having to bear the weight of all this authority.\u201d</p></blockquote>\n<p><a href=\"https://logfall.wordpress.com/absence-of-evidence-fallacy/\"><span style=\"color: #0000ff;\">The absence-of-evidence logical fallacy</span></a> is when one argues that finding no evidence for something (X) is evidence for the absence of that thing (X). In Cornwall\u2019s case, her logical fallacy is in arguing that the absence of Jesus\u2019 body (for us to examine and analyze) constitutes evidence that he is not a male.</p>\n<p>As several <em>Telegraph</em> readers point out, Corwall\u2019s absence of evidence (Jesus\u2019 body) is a ridiculous reason to doubt Jesus\u2019 masculinity:</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<blockquote><p>Baluba Swinarska: \u201cAnd\u2026we don\u2019t know if he was 10 feet tall, had green hair, and a tail \u2013 all because we have no body to examine. That doesn\u2019t mean he was one.\u201d</p>\n<p>Lev Kalman: \u201cWe also don\u2019t know if he was an armadillo, because we have no body.\u201d</p></blockquote>\n<p>Two <em>Telegraph</em> readers demonstrate they have more intelligence in their respective right toe than Dr. Susannah Cornwall has in all of what passes as her brain:</p>\n<blockquote><p>James A: \u201cHer claims cannot be tested. Her hypothesis is unproveable.\u201d</p>\n<p>Rogan M: \u201cWhat a stupid claim to make. \u2018It can\u2019t be proved\u2019 does not trump \u2018There is no evidence to suggest!\u2019. Where Jesus was circumcised.\u201d</p></blockquote>\n<p><strong><span style=\"color: #ff0000;\">*</span></strong>Note: In Luke 2:21, it is said: \u201cAt the end of eight days, when he was circumcised, he was named Jesus, the name given by the angel before he was conceived in the womb.\u201d</p>\n<p><em>~Eowyn</em></p>\n<p><em>Dr. Eowyn&#8217;s post first appeared at <a href=\"https://fellowshipoftheminds.com/2017/03/03/feminist-theologian-jesus-may-have-been-a-hermaphrodite/\" target=\"_blank\">Fellowship of the Minds</a></em></p>\n\n\n<h4><strong>Don't forget to follow the D.C.
