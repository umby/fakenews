that is already going on in other parts of the globe will soon reach U.S. shores.</p>\n<p>I have repeatedly warned my readers that \u201c<a title=\"Sears is going to zero\" It has posted a loss in all but two of the last 24 quarters, according to S&amp;P Global Market Intelligence.</p></blockquote>\n<p>How in the world is it possible for a retailer to lose that amount of money in just three months?</p>\n<p>As I have said before, if they had employees flushing dollar bills down the toilet 24 hours a day they still shouldn\u2019t have losses that big.</p>\n<p>This week we also learned that Payless is heading for bankruptcy.\u00a0 According to <a title=\"Bloomberg\" href=\"https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-03-21/payless-is-said-to-be-filing-for-bankruptcy-as-soon-as-next-week\" target=\"_blank\">Bloomberg</a>, the chain is planning to imminently close at least 400 stores\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p><a title=\"Company Overview\" href=\"https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/PSS:US\" target=\"_blank\">Payless Inc.</a>, the struggling discount shoe chain, is preparing <strong>to file for bankruptcy</strong> as soon as next week, according to people familiar with the matter.</p>\n<p>The company is initially planning to close <strong>400 to 500 stores</strong> as it reorganizes operations, said the people, who asked not to be identified because the deliberations aren\u2019t public. income and shopper traffic from that store\u2019s business, but the closure often triggers \u201cco-tenancy clauses\u201d that allow the other mall tenants to terminate their leases or renegotiate the ago</a> I wrote of a time when we would see boarded-up storefronts all across America, and now it is happening.</p>\n<p>Instead of asking which retailers are going to close, perhaps we should be asking which ones are going to survive this retail cataclysm.</p>\n<p>In the past, you could always count on middle class U.S. consumers to save the day, but today the middle class is steadily shrinking and U.S. consumers are increasingly tapped out.</p>\n<p>For instance, just look at what is happening to delinquency rates <a title=\"on auto loans\" and in <a title=\"the financial markets\" href=\"http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/have-we-reached-a-turning-point-for-stocks-tuesday-was-the-worst-day-for-the-stock-market-in-6-months\">the financial markets</a>.</p>\n<p>The last recession probably should have started back in late 2015, but thanks to manipulation by the Fed and an <a title=\"unprecedented debt binge\"
