unpopular, false candidate of \u201cchange\u201d working on behalf of big money interests \u2013 make friends with a hostile corporate media? Bomb Middle Eastern countries.<span id=\"more-71412\"></span></p>\n<p>Dropping more bombs in Yemen in one week <a href=\"http://foreignpolicy.com/2017/03/09/trumps-ramped-up-bombing-in-yemen-signals-more-aggressive-use-of-military/\" target=\"_blank\">than the annual total for any prior year</a>, or <a href=\"http://www.seattletimes.com/nation-world/trump-moves-to-ease-obama-rules-on-avoiding-civilian-casualties-in-yemen-somalia/\" target=\"_blank\">loosening the standards for \u201cacceptable\u201d civilian casualties</a>, wasn\u2019t enough for the war machine. Trump had to carry out his \u201cfirst major military action\u201d in Syria \u2014 as media figures gleefully stated \u2014 to earn accolades from the Establishment and the Deep State.</p>\n<p>Hillary Clinton most likely would have carried out her \u201cfirst major military action\u201d in Syria at this point, too. Clinton was ready to pursue the establishment of \u2018safe zones\u2019 which would be a likely path for direct conflict with Russia.</p>\n<p>Perhaps \u2018safe zones\u2019 or \u2018no-fly zones\u2019 will be on Trump\u2019s teleprompter soon. Considering his call for increasing the already bloated military budget and giving the Pentagon \u2013 who\u2019s never had an audit \u2013 much more control over what transpires in the various theaters of war, Trump should be very willing to accommodate American imperialism.</p>\n<p>There was no shortage of fawning admirers in the corporate press, spanning the political spectrum, after the strike on Syria. Neoconservatives and liberal interventionists joined hands in praise of Tomahawk missiles, with Brian Williams <a href=\"http://www.nj.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2017/04/brian_williams_calls_missile_beautiful_syria_msnbc.html#incart_river_home\" target=\"_blank\">gushing</a> about how \u201cbeautiful\u201d the missile strikes were.</p>\n<p>Media have simply repeated the government\u2019s line \u2013 without question \u2013 that US spies witnessed a Syrian plane dropping chemical weapons on a Syrian town. The media\u2019s constant skepticism of Trump, deserved or not, suddenly disappears when he lets the missiles fly on Syria.</p>\n<p>Granted, the Assad regime is certainly guilty of brutality and crimes, just as the Bush, Obama, Trump and Putin regimes are. But the accusation of chemical weapon use must be subject to the most intense scrutiny, and the mainstream media have clearly abandoned that role.</p>\n<p>We should care about the veracity of the claim that \u2018Syria did it,\u2019 especially since the corporate press portrayed a 2013 sarin gas attack in Syria as a \u201cred line\u201d that required US military attacks. However, thanks to independent media and Seymour Hersh, <strong>we now know <a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/pulitzer-prize-winning-journalist-hillary-sarin-gas-attack-syria-assad/\" target=\"_blank\">that gas attack more likely came from Syrian rebels</a></strong>.</p>\n<p>And apparently, the New York Times \u2013 an important voice in the interventionist cabal \u2013<a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2017/04/05/world/middleeast/syria-bashar-al-assad-atrocities-civilian-deaths-gas-attack.html?action=click&amp;contentCollection=Middle%20East&amp;module=RelatedCoverage&amp;region=EndOfArticle&amp;pgtype=article\" target=\"_blank\"> is no longer pushing the line that Assad carried out that 2013 chemical attack.</a></p>\n<p>Robert Parry at Consortium News <a href=\"https://consortiumnews.com/2017/04/06/nyt-retreats-on-2013-syria-sarin-claims/\" target=\"_blank\">reports</a>:</p>\n<blockquote><p>\u201cThe New York Times, which has never heard an allegation against Syrian President Bashar al-Assad that it hasn\u2019t immediately believed, has compiled a list of his alleged atrocities with a surprising omission: the Aug. 21, 2013 sarin gas attack outside Damascus.</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; The story of Obama\u2019s supposed \u201cred line\u201d retreat has become a treasured groupthink of all the \u201cimportant people\u201d in D.C.\u201d</p></blockquote>\n<p>While Saudi Arabia carries out war crimes in Yemen, bombing schools and hospitals in a proxy US \u2018war on terror,\u2019 the New York Times is intent on highlighting Assad\u2019s bad behavior in Syria\u2019s six-year civil war.</p>\n<p>The Times had previously reported \u201cslam-dunk proof of Assad\u2019s guilt\u201d in the 2013 sarin attack, but Parry notes that they backed away as the case fell apart. Seymour Hersh later found that the chemicals came from Turkish intelligence. Perhaps most revealing was that the likely firing position of the 2013 sarin attack was in rebel-controlled territory.</p>\n<p>The question must be asked: who stands to benefit from a chemical attack? Assad or the \u201crebels,\u201d which includes al-Qaida and ISIS-linked fighters? It also must be noted the attack and predictable Western response came about just as peace talks were getting underway in Brussels.</p>\n<p>If the hallowed New York Times admits the 2013 chemical weapon attack didn\u2019t come from Assad, why are the media once again parroting the government line that \u2018Assad did it\u2019 without question?</p>\n<p>The military-industrial complex always gets its way, regardless of who occupies the Oval Office.
