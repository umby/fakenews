Group</a>, the Department of Homeland Security, under the Obama administration, attempted to <span id=\"more-69907\"></span>hack the Indiana State electoral system nearly 15,000 times. The news comes in the wake of months of post-election talk the Russians helped Donald Trump get elected, thwarting any chance of Hillary Clinton to win in November of 2016.</p>\n<p>Yet even with the bipartisan parade of politicians, including former president Barack H. Obama, Lindsey Graham, John McCain, and others, the American people have not been presented one shred of evidence to suggest the Russians attempted to meddle with the elections. Free thinkers everywhere are scratching their heads, and asking themselves if they\u2019re supposed to believe the so-called leaders in Washington, without being presented with any proof the Kremlin had anything to do with Clinton\u2019s loss.</p>\n<p>The bully in Washington may have used his executive powers to go after states such as Indiana, that \u201cresisted then-President Barack Obama\u2019s attempt to increase federal involvement in state and local election systems by designating them as \u2018critical infrastructure\u2019 for national security,\u201d writes DCNF\u2019s Richard Pollack. To better describe the hacking attempt, Pollack described it as a \u201cscanning assault.\u201d</p>\n<p>Apparently, the National Association fo Secretaries of State (NASS) aren\u2019t too happy about the intrusive big-brother cyber attacks by the Feds, and are calling on Trump to overturn the measures put in place by his predecessor to increase federal involvement in their election systems. Once again, the states are pushing back against executive overreach by a president who is no longer in office, which may serve to demonstrate just how long-lasting Obama\u2019s rule-based, executive order leadership actually is.</p>\n<p>Seemingly miffed by the IT intrusion is Indiana Secretary of State Connie Lawson, who also serves as president of NASS. She told Pollack, \u201cwe know that <strong>between November 1 and December 16, we were scanned with about 14,800 scans, nearly 15,000 different times,\u201d</strong> an action which was traced back to a Department of Homeland Security computer\u2019s IP address, which was the same system used to hack into <a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/georgias-election-servers-hacked-dhs/\">Georgia\u2019s electoral system as well</a>, making it the second time the federal government has been implicated in attempting to hack into state election systems. She points the finger squarely at the Obama administration\u2019s DHS, saying she believed the scans were possibly retaliatory since the state chose not to allow DHS to get their hands on Indiana\u2019s electoral infrastructure. \u201cWe declined their assistance,\u201d she said but added, \u201cour voter registration system was not penetrated.\u201d</p>\n<p>Lawson points the finger squarely at the Obama administration\u2019s DHS, saying she believed the scans were possibly retaliatory since the state chose not to allow DHS to get their hands on Indiana\u2019s electoral infrastructure. \u201cWe declined their assistance,\u201d she said but added, \u201cour voter registration system was not penetrated.\u201d</p>\n<p>Pollack spoke with James Scott, a cyber security specialist and senior fellow at the Institute for Critical Infrastructure Technology. So, censorship in the digital landscape is simply just transference of methods\u2026I think within federal agencies it is possible to imagine that there are some overreaching aggressive managers.\u201d But Scott\u2019s words seem to downplay the intrusion with has now been discovered twice in Red states.</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<p>In terms of proof, the only evidence presented, which indicates the Election of 2016 was tampered with, is coming from sovereign states, two of whom are now pointing the finger at the Obama administration\u2019s DHS, for attempting to hack their own state\u2019s system.
