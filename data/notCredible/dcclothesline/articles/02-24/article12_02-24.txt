class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p>Did you know that at present, Obamacare makes it legal for insurers to charge older Americans a health insurance premium that\u2019s three times more than what younger Americans pay?</p>\n<p>That, of course, is tantamount to an age health tax.<span id=\"more-69851\"></span></p>\n<p>To make matters worse, <strong><span style=\"color: #ff0000;\">a new bill in Congress means to actually INCREASE it to as much as 5 times or more. </span></strong></p>\n<p>The bill is <a href=\"https://www.congress.gov/bill/115th-congress/house-bill/708\"><span style=\"color: #0000ff;\">H.R. 708: State Age Rating Flexibility Act of 2017</span></a>, the purpose of which is to amend title XXVII of the Public Health Service Act <span style=\"color: #333333;\">\u201c</span><strong><span style=\"color: #ff0000;\">to increase the permissible age variation based on age for health insurance premiums</span></strong> for coverage offered in the individual or small group market <strong><span style=\"color: #ff0000;\">from a factor of three to a factor of five, or to a factor determined by the state.</span></strong>\u201d</p>\n<p>To top it off, <strong><span style=\"color: #ff0000;\">this age-discriminatory bill was introduced by a Republican</span></strong>! \u2014 <strong><a href=\"https://bucshon.house.gov\"><span style=\"color: #0000ff;\">Rep. Larry Bucshon</span></a></strong> (R-Indiana). At present, there are no co-sponsors. On January 27, 2017, H.R. 708 was referred to the House Committee on Energy and Commerce.</p>\n<p><a href=\"https://fellowshipofminds.files.wordpress.com/2017/02/rep-larry-bucshon-m-d.jpg\"><img \u2014 it would be an age tax \u2014 charging older Americans not yet eligible for Medicare a penalty of five times what others must pay for health insurance.
