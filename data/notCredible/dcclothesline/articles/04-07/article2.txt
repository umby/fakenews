Those conversations are recorded by US intelligence agencies and turned into secret intelligence reports\u2026<span id=\"more-71337\"></span></p>\n<p>Former Obama National Security Advisor, <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/04/05/body-language-expert-susan-rice-was-clearly-lying-when-she-denied-obama-surveillance-of-trump/\" target=\"_blank\">Susan Rice</a>, <a href=\"https://jonrappoport.wordpress.com/2017/04/04/please-somebody-play-the-race-card-like-white-on-susan-rice/\" target=\"_blank\">is accused of \u201cunmasking\u201d the names of Trump team members</a> contained in those US intelligence reports and illegally leaking their names to the press. Bloomberg reports: \u201cOne U.S. official familiar with the reports said they contained valuable political information on the Trump transition such as whom the Trump team was meeting, the views of Trump associates on foreign policy matters and plans for the incoming administration.\u201d</p>\n<p>In other words, US intelligence agencies, under Obama, were spying on Trump associates\u2014and Rice obtained the names of those associates, which are supposed to be kept confidential.</p>\n<p>Leaking the names to the press, in the current political atmosphere, would result in the impression that Trump associates were having improper conversations with foreign leaders, or even \u201ccolluding\u201d with them. Lots of innuendo here.</p>\n<p>The <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/04/04/eli-lake-top-obama-aide-susan-rice-behind-unmasking-of-trump-associates/\" target=\"_blank\">Susan Rice</a> spying scandal points to what? More. Other Americans the Obama team spied on. Other Americans who were opposed to the Obama agenda. Other Americans who were critical of the Obama administration. Other Americans who were exposing the Obama administration.</p>\n<p>For example, former CBS star investigative reporter, <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/02/04/actual-hacking-every-reporter-needs-to-understand-sharyl-attkissons-case-against-the-us-government/\" target=\"_blank\">Sharyl Attkisson</a>\u2014who has sued members of the Obama team and several federal agencies. That lawsuit has just been referred to another venue by the judge in the case. He could have dismissed the suit, but he didn\u2019t. He wants it to proceed. He wants Attkisson to have her day in court.</p>\n<p>You might remember Attkisson was uncovering highly embarrassing details about the gun-walking operation, Fast &amp; Furious, and the attack on the US diplomatic mission in Benghazi. She was making the Obama administration extremely uncomfortable.</p>\n<p>But let me quote Judge Emet Sullivan\u2019s recent order transferring Attkisson\u2019s suit:</p>\n<p>\u201cIn 2011\u2014\u2014at the same time that Ms. Attkisson was conducting investigations and issuing certain of her high-profile news reports\u2014\u2014the Attkissons \u2018began to notice anomalies in numerous electronic devices at their home in Virginia.\u2019 These anomalies included Ms. Attkisson\u2019s work-issued laptop computer and a family desktop computer \u2018turning on and off at night without input from anyone in the household,\u2019 \u2018the house alarm chirping daily at different times,\u2019 and \u2018television problems, including interference.\u2019 All of these electronic devices used \u2018the Verizon FiOS line installed in [the Attkissons\u2019] home,\u2019 but Verizon was unable to stanch the anomalous activity despite multiple attempts. In January 2012, the Attkissons\u2019 residential internet service \u2018began constantly dropping off\u2019.\u201d</p>\n<p>\u201cIn February 2012, \u2018sophisticated surveillance spyware\u2019 was installed on Ms. Attkisson\u2019s work-issued laptop computer. A later forensic computer analysis revealed that Ms. Attkisson\u2019s laptop and the family\u2019s desktop computer had been the \u2018targets of unauthorized surveillance efforts.\u2019 That same forensic analysis revealed that Ms. Attkisson\u2019s mobile phone was also targeted for surveillance when it was connected to the family\u2019s desktop computer. The infiltration of that computer and the extraction of information from it was \u2018executed via an IP address owned, controlled, and operated by the United States Postal service.\u2019 Additionally, based on the sophisticated nature of the software used to carry out the infiltration and software fingerprints indicating the use of the federal government\u2019s proprietary software, the infiltration and surveillance appeared to be perpetrated by persons in the federal government.\u201d</p>\n<p>\u201cAn independent forensic computer analyst hired by CBS subsequently reported finding evidence on both Ms. Attkisson\u2019s work-issued laptop computer and her family\u2019s desktop computer of \u2018a coordinated, highly-skilled series of actions and attacks directed at the operation of the computers.\u2019 Computer forensic analysis also indicated that remote actions were taken in December 2012 to remove the evidence of the electronic infiltration and surveillance from Ms. Attkisson\u2019s computers and other home electronic equipment.\u201d</p>\n<p>\u201cAs Ms. Attkisson\u2019s investigations and reporting continued, in October 2012 the Attkissons noticed \u2018an escalation of electronic problems at their personal residence, including interference in home and mobile phone lines, computer interference, and television interference.\u2019 In November of that year, Ms. Attkisson\u2019s mobile phones \u2018experienced regular interruptions and interference, making telephone communications unreliable, and, at times, virtually impossible\u2019.\u201d</p>\n<p>\u201cAdditionally, in December 2012, a person with government intelligence experience conducted an inspection of the exterior of the Attkissons\u2019 Virginia home. That investigator discovered an extra Verizon FiOS fiber optics line. Soon thereafter, after a Verizon technician was instructed by Ms. Attkisson to leave the extra cable at the home, the cable disappeared, and the Attkissons were unable to determine what happened to it. In March 2013, the Attkissons\u2019 desktop computer malfunctioned, and in September of that year, while Ms. Attkisson was working on a story at her home, she observed that her personal laptop computer was remotely accessed and controlled, resulting in data being deleted from it. On April 3, 2013, Ms. Attkisson filed a complaint with the Inspector General of the Department of Justice. The Inspector General\u2019s investigation was limited to an analysis of the compromised desktop computer, and the partially-released report that emerged from that investigation reported \u2018no evidence of intrusion,\u2019 although it did note \u2018a great deal of advanced mode computer activity not attributable to Ms. Attkisson or anybody in her household\u2019.\u201d</p>\n<p>\u201cThe Attkissons allege that the \u2018cyber-attacks\u2019 they \u2018suffered in [their] home\u2019 were perpetrated by \u2018personnel working on behalf of the United States.\u2019 Accordingly, they have asserted various claims against the United States and against former Attorney General Eric Holder, former Postmaster General Patrick Donahoe, and unknown agents of the Department of Justice, the United States Postal Service, and the United States, all in their individual capacities. Those claims include claims against the United States under the FTCA and claims against the individual federal officers for violations of constitutional rights under Bivens v.
