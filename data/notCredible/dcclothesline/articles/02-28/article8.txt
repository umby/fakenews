Brown</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p>Well, Connecticut Governor <a href=\"http://freedomoutpost.com/?s=dannel+malloy\">Dannel Malloy</a> is back at the center of attention as he instructed Connecticut police officers on how to refuse to comply with the united States&#8217; immigration laws and the orders given by President Donald Trump regarding enforcement of those laws by Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents.<span id=\"more-70035\"></span></p>\n<p>According to a memo sent by Governor Malloy sent to state police and school district representatives, he said that there should be no attempt to detain anyone solely on the basis of their immigration status. Additionally, he advised them to not give ICE officers access to individuals in state or local jails.</p>\n<p>&#8220;Law enforcement should not take action that is solely to enforce federal immigration law,&#8221; Malloy and two of his commissioners wrote. &#8220;The federal government cannot mandate states to investigate and enforce actions that have no nexus to the enforcement of Connecticut law or local ordinances.&#8221;</p>\n<p>\u201cPutting all opinions about this presidential executive order aside, its enforcement is going to have a local impact, especially given the constrained resources and financial impacts this will have on state and municipal budgets, which we already know are stretched to their limits, in addition to giving rise to serious concerns in affected communities,\u201d Malloy <a href=\"http://portal.ct.gov/en/Office-of-the-Governor/Press-Room/Press-Releases/2017/02-2017/Malloy-Administration-Issues-Guidance-to-Law-Enforcement-and-School-Districts-Regarding-Immigration\" target=\"_blank\">said</a> in a statement. \u201cAbove all, we are obligated to protect the rights afforded to all our residents and ensure that students attend safe, welcoming schools. The best approach for local communities is to have a plan in place so that everyone in our state, including young students, are supported respectfully and fairly under the laws of our state and our nation.\u201d</p>\n<p>The Hill <a href=\"http://thehill.com/homenews/state-watch/320715-connecticut-governor-to-cops-dont-follow-trump-immigration-orders\">reported</a>:</p>\n<blockquote><p>Connecticut is one of a handful of states that call themselves sanctuaries for undocumented immigrants. A state law passed in 2013 requires law enforcement agencies to detain an individual for breaking immigration laws if they have been convicted of a felony, are a known gang member, are on a federal terrorism watch list or face criminal charges and have not posted bond.</p>\n<p>In a <a href=\"http://www.trbas.com/media/media/acrobat/2017-02/70230951705300-22074645.pdf\">memo to state school districts</a>, Malloy\u2019s office urged education officials to develop a plan in the event that immigration agents show up at schools. Malloy said he expected the Trump administration to adhere to the same guidelines as the Obama administration, which put schools, courthouses and other \u201csensitive locations\u201d off limits to immigration enforcement actions.</p>\n<p>One provision of the new immigration guidelines, issued Tuesday, directs the Department of Homeland Security and immigration enforcement agencies to enlist local law enforcement agencies in efforts to enforce federal immigration law. There is no federal law requiring local agencies to adhere to those guidelines.</p></blockquote>\n<p>&#8220;The message is loud and clear, the state of Connecticut will not allow the Trump administration&#8217;s racist and xenophobic actions to destroy our community,&#8221; Ana Maria Rivera-Forastieri, director of advocacy at Junta for Progressive Action, <a href=\"http://www.courant.com/politics/hc-malloy-immigration-order-20170222-story.html\">said</a> in a written statement.</p>\n<p>Of course, this has nothing to do with racism or xenophobia. It has to do with the law, period.</p>\n<p>&#8220;Regardless of whether your family settled in Connecticut 300 years ago or three days ago, you are welcome here,&#8221; Malloy said in his State of the State address in January.</p>\n<p>Again, settling in the state is not the issue. Settling illegally is the issue.</p>\n<p>The memo is purely a political move. It is a recommendation, not an order. Among other recommendations are the following:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Contacting an attorney if an ICE agent shows up at a school</li>\n<li>Confirming that agents have a warrant before letting them into the school and if an agent presents a warrant, reviewing it carefully &#8220;to determine exactly what it authorizes ICE to do, and who issued it.&#8221;</li>\n</ul>\n<p>These seem like common sense things to me.</p>\n<p>Leslie Torres-Rodriguez, Hartford&#8217;s acting superintendent of schools, issued support for children who are in the state illegally.</p>\n<p>&#8220;Hartford&#8217;s legal and moral obligation to educate all children will not be derailed by any of the language in recent presidential executive orders,&#8221; she said. &#8220;We want each and every student to be in school and ready to learn in safe environment.&#8221;</p>\n<p>While I realize the issue of children being brought in illegally by illegal parents is a delicate issue, the fact of the matter is that these people want to talk about &#8220;legal and moral obligations,&#8221; but don&#8217;t want to follow the law.</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<p>CTPost reported on a mayor and a police chief&#8217;s criticism of Malloy&#8217;s memo:</p>\n<blockquote><p>Danbury Mayor <a href=\"http://www.ctpost.com/search/?action=search&amp;channel=local&amp;inlineLink=1&amp;searchindex=gsa&amp;query=%22Mark+Boughton%22\">Mark Boughton</a> criticized Malloy\u2019s action and said that the city stands ready to help ICE with Trump\u2019s crackdown and mass deportation plans. He estimated that Danbury has approximately 5,000 undocumented residents.</p>\n<p>For now, Boughton said nothing has changed in Danbury regardless of what federal or state officials have issued this week in memos and news releases. But that could change if formal action is taken by the feds.</p>\n<p>\u201cIf any federal agency, including ICE, asks for our assistance, we\u2019re going to provide it as we always have done,\u201d he said. \u201cWe\u2019ll work as a force multiplier.\u201d</p>\n<p>\u201cThe governor just wants to score political points by saying \u2018we\u2019re going to stand against the president,\u2019\u201d Boughton said.</p>\n<p>City police will comply with the state\u2019s TRUST Act, which limits local police coordination with federal immigration agents, Police Chief <a href=\"http://www.ctpost.com/search/?action=search&amp;channel=local&amp;inlineLink=1&amp;searchindex=gsa&amp;query=%22Patrick+Ridenhour%22\">Patrick Ridenhour</a> said Wednesday.</p>\n<p>\u201cRight now we have the TRUST act,\u201d Danbury Police Chief Patrick Ridenhour said. \u201cIf someone\u2019s arrested on minor offenses, with no record, no detainers or warrants from ICE, we\u2019re not calling them. The bulk of our work is enforcing local ordinances and state laws. We don\u2019t make the first call to ICE, we don\u2019t start something.\u201d</p>\n<p>Ridenhour said that circumstances could change with further guidance from the federal government.</p>\n<p>\u201cUntil we get more clarification, we\u2019re going to handle this the way we have,\u201d he said. \u201cRight now, we have the TRUST act, I\u2019ve already reminded my staff of the TRUST act.\u201d</p></blockquote>\n<p>Mayor Boughton also said, \u201cICE doesn\u2019t do random stops. When they call, they\u2019re looking for somebody.\u201d</p>\n<p>Random stops would be a clear violation of the 4th Amendment, much like the TSA engages in each and every time it detains, searches or seizes anything from an American citizen at the airport.</p>\n<p>\u201cIf you\u2019re in the country and your undocumented, it\u2019s a problem,\u201d Boughton said. \u201cYou can\u2019t say it\u2019s not a problem.\u201d</p>\n<p>Thank you Mayor Boughton.
