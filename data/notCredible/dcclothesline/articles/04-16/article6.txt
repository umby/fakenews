hentry category-uncategorized\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<h1 class=\"entry-title\">The Last Country We \u201cLiberated\u201d from an \u201cEvil\u201d Dictator Is Now Openly Trading Slaves</h1>\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-meta\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<span class=\"meta-prep meta-prep-author\">Posted on</span> <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/04/16/the-last-country-we-liberated-from-an-evil-dictator-is-now-openly-trading-slaves/\" title=\"5:41 am\" rel=\"bookmark\"><span class=\"entry-date\">April 16, 2017</span></a> <span class=\"meta-sep\">by</span> <span class=\"author vcard\"><a class=\"url fn n\" href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/author/carey-wedler/\" title=\"View all posts by Carey Wedler\">Carey Wedler</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p><b>(</b><a href=\"http://theantimedia.org/\"><b>ANTIMEDIA</b></a><b>)</b> It is widely known that the U.S.-led NATO intervention to topple Libya\u2019s Muammar Gaddafi in 2011 resulted in a power vacuum that has <a href=\"http://www.newyorker.com/news/news-desk/isis-rises-in-libya\" target=\"_blank\">allowed</a> terror groups like ISIS to gain a foothold in the country.<span id=\"more-71637\"></span></p>\n<p>Despite the destructive consequences of the 2011 invasion, the West is currently taking a similar trajectory with regard to Syria. Just as the Obama administration excoriated Gaddafi in 2011, highlighting his human rights abuses and insisting he must be removed from power to protect the Libyan people, the Trump administration is now pointing to the repressive policies of Bashar al-Assad in Syria and <a href=\"http://www.scmp.com/news/world/europe/article/2086806/no-place-assad-syrias-future-says-us-secretary-state-rex-tillerson\">warning</a> his regime will soon <a href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GFmOzeLbu0c\">come to an end</a> \u2014 all in the name of protecting Syrian civilians.</p>\n<p>But as the U.S. and its allies fail to produce legal grounds for their recent air strike \u2014 let alone provide concrete evidence to back up their claims Assad was responsible for a deadly chemical attack last week \u2014 more hazards of invading foreign countries and removing their heads of state are emerging.</p>\n<p>This week, new findings <a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/apr/10/libya-public-slave-auctions-un-migration\">revealed</a> another unintended consequence of \u201chumanitarian intervention\u201d: the growth of the human slave trade.</p>\n<p>The <i>Guardian</i> <a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/apr/10/libya-public-slave-auctions-un-migration\">reports</a> that while \u201cviolence, extortion and slave labor\u201d have been a reality for people trafficked through Libya in the past, the slave trade has recently expanded. Today, people are selling other human beings out in the open.</p>\n<p>\u201c<i>The latest reports of \u2018slave markets\u2019 for migrants can be added to a long list of outrages [in Libya],</i>\u201d said Mohammed Abdiker, head of operation and emergencies for the International Office of Migration, an intergovernmental organization that promotes \u201c<em>humane and orderly migration for the benefit of all</em>,\u201d <a href=\"https://www.iom.int/about-iom\">according</a> to its website. \u201c<i>The situation is dire. The more IOM engages inside Libya, the more we learn that it is a vale of tears for all too many migrants</i>.\u201d</p>\n<p>The North African country is commonly used as a point of exit for refugees fleeing other parts of the continent. But since Gaddafi was overthrown in 2011, \u201c<i>the vast, sparsely populated country has slid into violent chaos and migrants with little cash and usually no papers are particularly vulnerable,</i>\u201d the <i>Guardian</i> explains.</p>\n<p>One survivor from Senegal said he was passing through Libya from Niger with a group of other migrants attempting to flee their home countries. They had paid a smuggler to transport them via bus to the coast, where they would risk taking a boat to Europe. But rather than take them to the coast, the smuggler took them to a dusty lot in Sabha, Libya. According to Livia Manente, an IOM officer who interviews survivors, \u201c<i>their driver suddenly said middlemen had not passed on his fees and put his passengers up for sale.</i>\u201d</p>\n<p>\u201c<i>Several other migrants confirmed his story, independently describing kinds of slave markets as well as kinds of private prisons all over in Libya,</i>\u201d she said, adding IOM Italy had confirmed similar stories from migrants landing in southern Italy.</p>\n<p>The Senegalese survivor said he was taken to a <a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/feb/28/refugee-women-and-children-beaten-raped-and-starved-in-libyan-hellholes\">makeshift prison</a>, which the <i>Guardian</i> notes are common in Libya.</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<p><i>\u201cThose held inside are forced to work without pay, or on meager rations, and their captors regularly call family at home demanding a ransom. His captors asked for 300,000 west African francs (about \u00a3380), then sold him on to a larger jail where the demand doubled without explanation.\u201d</i></p>\n<p>When migrants were held too long without having a ransom paid for them, they were taken away and killed. <i>\u201cSome wasted away on meager rations in unsanitary conditions, dying of hunger and disease, but overall numbers never fell,\u201d</i> the <i>Guardian</i> reported.</p>\n<p><i>\u201cIf the number of migrants goes down, because of death or someone is ransomed, the kidnappers just go to the market and buy one</i>,\u201d Manente said.</p>\n<p>Giuseppe Loprete, IOM Niger\u2019s chief of mission, confirmed these disturbing reports. <i>\u201cIt\u2019s very clear they see themselves as being treated as slaves,\u201d</i> he said. He arranged for the repatriation of 1,500 migrants just in the first three months of this year and is concerned more stories and incidents will emerge as more migrants return from Libya.</p>\n<p>\u201c<i>And conditions are worsening in Libya so I think we can also expect more in the coming months</i>,\u201d he added.</p>\n<p>As the United States government continues to entertain regime change in Syria as a viable solution to the many crises in that country, it is becoming ever-more evident that ousting dictators \u2014 however detestable they may be \u2014 \u00a0is not effective. Toppling Saddam Hussein led not only to the deaths of civilians and <a href=\"https://www.thenation.com/article/what-i-discovered-from-interviewing-isis-prisoners/\">radicalization</a>\u00a0within the population, but also the <a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/middle-east/how-saddam-husseins-former-military-officers-and-spies-are-controlling-isis-10156610.html\">rise of ISIS</a>.</p>\n<p>As Libya, once a beacon of stability in the region, continues to devolve in the fallout from the Western \u201chumanitarian\u201d intervention \u2013 and as human beings are dragged into emerging slave trades while <a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/feb/28/refugee-women-and-children-beaten-raped-and-starved-in-libyan-hellholes\">rapes and kidnappings</a> plague the <a href=\"https://www.amnesty.org.uk/press-releases/libya-wave-abductions-torture-and-gang-rape-driving-mediterranean-migrant-exodus-new\">population</a> \u2014 it is increasingly obvious that further war will only create even further suffering in unforeseen ways.</p>\n\n\n<h4><strong>Don't forget to follow the D.C.
