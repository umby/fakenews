Anti-Semitic And Racist&#8217; Search Results</a></h1>\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-meta\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<span class=\"author-link\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\tPosted by \t\t\t\t\t\t<a .entry-meta -->\n\t\t</header><!-- .entry-header -->\n\n\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t<p><a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2016/11/19/facebook-and-google-ready-to-kill-alternative-media-for-the-government/\" target=\"_blank\">Google</a> is going full social justice mode and enlisting a 10,000 strong &#8220;army&#8221; to flag search results deemed &#8220;upsetting&#8221; or &#8220;offensive.&#8221;<span id=\"more-70621\"></span></p>\n<p>From <a href=\"http://searchengineland.com/google-flag-upsetting-offensive-content-271119\">SearchEngineLand</a>:</p>\n<blockquote><p>Google is undertaking a new effort to better identify content that is potentially upsetting or offensive to searchers. It hopes this will\u00a0prevent such content\u00a0from crowding out factual, accurate and trustworthy information in the top search results.</p>\n<p><b>&#8220;We&#8217;re explicitly avoiding the term &#8216;fake news,&#8217; because we think it is too vague,&#8221;</b> said Paul Haahr, one of Google&#8217;s senior engineers who is involved with\u00a0search quality. <b>&#8220;Demonstrably inaccurate information, however, we want to target.&#8221;</b></p></blockquote>\n<p>You can see in their guide this is a demonstrably false statement and they specifically told their army to flag websites which could be considered &#8220;upsetting&#8221; or &#8220;offensive.&#8221;</p>\n<blockquote><p>The effort revolves around Google&#8217;s <a href=\"http://searchengineland.com/library/google/google-search-quality-raters\">quality raters</a>, over 10,000 contractors that Google uses worldwide\u00a0to evaluate search results. These raters are given actual searches to conduct, drawn from real searches that Google sees. They then rate pages that appear in the top results as to how good those seem as answers.</p>\n<p>&#8230;Quality raters use\u00a0a set of <a href=\"https://static.googleusercontent.com/media/www.google.com/en//insidesearch/howsearchworks/assets/searchqualityevaluatorguidelines.pdf\">guidelines</a> that are nearly\u00a0200 pages long, instructing them on how to assess website quality and whether the results they review meet the needs of those who might search for particular queries.</p>\n<p><b>Those\u00a0guidelines have been updated with an entirely new section\u00a0about &#8220;Upsetting-Offensive&#8221; content that covers a new\u00a0flag that&#8217;s been added for raters to use. Until now, pages could not be flagged by raters with this designation.</b></p>\n<p>The guidelines say that upsetting or offensive content typically includes the following things (the bullet points below are quoted directly from the guide):</p>\n<ul>\n<li>Content that promotes hate or violence against a group of people based on criteria including (but not limited to) race or ethnicity, religion, gender, nationality or citizenship, disability, age, sexual orientation, or veteran status.</li>\n<li>Content with racial slurs or extremely offensive terminology.</li>\n<li>Graphic violence, including animal cruelty or child abuse.</li>\n<li>Explicit how\u00ad to information about harmful activities (e.g., how tos on human trafficking or violent assault).</li>\n<li>Other types of content which users in your locale would find extremely upsetting or offensive.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>The guidelines also include examples. For instance, here&#8217;s one for a search on &#8220;holocaust history,&#8221; giving two different results that might have appeared and how to rate them:</p></blockquote>\n<p><img class=\"aligncenter\" src=\"https://i2.wp.com/www.informationliberation.com/files/searchqualityevaluatorguidelines-20170314-FINAL-PREVIEW_pdf__page_128_of_160_-768x680.png?resize=543%2C481\" data-recalc-dims=\"1\" /></p>\n<blockquote><p>The first result is from a white supremacist\u00a0site. Raters are told it should be flagged as Upsetting-Offensive because many people would find Holocaust denial to be offensive.</p></blockquote>\n<p><img class=\"aligncenter\" src=\"https://i0.wp.com/www.informationliberation.com/files/searchqualityevaluatorguidelines-20170314-FINAL-PREVIEW_pdf__page_127_of_160_-768x439.png?resize=568%2C325\" data-recalc-dims=\"1\" /></p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<blockquote><p>The second result is from The History Channel. Raters are not told to flag this result as Upsetting-Offensive because it&#8217;s a &#8220;factually accurate source of historical information.&#8221;</p>\n<p>In two other examples given, raters are instructed to flag a result said to\u00a0falsely represent a scientific study in an offensive manner and a page that seems to exist solely to promote intolerance:</p></blockquote>\n<p><img class=\"aligncenter\" src=\"https://i2.wp.com/www.informationliberation.com/files/searchqualityevaluatorguidelines-20170314-FINAL-PREVIEW_pdf__page_130_of_160_-768x416.png?resize=518%2C281\" data-recalc-dims=\"1\" /></p>\n<blockquote><p>What happens if content is flagged this way? Nothing immediate. The results\u00a0that quality raters flag\u00a0is used as &#8220;training data&#8221; for Google&#8217;s human coders who write search algorithms, as well as for its machine learning systems. Basically, content of this nature is used to help Google figure out how to automatically\u00a0identify upsetting or offensive content in general.</p></blockquote>\n<p>Indeed, we can&#8217;t have anyone seeing anything which might be &#8220;upsetting&#8221; or &#8220;offensive&#8221; on the internet. Google must be a giant safe-space.</p>\n<p>Of course, if you&#8217;re a Christian who finds content promoting transgenderism and homosexuality &#8220;offensive&#8221; and don&#8217;t want your kid seeing it on YouTube, you&#8217;ll be out of luck.</p>\n<p>What made Google great in the distant past was the way in which their results were determined by sophisticated algorithms rather than by social justice warriors. That&#8217;s been gone for a while now and it&#8217;s clear it&#8217;s only going to get worse.</p>\n<p>Make no mistake, despite their flowery language, this is entirely about censoring the burgeoning &#8220;far-right&#8221; which is surging in America and Europe. Crushing this nationalist revival is their goal and it&#8217;s clear they&#8217;re willing to sacrifice aspects of their business to do it.</p>\n<p>While in the past I never used any search engine other than Google, these days I increasingly find myself being forced to use Bing.</p>\n<p><img class=\"aligncenter\" src=\"https://i1.wp.com/www.informationliberation.com/files/george-soros-is-google.png?resize=521%2C284\" border=\"1\" data-recalc-dims=\"1\" /> <em>Courtesy of <a href=\"http://www.informationliberation.com/?id=56431\" target=\"_blank\">Information Liberation</a></em></p>\n\n\n<h4><strong>Don't forget to follow the D.C.
