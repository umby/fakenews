Zakharova suggested President Barack Obama will go down in history as one of America\u2019s evilest presidents.</p>\n<p>In the program, Zakharova explains that Russia was warned countless times in 2015 during diplomatic talks that if they were to go into Syria to fight \u201cterrorists,\u201d their work would be undone by the media, which would label them the aggressor. This has<a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/sep/25/russia-accused-war-crimes-syria-un-security-council-aleppo\" target=\"_blank\"> clearly has been the case for the past year-and-a-half</a>.</p>\n<p>Zakharova continued:<i>\u201c[T]he media was one thing,\u201d</i> Zakharova noted. <i>\u201cThe U.S. State Department and the White House, from the mouths of their official representatives, directly threatened to cause us pain.\u201d</i></p>\n<p><i>\u201cThen, official Foreign Affairs representatives began to call upon their people to come out and protest in front of Russian embassies.\u201d</i></p>\n<p><i>\u201cThat which began in October 2015 \u2013 ended with the</i><a href=\"http://theantimedia.org/5-stories-instead-oscars/\" target=\"_blank\"> <i>murder of our Ambassador</i></a><i> in December 2016.\u201d</i>This is likely a <a href=\"http://www.bbc.com/news/uk-politics-37628774\" target=\"_blank\">reference</a> to British Foreign Secretary Boris Johnson\u2019s calls for anti-war protesters to target Russia.</p>\n<p><i>\u201cI\u2019ll remind you of one more thing \u2013 we\u2019ve talked about this \u2013 let\u2019s remember the real attacks. Not those campaigns with plastic arms and legs in London, or posters in front of our embassies in the E.U., but the shelling of the Russian Embassy in Damascus,\u201d </i>Zakharova also noted.</p>\n<p>She then vented her frustrations regarding the instances when Vitaly Churkin (another diplomat who<a href=\"http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/russian-ambassador-vitaly-churkin-death-unclear-article-1.2978365?cache=false%252525253fq%252525253dxsshbjlc%252525253c%252525253ed\" target=\"_blank\"> died mysteriously last month</a>) questioned attacks committed by NATO-backed rebels in Syria at the U.N. According to Zakharova, the same diplomats who promised that Russia would feel pain for intervening in Syria were the same people blocking statements condemning these attacks, hindering progress on the issue. In doing so, Zakharova was unable to confirm if she was referring specifically to former secretary of state John Kerry, though this appears to be the implication of her allegations.</p>\n<p>Asked about Obama\u2019s failure to offer condolences for the death of Russia\u2019s ambassador in Turkey in December 2016, Zakharova responded:</p>\n<p><i>\u201cObama, I think, is a unique person\u2026But Obama is a person, and I mean, the whole administration under him, whose team \u2014 as it worked out on the international arena \u2014 was bad for everyone. I think during his 8-year term, under the pretext of their \u201cexclusivity,\u201d they became the subject of disgust for the entire world.
