22, 2017</span></a> <span class=\"meta-sep\">by</span> <span class=\"author vcard\"><a class=\"url fn n\" href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/author/claire-bernish/\" title=\"View all posts by Claire Bernish\">Claire Bernish</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p>Wikileaks founder Julian Assange called <i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cvery, very, very inaccurate\u201d</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> corporate presstitutes </span><b><i>\u201copportunistic snipers\u201d</i></b><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> in an interview over the weekend, accusing newspapers of printing </span><b><i>\u201cweaponized text.\u201d</i></b><span id=\"more-69831\"></span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">And the outspoken publisher isn\u2019t at all dissatisfied with the current propaganda of \u201c<a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/wapost-stop-using-fake-news-failure/\">fake news</a>\u201d \u2014 because, as Assange explained to Australian comedian Chas Licciardello, </span><a href=\"https://www.cnet.com/news/julian-assange-wikileaks-very-happy-about-fake-news-election-clinton-podesta-emails/\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">according to</span></a> <i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">CNET</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">,</span></p>\n<p><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cWikiLeaks is very happy that there is a narrative about fake news out there because we have a perfect record of having never got it wrong in terms of authentications.\u201d</span></i></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">While the corporate press continues misrepresenting information, quoting unnamed and thus unverifiable sources, and even printing flagrant fabrications, Wikileaks indeed maintains an unparalleled streak of publishing authentic and undoctored data \u2014 despite excoriation from the establishment \u2014 thus eliminating all bias or unintended insertion of opinion.</span></p>\n<p><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cWhen the narrative of fake news came out and was then taken off effectively by the neo-liberal press and pushed around,\u201d</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> Assange elaborated. </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cI could see exactly where that was going. I was rather happy about it.\u201d</span></i></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">But the outspoken publisher\u2019s opinion on so-called fake news emphasizes the disastrous failings of a mainstream media hell bent on propping up its own establishment agenda \u2014 regardless of which political affiliation a given outlet claims.</span></p>\n<p><b><i>\u201cWhat is special about WikiLeaks is that it\u2019s not just another damn story,\u201d</i></b><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> noted Assange, </span><b><i>\u201cit\u2019s not just another damn journalist putting their damn byline, advertising themselves and their position on another damn story.\u201d</i></b></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Further, he said, discussing Wikileaks\u2019 searchable database, </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cYou\u2019re not reading pre-weaponised knowledge. </span></i><b><i>When you read a newspaper article, you are reading weaponised text that is designed to affect a person just like you.\u201d</i></b></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Assange\u2019s description of weaponized text characterizes the corporate press\u2019 penchant for propaganda \u2014 a political establishment-friendly slant that has once-illustrious media institutions like the <em>Washington Post</em> and <em>New York Times</em> frivolously <a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/washington-post-fake-hacking-story/\">abandoning</a> journalistic integrity whenever it\u2019s deemed necessary.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">In fact, after the </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Post</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> published a slew of articles without any basis in fact, </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">The Free Thought Project</span></i> <a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/wapost-fake-news-weaponized-journalism/\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">noted</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> in January,</span></p>\n<p><b><i>\u201cThis latest astounding deviation from the facts, however, makes indisputably clear the weaponization of news.</i></b><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> Journalists and media outlets make mistakes from time to time, but a pattern and practice of publishing unfounded, unverified, and fraudulent articles cannot be characterized simply as irresponsible.</span></i></p>\n<p><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cWe are in the midst of an information war of epic proportions \u2014 led haplessly astray of the truth with the Post leading the way \u2014 and it\u2019s a dangerous and frightening portent of things to come, not the least of which will be propagandized truth and heavy-handed censorship.\u201d</span></i></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">That assessment has proven true, as what appears to be recklessness in corporate journalism generally gains editorial approval if the article appeals to emotion and will manipulate readers into a specific thought pattern \u2014 like both anti-Russia and anti-Iran reports so frequently topping mainstream headlines.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Of course, no coincidence exists in the corporate press\u2019 continued stark criticism of Wikileaks as a politically-motivated organization \u2014 an allegation oft repeated during the last presidential election cycle.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Such accusations only act to divert attention to the corrupt ugliness
