reported missing in D.C. since the beginning of the year. This startling number has forced the hands of several officials who\u2019ve written a letter to call on special help from the Justice Department in investigating the matter.<span id=\"more-70898\"></span></p>\n<p>The letter, <a href=\"http://staging.hosted.ap.org/dynamic/stories/U/US_CONGRESS_MISSING_BLACK_CHILDREN?SITE=AP&amp;SECTION=HOME&amp;TEMPLATE=DEFAULT&amp;CTIME=2017-03-23-17-16-58#e9c1bdf1-72b8-4a0b-9a3f-35e7f9f58649\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"nofollow\">obtained by the Associated Press</a>, asked FBI Director James Comey and Attorney General Jeff Sessions to <strong>\u201cdevote the resources necessary to determine whether these developments are an anomaly or whether they are indicative of an underlying trend that must be addressed.\u201d</strong> It was signed by Congressional Black Caucus Chairman Cedric Richmond (D-La) and Del. Eleanor Holmes Norton, who represents D.C. in Congress.</p>\n<p>\u201cTen children of color went missing in our nation\u2019s capital in a period of two weeks and at first garnered very little media attention. That\u2019s deeply disturbing,\u201d Richmond\u2019s letter said.</p>\n<p>As the AP reports, the District of Columbia logged 501 cases of missing juveniles, many of them black or Latino, in the first three months of this year, according to the Metropolitan Police Department, the city\u2019s police force. Twenty-two were unsolved as of March 22, police said.</p>\n<p>The Twitter profile for the DC police department is quite literally riddled with images of missing young black and latino girls. In spite of the officials\u2019 concern and the posts on Twitter, police are assuring the public that there is nothing out of the ordinary.</p>\n<blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"550\">\n<p lang=\"en\" dir=\"ltr\">Missing Person: Anjel Burl, 16, last seen 3/22 in 200 block of 43rd Rd., NE. Police Department, <a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/d-c-cop-caught-running-child-prostitution-ring-apartment-missing-girl/\">was exposed by the Free Thought Project</a> for running a child sex ring out of his house.
