Darius Shahtahmasebi\">Darius Shahtahmasebi</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p>On \u00a0February 7, 2014, the <i>BBC</i> published a <a href=\"http://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-26079957\" target=\"_blank\">transcript</a> of a bugged phone conversation between Assistant Secretary of State <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2014/02/07/fk-e-u-victoria-nuland-recorded-alleged-phone-chat-regarding-ukraine/\" target=\"_blank\">Victoria Nuland</a> and the U.S. Ambassador to Ukraine, Geoffrey Pyatt. The media reports that followed the leak distracted audiences from the <span id=\"more-71290\"></span>actual content of the phone call, <a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/feb/07/angela-merkel-victoria-nuland-eu-unacceptable\" target=\"_blank\">focusing</a> instead on Nuland\u2019s crass statement, \u201c<a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2014/02/07/fk-e-u-victoria-nuland-recorded-alleged-phone-chat-regarding-ukraine/\" target=\"_blank\"><i>Fuck the EU</i></a>.\u201d</p>\n<p>If the media had done its job, a simple analysis of the phone call would have revealed something wholly controversial about the Ukrainian crisis: the U.S. was handpicking who they wanted to form Ukraine\u2019s government after its former president, Viktor Yanukovych, was ousted from power.</p>\n<div class=\"insert-post-ads\"></div>\n<p><i>\u201cGood. I don\u2019t think Klitsh should go into the government. I don\u2019t think it\u2019s necessary, I don\u2019t think it\u2019s a good idea,\u201d</i> Nuland said in the call.</p>\n<p>Pyatt responded:</p>\n<div class=\"insert-post-ads\"></div>\n<p><i>\u201cYeah. I guess\u2026 in terms of him not going into the government, just let him stay out and do his political homework and stuff. I\u2019m just thinking in terms of sort of the process moving ahead we want to keep the moderate democrats together. The problem is going to be Tyahnybok [Oleh Tyahnybok, the other opposition leader] and his guys and I\u2019m sure that\u2019s part of what [President Viktor] Yanukovych is calculating on all this.</i><i>\u201d</i></p>\n<p>Nuland added:</p>\n<p><i>\u201cI think Yats is the guy who\u2019s got the economic experience, the governing experience. He\u2019s the\u2026 what he needs is Klitsch and Tyahnybok on the outside. He needs to be talking to them four times a week, you know. I just think Klitsch going in\u2026 he\u2019s going to be at that level working for Yatseniuk, it\u2019s just not going to work.</i><i>\u201d</i></p>\n<p>According to a cable<a href=\"https://wikileaks.org/plusd/cables/06KIEV1706_a.html\" target=\"_blank\"> obtained</a> by Wikileaks, Yanukovych\u2019s successor, Petro Poroshenko, previously worked as a mole for the U.S. State Department. The State Department even referred to Poroshenko as \u201cour Ukrainian insider.\u201d He <a href=\"https://www.foreignaffairs.com/articles/russian-federation/2017-03-10/russia-trump-and-new-d-tente?cid=int-now&amp;pgtype=hpg&amp;region=br2\" target=\"_blank\">currently</a> has a 17 percent approval rating.</p>\n<p>What this amounts to is tangible and credible evidence that the U.S. interfered or attempted to interfere in a government that borders Russia in order to remove a pro-Russian leader and replace him with a very well-established American puppet. History shows that Ukraine is no exception to this American habit, as there is documented evidence to show the U.S. has<a href=\"http://theantimedia.org/media-ignoring-russia-hillary-losing/\" target=\"_blank\"> interfered</a> overtly and covertly in the governments of Guatemala, Congo, Dominican Republic, South Vietnam, Brazil, Chile, Hawaii, Cuba, the Philippines, Puerto Rico, Nicaragua, Honduras, Panama, Grenada, Afghanistan, Iraq, and Iran, to name a few.</p>\n<p>These are undisputed facts of history. In fact, these events are so common that no one seems to give the incidents so much as an afterthought. Despite this evidence, the media is unironically <a href=\"https://theintercept.com/2017/03/16/key-democratic-officials-now-warning-base-not-to-expect-evidence-of-trumprussia-collusion/\" target=\"_blank\">claiming</a> it was Russia that interfered in American politics to promote a pro-Russian candidate and have him take control of the White House.</p>\n<p>Since Trump was elected at the end of 2016, we have seen<a href=\"https://theintercept.com/2016/12/10/anonymous-leaks-to-the-washpost-about-the-cias-russia-beliefs-are-no-substitute-for-evidence/\" target=\"_blank\"> story after story</a>,<a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/americas/us-politics/donald-trump-russia-links-scandal-robert-reich-us-labor-secretary-bill-clinton-american-history-a7660591.html\" target=\"_blank\"> headline after headline</a>, and<a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/americas/donald-trump-staff-wiping-electronic-devices-subpoena-fbi-investigation-russia-a7651276.html\" target=\"_blank\"> rabbit hole after rabbit hole</a> alleging that somehow, Russia was able to \u201chack\u201d the election and bolster support for Trump, which eventually culminated in Trump\u2019s victory.</p>\n<p>So, let\u2019s review the evidence for these claims.</p>\n<p><a href=\"https://theintercept.com/2017/03/16/key-democratic-officials-now-warning-base-not-to-expect-evidence-of-trumprussia-collusion/\" target=\"_blank\">There is none</a> \u2014 and if there is, it still hasn\u2019t been made public.</p>\n<p>Is there a leaked phone call of Trump and/or Putin? No.</p>\n<p>Considering the CIA and other intelligence agencies\u2019 long reach and incredible resources, is there any evidence to show Russia hacked the election \u2014 or that they interfered at all with a view of promoting Trump?</p>\n<p>No<i>.</i></p>\n<p>This is a<a href=\"http://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-39435786\" target=\"_blank\"> direct quote</a> from <i>BBC</i> on this topic from as recently as last week:</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<p><i>\u201cSo far, no single piece of evidence has been made public proving that the Trump campaign joined with Russia to steal the US presidency \u2013 nothing.</i><i>\u201d</i></p>\n<p>No one even seems to be remotely concerned with the illogical and overly imaginative effluvium contaminating this story. Clinton\u2019s election campaign had the<a href=\"http://theantimedia.org/trump-brexit-media-failed-public/\" target=\"_blank\"> backing</a> of 57 of America\u2019s 100 largest newspapers. Globally, she had the backing of 500 outlets; Trump had the support of a mere 26. The Clinton Foundation received substantial donations from Saudi Arabia and Qatar,<a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/julian-assange-clinton-foundation-isis-same-money-saudi-arabia-qatar-funding-a7397211.html\" target=\"_blank\"> two known financial supporters of the terror group ISIS</a>. Even the<a href=\"http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&amp;objectid=11780357\" target=\"_blank\"> New Zealand government donated millions of taxpayer dollars to Clinton</a>.</p>\n<p>Despite this overwhelming worldwide support, anti-Russian conspiracy theorists are convinced Russia was somehow able to hack the election and portray Hillary Clinton as an untrustworthy, pathological liar \u2014 and as a result, gift the election win over to Trump.</p>\n<p>But Clinton earned her<a href=\"http://www.counterpunch.org/2016/10/21/hillary-clinton-and-the-brutal-murder-of-qaddafi/\" target=\"_blank\"> detestable reputation all by herself</a> \u2014 without Russian interference. Further, it was actually<a href=\"http://www.salon.com/2016/11/09/the-hillary-clinton-campaign-intentionally-created-donald-trump-with-its-pied-piper-strategy/\" target=\"_blank\"> Clinton\u2019s election strategy to \u201celevate\u201d Trump in the first place</a>, not Russia\u2019s. This, in essence, gave him free airtime and ultimately underestimated how popular he was going to be with the American public.</p>\n<p>Finally, Clinton still<a href=\"http://edition.cnn.com/2016/12/21/politics/donald-trump-hillary-clinton-popular-vote-final-count/\" target=\"_blank\"> won the popular vote</a>\u00a0by a considerable margin. Did the Russian strategy of \u201chacking\u201d the election and boosting Trump factor this into account, as well? Was Russia able to strategically generate the support required from each of the states needed to win the election and discount the popular vote? How would that have even worked?</p>\n<p>There is an overwhelming amount of evidence to suggest that if the DNC hadn\u2019t <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2016/07/23/us/politics/dnc-emails-sanders-clinton.html?_r=0\" target=\"_blank\">purposely killed</a> Bernie Sanders\u2019 campaign, <a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/americas/us-elections/bernie-sanders-beaten-donald-trump-pre-election-poll-a7412636.html\" target=\"_blank\">Trump would never have won the election in the first place</a>.
