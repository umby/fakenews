id=\"more-70946\"></span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Even though audits of all federal agencies became mandatory in 1996, the Pentagon has apparently <a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2017/mar/20/pentagon-never-audited-astonishing-military-spending\">made itself an exception</a>, and \u2014 fully 20 years later \u2014 stands obstinately orotund in never having complied.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Because, as defense officials insist \u2014 summoning their best impudent adolescent \u2014 an audit the </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Guardian</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> recently. </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cMeanwhile, Congress has more than doubled the Pentagon\u2019s budget.\u201d</span></i></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Worse, President Trump\u2019s newly-proposed budget seeks to toss an </span><a evidently bottomless pit that is the U.S. military \u2014 more for interventionist policy, more for resource-plundering, more for proxy fighting, and, of course, more for jets and drones to drop more bombs suspiciously often on civilians.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Maybe.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Because, without the mandated audit, the DoD could be purchasing damned near </span><a href=\"http://theantimedia.org/the-pentagon-doesnt-know-what-it-spent-8-5-trillion-dollars-on/\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">anything</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">, at any cost, and use, or give, it \u2014 to anyone, for any reason.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Officials with the Government Accountability Office and Office of the Inspector General have </span><a href=\"http://www.dodig.mil/pubs/report_summary.cfm?id=5890\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">catalogued</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: literally, on-the-books-corrupt practices in tandem \u2014 to what would incontrovertibly be a most unpleasant audit, indeed.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Take the following of incapable of performing the job without doing so \u2014 without ever having faced consequences for this brazen cooking of books.</span></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">To wit, </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Reuters</span></i> <a href=\"http://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-pentagon-waste-specialreport-idUSBRE9AH0LQ20131118\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">reported</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> the results of an investigation into Defense\u2019s magical number-crunching \u2014 </span><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">well over three years ago</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">, on November 18, 2013 \u2014 detailing the illicit tasks of 15-year employee, </span><b><i>\u201cLinda Woodford [who] spent the last 15 years of her career inserting phony numbers in the U.S. Department of Defense\u2019s accounts.\u201d</i></b></p>\n<p><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">Woodford, who has since retired, and others like her, act as individual pieces in the amassing chewed gum only appearing to plug a damning mishandling of funds pilfered from the American people to fund wars overseas for resources in the name of U.S. defense.</span></p>\n<p><i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">\u201cEvery month until she retired in 2011,\u201d</span></i><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\"> Scot J. Murkily </span><a href=\"http://www.reuters.com/investigates/pentagon/#article/part2\"><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">deemed</span></a><span style=\"font-weight: 400;\">, \u201cunsubstantiated
