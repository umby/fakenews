shifts that we are now witnessing.\u00a0 Of course there are a whole host of reasons why people would want to move away from huge cities such as Chicago, Detroit, Baltimore and Cleveland.\u00a0 For some families, it simply comes down to wanting a better life for their children.\u00a0 But as you will see below, there are others that believe that things in this country are about to take an apocalyptic turn, and the big cities will not be a place that you want to be when economic collapse, rioting, looting, civil unrest and crime are all spiraling out of control.</p>\n<p>According to data just released by the U.S. Census Bureau, Chicago took the prize for the biggest population loss from 2015 to 2016, and it was followed by <a title=\"Detroit and Baltimore\" Census Bureau</a>.</p>\n<p>Cook County, Ill., where Chicago is the county seat, had the largest population loss of any county in the country from 2015 and 2016.</p></blockquote>\n<p>Cook County alone had a \u201cdomestic migration\u201d loss of more than 66,000 people.\u00a0 That is a staggering number of people to lose in a single year.</p>\n<p>Cleveland and Milwaukee were also very high on the list, and some are pointing out that all of these cities are in relatively colder climates and are all struggling economically.</p>\n<p>So you certainly can\u2019t blame people living in these cities for wanting to find somewhere warmer and more economically prosperous to live.</p>\n<p>But others that are moving away from large cities are deeply concerned about where things are ultimately headed in this country.\u00a0 It doesn\u2019t take a genius to see that anger, frustration and hatred are rising all around us, and many believe that conditions are ripe for civil unrest and civil conflict.\u00a0 In fact, best-selling author Doug Casey believes that we could soon see a <a title=\"\u201ccivil war\u201d\" time when the U.S. political landscape was transitioning from the Carter Administration to the Reagan Administration. Now, Casey sees a coming crisis that is equal or worse than the Civil War.\u00a0 Casey explains, \u201cIn the U.S. right now, there seems to be so much antagonism it\u2019s almost like pre-Civil War.\u00a0 There is actually hatred in the U.S. at this point. \u00a0It used to be the Republicans and Democrats could disagree, but they could have a civil conversation about a difference of opinion.\u00a0 Now, it\u2019s active hatred between these two groups. \u00a0This is not going to end well.\u201d</p>\n<p><span id=\"more-18729\"></span>Casey thinks the coming financial collapse will be the trigger. Casey says, \u201cIt\u2019s going to come down eventually.\u00a0 I am worried about that, but we are in a situation where the country seem like it is just before a civil war.\u00a0 It will be more serious than just a financial collapse, and it is likely to be set off by a financial collapse.\u201d</p></blockquote>\n<p>Without a doubt, our financial system is <a title=\"certainly primed\" href=\"http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/have-we-reached-a-turning-point-for-stocks-tuesday-was-the-worst-day-for-the-stock-market-in-6-months\">certainly primed</a> for a financial collapse, and when things get really, really bad in this country how will people respond?</p>\n<p>Many have decided that they want to get away from the major population centers before we find out the answer to that question.</p>\n<p>For example, not too long ago <a title=\"the Chicago Tribune\" href=\"http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/nationworld/ct-american-redoubt-prepper-movement-20160827-story.html\" target=\"_blank\">the Chicago Tribune</a> ran a story about why so many preppers are moving to the Great Northwest.\u00a0 One of the individuals profiled was an ex-resident of California named Trevor Treller who moved up to north Idaho prior to the recent election\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p>Trevor Treller, 44, who carries a small Smith &amp; Wesson pistol on his hip, moved to north Idaho last year from Long Beach, California, and recently paid a little less than $400,000 for a defensible three-bedroom house on five wooded acres.</p>\n<p>Treller, a sommelier at a local resort, said Obama was a key factor in his decision. He said the president has inflamed racial tensions in America, presided over a dangerous expansion of the national debt, been \u201chostile\u201d to Second Amendment rights and failed to curtail the nuclear ambitions of North Korea and Iran.</p>\n<p>Treller said any one of those factors could lead to crippling chaos, so he and his wife have laid in food, weapons and ammunition and are installing
