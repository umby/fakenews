href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/author/michael-snyder/\" title=\"View all posts by Michael Snyder\">Michael Snyder</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p><a href=\"http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/virtually-everyone-agrees-that-current-stock-market-valuations-are-not-sustainable-and-that-a-great-crash-is-coming/stock-market-collapse-toilet-paper-public-domain-3\" rel=\"attachment wp-att-11838\"><img class=\"aligncenter size-large wp-image-11838\" src=\"https://i0.wp.com/theeconomiccollapseblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Stock-Market-Collapse-Toilet-Paper-Public-Domain-460x306.jpg?resize=460%2C306\" alt=\"Stock Market Collapse Toilet Paper - Public Domain\" data-recalc-dims=\"1\" /></a>Current stock market valuations are not sustainable.\u00a0 If there is one thing that I want you to remember from this article, it is that cold, hard fact.\u00a0 In 1929, 2000 and 2008, stock prices soared to absolutely absurd levels just before horrible stock market crashes.\u00a0 What goes up must eventually come down, and the stock market bubble of today will be no <span id=\"more-69844\"></span>exception.\u00a0 In fact, virtually everyone in the financial community acknowledges that stock prices are irrationally high right now.\u00a0 Some are suggesting that there is still time to jump in and make money before the crash comes, while others are recommending a much more cautious approach.\u00a0 But what almost everyone agrees on is the fact that stocks cannot go up like this forever.</p>\n<p>On Tuesday, the Dow, the S&amp;P 500 and the Nasdaq all set brand new record highs once again.\u00a0 Overall, U.S. stocks are now up <a title=\"more than 10 percent\" href=\"http://www.cnbc.com/2017/02/21/us-markets.html\" target=\"_blank\">more than 10 percent</a> since the election, and this is probably the greatest post-election stock market rally in our entire history.</p>\n<p>But stocks were already tremendously overvalued before the election, and at this point stock prices have reached a level of ridiculousness only matched a couple of times before in the past 100 years.</p>\n<p>Only the most extreme optimists will try to tell you that stock prices can stay this disconnected from economic reality indefinitely.\u00a0 We are in the midst of one of the most outrageous stock market bubbles of all time, and as <a title=\"MarketWatch has noted\" href=\"http://www.marketwatch.com/story/this-money-making-market-bubble-is-about-to-burst-2017-02-08\" target=\"_blank\">MarketWatch has noted</a>, all stock market bubbles eventually burst\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p>The U.S. stock market at this level reflects a combination of great demand, great complacency, and great greed. <strong>Stocks are clearly in a bubble, and like all bubbles, this one is about to burst.</strong></p></blockquote>\n<p>If corporations were making tremendous amounts of money, rapidly rising stock prices would make logical sense.</p>\n<p>But that is not the case at all.\u00a0 Corporate earnings for the fourth quarter of 2016 were actually quite dismal, and this disconnect between Wall Street and economic reality is starting to really bug financial analysts <a title=\"such as Brian Sozzi\" href=\"https://www.thestreet.com/story/14009208/1/why-the-stock-market-may-finally-be-dangerously-overvalued.html\" target=\"_blank\">such as Brian Sozzi</a>\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p><strong>The S&amp;P 500 has gone 89 straight sessions without a 1% decline</strong>. Considering that Corporate America didn\u2019t exactly light up on the top and bottom lines during the fourth quarter, such a streak is rather troublesome. Granted, the stock market is a forward-looking mechanism that appears to be trading on hopes that Trump\u2019s unannounced stimulus and tax plans will be lifting economic growth in 2018. Even so, the inability of investors to at least acknowledge persistent struggles among companies and ongoing chaos in Washington is starting to become disturbing.</p></blockquote>\n<p>It is a basic fact of economics that stock prices should accurately reflect current and future earnings.</p>\n<p>So if corporate earnings are at the same level they were at in 2011, why has the S&amp;P 500 risen by 87 percent since then?\u00a0 The following comes from <a title=\"Wolf Richter\" href=\"http://wolfstreet.com/2017/02/19/2016-sp-500-earnings-back-at-2011-levels-as-stocks-ballooned-87-percent/\" target=\"_blank\">Wolf Richter</a>\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p>The S&amp;P 500 stock index edged up to an all-time high of 2,351 on Friday. Total market capitalization of the companies in the index exceeds $20 trillion. <strong>That\u2019s 106% of US GDP, for just 500 companies!</strong> At the end of 2011, the S&amp;P 500 index was at 1,257. <strong>Over the five-plus years since then, it has ballooned by 87%!</strong></p>\n<p>These are superlative numbers, and you\u2019d expect superlative earnings performance from these companies. Turns out, reality is not that cooperative. <strong>Instead, net income of the S&amp;P 500 companies is now back where it first had been at the end of 2011.</strong></p></blockquote>\n<p>The cyclically adjusted price-to-earnings ratio was originally created by <a title=\"author Robert Shiller\" href=\"http://amzn.to/2kJQcgF\" target=\"_blank\">author Robert Shiller</a>, and it is widely regarded as one of the best measures of the true value of stocks in existence.\u00a0 According to <a title=\"the Guardian\" href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/business/2017/feb/16/stock-market-crash-optimism-recklessness-boom-bust\" target=\"_blank\">the Guardian</a>, there have only been two times in our entire history when this ratio has been higher.\u00a0 One was just before the stock market crash of 1929, and the other was just before the bursting of the dotcom bubble\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p>Traditionally, one of the best yardsticks for whether shares are over-valued or under-valued has been the <a class=\"u-underline\" title=\"cyclically adjusted price earnings ratio\" href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclically_adjusted_price-to-earnings_ratio\" target=\"_blank\" data-link-name=\"in body link\">cyclically adjusted price earnings ratio</a> constructed by the economist Robert Shiller. This ratio is currently at about 29 and has only twice been higher: <strong>in 1929 ahead of the Wall Street Crash, and in the last frantic months of the dotcom bubble of the late 1990s.</strong></p></blockquote>\n<p>We can definitely wish for the current euphoria on Wall Street to last for as long as possible, but let there be absolutely no doubt that it is going to end at some point.</p>\n<p>It would take a market decline of 40 or 50 percent to get the cyclically adjusted price-to-earnings ratio back to a level that makes economic sense.\u00a0 Let us hope that the market does not make such a violent move very rapidly, because that would likely be absolutely crippling for our financial system.</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<p>Markets tend to go down a lot faster than they go up, and every other major stock market bubble in U.S. history has ended very badly.</p>\n<p>And this bubble is definitely overdue to burst.\u00a0 The bull market that led up to the great crash of 1929 lasted for 2002 days, and this week the current bull market will finally exceed that record.</p>\n<p>Trying to pick a specific date for a market crash is typically a fruitless exercise, but market watchers are becoming very concerned about some of the signs that we are now seeing.\u00a0 For example, the \u201cCCT indicator\u201d is currently showing <a title=\"\u201cthe lowest bullish energy ever\u201d\" href=\"http://www.marketwatch.com/story/this-money-making-market-bubble-is-about-to-burst-2017-02-08\" target=\"_blank\">\u201cthe lowest bullish energy ever\u201d</a>\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p>The first factor is the CCT indicator. This indicator is a proprietary internal measurement of the general volume of the New York Stock Exchange. The measurements take into account the institutional participation as a ratio of the overall volume. Also measured is the duration of heavy block buying in rallies.</p>\n<p><strong>The sum total of all the measurements now shows the lowest bullish energy ever \u2014 even lower than in 2008, just before the market crash.</strong></p></blockquote>\n<p>In other words, this current bull market appears to be completely and utterly exhausted.</p>\n<p>The laws of economics cannot be defied forever.\u00a0 Traditionally, commodity prices and stock prices have tended to move in unison.\u00a0 And this makes perfect sense, because commodity prices tend to rise when economic conditions are good, and in such an environment stock prices are typically going to move up.</p>\n<p>But now we are in a time when commodity prices and stock prices have become completely disconnected.\u00a0 In order to bring this ratio back into line, the S&amp;P 500 would need to fall by about 1000 points, and such a decline would cause a level of financial chaos that would be absolutely unprecedented.</p>\n<p>This current stock market bubble has lasted much longer than many of the experts originally anticipated, but that just means that the eventual crash will likely be that much more devastating.</p>\n<p>In the end, you don\u2019t need to know all of the technical details in this article.</p>\n<p>But what you do need to know is that current stock market valuations are not sustainable and that a great crash is coming.</p>\n<p>It may not happen next week or next month, but it is going to happen.\u00a0 And when it does happen, it is likely to make what happened in 2008 look like a Sunday picnic.</p>\n<p><em>Courtesy of <a href=\"http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/virtually-everyone-agrees-that-current-stock-market-valuations-are-not-sustainable-and-that-a-great-crash-is-coming\" target=\"_blank\">The Economic Collapse Blog</a></em></p>\n\n\n<h4><strong>Don't forget to follow the D.C.
