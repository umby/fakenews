brands of agricultural and corporate farming herbicides used in the growing fields; in GMO seed crop cultures; and in what\u2019s called \u201cpreharvest staging\u201d [1]. That\u2019s when the herbicide is sprayed several days [3 to 5 <span id=\"more-69981\"></span>days] prior to crop harvest to \u201censure\u201d seed heads mature evenly. Some consider that process acts as a \u201cdesiccant.\u201d</p>\n<p>The more commonly-used herbicide is <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/01/18/roundup-now-proven-to-cause-liver-disease-and-its-in-your-food/\" target=\"_blank\">Roundup\u00ae</a> manufactured by Monsanto. In GMO farming, there is Roundup Ready\u00ae seeds, which are totally different from heirloom or non-GMO seeds. One specific difference is GMO seeds have patents on them, meaning something has been done to modify the seed from the parent or original plant strain produced by Nature.</p>\n<p>Recently on an Internet talk show, I heard a professor talking about the non-browning apple, i.e., the apple\u2019s protein is turned off to make the GMO-non-browning apple not brown when cut and exposed to air, as a normal apple does.</p>\n<p>Well, the question I, as a <em>natural nutritionist,</em> have is: If the protein (0.3 gram or 1% of Daily Value) [3] in the GMO non-browning apple is turned off, does that mean the apple protein is not functional within that GMO apple as a nutrient for human nutrition? Has a scientific nutritional analysis proven that factually one way or the other? Or does science indicate that protein is viable as human nutrition? Because, if not, that would make a real nutritional difference in the GMO non-browning apple!</p>\n<div class=\"sb-widget clearfix\"></div>\n<p>Furthermore, what\u2019s called the \u201cequivalence factor\u201d of GMO phoods really is this, in my opinion: GMO plants have \u2018things\u2019 either inserted or turned off or modified (<em>allowing patents to be issued making food seeds/plants corporate property subject to legal redress</em>) from original parent plants, therefore, GMOs are NOT equivalent to the original plant food, regardless of what GMO science claims!</p>\n<p>Then there\u2019s the inconvenient \u2018byproduct\u2019 of corporate farming; it\u2019s glyphosate residues in processed foods, which has been confirmed scientifically by Food Democracy Now, The Detox Project and their 29-page report \u201cGlyphosate: UNSAFE ON ANY PLATE\u201d[2].The fact food crop seeds or plants have \u201cpatents\u201d should be the prime exclusionary criterion difference, in my opinion, since historical and heirloom foods/seeds/plants did not, and do not, have patents! That\u2019s why the U.S. FDA is out to lunch on GMO phood science, I say, and all GMO phoods legally must be labeled correctly to comply with truth in advertising laws in the USA.</p>\n<p>Below are two charts showing the glyphosate food testing results in parts per billion (ppb) FDN had performed, and the results are nothing short of stunning!
