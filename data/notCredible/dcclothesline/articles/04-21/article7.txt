alt=\"shotgun\" data-recalc-dims=\"1\" /></p>\n<p><a href=\"http://smartgunlaws.org/californias-new-gun-violence-restraining-order-law/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">California</a> has done it, <a href=\"http://clashdaily.com/2017/03/breaking-state-now-pushing-gun-confiscation/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Illinois</a> is trying to do it, and Oregon may be next. These are the states that have laws, or have attempted to pass laws that make <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2015/11/05/mainstream-media-now-openly-admitting-that-gun-control-advocates-want-mass-gun-confiscation-by-force-2/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">gun confiscation</a> very easy. These laws basically allow any police officer or civilian to ask a court to confiscate the <span id=\"more-71791\"></span>firearms of a family member, if that person may be at risk of suicide or domestic violence. That person doesn\u2019t get to offer any input, nor does their need to be very much evidence that the person in question is dangerous.</p>\n<p><a href=\"http://www.breitbart.com/big-government/2017/04/18/republican-state-sen-pushing-gun-confiscation-law-in-oregon/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">The latest proposal comes from Oregon state senator Brian Boquist</a>, whose Navy veteran son committed suicide last year. His bill <em>\u201cwould enable immediate family members to obtain a court order to prevent a person showing signs of wanting to commit suicide or hurt others from obtaining a gun for a year.\u201d</em> The order would also force that person to give up all of their firearms.</p>\n<p>As with California\u2019s version of this law, this bill allows for ex parte court hearings, which means that the gun owner who is going to have his rights taken away, doesn\u2019t have to be present during the hearing.
