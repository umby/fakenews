class=\"entry-date\">March 13, 2017</span></a> <span class=\"meta-sep\">by</span> <span class=\"author vcard\"><a class=\"url fn n\" href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/author/michael-snyder/\" title=\"View all posts by Michael Snyder\">Michael Snyder</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p><a href=\"http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/21714-for-every-man-woman-and-child-in-the-world-this-global-debt-bomb-is-ready-to-explode/debt-bomb-globe-public-domain\" rel=\"attachment wp-att-11913\"><img class=\"aligncenter size-large wp-image-11913\" src=\"https://i1.wp.com/theeconomiccollapseblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/Debt-Bomb-Globe-Public-Domain-460x422.png?resize=460%2C422\" alt=\"Debt Bomb Globe - Public Domain\" data-recalc-dims=\"1\" /></a>According to the International Monetary Fund, global debt has grown to a staggering grand total of <a title=\"152 trillion dollars\" href=\"http://www.cnbc.com/2016/10/06/global-debt-balloons-to-all-time-high-of-152-trillion-imf-warns.html\" target=\"_blank\">152 trillion dollars</a>.\u00a0 Other estimates put that figure closer to <a title=\"200 trillion dollars\" href=\"https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-02-22/the-world-s-debt-is-alarmingly-high-but-is-it-contagious\" target=\"_blank\">200 trillion dollars</a>, but for the purposes of this article let\u2019s use the more conservative number.\u00a0 If you <span id=\"more-70508\"></span>take 152 trillion dollars and divide it by the seven billion people living on the planet, you get $21,714, which would be the share of that debt for every man, woman and child in the world if it was divided up equally.</p>\n<p>So if you have a family of four, your family\u2019s share of the global debt load would be $86,856.</p>\n<p>Very few families could write a check for that amount today, and we also must remember that we live in some of the wealthiest areas on the globe.\u00a0 Considering the fact that <a title=\"more than 3 billion people\" href=\"https://www.google.com/?gws_rd=ssl#q=how+much+of+the+world+lives+on+two+dollars+a+day&amp;*\" target=\"_blank\">more than 3 billion people</a> around the world live on two dollars a day or less, the truth is that about half the planet would not be capable of contributing toward the repayment of our 152 trillion dollar debt at all.\u00a0 So they should probably be excluded from these calculations entirely, and that would mean that your family\u2019s share of the debt would ultimately be far, far higher.</p>\n<p>Of course global debt repayment will never actually be apportioned by family.\u00a0 The reason why I am sharing this example is to show you that it is literally impossible for all of this debt to ever be repaid.</p>\n<p>We are living during the greatest debt bubble in the history of the world, and our financial engineers have got to keep figuring out ways to keep it growing much faster than global GDP because if it ever stops growing it will burst and destroy the entire global financial system.</p>\n<p>Bill Gross, one of the most highly respected financial minds on the entire planet, <a title=\"recently observed\" href=\"https://www.janus.com/insights/bill-gross-investment-outlook/\" target=\"_blank\">recently observed</a> that <strong>\u201cour highly levered financial system is like a truckload of nitro glycerin on a bumpy road\u201d</strong>.</p>\n<p>And he is precisely correct.\u00a0 Everything might seem fine for a while, but one day we are going to hit the wrong bump at the wrong time and the whole thing is going to go KA-BOOM.</p>\n<p>The financial crisis of 2008 represented an opportunity to learn from our mistakes, but instead we just papered over our errors and cranked up the global debt creation machine to levels never seen before.\u00a0 Here is more <a title=\"from Bill Gross\" href=\"https://www.janus.com/insights/bill-gross-investment-outlook/\" target=\"_blank\">from Bill Gross</a>\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p>My lesson continued but the crux of it was that in 2017, <strong>the global economy has created more credit relative to GDP than that at the beginning of 2008\u2019s disaster. In the U.S., credit of $65 trillion is roughly 350% of annual GDP and the ratio is rising. In China, the ratio has more than doubled in the past decade to nearly 300%. Since 2007, China has added $24 trillion worth of debt to its collective balance sheet. Over the same period, the U.S. and Europe only added $12 trillion each.</strong> Capitalism, with its adopted fractional reserve banking system, depends on credit expansion and the printing of additional reserves by central banks, which in turn are re-lent by private banks to create pizza stores, cell phones and a myriad of other products and business enterprises. But the credit creation has limits and the cost of credit (interest rates) must be carefully monitored so that borrowers (think subprime) can pay back the monthly servicing costs. If rates are too high (and credit as a % of GDP too high as well), then potential Lehman black swans can occur. On the other hand, if rates are too low (and credit as a % of GDP declines), then the system breaks down, as savers, pension funds and insurance companies become unable to earn a rate of return high enough to match and service their liabilities.</p></blockquote>\n<p>There is always a price to be paid for going into debt.\u00a0 It mystifies me that so many Americans seem to not understand this very basic principle.</p>\n<p>On an individual level, you could live like a Trump (at least for a while) by getting a whole bunch of credit cards and maxing all of them out.</p>\n<p>But eventually a day of reckoning would come.</p>\n<p>The same thing happens on a national level.\u00a0 In recent years we have seen examples in Greece, Cyprus, Zimbabwe, Venezuela and various other European nations.</p>\n<p>Here in the United States, more than 9 trillion dollars was added to the national debt during the Obama years.\u00a0 If we had not taken more than 9 trillion dollars of consumption and brought it into the present, we would most assuredly be in the midst of an epic economic depression right now.</p>\n<p>Instead of taking our pain in the short-term, we have sold future generations of Americans as debt slaves, and if they get the chance someday they will look back and curse us for what we have done to them.</p>\n<p>Many believe that Donald Trump can make short-term economic conditions even better than Obama did, but how in the world is he going to do that?</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<p>Is he going to borrow another 9 trillion dollars?</p>\n<p>A big test is coming up.\u00a0 A while back, Barack Obama and the Republican Congress colluded to suspend the debt ceiling until March 15th, 2017, and this week we are going to hit that deadline.</p>\n<p>The U.S. Treasury will be able to implement \u201cemergency measures\u201d for a while, but if the debt ceiling is not raised the U.S. government will not be able to borrow more money and will run out of cash very quickly.\u00a0 The following comes from <a title=\"David Stockman\" href=\"https://dailyreckoning.com/mystery-treasurys-disappearing-cash/\" target=\"_blank\">David Stockman</a>\u2026</p>\n<blockquote><p><strong>The Treasury will likely be out of cash shortly after Memorial Day</strong>. That is, the White House will be in the mother of all debt ceiling battles before the Donald and his team even see it coming.</p>\n<p>With just <i>$66 billion </i>on hand it is now going to run out of cash before even the bloody battle over Obamacare Lite now underway in the House has been completed. <strong>That means that there will not be even a glimmer of hope for the vaunted Trump tax cut stimulus and economic rebound on the horizon</strong>.</p></blockquote>\n<p>Trump is going to find it quite challenging to find the votes to raise the debt ceiling.\u00a0 After everything that has happened, very few Democrats are willing to help Trump with anything, and many Republicans are absolutely against raising the debt ceiling without major spending cut concessions.</p>\n<p>So we shall see what happens.</p>\n<p>If the debt ceiling is not raised, it will almost certainly mean that a major political crisis and a severe economic downturn are imminent.</p>\n<p>But if the debt ceiling is raised, it will mean that Donald Trump and the Republicans in Congress are willingly complicit in the destruction of this country\u2019s long-term economic future.</p>\n<p>When you go into debt there are consequences.</p>\n<p>And when the greatest debt bubble in human history finally bursts, the consequences will be exceedingly severe.</p>\n<p>The best that our leaders can do for now is to keep the bubble alive for as long as possible, because what comes after the bubble is gone will be absolutely unthinkable.</p>\n<p><em>Courtesy of <a href=\"http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/21714-for-every-man-woman-and-child-in-the-world-this-global-debt-bomb-is-ready-to-explode\" target=\"_blank\">The Economic Collapse Blog</a></em></p>\n\n\n<h4><strong>Don't forget to follow the D.C.
