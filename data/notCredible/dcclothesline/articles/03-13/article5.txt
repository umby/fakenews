Totalitarianism\u201d</h1>\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-meta\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<span class=\"meta-prep meta-prep-author\">Posted on</span> <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/03/13/16-year-congressman-says-cia-leaks-prove-usa-rapidly-moving-toward-totalitarianism/\" title=\"4:40 am\" rel=\"bookmark\"><span class=\"entry-date\">March 13, 2017</span></a> <span class=\"meta-sep\">by</span> <span class=\"author vcard\"><a class=\"url fn n\" href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/author/annabelle-bamforth/\" title=\"View all posts by Annabelle Bamforth\">Annabelle Bamforth</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p>Democratic Congressman Dennis Kucinich recently published a searing critique of the federal government\u2019s spying powers in light of Wikileaks data releases that allege various <span id=\"more-70494\"></span>vulnerabilities inside the CIA, as well as the agency\u2019s capabilities to pursue surveillance of Americans with impunity. For a great number of years, Kucinich has stressed the importance of privacy and the consequences of federal overreach and was one of relatively few in Congress to stand up against legislation including the Patriot Act that has led to widespread spying with scarce oversight.</p>\n<p><strong>\u201cIf Tuesday\u2019s WikiLeaks document dump is authentic, as it appears to be, then the agency left open electronic gateways that make all Americans vulnerable to spying, eavesdropping and technological manipulation that could bring genuine harm,\u201d</strong> Kucinich <a href=\"http://www.foxnews.com/opinion/2017/03/08/dennis-kucinich-new-wikileaks-reveal-proof-are-sliding-down-slippery-slope-toward-totalitarianism.html\" target=\"_blank\">wrote.</a></p>\n<p>\u201cIt is bad enough that the government spies on its own people. <strong>It is equally bad that the CIA, through its incompetence, has opened the cyberdoor to anyone with the technological skills and connections to spy on anyone else,\u201d </strong>he said.</p>\n<p>\u201cThe constant erosion of privacy at the hands of the government and corporations has annihilated the concept of a \u201cright to privacy,\u201d which is embedded in the rationale of the First, Third, Fourth, Ninth and Fourteenth Amendments to the U.S. Constitution.</p>\n<h3><strong>\u201cIt is becoming increasingly clear that we are sliding down the slippery slope toward totalitarianism, where private lives do not exist.\u201d</strong></h3>\n<p>Kucinich went on to warn that \u201cWe have crossed the threshold of a cowardly new world, and <strong>it\u2019s time we tell the government and the corporations who have intruded to stop it.\u201d</strong></p>\n<p>Unfortunately, voices of dissent such as Kucinich are much harder to find than reports that gloss over or dismiss the revelations shared by Wikileaks. In the wake of last week\u2019s <a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/cia-vault-7-wikileaks-revelations/\">Wikileaks bombshell unveiling of \u201cVault 7\u201d</a> \u2013 the leak of an expansive and unsettling collection of documents that detail just how intrusive the CIA has become \u2013 the mainstream media\u2019s collective reaction has been nearly as telling as the documents themselves. Rather than providing an objective look at the data within the leak itself, a number of major news outlets have predictably opted to echo one another and focus instead almost entirely on their distaste for Wikileaks and its founder, Julian Assange.</p>\n<p>A plethora of popular news articles over the last few days show passive yet unified rejection of the information\u2019s legitimacy. The Washington Post wasted little time in attempting to <a href=\"https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/monkey-cage/wp/2017/03/08/wikileaks-strikes-again-here-are-4-big-questions-about-vault-7/?utm_term=.44493bc28ae3\" target=\"_blank\">minimize</a> Wikileaks\u2019 credibility, as well as questioning whether the Vault 7 leak has Russian ties. The Post claimed the release of documents \u201cshifts the narrative away from the hacking of the DNC and Russia\u2019s relationship with Trump\u201d and suggesting that people should \u201cconsider where the information came from and the lack of credibility WikiLeaks has as a news source.\u201d NBC\u2019s Today similarly <a href=\"http://www.today.com/video/cia-leaks-could-there-be-ties-between-russia-and-wikileaks-895610947511\" target=\"_blank\">indicated</a> a potential connection between Russia and the documents, although posed as a question.</p>\n<p>However, as we\u2019ve pointed out in the past, <strong>WikiLeaks has never released anything that was not entirely genuine and verifiable.</strong></p>\n<p>Fortune <a href=\"http://fortune.com/2017/03/11/wikileaks-cia-vault7-best-worst/\" target=\"_blank\">summarized</a> the leak as a collection of \u201cmisinformation\u201d and accused Wikileaks of serving as \u201ca well-oiled machine of information warfare, sowing panic, fear, and paranoia among the populace.\u201dA Forbes article, <a href=\"https://www.forbes.com/sites/leemathews/2017/03/08/the-wikileaks-vault-7-cia-dump-shouldnt-terrify-you/#700073626b8a\" target=\"_blank\">titled</a> \u201cWikiLeaks Vault 7 CIA Dump Offers Nothing But Old News,\u201d seeks to reassure the reader that \u201cthe overwhelming majority of us aren\u2019t likely to be targeted by the CIA.\u201d</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<p>A Foreign Policy post, titled \u201cWikileaks Has Joined the Trump Administration,\u201d <a href=\"http://foreignpolicy.com/2017/03/08/wikileaks-has-joined-the-trump-administration/\" target=\"_blank\">described</a> Wikileaks as an \u201canti-American group\u201d that \u201chas become the preferred intelligence service for a conspiracy-addled White House.\u201d This article centered around a possible Trump-Wikileaks kinship, noting that the president may be keeping quiet about the Vault 7 leaks because he could \u201cbenefit from\u201d them.</p>\n<p>However, beneath the surface of Vault 7 reports that seek to obfuscate the data, summaries, and analyses that expose the CIA\u2019s troubling hacking capabilities <a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/?s=%22vault+7%22\" target=\"_blank\">are able to be found</a> and are vital to bringing these disconcerting but crucial topics to light.</p>\n<p>The Independent published a straightforward <a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/gadgets-and-tech/features/wikileaks-cia-files-how-to-protect-privacy-tips-advice-what-to-do-spying-secrets-a7623366.html\" target=\"_blank\">explanation</a> of the Vault 7 leak as well as steps that can be taken to protect some personal data. The article also points out how Apple\u2019s response to the leaks serve as an admission of sorts regarding vulnerabilities exploited by the CIA: \u201cWhile our initial analysis indicates that many of the issues leaked today were already patched in the latest iOS, we will continue work to rapidly address any identified vulnerabilities.\u201d</p>\n<p>In addition, Edward Snowden publicly recognized the authenticity and implications of the information released through <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/03/08/vault-7-leak-confirms-cia-turns-smart-tvs-iphones-gaming-consoles-and-many-other-consumer-gadgets-into-open-microphones/\" target=\"_blank\">Vault 7</a>. \u201cEvidence mounts showing CIA &amp; FBI knew about catastrophic weaknesses in the most-used smartphones in America, but kept them open \u2014 to spy,\u201d he <a href=\"https://twitter.com/Snowden/status/839193727751098368\" target=\"_blank\">noted</a> in one tweet. In another, he <a href=\"https://twitter.com/Snowden/status/839168025517522944\">declared</a> that the leaks are \u201cfirst public evidence USG secretly paying to keep US software unsafe.\u201d</p>\n<p>It\u2019s important to note that Wikileaks dumped a massive amount of data at once in its <a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/03/10/5-major-revelations-from-vault-7-the-cia-media-do-not-want-you-to-know/\" target=\"_blank\">Vault 7</a> release, and proper, thorough analysis of this information is critical. When troves of documents are unleashed so quickly, it is possible for some parts of information to be initially overstated or misunderstood. But for the media to uniformly proclaim that all is settled- that the leaks are nothing but old news, insignificant bits of data not worthy of concern, or a nefarious disinformation campaign- is a disservice to the public.</p>\n<p><em>Courtesy of <a href=\"http://thefreethoughtproject.com/dennis-kucinich-vault-7-wikileaks-prove-private-lives-do-not-exist/\" target=\"_blank\">The Free Thought Project</a></em></p>\n\n\n<h4><strong>Don't forget to follow the D.C.
