Davis</a></span>\t\t\t\t\t</div><!-- .entry-meta -->\n\n\t\t\t\t\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<p>We are in the post-constitutional era in the United States, a time when the government does whatever it wants to whomever it wants, and there is not a thing anyone can do about it.<span id=\"more-71714\"></span></p>\n<p>How it is possible that the president can <a href=\"http://wakingtimesmedia.com/syria-strikes-conspiracy-theory/\" target=\"_blank\">bomb a foreign country</a> or <a href=\"http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/americas/us-politics/donald-trump-north-korea-nuclear-war-missile-tests-long-range-ballistic-aircraft-carrier-military-a7685616.html\" target=\"_blank\">threaten a full-scale international war</a> without so much as even mentioning the need for Congress to chime in, let alone actually vote on a declaration of war?</p>\n<p>The short answer is, disinformation and propaganda, which is <a href=\"http://www.wakingtimes.com/2015/11/24/4-examples-mainstream-media-fabricating-news-world-war/\" target=\"_blank\">the domain of mainstream media</a>. When people are confused, when truth is hidden, when agendas are presented as life or death options, and when the public has no clue about which laws government is bound to, then anything goes.</p>\n<p>Regarding disinformation, the following list of 25 rules of disinformation is from\u00a0<a href=\"http://www.whale.to/m/disin.html\" target=\"_blank\"><em>Twenty-Five Ways To Suppress Truth: The Rules of Disinformation (Includes The 8 Traits of A Disinformationalist) </em>by H. Michael Sweeney</a>, and offers a comprehensive way of processing information in a time of universal deceit.</p>\n<p>1. Hear no evil, see no evil, speak no evil. Regardless of what you know, don\u2019t discuss it \u2014 especially if you are a public figure, news anchor, etc. If it\u2019s not reported, it didn\u2019t happen, and you never have to deal with the issues.</p>\n<p>2. Become incredulous and indignant. Avoid discussing key issues and instead focus on side issues which can be used show the topic as being critical of some otherwise sacrosanct group or theme. This is also known as the \u201cHow dare you!\u201d gambit.</p>\n<p>3. Create rumor mongers. Avoid discussing issues by describing all charges, regardless of venue or evidence, as mere rumors and wild accusations. Other derogatory terms mutually exclusive of truth may work as well. This method works especially well with a silent press, because the only way the public can learn of the facts are through such \u201carguable rumors\u201d. If you can associate the material with the Internet, use this fact to certify it a \u201cwild rumor\u201d which can have no basis in fact.</p>\n<p>4. Use a straw man. Find or create a seeming element of your opponent\u2019s argument which you can easily knock down to make yourself look good and the opponent to look bad. Either make up an issue you may safely imply exists based on your interpretation of the opponent/opponent arguments/situation, or select the weakest aspect of the weakest charges. Amplify their significance and destroy them in a way which appears to debunk all the charges, real and fabricated alike, while actually avoiding discussion of the real issues.</p>\n<p>5. Sidetrack opponents with name calling and ridicule. This is also known as the primary attack the messenger ploy, though other methods qualify as variants of that approach. Associate opponents with unpopular titles such as \u201ckooks\u201d, \u201cright-wing\u201d, \u201cliberal\u201d, \u201cleft-wing\u201d, \u201cterrorists\u201d, \u201cconspiracy buffs\u201d, \u201cradicals\u201d, \u201cmilitia\u201d, \u201cracists\u201d, \u201creligious fanatics\u201d, \u201csexual deviates\u201d, and so forth. This makes others shrink from support out of fear of gaining the same label, and you avoid dealing with issues.</p>\n<p>6. Hit and Run. In any public forum, make a brief attack of your opponent or the opponent position and then scamper off before an answer can be fielded, or simply ignore any answer. This works extremely well in Internet and letters-to-the-editor environments where a steady stream of new identities can be called upon without having to explain criticism reasoning \u2014 simply make an accusation or other attack, never discussing issues, and never answering any subsequent response, for that would dignify the opponent\u2019s viewpoint.</p>\n<p>7. Question motives. Twist or amplify any fact which could so taken to imply that the opponent operates out of a hidden personal agenda or other bias. This avoids discussing issues and forces the accuser on the defensive.</p>\n<div style=\"clear:both; margin-top:0em; margin-bottom:1em;\"><a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/04/16/us-corporate-media-runs-fake-chinese-headline-that-almost-kicked-off-ww3/\" target=\"_blank\" class=\"u200c9fb1409905cf5068773f5e613306\"><!-- INLINE RELATED POSTS 1/2 //--><style> .u200c9fb1409905cf5068773f5e613306 { padding:0px; margin: 0; padding-top:1em!important; padding-bottom:1em!important; width:100%; display: block; font-weight:bold; background-color:inherit; border:0!important; border-left:4px solid inherit!important; text-decoration:none; } .u200c9fb1409905cf5068773f5e613306:active, .u200c9fb1409905cf5068773f5e613306:hover { opacity: 1; transition: opacity 250ms; webkit-transition: opacity 250ms; text-decoration:none; } .u200c9fb1409905cf5068773f5e613306 { transition: background-color 250ms; webkit-transition: background-color 250ms; opacity: 1; transition: opacity 250ms; webkit-transition: opacity 250ms; } .u200c9fb1409905cf5068773f5e613306 .ctaText { font-weight:bold; color:inherit; text-decoration:none; font-size: 16px; } .u200c9fb1409905cf5068773f5e613306 .postTitle { color:inherit; text-decoration: underline!important; font-size: 16px; } .u200c9fb1409905cf5068773f5e613306:hover .postTitle { text-decoration: underline!important; } </style><div style=\"padding-left:1em; padding-right:1em;\"><span class=\"ctaText\">READ MORE</span>&nbsp; <span class=\"postTitle\">US Corporate Media Runs Fake Chinese Headline that Almost Kicked Off WW3</span></div></a></div><p>8. Invoke authority. Claim for yourself or associate yourself with authority and present your argument with enough \u201cjargon\u201d and \u201cminutiae\u201d to illustrate you are \u201cone who knows\u201d, and simply say it isn\u2019t so without discussing issues or demonstrating concretely why or citing sources.</p>\n<p>9. Play Dumb. No matter what evidence or logical argument is offered, avoid discussing issues with denial they have any credibility, make any sense, provide any proof, contain or make a point, have logic, or support a conclusion. Mix well for maximum effect.</p>\n<p>10. Associate opponent charges with old news. A derivative of the straw man usually, in any large-scale matter of high visibility, someone will make charges early on which can be or were already easily dealt with. Where it can be foreseen, have your own side raise a straw man issue and have it dealt with early on as part of the initial contingency plans. Subsequent charges, regardless of validity or new ground uncovered, can usually them be associated with the original charge and dismissed as simply being a rehash without need to address current issues \u2014 so much the better where the opponent is or was involved with the original source.</p>\n<p>11. Establish and rely upon fall-back positions. Using a minor matter or element of the facts, take the \u201chigh road\u201d and \u201cconfess\u201d with candor that some innocent mistake, in hindsight, was made \u2014 but that opponents have seized on the opportunity to blow it all out of proportion and imply greater criminalities which, \u201cjust isn\u2019t so.\u201d Others can reinforce this on your behalf, later. Done properly, this can garner sympathy and respect for \u201ccoming clean\u201d and \u201cowning up\u201d to your mistakes without addressing more serious issues.</p>\n<p>12. Enigmas have no solution. Drawing upon the overall umbrella of events surrounding the crime and the multitude of players and events, paint the entire affair as too complex to solve. This causes those otherwise following the matter to begin to loose interest more quickly without having to address the actual issues.</p>\n<p>13. Alice in Wonderland Logic. Avoid discussion of the issues by reasoning backwards with an apparent deductive logic in a way that forbears any actual material fact.</p><div class=\"wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle\" style=\"margin: 5px; padding: 0px;\"><div id=\"ld-2901-1196\"></div><script>(function(w,d,s,i){w.ldAdInit=w.ldAdInit||[];w.ldAdInit.push({slot:8985198184511335,size:[0, 0],id:\"ld-2901-1196\"});if(!d.getElementById(i)){var j=d.createElement(s),p=d.getElementsByTagName(s)[0];j.async=true;j.src=\"//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/ajs.js\";j.id=i;p.parentNode.insertBefore(j,p);}})(window,document,\"script\",\"ld-ajs\");</script></div>\n<p>14. Demand complete solutions. Avoid the issues by requiring opponents to solve the crime at hand completely, a ploy which works best for items qualifying for rule 10.</p>\n<p>15. Fit the facts to alternate conclusions. This requires creative thinking unless the crime was planned with contingency conclusions in place.</p>\n<p>16. Vanishing evidence and witnesses. If it does not exist, it is not fact, and you won\u2019t have to address the issue.</p>\n<p>17. Change the subject. Usually in connection with one of the other ploys listed here, find a way to side-track the discussion with abrasive or controversial comments in hopes of turning attention to a new, more manageable topic. This works especially well with companions who can \u201cargue\u201d with you over the new topic and polarize the discussion arena in order to avoid discussing more key issues.</p>\n<p>18. Emotionalize, Antagonize, and Goad Opponents. If you can\u2019t do anything else, chide and taunt your opponents and draw them into emotional responses which will tend to make them look foolish and overly motivated, and generally render their material somewhat less coherent. Not only will you avoid discussing the issues in the first instance, but even if their emotional response addresses the issue, you can further avoid the issues by then focusing on how \u201csensitive they are to criticism\u201d.</p>\n<div style=\"clear:both; margin-top:0em; margin-bottom:1em;\"><a href=\"http://www.dcclothesline.com/2017/04/16/the-last-country-we-liberated-from-an-evil-dictator-is-now-openly-trading-slaves/\" target=\"_blank\" class=\"uf02c96e7005586adcdae41bdd225f086\"><!-- INLINE RELATED POSTS 2/2 //--><style> .uf02c96e7005586adcdae41bdd225f086 { padding:0px; margin: 0; padding-top:1em!important; padding-bottom:1em!important; width:100%; display: block; font-weight:bold; background-color:inherit; border:0!important; border-left:4px solid inherit!important; text-decoration:none; } .uf02c96e7005586adcdae41bdd225f086:active, .uf02c96e7005586adcdae41bdd225f086:hover { opacity: 1; transition: opacity 250ms; webkit-transition: opacity 250ms; text-decoration:none; } .uf02c96e7005586adcdae41bdd225f086 { transition: background-color 250ms; webkit-transition: background-color 250ms; opacity: 1; transition: opacity 250ms; webkit-transition: opacity 250ms; } .uf02c96e7005586adcdae41bdd225f086 .ctaText { font-weight:bold; color:inherit; text-decoration:none; font-size: 16px; } .uf02c96e7005586adcdae41bdd225f086 .postTitle { color:inherit; text-decoration: underline!important; font-size: 16px; } .uf02c96e7005586adcdae41bdd225f086:hover .postTitle { text-decoration: underline!important; } </style><div style=\"padding-left:1em; padding-right:1em;\"><span class=\"ctaText\">READ MORE</span>&nbsp; <span class=\"postTitle\">The Last Country We \u201cLiberated\u201d from an \u201cEvil\u201d Dictator Is Now Openly Trading Slaves</span></div></a></div><p>19. Ignore proof presented, demand impossible proofs. This is perhaps a variant of the \u201cplay dumb\u201d rule. Regardless of what material may be presented by an opponent in public forums, claim the material irrelevant and demand proof that is impossible for the opponent to come by (it may exist, but not be at his disposal, or it may be something which is known to be safely destroyed or withheld, such as a murder weapon). In order to completely avoid discussing issues may require you to categorically deny and be critical of media or books as valid sources, deny that witnesses are acceptable, or even deny that statements made by government or other authorities have any meaning or relevance.</p>\n<p>20. False evidence. Whenever possible, introduce new facts or clues designed and manufactured to conflict with opponent presentations as useful tools to neutralize sensitive issues or impede resolution. This works best when the crime was designed with contingencies for the purpose, and the facts cannot be easily separated from the fabrications.</p>\n<p>21. Call a Grand Jury, Special Prosecutor, or other empowered investigative body. Subvert the (process) to your benefit and effectively neutralize all sensitive issues without open discussion. Once convened, the evidence and testimony are required to be secret when properly handled. For instance, if you own the prosecuting attorney, it can insure a Grand Jury hears no useful evidence and that the evidence is sealed an unavailable to subsequent investigators. Once a favorable verdict (usually, this technique is applied to find the guilty innocent, but it can also be used to obtain charges when seeking to frame a victim) is achieved, the matter can be considered officially closed.</p>\n<p>22. Manufacture a new truth. Create your own expert(s), group(s), author(s), leader(s) or influence existing ones willing to forge new ground via scientific, investigative, or social research or testimony which concludes favorably. In this way, if you must actually address issues, you can do so authoritatively.</p>\n<p>23. Create bigger distractions. If the above does not seem to be working to distract from sensitive issues, or to prevent unwanted media coverage of unstoppable events such as trials, create bigger news stories (or treat them as such) to distract the multitudes.</p>\n<p>24. Silence critics. If the above methods do not prevail, consider removing opponents from circulation by some definitive solution so that the need to address issues is removed entirely. This can be by their death, arrest and detention, blackmail or destruction of their character by release of blackmail information, or merely by proper intimidation with blackmail or other threats.</p>\n<p>25. Vanish.
