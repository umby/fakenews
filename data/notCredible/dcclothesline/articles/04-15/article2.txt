and while it\u2019s now relishing in the news it has dropped the nation\u2019s largest most-powerful non-nuclear bomb on a cave complex in Eastern Afghanistan, one critic was quick to point out one little-known fact. Wikileaks tweeted a simple but true <span id=\"more-71619\"></span>statement concerning the origin of the cave complex the Americans are so proud to have reportedly destroyed. <strong>\u201cThose tunnels the U.S is bombing in Afghanistan? They were built by the CIA,\u201d</strong> <a href=\"https://mobile.twitter.com/wikileaks/status/852681705391521792\" target=\"_blank\">Wikileaks tweeted</a>.</p>\n<blockquote class=\"twitter-tweet\" data-width=\"550\">\n<p lang=\"en\" dir=\"ltr\">Those tunnels the U.S is bombing in Afghanistan? They were built by the CIA <a href=\"https://t.co/u66ScoiNpX\">https://t.co/u66ScoiNpX</a> (via <a href=\"https://twitter.com/GabiElenaDohm\">@GabiElenaDohm</a>) <a href=\"https://t.co/mAaUrQ0yLk\">pic.twitter.com/mAaUrQ0yLk</a></p>\n<p>&mdash; WikiLeaks (@wikileaks) <a href=\"https://twitter.com/wikileaks/status/852681705391521792\">April 14, 2017</a></p></blockquote>\n<p><script async src=\"//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js\" charset=\"utf-8\"></script></p>\n<p>Linked to the tweet was a <a href=\"http://www.nytimes.com/2005/09/11/magazine/lost-at-tora-bora.html\" target=\"_blank\">New York Times article from 2005</a>, which described a similar cave complex in detail, and added a few additional details worth noting. \u201cTora Bora\u201d as it\u2019s known, contains \u201cfortified caves\u201d which are reported to contain, \u201cmiles of tunnels, bunkers and base camps, dug deeply into the steep rock walls.\u201d The tunnels were built by the \u201cC.I.A.\u201d, with the help of the Bin Laden family, who constructed the complex.</p>\n<p>Also jumping in on the mockery of the Trump administration is Edward Snowden, who <a href=\"https://mobile.twitter.com/Snowden/status/852597443237732352?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw&amp;ref_url=http%3A%2F%2Ftheduran.com%2Fsnowden-afghan-bunker-moab-bomb%2F\" target=\"_blank\">tweeted</a>, \u201cThe bomb dropped today in the middle of nowhere, Afghanistan, cost $314,000,000.\u201d And in a follow up tweet, also said, \u201cThose mujahedeen tunnel networks we\u2019re bombing in Afghanistan? We paid for them.\u201d He, too, linked his tweet to The NY Times article mentioned above. Apparently, the ant farm network of tunnels built by the CIA can now be destroyed by the American military in yet another pseudo-show-of-force meant to make the Trump administration seem tough on Syria, ISIS, and Al Quaeda.</p>\n<p>It was said Bin Laden even helped to run the bulldozers at Tora Bora and knew the labyrinth of tunnels like the proverbial back of his hand. Financed by the intelligence agency to provide a headquarters and safe haven for the Mujahedeen, the Afghani fighting force was used to prevent the Russian takeover of Afghanistan during the Afghan War. After the Russians were defeated, the same Mujahedeen became known as Al-Qaeda, led by Osama Bin Laden.</p>\n<p>After the 9/11 attacks on the World Trade Center, Bin Laden became the United States\u2019 most wanted man. Not surprisingly, he took up refuge inside Tora Bora, within its miles and miles of complex, well-fortified tunnels. Times writer Mary Anne Weaver \u201casked Masood Farivar, a former Khalis officer who had fought in Tora Bora during the jihad, to tell me why the caves were so important. \u2018They\u2019re rugged, formidable and isolated,\u201d he said.</p>\n<p>\u201cIf you know them, you can come and go with ease. But if you don\u2019t, they\u2019re a labyrinth that you can\u2019t penetrate. They rise in some places to 14,000 feet, and for 10 years the Soviets pummeled them with everything they had, but to absolutely no avail. Maddox is now the Trump Administration\u2019s Secretary of Defense, and appears to have attempted to destroy at least a portion of Tora Bora\u2019s system of tunnels with the drop of the MOAB bomb, known as the \u201cMother Of All Bombs.\u201d</p>\n<p>The aging general who\u2019s now the nation\u2019s most powerful military leader, with the exception of Trump as Commander-in-Chief, appears to be following through with a sixteen year old vendetta he may have been holding onto. The Times article from 2005 writes, \u201cBrig. Gen. James N. Mattis\u2026along with another officer with whom I spoke, was convinced that\u2026he could have surrounded and sealed off bin Laden\u2019s lair\u2026He argued strongly that he should be permitted to proceed to the Tora Bora caves.\u201d</p>\n<p>However, according to Weaver, \u201cThe general was turned down,\u201d and the decision not to take Bin Laden there at Tora Bora was one of the greatest military mistakes of the Bush Administration\u2019s attempt to get him. Bin Laden escaped Tora Bora on December 16th, 2001.</p>\n<p>It is also important to note that Wikileaks\u2019 tweet may or may not be 100 percent accurate. <a href=\"http://www.cnn.com/2017/04/13/opinions/why-mother-of-all-bombs-and-why-now-bergen/\" target=\"_blank\">CNN</a> reported the MOAB bomb was dropped on a tunnel complex about 12 miles from Tora Bora. However, those tunnels likely all connected. CBS reported the airstrike destroyed several caves used by ISIS with it\u2019s 11-ton ordinance. The news agency also interviewed a military expert who said the U.S. has at least 14 more MOAB missiles and may be prepared to lay waste to all such cave systems.</p>\n<p>Meanwhile, on Thursday, the director of the CIA, Mike Pompeo, targeted Wikileaks as a \u201cnon-state hostile intelligence service often abetted by state actors like Russia.\u201d.
