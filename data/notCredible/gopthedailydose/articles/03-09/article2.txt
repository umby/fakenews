Benedict XVI, becoming the first pope to do so in almost 600 years. The official resignation statement said the swift exit was due to the pope&#8217;s waning physical and mental powers, but observers and analysts suspected there was more to the resignation that the church would let on. Questions as to the real reason behind Pope Benedict&#8217;s resignation were never fully answered that time.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<p>An Italian archbishop close to Pope Benedict XVI has claimed that the Obama administration may have been complicit in the \u201ctremendous pressures\u201d that led the former pope to resign in 2013.</p>\n<p>It is \u201cno coincidence\u201d that some Catholic groups \u201chave asked President Trump to open a commission of inquiry to investigate whether the administration of Barack Obama exerted pressure on Benedict,\u201d said Archbishop Luigi Negri in an\u00a0<a href=\"http://www.riminiduepuntozero.it/gravi-responsabilita-dentro-e-fuori-il-vaticano-per-le-dimissioni-di-benedetto-xvi-parla-mons-negri/\">interview</a>\u00a0Monday, citing other revelations by Wikileaks regarding efforts by the Democratic Party to sway the direction of the Catholic Church in the United States.</p>\n<p>\u201cIt remains shrouded in mystery for now,\u201d he said to news outlet Rimini 2.0, \u201cbut I am sure that those responsible will be found out.\u201d</p>\n<p>The archbishop was making reference to a\u00a0<a href=\"https://remnantnewspaper.com/web/index.php/articles/item/3001-did-vatican-attempt-to-influence-u-s-election-catholics-ask-trump-administration-to-investigate\">letter</a>\u00a0written by a group of American Catholics to President Trump last January requesting that the administration conduct an investigation into a possible Soros-Obama-Clinton conspiracy behind the resignation of Pope Benedict.</p>\n<p>The letter stated specifically that \u201cwe have reason to believe that a Vatican \u2018regime change\u2019 was engineered by the Obama administration.\u201d</p>\n<p>Approximately a year after an e-mail conversation released by Wikileaks, the authors contended, \u201cwe find that Pope Benedict XVI abdicated under highly unusual circumstances and was replaced by a pope whose apparent mission is to provide a spiritual component to the radical ideological agenda of the international left.\u201d</p>\n<p>In startling\u00a0<a style=\"font-style: inherit; font-weight: inherit;\" href=\"http://www.breitbart.com/big-government/2016/10/18/wikileaks-podestas-phony-catholic-group-pushed-church-support-iran-deal/\">revelations</a>\u00a0last October, Clinton campaign chief John Podesta was found to have created phony \u201cCatholic\u201d organizations in order to use Church leaders to push a liberal agenda in congress and to promote the agenda of the Democratic Party.</p>\n<p>In the midst of the hundreds of John Podesta\u2019s emails released by Wikileaks, one contained a report by Catholics in Alliance for the Common Good, a faux Catholic association founded by Podesta to provoke a revolution in the Catholic Church.</p>\n<p>The executive director of the organization, Christopher J. Hale, referred to the group\u2019s \u201cgrueling preparation for Pope Francis\u2019s apostolic trip to the US.\u201d</p>\n<p>Along with efforts to infiltrate the Church hierarchy, the organization\u2019s collusion with the Obama administration was also made apparent in Hale\u2019s report.</p>\n<p>\u201cI spoke to the White House yesterday and they assure us the media\u2019s moniker calling us \u2018God Squad\u2019 isn\u2019t just sweet nothings, but actually a fair assessment of the substantial difference we\u2019re making in this conversation,\u201d he wrote.</p>\n<p>Philadelphia Archbishop Charles Chaput described a meeting with two members of the other \u201cCatholic\u201d organization that Podesta claims to have founded\u2014Catholics United. In a published essay, the archbishop depicted a methodology eerily similar to that employed by Catholics in Alliance.</p>\n<p>Chaput said that the two men were \u201cobvious flacks for the Obama campaign and the Democratic Party\u2014creatures of a political machine, not men of the Church,\u201d who showed remarkable \u201ctalents of servile partisan hustling.\u201d</p>\n<p>In a separate statement, the head of the U.S. Bishops Conference, Archbishop Joseph E. Kurtz, issued an indictment of the Clinton team, calling them out for interfering in the Church\u2019s self-governance for \u201cshort-term political gain.\u201d</p>\n<p>Kurtz suggested that Podesta\u2019s actions in creating \u201cCatholic\u201d lobby groups for the Democratic Party constituted a breach of religious freedom, \u201cone of the founding principles of our republic,\u201d which ensures the right of faith communities to \u201cpreserve the integrity of their beliefs and proper self-governance.\u201d</p>\n<p>In his interview Monday, Archbishop Negri said he has visited Pope Benedict \u201cseveral times\u201d since the pontiff\u2019s resignation in 2013. Negri resigned last month as archbishop of Ferrara-Comacchio, a post assigned him by Pope Benedict, upon reaching the mandatory retirement age of 75.</p>\n<p>Archbishop Negri did not lay all the blame for Benedict\u2019s resignation on the Obama administration, but said he was \u201ccertain that the truth will emerge one day showing grave liability both inside and outside the Vatican.\u201d</p>\n<p>In the interview, Negri was also critical of Pope Francis for failing to answer the\u00a0<em style=\"font-weight: inherit;\">dubia</em>\u2014or \u201c<a style=\"font-style: inherit; font-weight: inherit;\" href=\"http://www.breitbart.com/national-security/2016/11/16/cardinal-burke-threatens-formal-act-correction-pope-francis/\">questions</a>\u201d\u2014made by four Cardinals regarding certain teachings in the Pope\u2019s letter\u00a0<em style=\"font-weight: inherit;\">Amoris Laetitia</em>\u00a0(The Joy of Love) that seem unclear to many in the Church.</p>\n<p>\u201c<em style=\"font-weight: inherit;\">Amoris Laetitia</em>\u00a0needs clarification, unfortunately, the current leader of the Church still remains silent,\u201d he said. \u201cI think that the Holy Father should respond, although he seems to have decided to the contrary.\u201d</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n<p>h/t:\u00a0usatwentyfour</p>\n<div class='code-block code-block-2'
