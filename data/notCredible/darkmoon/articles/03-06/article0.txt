<em><strong>The Truthseeker</strong>)</em></h5>\n<p><strong><img class=\"alignleft size-medium wp-image-62124\" src=\"https://www.darkmoon.me/uploads/xTrump-press-conference-300x187.jpg.pagespeed.ic.sDLy9k4umP.jpg\" alt=\"\" width=\"300\" height=\"187\" srcset=\"https://www.darkmoon.me/uploads/Trump-press-conference-300x187.jpg 300w, https://www.darkmoon.me/uploads/Trump-press-conference-150x93.jpg 150w, https://www.darkmoon.me/uploads/Trump-press-conference.jpg 549w\" sizes=\"(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px\"/>President Donald Trump is accusing Barack Obama of ordering his telephones tapped during last year\u2019s elections, offering no evidence while invoking politically charged references to Watergate, Nixon and McCarthyism.</strong></p>\n<p><strong>An Obama spokesman responded that the assertion against the former president was \u201csimply false.\u201d Trump\u2019s claim also drew bipartisan rebukes from Democrats and Republicans alike.</strong></p>\n<p><strong>In a series of morning tweets Saturday, Trump suggested Obama was behind a politically motivated plot to upend his campaign. He compared the alleged events to \u201cNixon/Watergate\u201d and \u201cMcCarthyism!\u201d And he called Obama a \u201cBad (or sick) guy.\u201d</strong><span id=\"more-62123\"></span></p>\n<p>The Watergate break-in during the Nixon administration led to President Richard Nixon\u2019s resignation and the conviction of several aides. Republican Sen. Joe McCarthy\u2019s reckless and unsupported charges of communist infiltration in federal government during the 1950s gave rise to the term \u201cMcCarthyism.\u201d</p>\n<p>After Trump\u2019s well-received speech to Congress on Tuesday, the tweets reflected the president\u2019s growing frustration with the swirling allegations about his advisers\u2019 ties to Russia, which are under FBI investigation, and his team\u2019s inability to overcome them. Trump lashed out at his senior team during an Oval Office meeting Friday, according to one White House official.</p>\n<p>The White House did not respond to questions about what prompted the president\u2019s accusations that Obama had tapped his phones. Presidents cannot legally order wiretaps against U.S. citizens. Obtaining wiretaps would require officials at the Justice Department to seek permission from the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court, which is shrouded in secrecy.</p>\n<p>Trump said Saturday morning he had \u201cjust found out\u201d the information, though it was unclear whether he was referring to a briefing, a conversation or a media report. The president has in the past tweeted about unsubstantiated and provocative reports he reads on blogs or conservative websites.</p>\n<p>The morning tweets stand out, even for the perpetually piqued Trump, given the gravity of the charge and the strikingly personal attack on the former president. Trump spoke as recently as last month about how much he likes Obama and how much they get along, despite their differences.</p>\n<p>In his morning tweets, Trump said the wiretapping occurred in October at Trump Tower, the New York skyscraper where he ran his campaign and transition. He also maintains a residence there.</p>\n<p>\u201cHow low has President Obama gone to tapp my phones during the very sacred election process. This is Nixon/Watergate. Bad (or sick) guy!\u201d he tweeted, misspelling \u2018tap.\u2019</p>\n<p>Obama spokesman Kevin Lewis said a \u201ccardinal rule\u201d of the Obama administration was that no White House official ever interfered in Justice Department investigations, which are supposed to be conducted free of political influence.</p>\n<p>\u201cAs part of that practice, neither President Obama nor any White House official ever ordered surveillance on any U.S. citizen,\u201d Lewis said, adding that \u201cany suggestion otherwise is simply false.\u201d</p>\n<p>Trump has been trailed for months by questions about his campaign\u2019s ties to Russia. The questions have been compounded by U.S. intelligence agencies\u2019 assessment that Russia interfered with the election to help Trump triumph over Hillary Clinton, along with disclosures about his aides\u2019 contacts with a Russian official.</p>\n<p>Those disclosures have already cost retired Gen. Michael Flynn his job as national security adviser and prompted calls from Democrats for Attorney General Jeff Sessions to resign.</p>\n<p>On Thursday, Sessions recused himself from the FBI probe after acknowledging he did not disclose his campaign-season contacts with Russia\u2019s ambassador to the United States when asked during his confirmation proceedings. Sessions, a U.S. senator at the time, was Trump\u2019s earliest Senate supporter.</p>\n<p>The Sessions revelations deepened the president\u2019s anger over what he sees as his team\u2019s inability to get ahead of the Russia allegations. In the Oval Office meeting Friday shortly before departing for Florida, he angrily told senior advisers that what had the potential to be a good week following his address to Congress had been overtaken by the Russia controversy, according to a White House official who insisted on anonymity in order to discuss the private meeting.</p>\n<p>The president\u2019s allegations may be related to anonymously sourced reports in British media and blogs, and on conservative-leaning U.S. websites, including Breitbart News. Those reports claimed that U.S. officials had obtained a warrant under the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act to review contacts between computers at a Russian bank and Trump\u2019s New York headquarters.</p>\n<p>The Associated Press has not confirmed these contacts or the investigation into them. Trump\u2019s chief strategist Steve Bannon is a former executive chairman of Breitbart News.</p>\n<p style=\"text-align: center;\"><strong>Sourced from <a href=\"http://www.thetruthseeker.co.uk/?p=148022\">The Truthseeker</a></strong></p>\n<hr/>\n<p><em><strong>Watch this relevant video on the Orwellian US media as seen through Russian eyes.
